<div><div><p>Like that loungewear, this plate is pure elegance and ease, a finger food of crisp endive spears cradling succulent crab salad. (If you can’t find endive, little gem lettuce leaves work well.)</p></div></div>
<div><div><p>Showered with fresh cilantro, the salad looks like a celebration, and it tastes like one, too. It’s my edible wish to all, for a bright, healthy and pleasurable year to come.</p></div></div>

<div><div><h3><div>Ingredients</div></h3></div></div>
<li><span><b>8 ounces lump crabmeat, picked over for shells and cartilage</b></span></li>
<li><span><b>1/4 cup finely diced ripe mango</b></span></li>
<li><span><b>1 medium scallion (white and green parts), thinly sliced</b></span></li>
<li><span><b>2 tablespoons finely diced jalapeño (seeded for less heat)</b></span></li>
<li><span><b>1 lime, finely zested and juiced (you will need 1 teaspoon zest and 2 tablespoons juice)</b></span></li>
<li><span><b>1 tablespoon mayonnaise</b></span></li>
<li><span><b>1/4 teaspoon fine salt</b></span></li>
<li><span><b>12 large endive leaves (from 2 large endives)</b></span></li>
<li><span><b>2 tablespoons torn fresh cilantro leaves</b></span></li>
<div><div><h3><div>Step 1</div></h3></div></div>
<div><div><p>In a medium bowl, stir together the crab, mango, scallion, jalapeño, lime zest and juice, mayonnaise and salt until combined.</p></div></div>
<div><div><h3><div>Step 2</div></h3></div></div>
<div><div><p>Divide the crab salad among the endive leaves and place on a serving plate. Sprinkle with the cilantro leaves and serve.</p></div></div>

<h3>Chicken Tender Fajitas</h3>
<div> <div>More from the Food Section</div> </div>
<div> <div> <div> <ul> <li> Accessibility for screenreader </li> </ul> </div> </div> </div>
<div> <div> <div> <a> <div> Link to homepage </div> </a><div>Resize Text</div><a> <div>Print Article</div> </a></div> </div> </div>
<p><b>Active time:</b> 30 mins; <b>Total time:</b> 1 hour</p>
<p>Storage Notes: Leftover soup can be refrigerated for up to 3 days.</p>
<select> <option> 2 </option> <option> 4 </option> <option> 6 </option> <option> 12 </option> <option> 18 </option> </select>
<div> <p>When you scale a recipe, keep in mind that cooking times and temperatures, pan sizes and seasonings may be affected, so adjust accordingly. Also, amounts listed in the directions will not reflect the changes made to ingredient amounts.</p> </div>
<p>Tested size: 6 servings; scant 12 cups</p>
<li><div>2 tablespoons olive oil</div></li>
<li><div>2 medium carrots (5 ounces total), diced</div></li>
<li><div>2 stalks celery, diced</div></li>
<li><div>1 medium yellow onion (about 8 ounces), diced</div></li>
<li><div>1 tablespoon finely grated fresh ginger</div></li>
<li><div>2 cloves garlic, minced or finely grated</div></li>
<li><div>1 teaspoon fine salt</div></li>
<li><div>1/2 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper</div></li>
<li><div>1/2 teaspoon ground turmeric</div></li>
<li><div>8 cups low-sodium chicken broth</div></li>
<li><div>8 ounces boneless, skinless chicken breast</div></li>
<li><div>One (15-ounce) can no-salt-added chickpeas, drained and rinsed or 1 1/2 cups cooked chickpeas</div></li>
<li><div>4 ounces green beans, trimmed and cut into 1-inch pieces (1 cup)</div></li>
<li><div>2 cups baby spinach, coarsely chopped</div></li>
<p>Reduce the heat to medium-low and add the chicken. Simmer, uncovered, until the chicken is cooked through, 20 to 25 minutes. Transfer the chicken to a plate.</p>
<p>Add the chickpeas and green beans to the pot, increase the heat to medium-high and return the broth to a boil. Reduce the heat to medium-low and simmer, uncovered, until the green beans are tender, about 10 minutes. </p>

<div><div><p>This salad, from “Spicebox Kitchen” by Linda Shiue, looks to the Indonesian island of Bali for a refreshing way to spice up everyday staples — apple, celery and onion.</p></div></div>
<div><div><p>Tossed in a bright, tart lime dressing spiked with fresh ginger and chiles, the salad provides a crisp counterpoint for roasted or grilled proteins.</p></div></div>
<div><div><p>Shiue, a trained chef and physician, says that taking full advantage of the flavor that spices such as these have to offer<b> </b>helps home cooks "rely less on sugar, salt and fat.”</p></div></div>

<div><div><p>Extra dressing will pool on the bottom of the bowl; spoon some over whatever else you’re having with your salad, such as rice or a protein.</p></div></div>
<div><div><p><b>Storage Notes:</b> Fish sauce can be found at well-stocked supermarkets, Asian markets or online.</p></div></div>
<div><div><p><b>NOTE</b>: Thai bird chile peppers can be quite spicy. Remove the seeds and membrane to reduce the heat, before slicing; add more peppers for a spicier salad.</p></div></div>

<div><div><h3><div>Ingredients</div></h3></div></div>
<div><div><p><b>For the dressing</b></p></div></div>
<li><span><b>⅔ cup fresh lime juice (from about 5 limes)</b></span></li>
<li><span><b>1½ teaspoons grated fresh ginger</b></span></li>
<li><span><b>2 teaspoons granulated sugar</b></span></li>
<li><span><b>2 teaspoons fish sauce (may be substituted with soy sauce)</b></span></li>
<li><span><b>2 teaspoons water</b></span></li>
<li><span><b>2 fresh Thai bird chile peppers or another hot peppers, thinly sliced (see NOTE)</b></span></li>
<div><div><p><b>For the salad</b></p></div></div>
<li><span><b>1 large or 2 small Granny Smith apples, unpeeled</b></span></li>
<li><span><b>½ small red onion (2 to 3 ounces) or 1 large shallot, thinly sliced</b></span></li>
<li><span><b>1 stalk celery, finely chopped</b></span></li>
<li><span><b>¼ cup roasted peanuts, finely chopped or crushed</b></span></li>
<div><div><h3><div>Step 1</div></h3></div></div>
<div><div><p>Make the dressing: In a large bowl, combine lime juice, ginger, sugar, fish sauce, water and chile peppers.</p></div></div>
<div><div><h3><div>Step 2</div></h3></div></div>
<div><div><p>Sprinkle the salad with the peanuts and serve family-style.</p></div></div>
