<p>Although intimacy, <a>friendship</a>, familiarity, connection, and safety are hallmarks of a successful relationship, there are seven essential principles that define a loving (and healthy) relationship.</p>
<p>1. You participate in each other’s learning and growth. </p>
<p>2. You value and respect each other’s individuality and boundaries. </p>
<p>We are all different. Our individual differences should never be seen as being “less than” someone else’s, but as opportunities to gain a new perspective. A healthy relationship nurtures and embraces each of our special qualities.</p>
<p>In a healthy, loving relationship you respect your partner’s boundaries. You give each other the space you need away from the relationship to be alone, to be with friends, and to pursue your own interests.</p>
<p>3. You encourage healthy communication and dialogue. </p>
<p>4. You share like or similar values.</p>
<p>5. You trust each other without question. </p>
<a>confidence</a> that no matter what happens, your partner will remain loyal to you and the relationship, will honor their commitments, will not lie, and will remain open to working out whatever difficulties arise.
<p>6. You share major life decisions and choices.</p>
<a>wisdom</a>. Learning how to give and take is an important process in problem-solving. But above all, each partner should contribute equally to making major decisions and choices.
<p>7. You are able to let things go and move on. </p>

<h1> The ABCs of a Caring Relationship </h1>
<h2> Identifying the qualities you're searching for in a partner. </h2>
<p> Posted September 29, 2017 </p>
<div> <div> <div> <div>Source: g-stockstudio/Shutterstock</div> </div> </div> </div>
<p>Here’s my list A to Z of what qualities create a caring relationship.</p>
<p>Benevolent: This means being kindhearted and compassionate to your partner, which allows for the creation of an environment and a general atmosphere where a partner feels heard and understood. </p>
<a>Conscientious</a>: Relationships need constant
<a>attention</a>, work, and tweaking. This implies the recognition that by being consistently attentive to your partner and the life you are building together, you are paving the way to accomplish your
<a>goals</a> and most desired achievements.
<p>Generous: There are so many ways to be generous. Certainly, you can give gifts, but true <a>generosity</a> means that you freely give of your time, energy, and effort.</p>
<a>Humor</a> helps enormously. Healthy-living implies the desire to take care of yourself, not only for your own benefit, but for the benefit of the couple as well.
<p>Just: This means being fair to your partner, honoring who they are as an individual apart from the couple. </p>
<p>Loving: What's an intimate, caring relationship without love?</p>
<p>Mature: This means often taking the high road, trying to understand someone else’s perspective (especially when it’s different from your own), and being able to see into the future for results over the long haul.</p>
<p>Nurturing: When we nurture, we take care of someone’s needs and desires, oftentimes even more than our own. This implies that we offer attention and compassion, things that feed the heart and soul.</p>
<p>Open: This is similar to honesty and says that we keep ourselves aware and willing to take in new ideas and points of view. We keep ourselves available and willing to listen. </p>
<p>Quality: This is the ability to distinguish that high ideal and/or standard may be far more important than quantity. We strive for the best we can achieve as individuals and as a couple in order to better our lives.</p>
<p>Reasonable: We are able to take things into consideration and weigh ideas, beliefs, and perspectives thoughtfully and without <a>prejudice</a>.</p>
<p>Soulful: This implies that we are in touch with our essence, that which makes us uniquely who we are. Bringing our essence, our soul, into a relationship with another enhances the quality of the relationship immeasurably.</p>
<p>Truthful: Although this is similar to honesty, it further implies that we openly express our personal views and feelings, as much as we know them at any point in our life, to the best of our ability. </p>
<p>United: Regardless of differences, the couple agrees to compromise in order to create a united front, to work together for a common goal that ultimately benefits everyone.</p>
<p>Virtuous: We strive for standards that are high-minded, morally and ethically. As individuals and as a couple, we honor those principled standards that are the foundation of what it means to be a human being. </p>
<p>Willing: This means that to the best of your ability you are present and open to meeting the challenges and tasks of life moving forward. It is the ability to want to try.</p>
<p>Yin/Yang: This may seem a bit odd, but for me, the ability of each partner to develop an appreciation for the male/female within each of us helps us to appreciate our partner’s perspective in a unique way.</p>
<p>Zeal: We wholeheartedly dive into life with enthusiasm and a sense of wonder.</p>
<p>As an exercise, it might be interesting to choose a word/concept that you then work with every day, week, or month to help you refocus on a quality that is important to you in a relationship, but that you might have lost sight of along the way.</p>
