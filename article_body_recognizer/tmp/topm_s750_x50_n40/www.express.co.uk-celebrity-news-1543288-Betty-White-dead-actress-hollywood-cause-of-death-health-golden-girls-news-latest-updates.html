US actress Betty White has died aged 99. The legendary star died on Friday morning, just weeks before turning 100. The Hollywood actor was known as Rose in The Golden Girls, as well as her nine-decades-long career in showbusiness.

Betty's death was confirmed New Year's Eve as her agent shared she had died earlier this morning. 

Currently, no cause of death has been confirmed for the legendary actor.

In a statement to Deadline, her agent and close friend Jeff Witjas relayed the news.

He said: "Even though Betty was about to be 100, I thought she would live forever.

"I will miss her terribly and so will the animal world that she loved so much."

He continued: "I don't think Betty ever feared passing because she always wanted to be with her most beloved husband Allen Ludden.

"She believed she would be with him again."

White had met the love of her life while working on his gameshow in 1961, and they were together until his death in 1981.

The star was well known for her long career on screen, which spanned over nine decaces.

Some of her biggest roles included The Golden Girls, The Mary Tyler Moore Show and Hot in Cleveland.

Born in 1922, the star began her career at just eight when she starred on her first radio show.

This means she's worked longer than anyone else in the television industry.

She had recently opened up about preparing for her 100th birthday this year.

Betty was due to mark the landmark occasion on January 17.

Speaking to PEOPLE earlier this month, she had said: "I'm so lucky to be in such good health and feel so good at this age. It's amazing."

She was gearing up to appear in a special to mark the occasion as well, which would screen in US cinemas.

It was set to feature stars like Ryan Reynolds, Tina Fey, Robert Redford and Lin Manuel- Miranda, and air on her birthday.

Betty is known for plenty of iconic television roles but her big break in acting came back in 1949 when she starred in Hollywood on Television opposite Al Jarvis.

She later landed The Betty White Show before she began acting in Life in Elizabeth. Then came The Golden Girls in the 1980s.

After the sitcom ended, she went on to appear in That '70s Show, Boston Legal, The Bold and the Beautiful and the highly-acclaimed Hot in Cleveland.

Alongside this, she has starred in countless films, including the hit The Proposal back in 2009.

She won five Primetime Emmys and was nominated three times for the Screen Actors Guild Awards.

Tributes have begun pouring in following the news of her death earlier today.
