Graham Norton blasted Brexit 'revolution' in impassioned rant

GRAHAM NORTON once delivered an impassioned rant about Brexit, during which he said Brexiteers had "hitched their wagon to a moron".

Graham Norton delivers his traditional Hogmanay chat this evening on BBC One. As the nation waves goodbye to a rollercoaster 2021, Graham is joined by a host of big names. Oscar nominee Jessica Chastain, actor Claire Foy, Game of Thrones star Peter Dinklage, Twilight’s Michael Sheen, actress Cush Jumbo and comedian Joe Lycett all join Graham in the studio for a final festive get-together.

Pop band The Divine Comedy will also perform their classic ‘Something for the Weekend’, and some final stories from the Big Red Chair will help viewers wave goodbye to a year filled with ups and downs.

Graham grew up in Clondalkin, County Dublin in a Protestant family. He moved to London in the late Eighties to attend the Central School of Speech and Drama.

The BBC’s third-highest paid star has voiced his political views on occasion.

One example came at the West Cork Literary Festival two years ago.

Graham said a “revolution” had occurred in the UK, from neither the right nor the left, but instead “from the stupid”.

He called on Brexiteers to “look at their victory parade” and watch who was “dancing the hardest and shouting the loudest”.

In a sly dig at Prime Minister Boris Johnson, a leading Brexit campaigner, he said those who had voted to leave had “hitched their wagon to a moron”.

It was not the first time he had spoken out on the Brexit debacle, however.

He appeared on The Late Late Show in 2018, and dished the dirt on the “pack of lies” told during the referendum.

Speaking to Ryan Turbidy, he said: “I was astonished that people bought the pack of lies they were sold and I feel sorry for the people who voted for it because they were lied to.

“They were promised things that are never going to happen and they were told [about] the things that are now unfolding: ‘Oh don’t worry, that won’t happen.’”

He expressed his dismay that the youth of today will be affected most by the UK’s decision to leave the bloc.

He said: “What is great about being young is you’ve so many options. Life — all the doors are open, every door is open.

“What is so sad about Brexit is that people over 60, because it was people over 60 passed that thing, closed so many doors on young people and shut down options.”

YouGov polling in 2016 found over-65s were more than twice as likely as under-25s to have voted to leave the EU.

Of the nearly 5,500 adults surveyed, just 29 percent of people aged between 18 and 24 voted to leave, compared with 64 percent in the over-65s.

He said he would not trust the Prime Minister to 'water my plants when I was away'. (Image: GETTY)

Graham said those who voted to leave had “shut down options about studying abroad, living abroad, working in places”.

He added: “It just seemed absolutely the wrong instinct.

“Don’t make the world smaller, don’t shut things down.

“I understand where the fear comes from but, actually, I think it is sad.”

Appearing on the same show a year later, Graham said it felt “in a sweet way” as if the UK was “embarrassed for America”.

He explained: “[Britain] felt like you’re all alone out on the world stage so we found our own angry Cabbage Patch Kid and made him the leader.”

He added he would not trust Boris Johnson “to water my plants when I was away but somehow he is the Prime Minister”.

The Graham Norton Show airs at 10:20pm on New Year’s Eve.
