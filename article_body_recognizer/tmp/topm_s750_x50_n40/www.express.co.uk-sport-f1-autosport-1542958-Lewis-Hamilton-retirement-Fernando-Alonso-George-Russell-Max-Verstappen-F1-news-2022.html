Lewis Hamilton retirement, Fernando Alonso plan: Five things to look out for in F1 in 2022

THE year 2022 holds plenty of excitement for the world of Formula One with new regulations incoming for the new season which begins in March. Lewis Hamilton, Max Verstappen, George Russell and perhaps Fernando Alonso are among those set to dominate the New Year's F1 headlines.

The year 2021 played host to a whole load of drama in the world of Formula One and there will be plenty more thrilling plotlines in 2022 with the new season, which will oversee some major regulations changes, getting underway towards the end of March. Express Sport takes a look at some of main talking points in F1 at the start of the New Year.

Trending

Hamilton to retire?

The thing everyone's talking about - will Lewis Hamilton really retire from F1? Despite his current social media silence following the misery of the Briton's Abu Dhabi Grand Prix, hopefully not.

The seven-time world champion was denied his eighth triumph in agonising circumstances at the Yas Marina Circuit and the Mercedes driver is taking some time away from the spotlight to fully digest it.

His brother Nicolas said: "I think he is just having a bit of a social media break, which I don't blame him for. Social media can be a very toxic place. But he's cool though. He's fine." While Toto Wolff has said that Hamilton is in constant contact with the team.

Hamilton is under contract with Mercedes until the end of the 2023 season and has always said he will remain an F1 driver while he retains his hunger. He may still be pained by missing out that record-breaking eighth championship in 2021 but in the races before that he showed he was as hungry, and as fast, as ever. Brazil is one such example.

He should line up for the Silver Arrows in the new season with he and the rest of his team looking to get their own back.

Max to go back-to-back?

While Hamilton still simmers, Red Bull's Max Verstappen will be loving every minute of his post-season break as the new world champion, and the first ever from Holland.

The Dutchman is still only 24 years old and last season showed remarkable consistency as he picked up 10 race wins, doubling his career total to now stand on 20.

He clinched a stunning 18 podiums in 21 races and only finished lower than second in one of the 20 races in which he finished, at the Hungarian Grand Prix where he ended ninth and only because of damage from an opening-lap collision.

Since 2010, only Nico Rosberg has failed to defend his title - and that was because he retired from the sport altogether after his 2016 triumph.

Between 2010 and 2013 Sebastian Vettel won four consecutive titles before Hamilton won back-to-back championships in 2014 and 2015. Then came Rosberg's victory, before Hamilton was triumphant four seasons on the spin until this year.

Now it's up to Red Bull and Verstappen to retain the crown. If Red Bull's 2022 produces similar pace to their current package, and Verstappen can avoid the kind of DNFs he suffered at Silverstone and in Baku, he's a very strong bet to become a two-time champion.

Russell to enter title fight?

We're all accustomed to Hamilton and Verstappen being chief protagonists in the fight for Drivers' Championship supremacy - but we may have a new contender in 2022.

George Russell replaces Valtteri Bottas at Mercedes after a very respectable tally of four points finishes in his final year with Williams, which included an unbelievable second-placed finish at the rained-off Belgian Grand Prix.

The 23-year-old has shown huge promise in F1, particularly in qualifying, both at Williams and with Mercedes having stepped in for a Covid-hit Hamilton at the 2020 Sakhir Grand Prix where he ended P9 only due to a team pitstop error and then a slow puncture.

It remains to be seen how Russell will fare at Mercedes in the greater pressures of driving for a championship challenger, though he says his hardships with Williams will stand him in good stead.

Hamilton's fellow Briton is seen as the future of the Silver Arrows, but can he also be the present? It will be interesting to see if the pair are as harmonious as Bottas and Hamilton were.

Russell will certainly hope to take more race wins than the Finn managed for Mercedes (10 in five years, two in the last 41 races.

Alonso's 'El Plan'

Fernando Alonso was one of F1's most impressive drivers in 2021, finishing 10th in the driver's standings and ending a seven-year wait for a podium at the Qatar Grand Prix.

The two-time world champion with Renault and former Ferrari driver has said for 2022 he expects to have "more fun" for Alpine.

"It's a big year with the new regulation changes," the Spaniard, now 40, said. "We hope to be one of the teams who capitalise on them and be more competitive next year. 'El Plan' is still loading and we'll have some fun that's for sure."

He also warned post-Qatar: "The preparation into next year is definitely another level now."

If Alpine get it right with their 2022 build, 32-time Grands Prix winner Alonso has the natural ability at the wheel to be a real problem for the likes of not just McLaren and Ferrari, but also Red Bull and Mercedes.

And that's something Verstappen would love to see, the 23-year-old saying recently of Alonso: "I would like to see him back at the front, he really deserves it."

Ferrari revival

Another team, like Alpine, looking to take full advantage of the tweaks in regulations are Ferrari. F1's most successful team of all time are without a driver's title since 2007 and a team title since 2008.

They are also without a race victory since Vettel's 2019 Singapore Grand Prix triumph, their future title hopeful Charles Leclerc having picked up just five podiums since then, one shy of as many team-mate Carlos Sainz managed in 2021.

In Leclerc and Sainz - who Ferrari say will be given equal status next season - they have a formidable partnership, one sporting director Laurent Mekies thinks gives them a "significant competitive advantage".

If they can supply the pair with stronger machinery for the new season, the duo can eat into the 262-point gap that separated the famous Prancing Horse and second-placed rivals Red Bull last year.

It is surely too early to dream of a Ferrari title triumph in 2022 but having the iconic Italian constructors on the podium more often would certainly be a welcome sight.
