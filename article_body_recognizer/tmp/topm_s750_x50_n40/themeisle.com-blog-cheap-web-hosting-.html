What to expect from cheap web hosting

Your first question is probably something like, “how can cheap web hosts offer rock-bottom prices while other options charge $30+ per month?”

Here’s how cheap web hosting providers keep the costs down:

  • Shared hosting. With this type of hosting, you’ll share server resources with other people and the performance might not be as good as more expensive hosts. Virtual private servers (VPS) and cloud hosting usually offer better performance, but at a higher price.
  • Not as many value-added features as more expensive hosts. While more expensive managed WordPress hosts might offer features like automatic backups and staging sites, cheap web hosting is usually a little more barebones.
  • Long commitments to get the lowest prices. Typically, you’ll need to commit to at least a year of hosting. And if you want the lowest advertised rate, you’ll need to commit to ~3 years.
  • Promotional pricing. Typically, the cheapest prices only apply to your first billing cycle. After that, it’s normal for the price to increase.

Is cheap web hosting a good option for you?

For a lot of webmasters, those trade-offs are worth the price savings that you get with cheap web hosting. If you have a small WordPress blog or your site doesn’t get a lot of traffic and you’re willing to give up some convenient features like automatic backups, you can find cheap web hosting that’s reliable and fast enough for most sites.

Six cheap web hosting platforms that are still worth it

1. Hostinger (www.hostinger.com)

Known for its affordable products, Hostinger is a well-rounded web hosting service. Regardless of the price, Hostinger also offers good uptime.

Pricing

When I say cheap hosting, Hostinger is among the very few companies who manage to stay below the $2 mark. Currently, the entry-level plans cost $1.39/mo. These low rates are available for long term subscription. Hostinger plans are:

  • Single – $1.39 per month for one site, 30 GB of storage, and up to 10,000 monthly visits.
  • Premium – $2.59 per month for 100 websites, 100 GB of SSD storage, free unlimited email and free domain.
  • Business – $3.99 per month for 100 sites, 200 GB storage, 100,000 visits a month, plus everything in Premium.

Ease of setup

The Hostinger dashboard does not run on classic cPanel, which we usually see with other hosting providers. Hostinger has put their own spin on it, calling it hpanel. It is beginner-friendly and easy to set up.

All the plans including shared web hosting come with WordPress features such as WordPress installers, which makes it easy to get started with a website quickly. You can see these features in the Website section, under Auto Installer in your Hostinger dashboard.

Customer support

Hostinger support is available 24/7 in multiple languages along with a knowledge base. You can reach out to their support via email or live chat. Hostinger does not have phone support.

2. Bluehost (www.bluehost.com)

Bluehost is one of the old reliables, seeing as how it was one of the first hosts to be endorsed by WordPress, and the fact that it has managed to always make hosting affordable.

Pricing

Bluehost pricing is strictly based on how many months you pay for in advance. For instance, a 36-month plan is cheaper per month than a 24-month plan.

We’ll outline the 36-month plans below:

  • Basic – $2.75 per month for one website, 50GB of storage, and five email accounts.
  • Plus – $5.45 per month for unlimited websites, unlimited storage, and unlimited email accounts, and spam support.
  • Choice Plus – $5.45 per month for unlimited websites, unlimited storage, unlimited email accounts, spam support, domain privacy, and site backups.

Ease of setup

The Bluehost dashboard is one of the easiest ones to use out there. There’s a simple one-click install for WordPress, as well as simple controls to take care of the basic customizations of your setup.

You also get a free SSL if you need to secure an online store.

We recommend you to check other cheap WordPress hosting providers and see which hosts actually offer the best value for your money.

Customer support

Technical support is available 24/7 through phone, online chat, or email. You can also access educational resources including FAQs, online articles, and setup information.

Go to Bluehost

3. A2 Hosting (www.a2hosting.com)

A2 Hosting has a reputation for offering faster speeds than most shared hosts. It’s also quite affordable and user-friendly for beginners.

Pricing

You can choose between Linux or Windows hosting. Both pricing plans are similar, but we’ll focus on the Linux options:

  • Startup – $2.99 per month for one site, 100 GB of storage, and free SSL.
  • Drive – $4.99 per month for unlimited sites, unlimited storage, free SSL, and automatic backups.
  • Turbo Boost – $9.99 per month for unlimited sites, unlimited storage, free SSL, automatic backups. Claims to be up to 20x faster.
  • Turbo Max – $14.99 per month for unlimited sites, unlimited storage, free SSL, automatic backups. Claims to be up to 20x faster plus 5x more resources.

Ease of setup

There’s a lot going on in the A2 Hosting dashboard, but once you get the hang of it you can launch a site within minutes. A2 Hosting offers one free migration, along with several one-click installer tools (like WordPress). Finally, the free SSL is great for eCommerce sites.

Customer support

A2 Hosting provides 24/7 email, chat, and phone support. The online resources are cluttered, but you can find plenty of articles for learning about the control panel, billing, and WordPress optimization.

Go to A2 Hosting

4. SiteGround (www.siteground.com)

SiteGround is an excellent hosting platform for beginners and companies with smaller sites. The pricing is reasonable, you receive tech support at all times, and the WordPress integration is seamless.

Pricing

SiteGround has a special price for those who sign up for one, two, or three years in advance. Being the most expensive service on our list, it can still be worth it as even their StartUp plan offers features such as daily backups, advanced WordPress security, dedicated WordPress support, and more.

Here are the three plans:

  • StartUp – $3.99 per month for one website, 10GB of web space, automatic WordPress and plugin updates, and support for up to 10,000 monthly visits.
  • GrowBig – $6.69 per month for multiple websites, 20GB of web space, and support for up to 25,000 monthly visits.
  • GoGeek – $10.69 per month for multiple websites, 30GB of web space, and support for up to 100,000 monthly visits.

Ease of setup

The SiteGround setup process is simple enough for beginners. You choose a plan, register a domain (or transfer one to SiteGround,) then fill out your payment information before being sent to the dashboard.

Once you sign in for the first time, SiteGround gives you a website setup wizard that will let you easily install WordPress or other common content management systems.

Customer support

SiteGround customer support is available 24/7 via live chat or phone. They also offer a detailed knowledge base.

👉 For a more detailed look, check out our full SiteGround review.

Go to SiteGround

5. HostGator (www.HostGator.com)

HostGator is one of the most affordable hosts on this list, at least when you sign up for the promos. It’s also known for its fast speeds and simple WordPress integrations.

Similar to Bluehost, the HostGator pricing gets lower per month when you sign up for more months.

Pricing

  • Hatchling – $2.75 per month for a single domain, unlimited bandwidth, and a free SSL.
  • Baby – $3.95 per month for unlimited domains, unlimited bandwidth, and a free SSL.
  • Business – $5.95 per month for unlimited domains, unlimited bandwidth, a free SSL, dedicated IP, and SEO tools.

Ease of setup

The HostGator sign up process takes no more than a few minutes. After that, you receive several one-click installs like the one for WordPress. The website builder has a drag and drop interface for rapid designing, and the eCommerce tools and free SSL should help you secure an online store.

Customer support

The HostGator customer support team is available 24/7. The online portal has documentation, video tutorials, and forums. You can also live chat with the team, send in an email, or contact them by phone.

👉 For a more detailed look, check out our HostGator vs SiteGround comparison.

Go to HostGator

6. InMotion Hosting (www.inmotionhosting.com)

InMotion Hosting offers both cheap shared hosting plans, as well as cheap managed WordPress hosting. We’ll focus on the shared plans for this post, though, as they’re the cheapest option.

Pricing

As with many of the cheapest hosting platforms, InMotion Hosting has discounted monthly pricing if you sign up for one or two years.

Here are the InMotion Hosting plans:

  • Lite – $2.29 per month hosting for one site, unlimited email and bandwidth, 10 GB of disk space, a free SSL.
  • Launch – $4.99 per month for a free domain, hosting for two sites, unlimited email and bandwidth, 50 GB of disk space, a free SSL.
  • Power – $4.99 per month for a free domain, hosting for 50 sites, unlimited email and bandwidth, 100 GB of disk space, improved performance, a free SSL.
  • Pro – $12.99 per month for a free domain, hosting for 100 sites, unlimited email and bandwidth, 200 GB of disk space, improved performance, a free SSL. This plan also gets you pro-level support.

Ease of setup

InMotion Hosting seems a bit more expensive than some of the competition, but that’s mainly because of the bonuses you receive. The setup goes smoothly for all types of users, with onboarding tools, a one-click install for WordPress, and an integration with Google Apps.

The dashboard is fairly easy to navigate, but the main reason I enjoy InMotion Hosting is because of free elements like an SSL, hack protection, and backups. Finally, InMotion offers a decent website builder for quickly designing your site.

Customer support

InMotion has an online support center filled with articles. You can also reach out to the 24/7 support team through online chat, Skype, email, or phone.

There’s also an online community to chat with other users.

Go to InMotion
