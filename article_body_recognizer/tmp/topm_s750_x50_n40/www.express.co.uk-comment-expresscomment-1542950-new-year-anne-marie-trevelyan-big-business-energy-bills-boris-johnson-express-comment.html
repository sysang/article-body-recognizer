Stepping in the New Year with fresh energy for deals and big business - EXPRESS COMMENT

INTERNATIONAL trade secretary Anne-Marie Trevelyan's prediction that 2022 will be a "five star year" for Britain trading with the world is exactly the type of ambition that the country needs.

When the country voted to leave the EU in 2016 the globe had yet to be shaken by the pandemic and multiple political convulsions, but it is clear that quitting that bloated bureaucracy was the right decision. Today, the UK is racing to strike trade deals with exciting emerging economic powerhouses and strengthening links with democracies that share our values. 

Trending

As we leave behind the era of lockdowns we can pursue the dream of global Britain with new energy. Ms Trevelyan says this will be the year when “we really get down to business as we open up even more opportunities”, and we share her ambition. There is a world to win and not a moment to lose.

Sensible solutions only

MINISTERS are right to explore radical measures to keep down bills. It is understood that a £20billion loan fund is being considered for energy companies to stop spiralling costs from being passed on to hard-pressed customers.

The Government suspended the pensions triple lock so it is honour bound to protect pensioners from being hit with bills that will wreck their household budgets. Creative solutions to this looming problem should be considered with urgency, but the Government must ensure that profit-hungry companies do not push the Government into creating a scheme that is not in the best interests of taxpayers. In both the energy market and public spending, people deserve and demand value for money.

We journey together...

BORIS Johnson summed up the journey the nation has been on in his New Year message when he said the situation we face together is “incomparably better than last year”. He celebrated the fact that there are “more people in work now than there were before the pandemic began” and boasted that the level of tech investment in the UK is “twice as much [as] Germany, three times as much as France”.

The foundation for this transformation in such circumstances is the success of the vaccine rollout. We urge everyone to seize the opportunity to get a booster jab and thank everyone who has made the mass vaccination of Britain a success story.

The UK has faced daunting challenges but the generosity, grit, honesty and kindness of the British people is our finest treasure and greatest natural resource. It is an honour to bring you stories every day in this newspaper, and we wish our fabulous readers a joyful 2022.
