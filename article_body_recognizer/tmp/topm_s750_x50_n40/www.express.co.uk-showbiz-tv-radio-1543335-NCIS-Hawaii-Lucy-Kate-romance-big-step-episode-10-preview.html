The first season of the latest NCIS spin-off is just getting started, and will soon return from its holiday break for episode 10, Lost. NCIS Hawaii agent Lucy Tara (played by Yasmine Al-Bustami) started dating DIA agent Kate Whistler (Tori Anderson) at the beginning of the series, and their relationship could be taken to new heights when the drama returns to CBS next week.

Episode 10 will air next Monday in the new year, but fans have already been treated to a first look clip at the next part of the season.

In Lost, the Pearl Harbour team crosses paths with the DIA once again during an investigation into an illegal shipment of weapons.

Their latest case will also give fledgling girlfriends Lucy and Kate a valuable chance to catch up.

A new clip has revealed their next encounter in the Pearl Harbour offices after winding down from another mission.

Lucy hurries to catch up with Kate and asks: “Hey, back from being a superspy?”

Kate isn’t so sure the moniker fits, but Lucy insists: “Just a smart, sexy woman sitting in a warehouse trying to save the world from evil men stealing big weapons.”

She’s then surprised to learn her new romantic partner and colleague had made dinner reservations for the two of them.

So far, Lucy and Kate have been keeping their burgeoning relationship a secret from their respective teams to avoid adding more tension to the stressful office environment.

NCIS Hawaii: A new clip of the next episode has been released (Image: CBS)

However, it seems their budding romance could soon turn into a full-time commitment after taking the step to have a public date.

While Kate is a little taken aback by Lucy’s forward-thinking, she agrees to go along with the plan as it’s unlikely any of her colleagues will be at the restaurant at the same time.

Now the couple finally has an official date lined up, Kate could soon start to loosen up to the idea of letting her office know about her new girlfriend.

Furthermore, executive producer Christopher Silber has previously teased a "very satisfying relationship" for Kate and Lucy, which includes the rest of the team eventually discovering the truth.

He told TVLine: “We have ALL the scoop on Lucy and Whistler, and can't wait to share the twists and turns of their very satisfying relationship.

“As for the team finding out… they will, indeed.”

Kate’s concerns about the restaurant may not be so far-fetched, and their impending date night could be setting the stage for their secret to be exposed.

Either way, fans are settling in for the long haul when it comes to NCIS’ adorable new couple and will be thrilled to see them take the next step when the show returns next week.
