London has celebrated the new year in “spectacular” fashion with a stunning Fireworks display.

Many Londoners were left disappointed after it was initially reported the fireworks were being cancelled due to the rapid spread of the Omicron variant. However, the firework display was given the green light with just hours to go before midnight, after being axed for the second year in a row in October.

It is thought that studies showing that the Omicron variant of COVID-19 is milder than previous variants have influenced this decision.

City Hall bosses had reportedly tried to keep the plans under wraps so as not to attract hoards of onlookers, who could potentially spread the virus.

UK records hottest New Year's Eve – Met Office

The UK has marked its hottest New Year's Eve since records began, with the mercury climbing to 15.8C in parts of the country.

Forecasters at the Met Office said the high was reached in Merryfield in Somerset shortly before 1pm, while Nantwich in Cheshire recorded the same temperature an hour later.

The previous record of 14.8C had already been broken at 11am when the mercury hit 14.9C in Ryehill, East Yorkshire, and then again at noon when temperatures in Coningsby, Lincolnshire, reached 15.3C.

UK set for hottest New Year on record

The UK is set for a mild start to 2022, with temperatures predicted to be higher than Athens in some parts of the UK. 

London could reach temperatures of 15C, hotter than the predicted 14C in Athens. 

The Met Office has warned this could be the hottest New Year on record. 

Met Office forecaster, Craig Snell said: "It will be 14-15C across the south and could potentially be the warmest New Year's Eve on record for the UK."

Scots flock to England for New Year's celebrations

People in Scotland have begun to flock across the border to England to see in the New Year after strict measures imposed by Nicola Sturgeon mean that Scottish celebrations have been restricted. 

It is believed that up to 100,000 Scottish and Welsh people could flock to English party hotspots such as Newcastle and Blackpool in a bid to avoid restrictions. 

In Scotland, nightclubs have been ordered to close and restaurants can only provide table service in a bid to curb the rise in Covid cases due to the Omicron variant. 

Scottish police have said that it is not illegal for people to cross the border and that they will not be setting up roadblocks or checkpoints at the border.
