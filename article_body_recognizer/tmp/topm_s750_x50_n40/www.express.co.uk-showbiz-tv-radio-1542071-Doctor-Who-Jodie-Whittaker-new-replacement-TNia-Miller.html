Doctor Who: Jodie Whittaker replaced by T'Nia Miller as Time Lord auditions begin?

DOCTOR WHO star Jodie Whittaker could be replaced by the Years and Years actor T'Nia Miller, it has been revealed.

Fans of the long-standing sci-fi series have been speculating who will take the place of Jodie Whittaker following her departure. Making history, the Broadchurch star became the first female Doctor cast on the BBC show since it began in 1963. Showrunner Chris Chibnall, who made the groundbreaking decision to cast Jodie, has also stepped down and has been replaced with It’s A Sin creator Russell T Davies. While the Huddersfield-born actor has thrilled audiences, Ladbrokes have released their latest figures which suggest actor T’Nia Miller could be the next to fill the iconic time-travelling role

The frontrunners of the race have been revealed with bookmakers speculating the Years and Years star with their odds at 6/1 in the betting order.

The east-London born actor, who would be the first black female Doctor, has previously worked alongside Russel T Davies on a long-lost Doctor Who story.

Originally, Mind of the Hodiac was a script written by the Welsh screenwriter in the 1980s that hadn’t been made for the purpose of TV.

Since its release, the story has now been adapted into an audio drama, which features T’Nia and is set to release in March next year

With close ties to Russell, Alex Apati of Ladbrokes said: "The race to replace Jodie Whittaker is wide open right now. 

“And there's a new frontrunner emerging in the shape of T'Nia Miller."

Levelling their odds, Queer as Folk star Fisayo Akinade has also been flagged by the bookies as the fourteenth incarnation. 

Aside from also having worked alongside the much-loved showrunner, Fisayo has been heavily praised by the TV producer.

T'Nia Miller: With close ties to Russell, Ladbrokes have put the actor in the running (Image: Getty)
T'Nia Miller: Could the actor be the next occupant in the Tardis? (Image: Netflix)

Speaking to the BBC, Russell said: “We've got a lad in this called Fisayo Akinade in Banana and Cucumber who is 26 years old.”

“And [has] never done TV before and he's an absolute star,” he added.

Just below Fisayo, actor Lydia West could inherit the sonic screwdriver taking the lead in the highly-anticipated sci-fi race. 

The It’s a Sin star has been given impressive odds of 3/1 by Ladbrokes setting her out as a strong contender.

Lydia’s co-star and former favourite Olly Alexander is just a 5/1 shot, slightly under his potential predecessor.

After becoming a fan-favourite in Russell’s hard-hitting drama It’s A Sin, the 31-year-old performer could take the reigns.

Slightly below, legendary actor Michael Sheen is just behind the chasing pack at an impressive 8/1.

With an impressive resume, the 52-year-old actor has featured in popular shows such as Good Omens, Prodigal Son and Staged, beside former Doctor David Tennant.

Doctor Who wrapped up its thirteenth series with The Flux, a six-part storyline that brought back iconic villains such as the Sontarans and the Weeping Angels.

Much to the excitement of fans, Jodie and Chris will return for the show’s New Year's Eve Special, called Eve of the Daleks.

Whittaker will be joined by her trusty aides Mandip Gill's Yaz, and John Bishop's Dan.

With a jam-packed selection of special guests, the show will also include Aisling Bea as Sarah, Adjani Salmon as Nick and Pauline McLynn in an unnamed role. 

The first Doctor Who Special will air on New Year's Day, January 1 at 6:45 pm on BBC One
