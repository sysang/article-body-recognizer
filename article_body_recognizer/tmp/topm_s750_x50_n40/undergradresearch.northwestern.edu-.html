Undergraduate Language Grant

Undergraduate Language Grants provide $5,000 towards the cost of intensive summer language study either in the US or abroad. The program’s goal is to fund students for whom this language training is central to the achievement of specific academic or professional goals. The application deadline is Friday, January 28 at 11:59pm. Learn more at an info session! RSVP here.

Emerging Scholars Program

Attention first years! The Emerging Scholars Program funds first year students who identify as first generation, lower income, people of color, and/or marginalized. This grant focuses on providing opportunities for students to get started in research and/or creative activities in the arts, humanities, journalism, and social sciences. No previous research experience required! The Emerging Scholars Program will provide you with stipend (living expense money) funding for 15 months. Learn more at an info session! RSVP here.
  • Wednesday, Jan 26 | 4-5p

Finding a Faculty/Finding a Lab Workshops

Looking to get started in research? Have no idea what to do first? Know you need to reach out to faculty but find them a tad bit scary? Never fear! OUR is here! If you are planning to apply to a Summer Undergraduate Research Grant, it is in your best interest to attend an earlier workshop.

In both the Finding a Faculty Mentor and Finding a Lab Mentor workshops, you will learn how to search for faculty, how to identify potential research mentors, and how to communicate effectively with the mentors you identify. We hold one of the workshops every week of the academic year, so it should be easy to find one that fits your schedule! Finding a Faculty Mentor workshops are appropriate for anyone considering doing any type of research, whereas Finding a Lab Mentor workshops are most appropriate for people interested in lab-based disciplines. No need to attend both! There is significant overlap.RSVP here.

Academic Year Undergraduate Research Grant

AYURGs provide up to $1,000 to pay for your research expenses to do an independent academic or creative project, in all fields of study, under faculty supervision. Apply to an earlier deadline for the chance to resubmit!

Get Started

Developing Your Interests

If you’re brand new to the concept of undergraduate research and are not sure where to begin, the best thing to do is work through the sections of our “Getting Started” tab. The resources here can be complemented by our Finding a Faculty/Lab Workshop! You don’t have to know exactly what you want to do, but it helps to start thinking about what could interest you.

Advising

The Office of Undergraduate Research operates on an advising based model. We never expect any student to know how to navigate this process or how to begin writing a grant. We help every student develop goals and a plan that is unique to them. There is no right or wrong way to get started in undergraduate research, and everyone’s path looks different!
