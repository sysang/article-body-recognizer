New driving laws 2022: The 7 major changes for road users starting today

CHANGES are on the way for drivers in 2022, including a major addition to the Highway Code all drivers need to be aware of. So what are the seven major changes in store for motorists this year?

A slew of new rules are now in place that will significantly change the way motorists need to behave when behind the wheel. From new rules around phones to a new hierarchy of responsibility, all road users need to be clued in on the upcoming changes to make sure they can drive safely in 2022.

Tougher laws on phones

Other than in an emergency, it is illegal to call or text using your phone when driving.

But from today, drivers will also be banned from using their phones to take photos or videos, scroll through playlists or play games.

Those caught will face a £200 fixed penalty notice and six points on their licence.

New fines from councils

Drivers could find themselves £70 out of pocket in new local authority fines that can be given out to motorists who perform bad manoeuvres.

Councils can currently only send out penalties for parking and driving in bus lanes.

But now, councils will be able to punish drivers for offences such as stopping in a yellow box junction and ignoring signage.

Amendments to the Highway Code are among the biggest changes coming in 2022 (Image: GETTY)

Parking on pavements

Scotland has already enacted a bill to outlaw all parking on pavements from 2023 - but the rules could soon be coming to England and Wales too.

While already illegal in London, parking on the pavement is permitted elsewhere, but this is likely to change sometime next year.

Under proposals, councils will be able to issue £70 fines to curb offenders - but the rules are not in place just yet.

Speed limiters

All news cars from July 6 will be built with speed limiters, in one of the most controversial moves for motorists in 2022.

Intelligent Speed Assistance (ISA) black boxes will use GPS to work out what the speed limit is and will then ensure the car doesn't break it.

The rule is an EU law passed in 2019 when the UK was still part of the EU - but the rule isn’t in place in the UK, rather it will apply to cars made in the EU and bought in the UK.

The UK has however retained many of the EU laws regarding motoring, meaning it could be used against speeding motorists in its own right in the UK.

Vehicle Excise Duty increase

Road tax will increase in 2022. However, the Government has not confirmed the new rates.

You will still pay according to how much CO2 your vehicle emits.

Petrol costs

While the price of petrol has risen to extortionate levels in the latter half of 2021, drivers will be relieved to hear that fuel duty has been frozen for this year.

Major Highway Code change

Perhaps the biggest and most serious change coming for road users is an amendment to the hierarchy of road users in the Highway Code.

For the first time, the law will require those who could cause the greatest harm to other to bear the responsibility of reducing danger.

This means that someone driving a car is more responsible than a cyclist for the safety of both parties.

The new hierarchy is as follows:

. Pedestrians

. Cyclists

. Horse riders

. Motorcyclists

. Cars/taxis

. Vans/minibuses

. Large passenger vehicles/heavy goods vehicles
