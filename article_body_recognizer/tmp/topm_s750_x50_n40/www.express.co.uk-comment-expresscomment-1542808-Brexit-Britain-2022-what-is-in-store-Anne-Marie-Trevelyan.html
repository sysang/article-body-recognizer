We put rocket boosters under our exporters, with our new Made in the UK – Sold to the World export strategy, targeting £1tn of international sales by the end of the decade.

And we’re only just getting started - 2022 is going to be the year when we really get down to business as we open up even more opportunities across the world.

It’s going to be a five-star year for international trade, where we will be working on deals with India, the Gulf states, Mexico, Canada, and the 500 million consumers of the huge Trans-Pacific Partnership. And that’s not even including New Zealand, where I’m looking forward to putting pen to paper on the final deal, after reaching agreement in principle in October.

And I am determined that the full force of my department will be brought to bear in driving economic growth, creating and supporting highly-paid jobs, and helping to level up the country.

We know international investors value our lowly-taxed, highly-skilled economy and that ‘Made in the UK’ remains a badge of quality around the world. So we will be boosting our Office for Investment to continue the flow of investment into the UK - which over 2021, was instrumental in securing nearly £18bn and will create nearly 20,000 jobs.

When Nissan announced it would build its all-new electric model in Sunderland, and Abu Dhabi’s Mubadala Investment Company committed £10 billion to the UK's energy transition and other priority industries, it showed the potential for the Office for Investment to work with the world’s biggest names to boost our efforts to build back better, and greener.

And we’ll also be working with existing exporters, would-be exporters, and should-be exporters across the length and breadth of the UK. Because we know that exporting businesses are more productive businesses and high-pay businesses.

Our new Made in the UK – Sold the World strategy is a 12-point plan of tailored support that gives our fantastic exporters everything they need to succeed. The potential is clear. We need to do more and we will do more.

Last, but not least, our UK Export Finance arm will carry on supporting UK exporters to take their goods and expertise to markets around the world – creating and supporting jobs in supply chains all over the UK.

So 2021 was a good year for the UK, but 2022 is going to be a great one. We can, we must and we will Build Back Better from everything we have been through during the pandemic. And my department is going to be right in the vanguard – supporting and creating great jobs and driving economic growth to help level up the UK.
