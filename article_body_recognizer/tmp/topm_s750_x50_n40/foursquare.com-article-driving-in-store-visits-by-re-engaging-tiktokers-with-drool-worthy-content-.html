Driving in-store visits by re-engaging TikTokers with drool-worthy content

By: Foursquare

December 14, 2021

The Objective

McDonald’s wanted to drive awareness and reach for its iconic favorites to generate and measure foot traffic at its restaurants. With a focus on a young, multicultural audience and brand love, McDonald’s looked to TikTok to re-engage audiences from a previous campaign as a way to push already-interested customers to its stores.

The Solution

Building on the success of a previous campaign, McDonald’s retargeted the same TikTokers with crave-inducing content. McDonald’s signature close-up and drool-worthy product shots paired with text overlay and voiceover on TikTok allowed them to bridge the gap between their traditional ad spots in a way that felt native to the platform.

For this campaign, McDonald’s took TV spots and added text overlay for a turn-key approach to making the content feel native to the platform while staying aligned with other creative. The up-close and personal creative highlighted customer truths like the fact that you can’t eat one fry at a time, which was a familiar feeling for TikTok users. The ad amassed over 45 million impressions, captivating users as the camera zoomed tight on multiple fries, slowly dipping into ketchup.

The TikTok community is full of young, multicultural users who align with McDonald’s target market. And after seeing the videos, there was no denying that stomachs were growling for more. Auction In-Feed Ads allowed McDonald’s to fine-tune its target audience to reach customers while also re-engaging existing audiences. The retargeted audience included users who completed a two-second video view on a previous campaign. The auction platform in TikTok Ads Manager brought significant efficiencies and measurable results in getting users to the drive-thru.

But, like crispy french fries and gooey cinnamon rolls, McDonald’s knows that how things are served matters—their ads included. Brand safety was a priority, and through TikTok’s partnership with OpenSlate, McDonald’s had transparency and control over its ads. OpenSlate is an independent, third-party provider of brand safety and suitability solutions that mitigate misalignment and reduce risk pre-campaign. This allowed McDonald’s to reach their target audience while feeling confident in how and where their ads showed up.

The Results

Mmm, tasty satisfaction. McDonald’s left the TikTok community hungry for more, with the campaign driving reach and awareness to get customers in store. McDonald’s first auction campaign via TikTok Ads Manager served 67 million impressions, reaching over 19 million unique users at a 3.46 average frequency. Simultaneously, brand safety goals surpassed the 98% benchmark, with 99.71% of impressions served alongside brand-safe content.

TikTok Ads Manager allowed McDonald’s to reach more people with more efficient CPMs, leading to a positive behavioral lift. Foursquare Attribution measured who was exposed to the ad and went into the restaurant after seeing it. The study showed a 2.85% increase in store traffic when comparing the exposed group with a control group. Moreover, the retargeting strategy showed a 3.32% increase in store traffic when comparing the two groups. As the jingle goes, "ba-da-ba-ba-baaa, I’m lovin’ it!
