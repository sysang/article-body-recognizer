The Tourist: Why Jamie Dornan was cast as The Man ‘As weird as possible'

JAMIE DORNAN is returning to screens to star in the BBC's new thriller The Tourist. Ahead of the action-packed drama airing, the show's director detailed why he cast the Fifty Shades of Grey star as the lead.

The Tourist is BBC One's six-part thriller set in the Australian Outback starring Jamie Dornan as the lead who is known simply as 'The Man'. The drama follows the story of an Irishman who wakes up with amnesia and soon realises someone is trying to kill him. Prior to the highly-anticipated series airing, director Chris Sweeney revealed why Jamie, 39, was the ideal protagonist for the "strange" show.

Jamie plays the eponymous tourist who suffers from a serious case of amnesia after being chased by a truck trying to knock him off the road. 

When he eventually wakes up in a hospital he has no idea what he's called, why he's in the middle of the Australian Outback or why someone would want him killed. 

Jamie is best known for starring in Fifty Shades Of Grey as Christian Grey, but he's also known for his role in the BBC crime drama The Fall where he filmed alongside The Crown's Gillian Anderson. 

The Tourist has been described by the BBC as a story of self-discovery with a ticking time-bomb underneath full of "shocking, surprising, funny and brutal turns".

The compelling drama has been directed by Chris, who told Express.co.uk the reason why he cast Jamie as 'The Man'. 

Chris, who also directed BBC's Liar, explained: "We needed someone who was quite enigmatic and if you have someone too much in mind you might [look] into it too much and we wanted a mysterious stranger in town kind of vibe. 

"We definitely talked about lots of people as we write each scene but this time we just tried to let it be as weird as possible." 

And that is where Jamie came in as the writers and director thought he would be able to portray the character perfectly. 

BBC: Jamie Dornan has also starred in The Fall on BBC One (Image: BBC)
The Tourist: Jamie's character wakes up with no memory and his helped by a local police officer (Image: BBC)

In the opening scene of The Tourist, Jamie's character is filmed driving through the barren Outback singing his heart out to Betty Davis Eyes by Kim Carnes.

Chris quipped: "He sang it way too high and I thought that was very funny. 

"But it was always in there and it was something I really loved and I'm really glad we kept it. 

"It gets stranger as it goes on which I think is good."

He added: "When it started we had this idea of this guy being chased and we thought he was going to be on his own and isolated and we thought there was no person better for that."

Other stars in The Tourist include Australian actress Danielle Macdonald who plays a police officer called Helen Chambers who is assigned to help Jamie's character. 

Danielle is perhaps best known for her role alongside Jennifer Aniston in the Netflix film Dumpling. 

The cast also includes Shalom Brune-Franklin, who has starred in Line of Duty and Alex Dimitriades, who acted alongside Jenna Coleman in The Cry.

The Tourist has been written by Golden Globe-nominated brothers Harry and Jack Williams

Discussing his enthusiasm over the script Chris explained: "I've known these two [writers] since I was a kid and I know they are really funny and I know they write thrillers. 

"But I didn't know anything about The Tourist I just knew it was something they loved. 

"And I started reading it and I was like, 'Oh my god, this is insane'. I read the first episode which was complete when I came on board and I knew we had to do this because it was so strange."

The Tourist lands on BBC One at 9pm tonight (January 1).
