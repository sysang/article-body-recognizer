  • June 16, 2020

    New TMEC Certificate of Origin

    The Canada-US-Mexico (TMEC) agreement, also known as “CUSMA” in Canada and “USMCA” in the US, will enter into force July 1st, 2020. Effective July 1st 2020, a certificate of origin, issued by the exporter, importer or producer, will be required. This certificate must contain a set of minimum data as explained in the TMEC Chapter 5 – Annex 5-A, or you can use the UPS suggested certificate of origin.

    Download the UPS suggested certificate of origin

  • March 20, 2019

    Government of Chile increases the maximum value of shipments for clearance through courier companies

    On March 12, 2019, general rule CVE 1558482 established that import and export shipments of up to a maximum value of US$3,000 FOB can be cleared without the need of a broker agent. Small and medium-sized companies doing business with international markets can now ship more merchandise (or merchandise of a greater value) without incurring additional expenses.

    Read more about the general rule CVE 1558482
