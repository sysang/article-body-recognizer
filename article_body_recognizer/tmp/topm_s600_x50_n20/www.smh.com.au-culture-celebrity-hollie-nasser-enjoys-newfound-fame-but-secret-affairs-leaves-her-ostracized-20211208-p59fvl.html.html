Hollie Nasser enjoys newfound fame, but secret affair leaves her ostracised

By Lucy Manly
December 12, 2021 — 12.00am
, register or subscribe to save articles for later.

It’s been several weeks since the scandal of the eastern suburbs social scene broke: the spectacular breakdown of the marriage of power couple Charlie and Ellie Aitken following the revelation of a secret affair between Charlie and Ellie’s former best friend, Hollie Nasser, wife of high net worth investor Chris “CJ” Nasser.

Last week the glamorous socialite Ellie announced on Instagram that she intended to withdraw from view, but it is understood that her former best friend Hollie is not as keen to keep her private life private.

Emerald City is reliably informed by a number of friends within her tight-knit circle that Hollie has been enjoying her newfound fame. Since the scandal broke, Hollie’s Instagram followers have doubled, with the petite mother-of-two accepting “follow” requests from media - including Emerald City. (Soon after the scandal broke her privacy settings were changed to public and have only recently reverted to private.)

Emerald City believes she has plans to launch a fashion label off the back of her new profile.

The image-conscious 36-year-old also put on a show for paparazzi less than a day after less-than-flattering photos were taken of her and Charlie arriving at her eastern suburbs home. The willowy blonde has been photographed in various outfits this week, immaculately groomed and all smiles for the camera. But will Hollie be all dressed up with nowhere to go?

Emerald City understands that her social calendar may have been quickly cleared, with friends distancing themselves from the new couple. Friends say a number of social invitations have been withdrawn, including a postponed “Christmas in July” event held in November, a 30th birthday and, most recently, a snub from bridesmaid’s duties.

A well-placed source says Nasser was due to act as bridesmaid and stand alongside long-time friend Polly Epov at her coming wedding, but the coveted role has since been revoked. Epov was a bridesmaid at the 2016 nuptials of Hollie and Chris Nasser and was regularly seen at Hollie’s side at social events. Awkward.

Hollie Nasser and Poly Epov did not respond to Emerald City’s repeated request for comment.

Alan Jones hosts knees-up after Murdoch soirée snub

It seemed fitting that right-wing commentator and former radio broadcaster Alan Jones host a celebratory dinner hours after announcing his media comeback - a day after snubbing (former employer) Lachlan Murdoch’s annual Christmas celebration.

The former “godfather” of radio hosted an intimate dinner at Sydney institution Beppi’s restaurant on Friday evening for a handful of associates, including young protégé Jake Thrupp.

The celebratory event came hours after Jones announced his return to Australia’s news landscape with online show Alan Jones Direct to the People set to launch free on Facebook and YouTube on Monday, promising to give “the voiceless a voice”.

If only the walls of the iconic Italian restaurant could talk! They would tell tales of high-profile diners for one of the city’s oldest kitchens; Frank Sinatra, Billy Joel, Mick Jagger and, more recently, Rihanna have all dined during their harbour city stays.

Jones arrived in a chauffeur-driven BMW for an early-bird sitting, where Emerald City is told he hosted a knees-up in a private dining room.

Jones wasn’t the only one making the most of the silly season. Emerald City spied a number of undercover police busting merry revellers meeting their dial-a-dealers for a “white Christmas”.

New beginnings for Double Bay salon

Hours after the DJ decks were put away at the opening of Mariah Rota’s new Double Bay salon, the salon’s namesake, ex-husband Tom Cole, was spotted on Thursday morning looking rather sombre, getting his morning coffee at Bills in Double Bay. The triumphant Rota made the bold move to leave behind her former premises across the road that she ran with her ex-husband before their highly publicised split, revealed by Emerald City after Cole was spotted canoodling with a client.

Rota, a highly sought-after hairstylist, unveiled the new luxurious Cole Hair digs designed by interior designer Nicholas Gurtler on Wednesday night. Returning to pre-COVID decadence, Sydney’s social scene was treated to free-flowing champagne, cocktails and food by the Double Bay IT venue Matteo.

Rota tends to the tresses of some eastern suburbs society ladies, so it was no surprise to find the guest list was star-studded, including Terry Biviano, Nellie Tilley, NRL’s Reuben Garrick and Corey Waddell, and AFL player for GWS Lachie Whitfield.

Paddo’s footpath of fame

Similar to the slightly more famous Hollywood Walk of Fame, chi-chi shopping precinct The Intersection Paddington, home to Australian designers, hosted its annual celebration to honour the nation’s fashion talent with a pavement of plaques on Oxford Street.

Doyenne of Australian fashion Maggie Tabberer was commemorated at the event, with daughter Brooke accepting her mum’s award days ahead of her 85th birthday.

Amid the fashion crowd was Akira Isogawa, Nicholas Huxley and newly married Nadia Fairfax with her husband, Michael. Property developer Theo Onisforou owns a lot of property in the area and celebrated the occasion alongside daughter Stephanie, a fashion lover who was dressed in designer label Aje and boots-of-the-moment from Paris Texas.
