Built For the Future of Human Behavior

The way people consume content has changed, advertising must adapt.

TV as a second screen while you're sitting on the couch

All of a sudden, the primary focus is on your phone even while a TV show is running.

Ads must follow the path of primary focus.

Feeds, not pages

No one searches Google for “what’s going on with my sport’s team?” — content (and ads) are discovered through feeds, not one-off pages.

The best ads are shared through the user experience, not inserted in the corners.

We keep our phones on mute 99.9% of the day

The primary content experience is muted for 99% of the day.

All content needs to adapt that reality.

Our phones vibrate in our pockets 120x per day

They have become an extension of our human bodies and demand our attention 10x more often than we eat on any given day.

Ads that demand attention must weave into this UX.

Everything can be skipped, blocked, muted

If we all have metaphorical (or literal!) skip buttons, the traditional idea of ads that force attention will only work in limited moments.

Long-term, attention must be earned, not forced.

Your phone is an extension of your own body

If our phones are a physical extension of our brains, how would a brain react to a foreign object being inserted?

Intimate devices demand intimate advertising experiences.
