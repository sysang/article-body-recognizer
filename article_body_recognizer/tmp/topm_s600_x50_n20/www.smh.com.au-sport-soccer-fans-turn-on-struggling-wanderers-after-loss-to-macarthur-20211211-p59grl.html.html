Fans turn on struggling Wanderers after loss to Macarthur

By Dominic Bossi
December 11, 2021 — 3.30pm
, register or subscribe to save articles for later.

Western Sydney Wanderers coach Carl Robinson has accepted responsibility for the club’s poor start to the season after his team was booed off the field by supporters following their 2-0 loss to Macarthur FC at Commbank Stadium on Saturday night.

Four days after being humbled by semi-professional club APIA Leichhardt in the FFA Cup, the Wanderers succumbed to a lacklustre defeat that added more pressure upon Robinson who had to bear the brunt of frustrated fans.

“Supporters have got every right to be disappointed. I’m disappointed, the players are disappointed,” Robinson said. “Don’t blame the players, if you want to blame anyone, blame me. I am thick-skinned enough to do that. I understand football is a results-driven business.”

Western Sydney were not so much as outplayed but outwitted by the Bulls with Robinson losing the tactical battle to his opposite in the dugout, Ante Milicic. His third win in a row was one hatched from the whiteboards and video rooms in Campbelltown.

An injury-stricken Bulls travelled up the M5 not seeking to dominate their rivals but expose their weaknesses. Macarthur sat back, absorbed pressure for lengthy spells before seeking to strike in transition.

“We knew that we would get a lot more clear chances in the transition phase of the game and fortunately for us we capitalised well on it,” Milicic said.

Without striker Tomer Hemed and former EPL star Jack Rodwell, the Wanderers lacked direction in attack and composure in the midfield. Bernie Ibini was deployed in a more central role but to little effect. His best contribution came from a set piece when he forced a fine save from goalkeeper Filip Kurto with a powerful header off a corner late in the first half.

The Wanderers’ wasteful finishing frustrated the meagre crowd at Commbank Stadium. Dimitri Petratos summed it up on the stroke of half-time, somehow giving away a foul from a clear goalscoring chance from inside the box.

Petratos was less forgiving to Macarthur’s Craig Noone after the restart, catching the winger with a stray elbow to the face. Petratos escaped punishment for the accidental elbow but the hosts were fuming at the ongoing leniency from officials. They were denied a penalty when the ball struck the arm of Macarthur’s Charles M’Mombwa in the 52nd minute before two more half-hearted claims for fouls in the box were waved away before the hour.

The Wanderers had nobody to blame but themselves when they eventually conceded in the 64th minute through their own poor marking. Daniel De Silva jinked around his marker, delivered a well-weighted cross into the path of defender Tomi Uskok inside the box. Uskok forced a save from Mejias with his first shot but pounced on the rebound to make it 1-0 from close range.

Ten minutes later, Macarthur sealed the win. A swift, precise and classy counter-attack completely eroded the Wanderers’ defence. Lachlan Rose and De Silva opened up space behind the Wanderers’ backline with a quick exchange before youngster Jake Hollman was put through on goal. Against Mejias, Hollman slotted past the Spaniard to make it 2-0 and maintain their perfect record in Parramatta.

Bobo’s return welcome for depleted Sydney FC

Brazilian striker Bobo is set to return for Sydney FC on Sunday against Central Coast Mariners to provide a much-needed boost to the struggling Sky Blues.

The veteran centre-forward has overcome a six-week lay off due to a calf injury and will make his season debut in Gosford to bolster Sydney’s hopes of claiming their first league win of the campaign.

While the 36-year-old has been given the all clear, Sydney FC coach Steve Corica is still weighing up whether to start Bobo or use him as a substitute as he eases him back into first-team football.

“He’d be on limited minutes whether he starts of comes off the bench tomorrow,” Corica said. “Obviously he’ll be a big help to us. It will probably take him a few games to get back to his rhythm but the sooner we can get him up to match minutes, the better for us. He’s a big part of how we play.”

While the return of Bobo is a major boost for Sydney, they will still travel to the Central Coast with a threadbare squad with a number of first team players in doubt to face the Mariners.

Fullback Rhyan Grant remains out after testing positive to COVID-19 during the week, while forward Elvis Kamsoba was deemed a close contact of a COVID case and had to undergo three days of quarantine. He’ll be allowed out on game day but has not trained all week. Midfielder Paulo Retre suffered a corked calf during Sydney’s midweek FFA Cup win over Macarthur, young midfielder Calum Nieuwenhof was struck down with gastro while Luke Brattan is out for the season due to an ACL injury.

“We’ve got a few concerns with injuries, sickness and Rhyan Grant out as well. We’re down to bare bones at the moment,” Corica said. “We’re doing it a little bit tough, obviously. That’s fine, we’ll get through this. We’ll come back stronger and we hope to get everyone back.”

Sydney are enduring their worst start in the league in more than a decade, a run that has been heavily compounded by their absences. Having been so successful in his first three years in charge, Corica concedes this is the most difficult test of his coaching career.

“It’s definitely the toughest because there are a lot of things happening around you that are out of your control,” he said.

Sydney’s coach is already looking at reinforcements to bring in when the transfer window reopens on January 14, targeting an experienced midfielder to replace Brattan. Corica hasn’t ruled out going overseas for that player with the club still having one visa player spot available on their roster.

“We’ve got one visa spot available so we are definitely looking in that position but there’s obviously limited time trying to find someone,” Corica said. “It’s difficult at this time of year to find the right player. Brattan will be back at the start of next season so whether we can get someone for six months, I don’t know. We’ll try.”
