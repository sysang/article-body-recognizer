Low-key billionaire Kie Chie Wong buys $9.4m Southern Highlands farm

By Lucy Macken
December 12, 2021 — 1.27am
, register or subscribe to save articles for later.

Little-known billionaire Kie Chie Wong has kept his real estate interests fairly modest by iron ore oligarch standards, residing with his wife Ann Lim in a four-bedroom, 1970s-brick house in Maroubra.

But his weekends are now looking pretty glamorous after the couple purchased the luxury Sutton Forest cattle and thoroughbred farm Braebrook for $9.4 million.

Wong, an Australian emissary of a Sarawak timber-milling family, and Lim are ranked among Australia’s wealthiest people thanks to their $1 million investment 20 years ago in Andrew “Twiggy” Forrest’s Fortescue Metals, and are now worth an estimated $2.53 billion, according to this year’s Financial Review Rich List.

The Southern Highlands getaway is an 82-hectare property developed by the late Geoffrey and Pamela Coghlan and features a Rick Turner-designed homestead, with established gardens and a tennis court.

It hit the market on October 13 for $8.5 million to $9 million and, within days, Wong clearly had designs on it because he had established a corporate entity, Harvest Grace, in which name the property is now lodged.

Wong and Lim’s newfound interest in prestige real estate first emerged in April when they bought a house in Dover Heights for $6.7 million.

Byron Bay’s well-housed wellness warriors

High-profile nutritionist Simon Hill has bought into the wellness heartland of Byron Bay, paying $12.4 million for a house at Wategos Beach.

The three-bedroom house on a double block sold by Byron Bay Property Sales settled in Hill’s name last week, putting him in the same celebrity-studded property market as his most famous nutritional client Chris Hemsworth.

Hill first came to Title Deeds′ attention in 2016 when, aged just 29, the e-commerce entrepreneur purchased the North Bondi penthouse of Simon Baker for $3.69 million.

Since then, Hill has become better known for co-owning the Eden restaurant in Bondi, just down the road from another penthouse, this time in Bondi’s Sundeck building, which he bought six months ago for $3.1 million.

Meanwhile, fellow wellness warrior James Duigan – best known for training Elle MacPherson – and his wife Christiane have sold their Vaucluse house for $9 million and bought in Byron Bay’s Newrybar for $2.67 million.

The Vaucluse residence was a good earner, not just for the $3750 a week rent of late, but because it more than doubled in worth since it last traded in 2014 for $4 million.

The Vaucluse house is a good step up the property ladder for the 23-year-old son of socialite Lizzie Buttrose, Andrew Spira, founder and chief of lender Pineapple Funding, and his partner, The Capital Network’s Julia Maguire.

Returning to Byron Bay’s high-end market, Walter Ingham, of the chicken and horse-racing family, has listed his luxury 30-hectare property in the Myocum hinterland.

Liam Annesley, of Byron Bay Real Estate, is expecting about $18 million for the pavilion-style residence, based on the recent $18.5 million sale of the River Hawk Ranch in Brunswick Heads to Helen Sali, wife of former Swisse vitamin company chief Radek Sali.

Game on for Blue apartment

E-sports gaming entrepreneur Jack Wu is selling the sub-penthouse he bought off-the-plan in Aqualand’s Blue at Lavender Bay development for about $10 million.

The son of the rich list founders of vitamin brand Nature’s Care, Jina Chen and Alex Wu, purchased the three-bedder as his home in 2017 for $7.6 million when he was in his late 20s. However, in the four years since the building has been completed, sources say Wu has had three kids, prompting his rethink on apartment living.

Having just settled on the level 19 apartment in August – with views of both the Harbour Bridge and level with the penthouse of Nicole Kidman in the building next door – Wu has listed it with McGrath’s Claudia Portale.

Taynish finds its buyer

Mark Angles, son of the late philanthropist rag trader Emery Angles, has bought the Bellevue Hill residence Taynish from commercial property magnate Robert Christie.

Angles, who now heads up the family property and private equity investment office The Suave Group, was forced to dig deep at a heated auction for the 1930s architect F. Glynn Gilling-designed house, securing it for $20.9 million. The guide by Richardson Wrench’s Michael Dunn was $15 million.

To think it last traded in 2015 for $8.15 million.
