Have questions? We’ve got answers.

Location is everything. Fios (fiber optics) and High Speed Internet (DSL) are just a couple of the internet options that may be available if you’re in the northeast. If you live in any of the cities and suburbs around the country where Verizon 5G Home Internet is currently offered, you might be able to connect to this truly wireless internet service. In addition, in certain areas where Fios or 5G Home Internet isn’t available, we also offer LTE Home Internet, another home broadband internet service that delivers wireless home internet powered by our 4G LTE network. When shopping for ISPs, (Internet Service Providers) with fast internet and extensive coverage, consider one of the best internet providers like Verizon. To see which Verizon home internet services are available where you live, check here.

It’s all about the internet technology, how it’s delivered to your home and geographical location. Fios provides 100% fiber-optic internet with supercharged speeds and is widely available in metro and suburban areas of the northeast. Verizon 5G Home Internet offers a wireless internet connection over our 5G Ultra Wideband network with ultra-fast speeds up to 1GB*. It's already available in several major cities throughout the U.S. with more locations coming soon. LTE Home Internet uses the wireless 4G LTE network to deliver home broadband internet service to your residence.

*Max download speeds up to 1 Gbps, depending on location, with typical download speeds of 300 Mbps. Typical upload speeds around 50 Mbps. Depending on location, uploads over 5G Ultra Wideband or 4G LTE. 4G LTE backup.

Verizon 5G Home Internet service is the first 5G wireless network built to connect your home with ultra-fast internet that's ready for what comes next. Verizon 5G Home Internet provides a fixed wireless connection via a small modem that links to Verizon’s 5G Ultra Wideband network and its fiber resources. This technology makes it possible to wirelessly connect all your devices throughout your home. As the first internet provider in the U.S. to offer 5G, the Verizon 5G Ultra Wideband network has the power to deliver fast internet with speeds around 300 Mbps and, depending on your location, maximum speeds up to 1GB – making real time a reality.

Note: Actual speeds may vary.

Verizon Fios is a 100% fiber-optic network that delivers some of the fastest internet speeds to millions of homes in the mid-Atlantic and New England. It uses fiber optics to transmit data at the speed of light, providing a faster internet connection with superior reliability and powerful bandwidth for multiple devices. Verizon is one of the only internet service providers to offer nearly matching download and upload speeds, which is a major advantage when you’re video chatting, gaming and sharing large files or images.

LTE Home Internet is home broadband internet service that brings the Verizon 4G LTE network into your residence. It is available in certain areas with 4G LTE network service where there are no other Verizon broadband options (Fios, 5G Home, etc.). With LTE Home Internet you can stream video at 1080p, plus you can enjoy unlimited data with no data caps.
