Free train simulator set in India

Indian Railways Simulator is a free mobile game developed by Team Hikers. As the name suggests, this app simulates the railway system of India. With this, you can drive trains and travel around India's railroad.

Indian Railways Simulator boasts a highly detailed map and cabin interiors. It also comes with real thunder track sounds for a more realistic experience. However, unlike the Indian Train Simulator, this game does not have AI passengers. Additionally, while it has multiple camera angles, its control is too sensitive, switching the view automatically as the player touches the control buttons on the screen. 

Travel the tracks of India

Indian Railways Simulator offers an experience like any other train simulator. Only this time, you will take on the Indian tracks. As mentioned, it puts you in control of various Indian locomotives traveling to and from different stations. Although, unlike other similar titles, there are no passengers to pick up in this game. What you will do is drive the train from station to station.

The game makes up for it with a highly detailed map. The surroundings of the train tracks will really remind you of India's railroad system. Moreover, each cabin comes with realistic details. Even better, the game comes with real thunder track sound as you drive. However, the background noise will sound like the train is already running at a fast speed, although you've only started the game.

Indian Railways Simulator also has multiple camera angles, allowing you to shift from a bird's eye view to the cabin. You can also see the map from inside the cabin. However, as noted, this camera angle is too sensitive. Every time you touch the screen to control the train, the camera angle will also change. Players will also find the app too laggy. 

Should you play it?

At first glance, the Indian Railways Simulator seemed like a good game to pick up. The game captures the railway system of India. However, the player experience will say otherwise. It has no missions, reducing your gameplay to simply driving the trains. Additionally, the camera angle is too sensitive, switching without preamble whenever you touch the screen. It also doesn't help that the app is prone to lagging.
