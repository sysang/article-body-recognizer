A free and powerful music player

iTunes is a free and easy-to-use music application that lets users purchase and manage albums and songs. Designed for Windows, the application offers various features, including 90-second song previews, curated radio stations, automated playlist management, CD burning capabilities, and easy access to Apple Music. The app has a clean and minimalistic interface, and looks very much like its macOS counterpart.

A one-stop-shop for music

When you're a music enthusiast, there’s nothing better than discovering a platform that has a user-friendly design, numerous features, and a massive library of songs.

Apple’s iTunes for Windows is one such application. While the company is known for creating products that are only limited to Apple users, this app makes an exception. It is, in a sense, an entryway into the inclusive world of Apple.

iTunes download for Windows looks highly similar to the Mac app. The entertainment tool retains its white-colored interface, clean and minimalistic function placement, and convenient navigation. However, the features that make it stand apart from other similar applications are high-quality music downloads with no expiration date, multiple device support, family sharing, and a free trial of Apple Music.

The latest version offers additional content

iTunes latest version now gives you easy access to over 70 million ad-free songs, 100,000 TV shows, and over 5,000 audiobooks. 

You can either purchase them separately or subscribe to the Music Store and download all in high-quality AAC files. 

Once purchased, your library is yours forever! You can access it from up to 3 different computers, transfer content to other devices, or burn files on a DVD unlimited times.

Comes with a clean and user-friendly interface

When you download iTunes, you get access to a dashboard that looks like a well-lit music store. 

The primary menu offers five different tabs, namely, ‘Library’, ‘For You’, ‘Browse’, ‘Radio’, and ‘Store’. You can use these tabs to navigate to different segments of the app. If you make a purchase, it’ll instantly get added to your Library, which you can then organize by genre, artist, or album.

The open menu bar preceding this section gives you easy access to music controls, account settings, and the help section. Apart from this, the header shows the music being played, as well as “pause”, “back”, and “next” options. The right-side corner at the top of the screen includes the search box, where you can search for your favorite songs, artists, and audiobooks.

Preview songs before making a purchase

These days, there are so many artists, both independent and from famous labels, creating music. That means an influx of songs, covers, remixes, medleys, and more! 

Keeping this in mind, iTunes has come up with a preview system that lets you listen to a song for 90 seconds before deciding whether or not you wish to purchase it. If you want to listen to multiple songs before buying an album, you can do that too!

Listen to high-quality tunes

An additional quality of this Microsoft Windows app is that it provides users with 128Kbps files in AAC format. The format ensures that the music quality you hear is as good as a WMA file of 160Kbps. 

You can also choose sound quality while downloading a track, so you have the best quality file offline.

Explore several radio stations

While you need to purchase tracks from the Music Store, the Radio tab available on iTunes download for Windows is free.

 The app provides users with a list of the top radio stations that you can filter by the genre of your liking. Moreover, once you start listening to a particular station, Apple Music customizes the experience by curating a list of similar stations for you to enjoy.

Import and manage playlists

iTunes for PC offers an all-in-one music player that lets you import and organize your music library. You can use the app to access media saved on your desktop, add it to your library, and create different playlists. 

You can even set up an automatic selection that picks and saves files to manually-curated playlists. The only drawback to the program is that it doesn’t support WMA file format.

Burn music CDs for old-times' sake

iTunes download for Windows works as a smart music player and offers jukebox capabilities too. You can use the tool to rip CDs into different formats like MP3 or AAC or burn CDs and DVDs to share with your friends and family. As long as you don’t have a massive collection of songs in WMA format, the software will work perfectly well.

Share with others

iTunes works as a massive store where you can purchase your favorite music to keep forever. 

However, the functionality isn’t limited to just that! Once you buy an album or a song, you can share it with up to 6 people. All of these people can easily download your purchases an unlimited number of times. The app also lets you see and explore each other’s unique libraries, provided you’re both in the same vicinity.

Is iTunes free to use?

Yes. iTunes is free to download and use, and there is no monthly fee. 

Once downloaded, you can use it to access music files saved on your dashboard, listen to the radio, or buy music from the iTunes Store. All your purchases get saved in your library, and you can download them as and when you like. Downloading iTunes also gives you a free trial of Apple Music, a streaming service with over 70 million songs.

Are there any alternatives?

While iTunes is one of the few applications in the market that let you buy and manage music seamlessly, it does have competition. Some of these are Amazon Music for PC, Spotify, YouTube Music, and Google Play Music. Though these apps don’t let you buy albums or songs, they offer a massive library of tunes via a monthly or quarterly subscription plan.

Should I download iTunes?

If you have a keen interest in music and prefer to organize your playlists, offline as well as online, you should download iTunes. 

The minimalist application is a one-stop solution that offers incredible sound quality in a range of file formats, except for WMA. And, if you're not sure, then you'll want to not forget about the free trial of Apple Music, a music streaming service with millions of ad-free songs, tv shows, and movies.
