    Established in 2001, the David C. Driskell Center provides an intellectual home for artists, museum professionals, art administrators and scholars who are interested in broadening the field of African diasporic studies.

    The Driskell Center is committed to preserving the rich heritage of African American visual art and culture.

    Art Archive Collections

    The David C. Driskell Center is proud to present and preserve its collection of works by African American artists. The art collection includes drawings, paintings, prints, mixed media and sculptures with works by prominent artists, including Benny Andrews, Romare Bearden and Augusta Savage. The Driskell archive was assembled over more than six decades and consists of an estimated 50,000 objects.

    We’re back with another #ArchiveQuickDive! The archives at the Driskell Center recently had an exciting new development take place- the expansion of our shelving! And why yes, we do know that getting excited about shelving sounds a little silly. But, this increased shelving space is going to make a big difference. This shelving increases our overall capacity by about 20% and will allow us to rearrange for more maximum efficiency and organization, have the space necessary for the Hayes Benjamin Papers on African American Art and Artists Collection, as well as have the ability to house new collections we hope to acquire over the next couple of years! Sometimes the little things like shelves can make a big impact long term! #archives #africanamericanart #africanamericanartist #blackart #blackartist #driskellcenter
    It’s time for our weekly #ArchiveQuickDive! Both Dr. Tritobia Hayes Benjamin and her husband, Donald Benjamin, were actively involved in politics. Sprinkled throughout the Hayes Benjamin Papers on African American Art and Artists are remnants of their engagement. Today we have some interesting finds on that subject that Karina recently discovered. There is a letter from former President Bill Clinton - back when he was still Governor of Arkansas - congratulating her on being included in the 1988 edition of “Who’s Who Among Black Americans,” AND an invitation to attend his 1993 inauguration! We hope you’re enjoying our #ArchiveQuickDive series as much as we are.
