Tik Tok, originally called Musically, is one of the most popular video apps in the world. On the Tik Tok app, you can create videos, edit them with fun music and other elements, and share them online with a solid community. Good editing features and a huge community of creators

Tik Tok app is an easy-to-use and lively social networking platform. It has been designed around searching, sharing, and creating user-generated music videos. Focusing on short videos, the application has already passed the billion-download standard for both iOS and Android platforms. In 2019, the Tik Tok download was reported as one of the best performing apps on various game stores.

What is Tik Tok?

With Tik Tok, you can upload all kinds of videos, including dancing, singing, performing challenges, lip-syncing, and more. Since it’s user-generated content, you often come across fun and interactive challenges that become trending around the world. More often than not, these are based on specific themes, so users can include their own creativity and imagination with the features.

With this video app, you can add augmented reality (AR) animations, filters, and stickers to creations for live stream interactions with online audiences. There’s also a chat feature, so you can talk to followers if you'd like. Tik Tok gives you the ability to set videos to private or simply share them with everyone on the app.

If a video is publicly shared, it can be remixed, commented upon, or viewed by anyone. Tik Tok download comes with a built-in app store, where you can purchase in-app currency to buy virtual gifts. These are shareable with livestreamers during chats. For kids, there’s a separate section, which lets them watch only curated videos. In this section, a user can’t search, comment, or post content.

How to create an account?

Once you’ve downloaded and installed the app on your iOS device, you need to go through a simple sign up process. Just click on the ‘person’ icon, and sign up on Tik Tok with a phone number, email, or your existing Twitter, Facebook, Instagram, or Google account. 

The next step is to enter the date of birth, so the app can identify you as an adult or not. In case you’re below the age of 13, the application will not let you finish the sign-up process. 

Once done, you get access to ‘Feed Highlights’, which inform you about everything going on in the community. There’s a ‘For You’ section in the feed that offers personalized recommendations for your account.

Tik Tok app comes with a digital wellbeing aspect, alerting users who’ve been active on the app for over two hours. Compared to LIKE - Magic Movie Maker, Vigo Video, and other video creation apps, the signup, login, and account creation processes work seamlessly, and you shouldn’t experience any issues.

Is Tik Tok safe for children?

When you sign up for Tik Tok, the user profile is turned ‘public’ on default. This means anyone can watch the videos uploaded by a user. Since the app primarily focuses on music and video creations, suggestive dancing, clothing, and profanity are the leading sources of inappropriate or adult content.

Many users on the app encourage themes and challenges that feel more mature than the standard 16+ rating. Additionally, strangers can connect with other users, leaving them vulnerable to bullying, cyber-attacks, and other threats. Thus, if an account remains public, the user can receive messages from anyone.

Like on most apps, people who send direct messages are able to access certain data, such as a person’s location. Considering everything, parental guidance is highly recommended on this app. However, one of the major advantages is the digital wellbeing aspect, ensuring users don’t access the application for long periods of time.

From this setting, you can even limit the use, which can help kids moderate the amount of time they’re spending on the popular video app. Additionally, you can use this feature to place certain restrictions on the child’s account. Still, since some content might slip through, everything isn’t filtered, and you’ll have to be more cautious about kids using Tik Tok.
