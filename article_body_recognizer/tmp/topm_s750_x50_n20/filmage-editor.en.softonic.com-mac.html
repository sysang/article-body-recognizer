Professional-grade video editing software

Filmage Editor is a free video program for desktop that was developed by Filmage Pro Team Inc. It is a professional-grade video editing 

Filmage Editor is attempting a balance between the versatility of the ever-popular Adobe Premiere Pro, with the user-friendliness and accessibility of iMovie. It also offers upgrade options that will unlock all the advanced features, as well as the added benefit of higher priority customer support.

Is it any good?

Filmage Editor features a clean and modern user interface that is easy to navigate. The top half of the window is dedicated to a media manager and preview section. While the bottom half is where your timeline will reside along with various quick-access tools and options. You can start a new project from scratch or simply add an existing video that you want to work on.

Filmage Editor features a Split and Merge feature, as well as support for adding multiple audio tracks for individual videos. If you want to get more creative with your clips, there are various customization options available to your like text and stickers, cinematic filters, and various transition effects to truly make your creations stand out. Once done, you can immediately share and upload them to your socials.

Lastly, the editor has built-in presets that make it easy to convert and export your videos to mobile device-friendly file formats for instant playback. Now, before trying this program out, just be aware that the free version will include watermarks to all your saved work. Also, there is no support for 4K videos and you won't be able to access all of its features here.

Capable and intuitive

Overall, Filmage Editor is a good video editor that strikes a balance between features and usability. It looks great, intuitive to use, and offers plenty of tools and options to choose from. You do need to upgrade to be able to take advantage of all those advanced features, though. Nevertheless, this one is worth consideration.

PROS

  • Intuitive to use
  • Extensive list of features and options
  • Built-in presets

CONS

  • Free version has limited features
  • No 4K support
  • Watermarks included in videos
