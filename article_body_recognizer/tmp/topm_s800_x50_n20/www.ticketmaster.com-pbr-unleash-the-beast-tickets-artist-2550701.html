About PBR: Unleash The Beast

The top Professional Bull Riders (PBR) duke it out in this wildly entertaining tour. PBR: Unleash The Beast is the highest of three levels of PBR competition, in addition to the Velocity Tour for rising stars and the Touring Pro Division for qualifying professionals. The Unleash The Beast Series started in 2018, replacing the Built Ford Tough Series, which was the major PBR circuit from 2003 to 2017. PBR events differ from traditional rodeo by focusing on bull riding, while also adding to the spectacle with pyrotechnics and accompanying music throughout. Unleash The Beast events take place over a two-to-three day period, with national coverage provided by CBS and the CBS Sports Network.

History of PBR: Unleash The Beast

Professional Bull Riders, Inc. was established in 1992, when 20 bull riders gathered in a hotel room in Scottsdale, Arizona, and each put in a stake of $1,000 to incorporate the organization. PBR has since grown beyond anyone's wildest dreams, having had a majority of the company purchased by Spire Capital Partners in 2007, before being acquired by Endeavor in 2015. Over the years, safety regulations have been put into place to ensure the well-being of both the bull and the rider. In 2013, PBR mandated that all riders born after October 15, 1994, wear helmets while competing, allowing those born before that date to wear traditional or modified cowboy hats.

While there are 1,200 riders who compete worldwide in PBR sanctioned events, only the best of the best take part in this tour. The 35 riders who comprise the PBR: Unleash The Beast roster work a year-round season. It begins in January at Madison Square Garden in New York and culminates each November at Las Vegas' T-Mobile Arena with the PBR World Finals. In 2019, Jess Lockwood won his second PBR world championship in three years, becoming the youngest two-time winner at the age of 22.
