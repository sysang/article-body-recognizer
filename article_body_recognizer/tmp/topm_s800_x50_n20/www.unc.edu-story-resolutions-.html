New Year, New Goals

With a new year comes new goals and new resolutions, and Carolina’s experts are here to help you start 2022 off right.Whether you’re looking to reduce your stress or get healthier in the new year, our faculty and staff members have the expertise to help guide you to success this year.

We all daydream about our goals — improving our grades, eating healthier and spending less time on social media. But research shows that no matter how good our intentions, we reach our goals only about 50% of the time.

Paschal Sheeran, a professor of psychology in the College of Arts Science, first documented the gap between our intentions and our behaviors in 2002, and has dedicated his career to understanding why we struggle to reach our goals. And while it takes hard work to achieve a goal, it turns out the basic steps to get there are relatively simple.

To get you on the right track, Sheeran shared his tips for accomplishing any goal

We’re taught in our kindergarten classrooms to be kind to others. But what about being kind to ourselves?

Self-compassion — a close cousin of mindfulness — is a simple and powerful tool for managing stress and anxiety. Simply put, it’s the process of caring for ourselves the way we would care for a good friend during a difficult time.

Karen Bluth, an assistant professor of psychiatry at Carolina, says self-compassion can boost resilience, lessen symptoms of anxiety and even prevent depression in people who show initial signs.
