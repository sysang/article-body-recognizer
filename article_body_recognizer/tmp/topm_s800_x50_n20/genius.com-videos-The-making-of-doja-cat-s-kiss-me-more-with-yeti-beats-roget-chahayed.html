The Making Of Doja Cat’s “Kiss Me More” With Yeti Beats Rogét Chahayed

07:06
273,505
by Rahel Gebreyes
@rgebreyes

Doja Cat’s “Kiss Me More” featuring SZA peaked at No. 3 on the Billboard Hot 100 and broke the record once held by Brandy and Monica’s “The Boy Is Mine,” making it the longest-running all-female Top 10 hit in Hot 100 history.

The song is produced by longtime Doja Cat collaborator Yeti Beats and Rogét Chahayed, and appears on Doja’s third album, Planet Her. Genius recently sat down with both producers to break down how the song was made.

“We just have fun when we make music. Every time never feels like we’re in a session or there’s a pressure to make a hit song,” Chahayed said. “[Yeti Beats] really liked it when I started playing it and was like, ‘That sounds really dope. Let’s roll with that.’”

Yeti Beats then sped up the chord progression and added a few more tweaks to make it “funky or disco-y.” After reworking the beat a bit, he showed it to Doja.

“I literally brought it to the studio that night and Doja heard it and was like, "I love it. I got a song about kissing.”
