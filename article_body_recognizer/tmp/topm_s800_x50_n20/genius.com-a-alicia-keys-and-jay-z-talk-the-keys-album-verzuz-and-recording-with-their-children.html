Alicia Keys And Jay Z Talk the KEYS album, Verzuz and Recording With Their Children

news
Dec 22, 2021
by Rob Markman
@RobMarkman
Referenced Artists
JAY-Z
Alicia Keys
Mike WiLL Made-It
Referenced Albums
KEYS

The two music icons spoke with Genius on the Twitter Spaces platform.

When Alicia Keys released her eighth solo album Keys on December 10, she introduced a new artistic concept on the double LP. On Side A (Originals), she offers an array of stripped down, piano-driven, soul-bearing tracks, that she then serves up to producer Mike Will Made-It on Side B (Unlocked), so together they can remix, reimagine, and represent the album all on one project.

It is the type of boundary pushing Keys has exhibited her entire career, starting with her debut Songs in A Minor back in 2001. So when it came time to promote her latest, Keys linked with Genius on Tuesday (December 21) to present a one-of-a-kind conversation on Twitter Spaces— the social media giant’s new audio forum. The room opened up at 7pm ET and the event which was billed as a conversation with Alicia and “very special guests” quickly lived up to the hype, when Jay-Z entered the chat.

“I think she just broadened the space of what can be done,” Hov said of Keys’ latest. “I think it’s super brave and a brilliant album.”

From there, the convo (moderated by Genius’ VP of Content Strategy Rob Markman) shifted to a discussion on how artists of Alicia and Hov’s stature have been able to balance the art and the commerce. There are 100s of millions of records sold between the two artists, and their music has generated multi-million dollar paydays for themselves and the labels they’ve recorded with through the years. Still, both Keys and Jay have remained champions for artistic integrity.

Alicia tackled the question first. “The balance is that I’ve been fortunate enough to have had so much commercial success that I’m able to be free and create music,” she said before she tossed to Jay.

“I think it’s the music business, but the music always comes first,” he said. “Music can exist without record labels, record labels can’t exist without artists.”

It was a serious meeting of the minds, but there were fun and light hearted moments as well. Like when Jay was asked if he’d ever do a Verzuz— the platform created by Timbland and Alicia’s husband Swizz Beatz, where artists go against each other in a grand celebration of music.

“No disrespect. Everyone is amazing and [has] done what they’ve done,” Jay said, warming up for his chest-beating boast. “There’s not a chance in hell that anyone can stand on that stage with me.”

They also talked about collaborating musically with their children. Beyoncé and Hov’s daughter Blue Ivy appeared on Jay’s 4:44 album and Alicia recently recorded a remake of “Somewhere Over the Rainbow” with her son 11-Year-Old Egypt

“I was all up in my feelings, it’s a beautiful thing. It’s a memory never to be forgotten,” Keys said of the session.

And it was a night never to be forgotten, as the two talked for over 90 minutes, sharing their opinions, a few laughs, and perspective on the state of the music business. How cool is that?
