For The Record: Breaking Down Drake’s ‘Certified Lover Boy,’ Kanye Beef “IMY2”

44:45
141,823
by Rahel Gebreyes
@rgebreyes

Last week, Drake dropped his highly anticipated album, Certified Lover Boy. The album, which smashed streaming records on both Apple Music and Spotify, is packed with features from Travis Scott, JAY-Z, Kid Cudi, Nicki Minaj and many more. For the latest episode of For The Record, Genius' VP of Content Strategy Rob Markman sat down with TikTok’s Artist Partnership Lead Erin Lowers and Amazon’s Rap Rotation Radio host Letty Peniche to discuss the highlights of the album, Drake and Kanye’s ongoing beef, and the unexpected Cudi collaboration on “IMY2.”

For Peniche, Drake and Kid Cudi’s “IMY2” was a clear standout on the album. The song marks the first real collaboration between both rappers, who had a public falling out back in 2016. Peniche was happy to see them reconcile their differences on a song that represented the perfect “mesh” of Cudi and Drake’s styles.

“Neither fell into the other’s vibe too much, and sometimes you get that with collabs,” she said. “To see that come together and for them to be in each other’s good graces, ugh, I love it. That’s my fave.”

Lowers weighed in on “7AM On Bridle Path” and Drake’s decision to take his beef with Kanye further by leaking “Life Of The Party,” which featured a heartfelt verse from André 3000. She said she thought the leak was a fair move from Drake, but was disappointed that 3 Stacks was caught in the crossfire.

“There’s competition and I think it was all fair. And it really does suck that André was caught in the middle,” she said. “I think that’s not going to be the fun part. And I want to see Drake and Dre actually collaborate the way that Dre wants them to, but a casualty of war, I guess.”
