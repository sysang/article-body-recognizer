Ways To Keep Your Liver Healthy

Lower sugar intake

Credits: Unsplash

Credits: Unsplash

Lowering the consumption of sweetened beverages and total fructose intake, particularly from added sugars, may lessen the risk of chronic liver disease

Alcohol consumption

Credits: Unsplash

Credits: Unsplash

Studies suggest that more than four drinks (48 grams) of alcohol per day are linked to an elevated risk of cirrhosis and liver cancer

Immunization

Credits: Unsplash

Credits: Unsplash

Vaccines for liver diseases such as, hepatitis A and B, should be administered in a full course to keep the liver healthy 

Regulate supplements

Credits: Unsplash

Credits: Unsplash

Excessive iron from supplements, such as those found in over-the-counter multivitamins, can cause iron to build up in liver cells

Dietary fat

Credits: Unsplash

Credits: Unsplash

Patients with impaired liver functions should have an isocaloric diet with a moderate total fat intake and a low saturated fat content to reduce the risk of liver ailments
