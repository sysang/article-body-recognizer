Initiatives we support

Reforestation

Planting trees is one of the simplest and most efficient ways to reduce the effects of climate change. Trees absorb carbon and release oxygen, so seeding new forests helps soak up pollution and replace it with clean, breatheable air.

Direct Air Capture

Direct air capture is a unique technology that allows CO2 to be pulled directly from the atmosphere. Then, it’s used as fuel or safely stored within our earth’s natural geology. This process results in negative carbon emissions, making way for a truly carbon-neutral future.

Bio-oil

Quickly igniting natural waste, like wood and leaves, without using oxygen creates a useful byproduct called bio-oil. Bio-oil can be used in place of environmentally harmful crude oil in instances. Or, it can be placed deep inside the earth to safely store carbon for millions of years.

Biochar

The same chemical reaction that produces bio-oil also creates biochar, a solid, charcoal-like soil that improves farmland and helps grow plants. In addition to aiding agriculture, biochar stores carbon in a safe, solid form, ultimately contributing to a carbon-negative process.
