U.K. Rapper Central Cee Delivers His First “Daily Duppy” Freestyle

news
Dec 27, 2021
by Ken Partridge
@KPartridge
Referenced Artists
Central Cee
Referenced Songs
Daily Duppy (feat. GRM Daily)

The two-part song arrived on Christmas.

On December 25, London rapper Central Cee blessed fans with his first-ever entry in GRM Daily's “Daily Duppy” freestyle series. The song has two parts—the first was produced by British beatmaker Young Chencs, while the second was handled by New York City artist Cash Cobain. The boast-heavy track quickly landed in the Top 20 on the Genius Top Songs chart.

Cee begins the first part—which he teased on Instagram back in July—by comparing himself to a certain Italian explorer whose exploits in the Americas shook up the globe, to say the least.

My flow’s unmatched I talk to myself on my own, I’m tapped Columbus, the way I took over the map

Later in the first section, Cee alludes to famous women hitting him up on social media.

Bare blue ticks in my DM requests Got bare bad Bs that I ain’t holler back

Cee prides himself on being an independent artist, and in another pair of lines from the first section, he raps about how major labels can’t offer him anything—except maybe attractive AR (artist and repertoire) reps.

I got label execs tryna sway me I’m tryna fuck on the AR lady

To start the second part—teased on Instagram in November—Cee rhymes about the importance of creating generational wealth. He then compares himself to two of history’s greatest MCs, referencing a conspiracy theory linked to one and name-checking a classic album, Ready to Die, by the other.

I might do the same, get out of here sooner Fake my death like 2Pac, move out the fam to a crib in Cuba They wanna see a man go out like Biggie, but I ain’t ready to die

Cee must’ve had it bad for a certain AR woman, because he later returns the idea of romancing a record-company employee.

Can’t mix pleasure with business, but this AR lady’s fire Gotta keep this one quiet, gotta deny it, she might get fired

Toward the end of the song, Cee references a viral TikTok meme based on a snippet of Drake’s Instagram Live recorded in June 2019, right after the Toronto Raptors won the NBA title. Central Cee has yet to record with Drake, but he did appear in some promos for Drizzy’s Nike-affiliated NOCTA collection earlier this year.

What d’you mean, “Breathe, breathe”? Don’t tell me to breathe Bae, be yourself with me, what you mean it’s only me? Don’t sell me a dream
