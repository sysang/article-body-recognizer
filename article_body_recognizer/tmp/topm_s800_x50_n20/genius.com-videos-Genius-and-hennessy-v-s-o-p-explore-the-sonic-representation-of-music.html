Genius and Hennessy V.S.O.P Explore The Sonic Representation Of Music

56:23
10,468
by Genius
@Genius

A lot goes into making a piece of music. There’s the composition, the production, and the vibe in the studio. There’s also the lifetime’s worth of influences that each artist brings to the table. Then, there’s an element of collaboration. With the right people in the room, anything can happen.

Genius explores the sonic representation of music—and the nature of modern creativity—in the second installment of “A Genius Perspective,” a video series we’ve created in partnership with Hennessy V.S.O.P. Hosted by journalist and podcaster Brandon “Jinx” Jenkins, the episode features singer, songwriter, dancer, model, and animator Dawn Richard in conversation with Grammy-winning Florida record producer Erik “Rook” Ortiz, a member of the production and songwriting team J.U.S.T.I.C.E. LEAGUE. The spirited discussion supports Hennessy’s “More Is Made by The Many” campaign, which celebrates the power of embracing different perspectives to expand potential with a new creative platform.

Rook and Richard share a lot in common. They’re multitalented children of musicians who’ve grown into trailblazing artists willing to challenge the status quo.

“I felt like the escape was in art,” Richard says in the video. “I’m the awkward kid. I’m always the oddball. Art saved me. The moment I felt like the escape was artistic, that was the moment I said, ‘I’m an artist.’”
