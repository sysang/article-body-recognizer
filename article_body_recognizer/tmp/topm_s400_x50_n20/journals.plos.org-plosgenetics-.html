Research Article

Selective sorting of ancestral introgression in maize and teosinte along an elevational cline

Calfee et al. evaluate opposing selection forces on introgressed ancestry between maize and its wild teosinte relative. They find that the majority of teosinte ancestry tracts introgressed into maize over 1000 generations ago, maintained much of its diversity, and likely came from a common ancestral source.

Research Article

A CENH3 mutation promotes meiotic exit and restores fertility in SMG7-deficient Arabidopsis

Capitao et al. showe that Arabidopsis individuals partially deficient in the RNA decay factor SMG7 fail to exit meiosis and continue with attempts to undergo additional cycles of post-meiotic chromosome segregations without genome replication. They also find that reducing the amount of centromeric histone partially restores pollen formation and fertility in smg7 mutants.

Image credit: Capitao et al., pgen.1009779

10/20/2021

Research Article

Deficiency in cytosine DNA methylation leads to high chaperonin expression and tolerance to aminoglycosides in Vibrio cholerae

Carvalho et al. investigate the role of VchM, a DNA methyltransferase, in the response of the human pathogen Vibrio cholerae to aminoglycoside antibiotics. They show that deletion of vchM allows V. cholerae cells to cope with aminoglycoside stress at both sub-lethal and lethal concentrations.

Image credit: T.J. Kirn, M.J. Lafferty, C.M.P Sandoe R.K. Taylor

10/18/2021

Research Article

Machine learning to predict the source of campylobacteriosis using whole genome data

Arning et al. use core genome MLST, whole genome sequences, and multiple machine learning algorithms to find more accurate source attribution for foodborne illnesses-causing bacterium Campylobacter jejuni. They provide a scalable approach to global disease surveillance that can continuously incorporate novel samples for source attribution.

Image credit: Arning et al., pgen.1009436

10/21/2021

Research Article

Minimalistic mycoplasmas harbor different functional toxin-antitoxin systems

Hill et al. investigate three candidate toxin-antitoxin systems in a pathogenic subspecies of M. mycoides. They confirm the functionality and neutralization capacities of all three candidate TA systems, and show that several of the toxins tested in the study could be used to establish a kill switch in mycoplasmas for industrial applications.

Image credit: Hill et al., pgen.1009365

10/18/2021

Research Article

Deciphering the genetic architecture and ethnographic distribution of IRD in three ethnic populations by whole genome sequence analysis

Biswas et al. study inherited retinal dystrophies in Mexican, Pakistani, and European-American patients. They reveal a spectrum of mutations contributing to IRD and identify a large proportion of novel potentially causative variants.
