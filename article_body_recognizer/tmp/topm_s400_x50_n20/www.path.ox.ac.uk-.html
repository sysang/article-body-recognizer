The Dunn School and BREXIT

Scientists at the Dunn School aim to discover the molecular and cellular mechanisms underlying human health and disease. We are proud of our long history of exceptional achievements, and excited by the prospect of future discoveries.

The Dunn School has been fully international for nearly 100 years and Brexit will not change this. We continue to strive towards a community that celebrates and respects diversity of all kinds.

Around 300 scientists work at the Dunn School, including more than 100 postdoc scientists and 80 graduate students, from over 30 countries. Our outstanding facilities, mentorship and support provide them with a springboard to future success.
