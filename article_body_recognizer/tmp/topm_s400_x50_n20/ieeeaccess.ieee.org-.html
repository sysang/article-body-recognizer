Entrants who have already submitted a manuscript to IEEE Access without a video can still submit a video for inclusion in this Award so long as the video is submitted within 7 days of the article submission date. All videos must undergo peer review and be accepted along with the article submission. Videos may not be submitted after an article has already been accepted for publication. 

The criteria for an article to be accepted for publication in IEEE Access are:

  1. The article should be original writing that enhances the existing body of knowledge in the given subject area. Original review articles and surveys are acceptable even if new data/concepts are not presented.
  2. Results reported have not been submitted or published elsewhere (although expanded versions of conference publications are eligible for submission).
  3. Experiments, statistics, and other analyses are performed to a high technical standard and are described in sufficient detail.
  4. Conclusions are presented in an appropriate fashion and are supported by the data.
  5. The article is presented in an intelligible fashion and is written in standard English.
  6. Appropriate references to related prior published works must be included.
  7. The article falls within scope of IEEE Access
  8. Compliance with the IEEE PSPB Operations Manual.
  9. Completion of the required IEEE intellectual property documents for publication.
  10. The discretion of the IEEE Access Editor-in-Chief.
  1. Disqualification: The following items will disqualify a video from being considered a valid submission:
  1. The video is not original work.
  2. A video that is not accompanied with an article submission.
  3. The article and/or video is rejected during the peer review process.
  4. The article and/or video topic does not fit into the scope of IEEE Access.
  5. The article and/or do not follow the criteria for publication in IEEE Access.
  6. Videos posted in a comment on IEEE Xplore.
  7. Content ​is off-topic, offensive, obscene, indecent, abusive or threatening to others.
  8. Infringes the copyright, trademark or other right of any third party.
  9. Uploads viruses or other contaminating or destructive features.
