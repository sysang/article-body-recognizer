Nubank and HashiCorp Are Set to Trade Thursday After Pricing Their IPOs

Published: Dec. 8, 2021 at 7:11 p.m. ET
By

Luisa Beltran
comments

Referenced Symbols

    BRK.B
    -0.44%

Nubank, the Brazilian digital lender backed by Warren Buffett’s Berkshire Hathaway, priced its much anticipated initial public offering at $9 a share, a person familiar with the situation said. 

At $9, Nubank’s valuation is nearly $41.5 billion. 

Nubank had planned to offer roughly 289.2 million shares at $8 to $9 each, a Nov. 30 prospectus said. This is down from a previous range of $10 to $11. Nubank will trade Wednesday on the New York Stock Exchange under the ticker NU and on the São Paulo Stock Exchange using the symbol NUBR33.

Morgan Stanley, Goldman Sachs, Citigroup, and NuInvest are the lead underwriters on the IPO. 

Nubank, whose operational headquarters are in São Paulo, is one of the most valuable fintechs in Latin America. Founded in 2013, the company started out issuing credit cards with no annual fees. Today, it also offers debit cards, bank accounts, loans, and life insurance. The company describes itself as one of the largest digital-banking platforms, with 48.1 million customers across Brazil, Mexico, and Colombia as of Sept. 30. More than 70% of its customers are under 40, and about 28% are Brazilians 15 years or older, the prospectus said.

HashiCorp, a cloud software provider, also priced its IPO at $80 a share, above its $68 to $72 price range, the person said. HashiCorp was offering 15.3 million shares at $68 to $72 each, its prospectus said. 

At $80, HashiCorp’s valuation is $14.3 billion. It will trade on the Nasdaq under the ticker HCP. Morgan Stanley, Goldman Sachs and JP Morgan are lead underwriters on the deal. 

Founded in 2012, HashiCorp provides a suite of multi-cloud infrastructure automation products that help companies accelerate their time to market, reduce their cost of operations, as well as improve their security and governance of complex infrastructure deployments, according to a prospectus. It had 2,392 customers as of Oct. 31, including Roblox (RBLX), Bank of America (BAC), Progressive (PGR) and Stripe. 

Nubank and HashiCorp are two of three high-profile deals that are closing out the IPO market this month. December is typically a slower month for new issues. Samsara, an internet of things company, is going public next week.
