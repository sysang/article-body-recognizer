Moving can be extremely stressful. Not only are you uprooting your entire life, but the logistics of transporting all of your possessions can be overwhelming. Fortunately, a good moving company can help facilitate your move and ensure that everything makes the trip undamaged. It can even pack and unpack your belongings if you wish, placing them in their proper locations in your new home.

Below, we list the top-rated moving companies of 2021 to help you choose a company that best meets your needs and budget. We also explain how much a moving company costs and provide some key tips to make the process as smooth as possible.

International Van Lines: International Van Lines places No. 1 in our Best Moving Companies of 2021 rating. Although it specializes in international moves, International Van Lines also offers local and domestic long-distance moves. International Van Lines provides highly rated customer service, including assigning two managers to each long-distance move, which isn't typical.

International Van Lines is also a good choice if you want a full-service moving company but don't want to pay top dollar, as it charges middle-of-the-road prices in comparison to other companies in our rating while still providing expert, personalized service. However, while the company is a great choice for a variety of moves, it doesn't specialize in commercial moving services.

United Van Lines: United Van Lines is No. 2 in our Best Moving Companies of 2021 rating. It's one of the most recognized names in moving and has been in business since the 1920s. Although United Van Lines is a full-service moving company, it has particular expertise in corporate relocation and customizes its services for every company it works with. In addition, the mover partners with the U.S. military to relocate armed services personnel.

United Van Lines' website is clear and transparent, offering extensive insurance options and explaining them thoroughly. On the downside, United Van Lines isn't the best choice if you're on a budget, as it charges more than some other well-known, full-service moving companies.

Colonial Van Lines: Coming in at No. 3, Colonial Van Lines has a network of more than 200 agents in the moving and storage industry across the U.S. It offers both individual and corporate relocation services. Colonial Van Lines' MoveHub blog provides moving tips and tricks, along with information about each of the states the company serves. Its mobile app can help estimate the cost of your move after you upload photos and videos of your belongings, as well as assist with inventorying and tracking items in transit.

If you're looking for longevity, Colonial Van Lines is a good choice, having been in business for more than 50 years. The mover currently doesn't offer services to Alaska, Hawaii, or outside the U.S. Another downside is the 30%-40% deposit, which is higher than many companies require.

U-Pack: Also tied at No. 4, U-Pack's DIY approach is different from the full-service companies in our rating. U-Pack provides a truck or a smaller container (depending on the volume of stuff to be moved) that the company drops off at your home. You then pack and load your belongings into the truck or container yourself or hire a third party to do it. After that, U-Pack – through its primary carrier ABF Freight – transports the items to the final destination, where you unload and unpack everything.

The total cost is based on the amount of linear footage occupied by the items to be moved. If you're looking to save some money and don't mind doing the grunt work yourself, U-Pack is a good option, as it's one of the cheaper companies in our rating.

PODS: Tying for No. 6 in our rating is PODS, which is similar to U-Pack. PODS, short Portable On Demand Storage, claims to have invented portable storage in 1998. Unlike some other moving companies such as U-Haul, where customers typically rent a vehicle and do all the work (including driving), PODS customers don't transport their own items. Instead, they rent a storage container, pack and load it themselves, and hire PODS to transport it to a storage facility or new location on their behalf.

The company offers moving and storage services to residential and commercial customers across the U.S. and a few other countries.

U-Haul: If you want to save money by packing, loading, transporting, and unloading your own belongings, consider U-Haul. Tied for No. 6, it's the cheapest company in our rating for both short- and long-distance moves. The company prides itself on its smooth-riding, easy-to-drive trucks, and you can purchase various levels of insurance depending on your needs. Students can take advantage of the Collegeboxes program, which lets them pack up and store possessions from a college dorm room or apartment for a short period of time like over the summer.

However, if you're looking for more help with the process and don't want to do all the heavy lifting yourself, this probably isn't the right company. U-Haul has pickup locations for rentals across Canada but doesn't handle other international moves.

American Van Lines: Completing the three-way tie for No. 6 is American Van Lines. Its prices aren't the cheapest in our rating, but the mover offers flat-rate pricing and binding estimates that guarantee what your final price will be. This gives customers peace of mind because their costs won't go up if the move takes longer or is more difficult than expected. In addition, American Van Lines' movers are background-checked and certified to move heavy or delicate items like pianos and antiques, which require more expertise to avoid damage.

The company provides seemingly unfiltered reviews on its website, which means you can get a more realistic view of its services and whether it’s a good fit for your situation. American Van Lines requires a 50% deposit to book a move, which is higher than the other companies in our rating.

Mayflower: Coming in at No. 9, Mayflower has been in business for more than 90 years and provides both residential and commercial moving services. It offers services for those making a move locally, across the country, or internationally, and it has storage solutions for customers who need them. It also serves members of the military.

Mayflower doesn't require a deposit to schedule a move, and its online portal helps customers keep track of the moving process. Customers can use this portal to check the status of their move, use a provided moving checklist, keep track of moving contacts, and ensure everything is staying on track.
