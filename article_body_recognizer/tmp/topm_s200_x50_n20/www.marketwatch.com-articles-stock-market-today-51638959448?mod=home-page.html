The stock market posted modest gains Wednesday amid news that Pfizer and BioNTech’s Covid-19 vaccine works against the Omicron variant after three shots.

The Dow Jones Industrial Average rose 35 points, or 0.1%, on Wednesday, after the index had rallied 492 points Tuesday. The SP 500 gained 0.3%, while the Nasdaq Composite advanced 0.6%.

“[There is] great news from Pfizer that a booster will work in adding solid protection against Omicron and stocks are celebrating,” wrote Peter Boockvar, chief investment officer of Bleakley Advisory Group. 

Pfizer (ticker: PFE) said three doses of its Covid-19 vaccine neutralizes the Omicron variant.

But fears around Omicron had already been fading. The SP 500 has risen about 4% since the Dec. 1 bottom following the Black Friday selloff. For now, the SP 500 is sitting just a hair below its all-time high. 

That’s much of why stock gains were weaker Wednesday than those of Tuesday, as stocks were digesting the recent rally, wrote Michael Reinking, senior market strategist at New York Stock Exchange.

Investor attention is now turning to the meeting of the Federal Open Market Committee—the Federal Reserve’s monetary policy body—next week. Fed Chair Jerome Powell has signaled that the central bank is considering reducing its monthly asset purchases at a faster pace amid higher inflation.

Investors are upping their bets that the Fed will tighten policy and raise interest rates sooner rather than later. Before news of the Omicron variant broke, markets had largely priced in an initial interest rate increase at the Fed’s June 2022 meeting, but some market participants are betting on an increase coming earlier.

“What the Fed does from here should be the market’s predominant focus,” Boockvar wrote.

Overseas, the pan-European Stoxx 600 was 0.6% lower. In Hong Kong, the Hang Seng Index underperformed its Asian peers, rising less than 0.1%, amid concerns about how China’s embattled and heavily indebted property sector could affect Chinese economic growth in 2022.

Here are five stocks on the move Wednesday:

ChargePoint Holdings (CHPT) dropped 3.7% after reporting a wider-than-expected loss.

Stitch Fix (SFIX) tumbled 24% after beating earnings forecasts but offering disappointing guidance.

Tesla (TSLA) rose 1.6% after reporting its November China sales numbers.

Stanley Black Decker (SWK) stock gained 3.4% after the company said it will sell most of its security business to Securitas AB for $3.2 billion in cash and that it will buy back $4 billion in stock next year. 

Kraft Heinz (KHC) stock dipped 3.9% after getting downgraded to Neutral from Buy at Guggenheim.
