How to Hire Top Marketing Consultants

What is a marketing consultant?

Marketing consultants work with companies to understand their target market and create and implement marketing strategies. This can include creating a detailed marketing plan, updating the company’s brand, and identifying how to deliver the right message to their target audience.

How do you hire a marketing consultant?

You can source marketing consultants on Upwork by following these three steps:

  • Write a project description. You’ll want to determine your scope of work and the skills and requirements you are looking for in a marketing consultant.
  • Post it on Upwork. Once you’ve written a project description, post it to Upwork. Simply follow the prompts to help you input the information you collected to scope out your project.
  • Shortlist and interview marketing consultants. Once the proposals start coming in, create a shortlist of the professionals you want to interview. 

Of these three steps, your project description is where you will determine your scope of work and the specific type of marketing consultant you need to complete your project.

How much does it cost to hire a marketing consultant?

Rates can vary due to many factors, including expertise and experience, location, and market conditions.

  • An experienced marketing consultant may command higher fees but also work faster, have more-specialized areas of expertise, and deliver higher-quality work.
  • A contractor who is still in the process of building a client base may price their marketing consultant services more competitively. 

Which one is right for you will depend on the specifics of your project. 

How do you write a marketing consultant job post?

Your job post is your chance to describe your project scope, budget, and talent needs. Although you don’t need a full job description as you would when hiring an employee, aim to provide enough detail for a contractor to know if they’re the right fit for the project.

Job post title

Create a simple title that describes exactly what you’re looking for. The idea is to target the keywords that your ideal candidate is likely to type into a job search bar to find your project. Here are some sample marketing consultant job post titles:

  • Marketing talent needed to build a strategic digital marketing plan
  • Pro needed to expand company’s marketing reach into new markets and attract new customers
  • Marketing consultant needed to assist a new company in building brand recognition

Project description

An effective marketing consultant job post should include: 

  • Scope of work: From improving relationships with existing clients to developing digital marketing training for current staff, list all the deliverables you’ll need. 
  • Project length: Your job post should indicate whether this is a smaller or larger project. 
  • Background: If you prefer experience with certain industries, software, or social media platforms, mention this here. 
  • Budget: Set a budget and note your preference for hourly rates vs. fixed-price contracts.

Marketing consultant job responsibilities

Here are some examples of marketing consultant job responsibilities:

  • Identify new opportunities for a company to engage with existing clientele
  • Analyze current marketing materials, advertising, and strategies to determine how to successfully target customer population
  • Measure the success of deploying new marketing strategies with the generation of sales
  • Create a damage control plan if the company’s brand absorbs any bad publicity

Marketing consultant job requirements and qualifications

Be sure to include any requirements and qualifications you’re looking for in a marketing consultant. Here are some examples:

  • Working knowledge of marketing, business, or communications
  • Preferred marketing expertise (e.g., content marketing, digital marketing, social media)
  • Software experience with marketing software such as Marketo, Eloqua, or Pardot
  • Marketing experience on a variety of social media platforms including Twitter, Facebook, Instagram, and TikTok
