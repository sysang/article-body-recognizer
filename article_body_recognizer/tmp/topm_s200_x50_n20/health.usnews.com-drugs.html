Frequent Questions About Drugs and Treatments

What are the common types of medical treatments?Medical conditions and symptoms are treated or maintained in a variety of ways. Prescription medications and over-the-counter drugs are common medical treatment options. Some conditions benefit from the support of a medical device, like a blood glucose monitor for diabetes management. Others see improvement using natural treatment options, like cranberry supplements to treat a urinary tract infection.What does over the counter medication mean?OTC medications do not require a written prescription to obtain. They are bought in-store, typically at a pharmacy, or online without the supervision of a medical practitioner.What is prescription medicine?Prescription medications require a medical practitioner to advise and authorize the use of a drug or treatment option. Practitioner refers to physicians of any specialty, including physician assistants, nurse practitioners and dentists. The prescribed medication is intended for use only by the patient it has been prescribed for. The patient should follow the individualized dosage and instructions set out by their doctor.OTC or prescription medications: What should you ask?You should consult a health care professional before opting for any nonprescription medications, particularly if you have previously received a medical diagnosis or are taking any other medications or supplements. Some questions to ask a medical practitioner, such as a pharmacist, include:
  • What options are available to treat my symptoms?
  • Which one should I use?
  • Will this nonprescription medication interact with anything else?
For prescription medications, your medical provider has determined that prescribing a particular drug will be more beneficial to your recovery process than not taking it. Some questions you should ask your doctor before getting started include:
  • Why are you prescribing this drug?
  • How long will I be taking this?
  • When will I begin to feel better?
  • Will this drug interact with anything?
  • What are common side effects of this drug? When should I revisit a doctor?
  • Are there alternative treatment options I should consider?
What does a doctor consider when prescribing medications?Before prescribing a medicine, your doctor will make a diagnosis. They will take your medical history, social history, any drug allergies and other key factors into consideration before deciding which prescription medication will best address your diagnosis. Ultimately, the doctor has decided that the positive benefits outweigh any negative side effects of this medication.How long can you take medicine after the expiration date?The Food and Drug Administration requires over-the-counter and prescription medications to include an expiration date. Expiration dates designate the time period in which the drug should be safely consumed. Consuming drugs past their expiration date could mean:
  • Harmful effects. Drugs can degrade over time, causing the chemical makeup to shift and produce a potentially toxic result for the consumer.
  • Decreased effectiveness. Drugs can lose effectiveness and may not deliver the intended benefit over time.
Drugs should be disposed of after their expiration date. The FDA provides guidance on steps to take for proper disposal of expired medications.Do natural treatments really work?Natural treatments may provide relief from symptoms you’re experiencing. But these remedies should not serve as a replacement for diagnosis or treatment. Make sure to discuss any options you’re considering with a medical provider first.U.S. News, in partnership with Pharmacy Times, asked hundreds of pharmacists which over-the-counter products they recommend most often.
