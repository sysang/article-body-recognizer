13 Best 3-Row SUVs for Families in 2021

For These SUVs, Bigger Is Better

Not that long ago, family haulers were limited mostly to minivans. That's no longer the case. Today's three-row SUVs are big enough to handle a growing family or a pack of friends, as well as their luggage. Here, we highlight a baker's dozen of the best.

The majority of vehicles profiled here are midsize SUVs that start in the low- to mid-$30,000 range. Large SUVs, however, can push that starting price north of $50,000. A number of these three-row family haulers have a lengthy standard-features list. Still, many of these vehicles come with a range of engines, options, and/or trims that can quickly elevate prices.

The following SUVs are ranked according to their U.S. News overall scores.

The U.S. News overall scores cited here were correct at the time of writing. These scores are constantly updated as new data and professional automotive reviews become available. Therefore, scores on the site may differ from those here.

<h2><strong>11) 2021 Chevrolet Suburban (Tie)</strong></h2> <h3><strong>$51,700 | U.S. News Overall Score: 7.9/10</strong></h3> <p>The <a href="/cars-trucks/chevrolet/suburban/2021">2021 Chevrolet Suburban</a> is a large family's dream. The base V8 engine has plenty of power for most shoppers. For those who want exhilarating power, the available 6.2-liter V8 delivers. There's also a diesel option. Properly equipped, the Suburban can tow 8,300 pounds.</p> <p>The Suburban bests nearly all competition when it comes to cargo space, with 144.7 feet of maximum cargo capacity. The standard configuration seats eight. Swapping out the second-row bench seat for captain's chairs lowers that number to seven. The cabin is pleasant enough, but not eye-popping. The first two rows of seats are well-cushioned, but the third-row seats are a bit thin. Head- and legroom are not an issue.</p> <p>The infotainment system has a 10.2-inch touch screen, <a href="/cars-trucks/what-is-apple-carplay">Apple CarPlay</a>, <a href="/cars-trucks/what-is-android-auto">Android Auto</a>, and eight USB ports. A suite of advanced driver-assistance features comes standard.</p>

<h2><strong>11) 2021 Buick Enclave (Tie)</strong></h2> <h3><strong>$41,195 | U.S. News Overall Score: 7.9/10</strong></h3> <p>There are few midsize SUVs that can match the <a href="/cars-trucks/buick/enclave">2021 Buick Enclave's</a> cargo space. There's 23.6 cubic feet behind the third row. Fold the rear two rows down and you'll discover 97.6 cubic feet.</p> <p>For passengers, the Enclave provides plenty of head- and legroom in all three rows. Seats are well-padded, and climbing into the third row is a snap. The intuitive infotainment system is run through an 8-inch touch screen and includes Apple CarPlay, Android Auto, a Wi-Fi hot spot, and six USB ports.</p> <p>The Enclave has a V6 engine that produces plenty of power. Ride quality is settled and smooth, even over rough surfaces.</p> <p>No vehicle is perfect, however. Cabin materials are disappointing for an SUV in this price range. Also, city driving can be tough, as the Enclave doesn't maneuver well in tight spaces.</p>

<h2><strong>11) 2021 Volkswagen Atlas (Tie)</strong></h2> <h3><strong>$31,545 | U.S. News Overall Score: 7.9/10</strong></h3> <p>Space abounds in the <a href="/cars-trucks/volkswagen/atlas/2021">2021 Volkswagen Atlas</a>. This midsize SUV has 96.8 cubic feet of cargo space when the two back rows are folded down. With all seats in use, there's a respectable 20.6 cubic feet. Passengers in the first two rows will enjoy lots of head- and legroom, but third-row accommodations are a bit tighter. A user-friendly infotainment system is standard.</p> <p>The ride is smooth and refined. Engine power is decent, but not exhilarating. A nice added touch is the 8-inches of ground clearance. The Atlas is no <a href="/cars-trucks/jeep/wrangler">Jeep Wrangler</a>, but it can handle driving down snow-covered streets or dirt roads. Standard advanced driver-assistance features include blind spot monitoring and forward collision warning with pedestrian detection.</p> <p>The biggest complaint with the Atlas is with cabin materials. The use of hard plastics is out of step with most competitors.</p>

<h2><strong>9) 2021 Dodge Durango (Tie)</strong></h2> <h3><strong>$31,885 | U.S. News Overall Score: 8.0/10</strong></h3> <p>The <a href="/cars-trucks/dodge/durango/2021">2021 Dodge Durango</a> is available in six trims and with four available engines. The base V6 is a reasonably powerful engine. It's not great under hard acceleration, but it is otherwise sufficient enough for daily driving. The Durango can tow up to 8,700 pounds when properly equipped, which is excellent for a midsize SUV.</p> <p>All Durangos, except for the base model, come standard with three rows. It's optional in the base model. The first two rows are comfortable and offer plenty of passenger space. The third row, while smaller, is still comfortable enough for adults on shorter trips. Cabin materials are underwhelming in the base model, but get markedly better in higher trims.</p> <p>The infotainment system is good, but the number of standard active-safety features is minimal: a rearview camera is the lone feature in the base model. The Durango also has mixed crash-test ratings.</p>

<h2><strong>9) 2021 Chevrolet Traverse (Tie)</strong></h2> <h3><strong>$29,800 | U.S. News Overall Score: 8.0/10</strong></h3> <p>For cargo space in a midsize SUV, the Traverse is hard to beat. There's up to 98.2 cubic feet behind the first row. With a full complement of passengers, there's still a respectable 23 cubic feet behind the third row. Adults can sit in all three rows, though the third row is a bit tight. The standard infotainment system is excellent and includes a 7-inch touch screen, six USB ports, a Wi-Fi hot spot, Apple CarPlay, and Android Auto.</p> <p>The <a href="/cars-trucks/chevrolet/traverse/2021">2021 Chevrolet Traverse</a> is comfortable to ride in, but drivers will find the handling not particularly responsive. A 310-horsepower V6 is the sole engine option and is sufficient for daily driving.</p> <p>The standard driver-assistance features list is a bit thin. Forward collision warning and automatic emergency braking, which are standard on many midsize utes, are optional on the Traverse.</p>

<h2><strong>8) 2021 Volkswagen Tiguan</strong></h2> <h3><strong>$25,245 | U.S. News Overall Score: 8.1/10</strong></h3> <p>The <a href="/cars-trucks/volkswagen/tiguan/2021">2021 Volkswagen Tiguan</a> is an all-around solid compact SUV. Cargo capacity tops out at 65.7 cubic feet, which is quite good for the class. The cabin is also a pleasant place to be, with upscale materials and a clean, minimalist design. Passengers in the first two rows enjoy plenty of head- and legroom. The third row, unsurprisingly for a compact ute, is best left to children. The user-friendly infotainment system has Apple CarPlay, Android Auto, and a Wi-Fi hot spot.</p> <p>The frustrations start under the hood. There's enough power for scooting around town, but accelerating to highway speeds or passing slower-moving cars requires some planning. Fuel economy – up to 22 mpg in the city and 29 on the highway – is below average for the class. Finally, the ride is comfortable, but the steering is numb and there's noticeable body roll through turns.</p>

<h2><strong>7) 2021 Toyota Highlander Hybrid</strong></h2> <h3><strong>$38,410 | U.S. News Overall Score: 8.2/10</strong></h3> <p>The <a href="/cars-trucks/toyota/highlander-hybrid/2021">2021 Toyota Highlander Hybrid</a> has a relatively hefty starting price, but it provides a lot in exchange. Cabin materials are among the nicest you'll find in a midsize SUV, and where plastics are used, they're soft-touch and pleasant in appearance. Cargo space for a midsize hybrid SUV is quite good. The first two rows are comfortable, and passengers will find plenty of space to stretch out. For adults, the third row is best avoided: the seats aren't comfortable and space is limited.</p> <p>A user-friendly 8-inch touch-screen infotainment system comes standard, and includes Apple CarPlay, Android Auto, and Amazon Alexa Auto. The Highlander Hybrid's list of active safety features is lengthy and includes forward collision warning, automatic emergency braking, and adaptive cruise control. Fuel economy is good – although not class-leading – and the engine makes plenty of power.</p>

<h2><strong>5) 2021 Chevrolet Tahoe (Tie)</strong></h2> <h3><strong>$49,000 | U.S. News Overall Score: 8.3/10</strong></h3> <p>The redesigned <a href="/cars-trucks/chevrolet/tahoe/2021">2021 Chevrolet Tahoe</a> is a cargo-hauling beast. This large SUV offers as much as 122.9 cubic feet behind the first row. There's 72.6 cubic feet behind the second row, and because the third row folds with the touch of a button it's easy to access. Seats in the front row are comfortable. Those in the second and third rows are decent, but they don't have as much padding. Passenger room is good everywhere but the third row. While not extremely cramped, it's simply not long-haul friendly for anyone but children.</p> <p>The base engine is plenty powerful. Properly equipped, the Tahoe can tow up to 8,400 pounds. One knock on the Tahoe is that the ride is quite firm.</p> <p>The 10.2-inch touch-screen infotainment system is one of the best in the industry, and there's a long list of standard active safety features.</p>

<h2><strong>5) 2021 Mazda CX-9 (Tie)</strong></h2> <h3><strong>$33,960 | U.S. News Overall Score 8.3/10</strong></h3> <p>Most three-row utes are passenger-focused; the <a href="/cars-trucks/mazda/cx-9">2021 Mazda CX-9</a> is driver-focused. An efficient turbo-four engine generates plenty of power to move this midsize SUV with ease. Throw the CX-9 into a curve, and it stays well-planted while the passengers stay snug in their seats.</p> <p>Passenger space in the first two rows is ample, and the seats are comfortable. However, the third row is not adult-friendly, and cargo space is below average for the class. An excellent 10.3-inch touch-screen infotainment system is the CX-9's nerve center and comes standard with Apple CarPlay, Android Auto, and a Wi-Fi hot spot. The interior build quality is top-notch, and road noise is kept to a minimum.</p> <p>Standard advanced driver-assistance aids include blind spot monitoring, forward collision warning, automatic emergency braking, and adaptive cruise control.</p>

<h2><strong>2) 2021 Hyundai Palisade (Tie)</strong></h2> <h3><strong>$32,525 | U.S. News Overall Score: 8.4/10</strong></h3> <p>There's not a great deal of difference between the <a href="/cars-trucks/hyundai/palisade/2021">2021 Hyundai Palisade</a> and the <a href="/cars-trucks/kia/telluride/2021">2021 Kia Telluride</a> (more on the Kia later). In fact, they're corporate cousins. Among the things they share in common are a wonderfully potent V6 engine, smooth-riding suspension, a user-friendly infotainment system, and a lengthy list of standard active safety features.</p> <p>If there's a significant difference, it's in the interior. Both SUVs have attractive cabins, but the Palisade is the more elegant of the two. Higher trim levels create a near-luxury-grade cabin experience.</p> <p>You can't lose with either, so choosing between them comes down to which more closely fits your aesthetic sense.</p>

<h2><strong>2) 2021 Honda Pilot (Tie)</strong></h2> <h3><strong>$32,250 | U.S. News Overall Score: 8.4/10</strong></h3> <p>The <a href="/cars-trucks/honda/pilot">2021 Honda Pilot</a> checks a lot of the right boxes for a midsize SUV. It features a large cargo area (up to 109.2 cubic feet), comfortable and adult-friendly seats in all three rows, good-quality cabin materials, and a potent standard V6 engine. Standard advanced driver-assistance aids include forward collision warning and adaptive cruise control.</p> <p>The Pilot's standard infotainment system is rudimentary. Jumping to the EX trim will cost about $2,700 more, but it adds a user-friendly 8-inch touch screen as well as Apple CarPlay and Android Auto. Additional active safety features include blind spot monitoring and rear cross traffic alert. Finally, the driver gains a 10-way power-adjustable seat.</p>

<h2><strong>2) 2021 Kia Telluride (Tie)</strong></h2> <h3><strong>$31,990 | U.S. News Overall Score 8.4/10</strong></h3> <p>It's hard to find fault with the <a href="/cars-trucks/kia/telluride/2021">2021 Kia Telluride</a>. This midsize SUV features attractive, high-end materials even in the base model. Adults can ride in all three rows, but the third row can feel cramped on longer trips. The seats in the first two rows are excellent. Cargo space, while not class-leading, is above average.</p> <p>Drivers will have no problem getting the Telluride up to speed. The V6 engine produces 291 horsepower, and the transmission makes the most of that power. Handling is crisp, making the Telluride feel smaller than it is. You can maneuver this SUV around mall parking lots easily. Unfortunately, as with its corporate sibling, the Hyundai Palisade, fuel economy isn't the best.</p> <p>The infotainment system is user-friendly and responsive. The long list of standard driver-assistance aids includes blind spot monitoring, forward collision warning with pedestrian detection, and adaptive cruise control.</p>

<h2><strong>1) 2021 Kia Sorento</strong></h2> <h3><strong>$29,390 | U.S. News Overall Score: 8.5/10</strong></h3> <p>Our top-rated midsize SUV is also our top-rated three-row SUV. The <a href="/cars-trucks/kia/sorento/2021">2021 Kia Sorento</a> is completely redesigned for 2021 and sports an outstanding predicted reliability score. While there are three rows of seats, only the first two are adult-friendly. The third row, like those in many midsize SUVs, is on the snug side. Front- and second-row seats are exceptionally comfortable. The UVO infotainment system is easy to use and comes with an 8-inch touch screen, Android Auto, and Apple CarPlay.</p> <p>The base engine does a good job, but the available turbo-four will put some pep in your accelerator step. Fuel economy with both engines is among the best in the class. The handling is relatively sporty, and while the ride is not perfectly smooth, it's a comfortable cruiser overall.</p>
