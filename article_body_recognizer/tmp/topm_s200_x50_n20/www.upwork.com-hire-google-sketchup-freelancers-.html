How to Hire Top SketchUp Designers

What is SketchUp?

SketchUp is a high-quality 3D modeling software program. It is a powerful application designed to allow everyone from amateurs to seasoned experts create a unique vision for their project.

How do you hire a SketchUp designer?

You can source SketchUp designer talent on Upwork by following these three steps:

  • Write a project description. You’ll want to determine your scope of work and the skills and requirements you are looking for in a SketchUp designer.
  • Post it on Upwork. Once you’ve written a project description, post it to Upwork. Simply follow the prompts to help you input the information you collected to scope out your project.
  • Shortlist and interview SketchUp designers. Once the proposals start coming in, create a shortlist of the professionals you want to interview. 

Of these three steps, your project description is where you will determine your scope of work and the specific type of SketchUp designer you need to complete your project. 

How much does it cost to hire a SketchUp designer?

Rates can vary due to many factors, including expertise and experience, location, and market conditions.

  • An experienced SketchUp designer may command higher fees but also work faster, have more-specialized areas of expertise, and deliver higher-quality work.
  • A contractor who is still in the process of building a client base may price their SketchUp designer services more competitively. 

Rates typically charged by SketchUp designers on Upwork are:

  • Beginner: $20 per hour
  • Intermediate: $30 per hour
  • Advanced: $50 per hour

Which one is right for you will depend on the specifics of your project. 

How do you write a SketchUp designer job post?

Your job post is your chance to describe your project scope, budget, and talent needs. Although you don’t need a full job description as you would when hiring an employee, aim to provide enough detail for a contractor to know if they’re the right fit for the project.

Job post title

Create a simple title that describes exactly what you’re looking for. The idea is to target the keywords that your ideal candidate is likely to type into a job search bar to find your project. Here are some sample SketchUp designer job post titles:

  • Need SketchUp of small cabin framing with dimensions and material list
  • Seeking 3D SketchUp modeler/landscape designer
  • Modeler needed to render small residential 3D drawing with SketchUp

Project description

An effective SketchUp designer job post should include: 

  • Scope of work: From measurements to topography, list all the deliverables you’ll need. 
  • Project length: Your job post should indicate whether this is a smaller or larger project. 
  • Background: If you prefer experience with certain industries or SketchUp versions, mention this here. 
  • Budget: Set a budget and note your preference for hourly rates vs. fixed-price contracts.

SketchUp designer responsibilities

Here are some examples of SketchUp designer job responsibilities:

  • Render 3D models for interior or exterior projects
  • Provide dimensions, measurements, and/or material list for project
  • Include floor plan and/or example furniture in the model
  • Create a 360° panorama tour

SketchUp designer requirements and qualifications

Be sure to include any requirements and qualifications you’re looking for in a SketchUp designer. Here are some examples:

  • 3D design, 3D graphic models, renderings
  • Working knowledge of SketchUp and site design
  • Background in architecture, land planning, or similar
  • Experience digitizing photos, hand drawings, and/or site plans
