Extraordinary story of how 'bow girl' JoJo Siwa built a $20 MILLION empire by just 18 after leaving Dance Moms - and how coming out has helped her shed her 'little kid' image

  • JoJo Siwa's star rose during Covid despite lockdowns and tour postponement 
  • She came second on Dancing With The Stars and revealed she is pansexual
  • JoJo has amassed A-List fans from the Kardashian children to Cardi-B
  • She's shed her 'little kid' image and has a new grown up look heading into 2022 
  •  Siwas Dance Pop Revolution streaming now, only on Hayu

    Teen phenomenon JoJo Siwa has used 2021 to complete a very adult transformation - from Nickelodeon darling to a bona fide star worth an estimated $20million.

    JoJo got her big break on Dance Moms when she was just 11 - before going on to sell her own line of signature 'JoJo bows' as well as bedding, toys, clothing and shoes. 

    She's sold more than 80 million bows globally and counting - bringing in an astonishing $400millon in revenue.

    Now 18, JoJo says she's shedding her 'little kid' image as her fans grow with her.

    JoJo came out as pansexual earlier this year - and while she and her girlfriend have since amicably split, she said it helped with cementing her new 'grown up' look. 

    Then she became one half of the first same-sex coupling on 'Dancing With The Stars' alongside professional dancer Jenna Johnson. 

    Teen phenom JoJo Siwa has year transitioned from Nickelodeon darling to a bona fide star worth an estimated $20million (pictured with Kim Kardashian and her daughter, North)

    JoJo Siwa attended the iHeartRadio Jingle Ball in Los Angeles last week

    Coming out publicly about her sexuality was not a decision JoJo took lightly, and one that has helped to change the trajectory of her career and personal life.

    It'll still be me and my unicorns... just a bit different.

    'I think it made a lot of people gain a lot of respect for me,' she told Daily Mail Australia. 

    'So many people thought I was a little kid and when I came out they thought ''wait a second, maybe there's something a little more'' and that's something I'm grateful for.'

    The shift is something she's interested to see play out in 'real life' when she begins touring again in the wake of Covid.

    She recently competed on Dancing With The Stars as one half of the first ever same sex coupling

    JoJo has a mature new look and told Daily Mail Australia she's ditched the signature side ponytail and bow 

    While she was unable to tour and perform live during lockdown, JoJo and her 'momager' Jessalyn Siwa utilised the time at home to begin looking for the new crop of tween superstars.

    'The girl group we've put together is magic,' JoJo said.

    She and Jessalyn created Siwa's Dance Pop Revolution to find a group of girls who could sing, dance and keep their cool under pressure to tour alongside JoJo and form a girl band that could rival the likes of One Direction and Little Mix. 

    JoJo's own demographic of fans has 'aged up' in the 12 months since her D.R.E.A.M tour was postponed - due in large part to her successful stint on Dancing With The Stars and a conscious decision on her behalf to begin a more mature dialogue with fans. 

    She got her big break on Dance Moms when she was just 11, starring alongside Maddie Ziegler and Abby Lee Miller on the hit Lifetime reality TV show

    Kim Kardashian dropped North off to JoJo's house last year to be babysat after revealing the seven-year-old was a huge fan

    Coming out publicly about her sexuality was not a decision JoJo took lightly, and one that has helped to change the trajectory of her career and personal life

    'My demographic has changed and my fans have aged up a bit while also staying the same,' she said. 'There are now teenagers that are fans whereas last time I toured, teens hated me.'

    With her signature big bows, side ponytail and glittery ensembles marketed to a young audience, JoJo is acutely aware she mightn't have been particularly appealing to teenagers desperate to look cool in front of their peers. 

    Despite her legions of new followers, JoJo is determined to give original fans the concert they've been expecting - with a twist.

    'It'll still be me and my unicorns,' she laughed. 'It's definitely going to be a JoJo spectacular... just a bit different.' 

    Pictured: JoJo Siwa attends the 2021 American Music Awards at Microsoft Theater on November 21 wearing a sophisticated black dress and heels

    She's ditching her signature side ponytail and bow, telling Daily Mail Australia her rescheduled D.R.E.A.M tour wardrobe will be getting a makeover to reflect her new style. 

    'That's just not who I am anymore, but the show is going to be great. I want my concerts to be kids pop meets PrideFest.' 

    There was no expectation from JoJo's team to maintain her youthful image or grow up before she was ready, despite 'constant' industry pressure to look or act a certain way.

    'Every day in the industry there is pressure. To do something, wear something, say something,' she said.

    Even among other A-List celebrities, the 18-year-old has amassed a cult-like following

    But the weight of that pressure has never been too heavy for the 18-year-old, who is now worth an estimated $20million.

    She'd always been thick skinned and her experience on Dance Moms only toughened her up further. 

    JoJo says she makes the decision every single day to 'ignore' the external pressures to be who she's not.

    'I've chosen to just be on my path. I live every day how I want to, I work how I want to and I think the best thing I've ever done is not letting anything get in my way.

    'I have chosen to ignore the pressure every single day that I've worked, every single day of my life.'

    The 18-year-old opened up about her sexuality earlier this year, revealing she identified as pansexual and had a girlfriend. The couple have since amicably split and remain friends

    It's 'crazy', JoJo says, that staying true to herself has afforded her so much success over the years.

    Even among other A-List celebrities, the 18-year-old has amassed a cult-like following.

    Kim Kardashian dropped North off to JoJo's house last year to be babysat after revealing the seven-year-old was a huge fan.

    'As a parent, you want your kids to admire positive figures,' Kardashian said at the time. 'There's no one more positive than JoJo. You just can't help but smile when you see her rainbow ponytail. She's a great role model for children.'

    Earlier this week, Cardi B pleaded with the teenager to come to her house and entertain her daughter, Kulture, for Christmas.

    'Every single person I've met whose kids are fans or they're fans, they're all amazing. It's almost confusing. It's kind of crazy that the Kardashian kids are fans,' JoJo said.
