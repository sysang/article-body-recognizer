Supreme Court to hear Maine case that could expand school voucher programs nationwide

John Fritze
USA TODAY

WASHINGTON – When the Supreme Court hears arguments Wednesday in a case about whether parents may use state education money for sectarian schools the central question will deal with religious freedom versus separation of church and state. 

But lurking just below the surface is a broader battle over school choice, including voucher programs, which could grow significantly depending on how the court rules.
