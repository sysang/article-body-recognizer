Bank of England may relax mortgage affordability checks to make it easier to borrow - but experts warn the move will stoke house price inflation

The Bank of England is considering relaxing the affordability checks that borrowers must pass in order to take out a mortgage. Currently, borrowers applying for a mortgage - whatever the initial rate - will need to satisfy their lender that they could afford to pay a 'reversion rate'. This is a hypothetical future mortgage payment, usually based on the lender's standard variable rate of interest, plus three per cent. It was introduced in 2014, in a bid to avoid a repeat of the financial crisis, and is designed to make sure the borrower can still pay their mortgage after their initial fixed term ends, even if rates rise. However, the BofE is said to be considering lowering the three per cent figure, according to the Telegraph. This would make it easier for borrowers to pass the checks and qualify for a loan.
