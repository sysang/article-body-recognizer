The Revlon Plus: When will it land in the UK?

Following on from the super-successful Revlon One Step Volumiser, the new Revlon Plus is taking the US by storm with its smaller barrel size and wider selection of heat settings.

Written by:

Katia Damborsky

Published: 08 Nov 2021
Last updated: 03 Dec 2021
Share Article
Link copied to clipboard.

Freshly launched and already set to become a TikTok-approved bestseller, the Revlon Plus is the latest hair tool engineered by the great minds at Revlon.

It’s the same winning mix as the original Revlon One Step Hair Dryer and Volumiser, but with a couple of key changes.

Read more: The viral Babyliss 9000 Cordless Hot Brush is probably about to sell out everywhere

What’s new about the Revlon Plus?

  • Smaller, 2.4 inch (61mm) barrel size
  • Handle and barrel are detachable (great for travel)
  • Additional heat setting
  • Upgraded motor for 40% longer life

What’s the same as the original Revlon One Step Hair Dryer and Volumiser?

  • Same oval design
  • Stay-cool tip
  • Same mix of boar hair and nylon bristles

If you read our review comparing the Revlon One Step Hair Dryer and Volumiser with the Dyson Airwrap, you’ll know that one of our biggest complaints about the Revlon offering was the size of the barrel. Its bulkier size, while great for volume and bounce, was hard to control and difficult to use at the roots, leading to damp patches that went frizzy and refused to lift.

Check out the Revlon Plus at Target

If you’re an avid reader of Daily Mail Best Buys, you might also know that our favourite hot air brush is the Tresemme Volume Smooth and Shape because of its bristle style and size of its brush. The bristles really cling to the hair and the small barrel is perfect for adding proper shape and definition- ideal if you want actual curls and movement.

This is where we’re particularly excited about the Revlon Plus. While we don’t think the plastic-topped bristles will be quite powerful enough for curling super-thick hair, we do think that the new brush size will be better for controlling your blow-dry and adding a bit more body where you need it.

But if this sounds familiar, you’re not wrong; Revlon actually launched a small version of its original brush a little while ago in the UK- the Revlon One-Step Hair Dryer And Volumiser Mid to Short Hair.

From what we can see, it has roughly the same 61x38mm barrel size as the new Revlon Plus (which is currently only available in the States), but it lacks the extra heat setting, detachable brush head and new and improved motor.

So, while we don’t know if and when the new Revlon Plus will land in the UK, you can still snap up the Small-to-Mid length version for the same effect.
