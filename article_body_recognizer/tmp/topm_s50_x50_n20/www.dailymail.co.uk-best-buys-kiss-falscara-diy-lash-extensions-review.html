Best Buys Approved: The cheapest, easiest way to get lash extensions at home

Let’s start off by saying that this review has been written by someone who can’t put on fake eyelashes to save her life. Until now.

Written by:

Katia Damborsky

Published: 08 Oct 2021
Share Article

Hands up, who else loves lash extensions?

Now hands up, who hates the price and the hassle of going to the salon to get infills?

We thought so.

Luckily, there’s a new solution for getting DIY lash extensions at home- and it’s unbelievably easy to use, even for someone that has two left hands when it comes to putting on false lashes.

No, it doesn’t last quite as long as salon lash extensions- but for the price and the ease of application, we’re willing to overlook it.

Let’s start off with the FAQ

What is Kiss Falscara?

Kiss Falscara is the name of a line of at-home DIY lash extension products that work by applying a glue-based bonding mascara to your lashes and then gently pressing small clusters of lash extensions to the underside of your own eyelashes. You then apply a layer of sealing gel to keep them in place.

Both the bonding product and the sealing product apply like normal mascara (the bonding gel is also black) so it’s incredibly easy to apply. When you’re done wearing the lashes, you can remove them using any oil-based makeup remover, or you can purchase the Kiss Falscara remover.

Is Kiss Falscara safe to use?

Yes, it’s very safe to use. The process might seem a little daunting at first- you have to place the glue pretty close to your eye and then stick the eyelashes to the bottom of your lashes rather than the top- but somehow it just works.

How long does Kiss Falscara last?

This really depends on how you apply the lashes and how well you take care of them. The normal bonding product makes no claims about the length of time it lasts, but Kiss has recently released an overnight bonding gel that claims to make your lash extensions last for up to 10 days.

Can you get different types of at-home lash extensions?

Yes! There’s the original Lengthening and you can also get Volumizing and Lifting sets. The Lengthening (also known as the Wisps 02) are the thinnest and most natural of the three, followed by the Lifting (also called Wisps 01), which has slightly more oomph. The Volumizing (Wisps 03) set is the closest to what professional-grade eyelash extensions look like.

How do you apply Kiss Falscara?

Brush on the bonding adhesive (a little goes a very long way) then place a mirror just in front of your chin so you’re looking almost straight down. For best results, use the false lash applicator that comes in the starter kit and grab a wisp firmly close to the middle-ends of the lashes and then place it below your lashes, as close to the lashline as possible. If you have a hand free, use it to pull the skin above your eye to widen your eye so you can get a better placement.

Buy Kiss Falscara Bond and Seal

Does Kiss Falscara damage your natural lashes?

Any product that contains chemicals is going to have an adverse effect on your natural lashes if you leave them on for too long. However, Kiss Falscara isn’t any worse for your lashes than salon-quality lash extensions. Plus, since the wisps are applied along your waterline there’s no risk of them weighing down your natural lashes.

Can you reuse Kiss Falscara Wisps?

Yes. They’re reusable up to three times with proper care.

Look: 10/10

I like my lashes to look as natural as possible when I get them done professionally, and I don’t often wear hybrids of Russians. To me, these look exactly like the lash extensions I usually go for in the salon. I choose the least voluminous and most ‘sparse’ of Falscara’s three wisp styles, and you can tell that I’m wearing lash extensions but they’re definitely not obvious.

I also really like my lashes to have a bit of a wing to them, and that’s easily achievable with the Falscara. I genuinely feel like I’m waking up with the lashes that princesses have in cartoons.

Snap up Kiss Falscara Wisps 01

Ease of application: 9/10

Let me start by saying that I’m horrible at applying false eyelashes. I’ve never been able to do it successfully and I’ve always had to ask my friends to help me. These completely changed the game for me- I think it’s because the wisps are small and easy to work with and the glue goes on like a mascara.

Be sure not to use too much of the glue, otherwise your lashes will look clumpy. And as we’ve said above, try and position a mirror directly under your face so you’re looking down into it, and use your other hand to pull the skin and widen your eye if possible. I usually apply the adhesive mascara across all my eyelashes first, then begin applying the wisps in a line using the applicator (which is an excellent tool- I would thoroughly recommend).

The mix of long, medium and short lashes is great for creating a true DIY lash extensions look, but if you decided not to use to the short or only use the long the finished result won’t look unbalanced or weird. I usually use the long ones exclusively and I only apply to the outer corner of my eye, and the result is the perfect almond-shaped eye.

Ease of removal: 6/10

I haven’t used the proper lash remover that Kiss sells, so it’s hard to judge this one. Plus, I tend to use too much of the bonding adhesive which means more clean-up when it’s time for removal. But generally speaking, an oil-based cleanser does a good job of removing the lashes and cleaning them ready for re-use.

The actual adhesive is harder to shift since it turns into a very sticky paste after a few days and it really clings to your eyelashes. You have to be very careful while you’re rubbing your eyes otherwise you can expect some lash shedding.

Anything else to note?

For die-hard lash extension lovers, Kiss Falscara might not pass muster, particularly if you really really love hybrids or Russians (having said that, the 03 style does make for a very convincing Russian lash extension style). These will work best for people who like a more natural look and don’t mind reapplying their lashes more often.

But for the price and the ease of use alone, the Kiss Falscara really is second to none. Start with the starter kit to see if you like it (and you'll get the applicator as well). Then if you're a fan, you can move on to trying out new styles and testing the overnight bonding mascara.
