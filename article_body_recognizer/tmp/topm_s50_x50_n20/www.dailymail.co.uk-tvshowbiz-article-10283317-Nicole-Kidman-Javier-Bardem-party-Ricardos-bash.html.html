    Nicole Kidman and Javier Bardem were in the mood to celebrate on Monday evening as they lived it up at the after-party of their upcoming movie Being The Ricardos premiere after-party. 

    The actress, 54, who plays on-screen icon Lucille Ball in the Amazon movie, swapped her ethereal red carpet gown for a sexy blazer mini dress as she lived it up with her on-screen husband, 52, who plays Desi Arnaz at the Academy Museum of Motion Pictures in LA.

    The duo shared a laugh throughout the evening and cosied up, before Javier proved to be a man of many talents by storming the stage and playing the drums - to the delight of his fellow revellers.

    Being The Ricardos: Who plays who?

    The Amazon film Being The Ricardos is a true story about the stars behind the classic sitcom I Love Lucy, Lucille Ball, and Desi Arnaz, as the film follows the power couple as they face a crisis that could end their careers and another that could end their marriage.

    So who are the modern-day actors portraying the quintessential players behind the beloved sitcom? 

    Incredible: Nicole Kidman (left) looked the spitting image of the iconic Lucille Ball (right) as she was featured in one of the character posters for Being The Ricardos released by Amazon Studios in November

    Nicole Kidman as Lucille Ball

     The 54-year-old American-born Australian actress looked the spitting image of the iconic I Love Lucy star as she was featured in one of the character posters for Being The Ricardos released by Amazon Studios in November.

    Nicole had the perfect signature red hair with old Hollywood curls as she contrasted her porcelain skin with a swipe of bright ruby red lipstick. 

    Ball was an American actress, comedian, model, studio executive, and producer as she portrayed the protagonist in the classic sitcom.

    She was nominated for 13 Primetime Emmy Awards, winning five times, and was the recipient of several other accolades, such as the Golden Globe Cecil B. DeMille Award and two stars on the Hollywood Walk of Fame.

    Big shoes to fill: Javier Bardem stars as Desi Arnaz who was both Lucille's husband on legendary 1950s-era sitcom I Love Lucy and in real life

     Javier Bardem as Desi Arnaz

    Javier Bardem stars as Desi Arnaz who was both Lucille's husband on legendary 1950s-era sitcom I Love Lucy and in real life.

     Bardem, 52, channeled the Cuban-American actor perfectly as he looks off into the distance while holding onto his signature basket-weave fedora.

    Desiderio Alberto Arnaz y de Acha III, better known as Desi Arnaz, was a Cuban-American actor, musician, bandleader, comedian and film and television producer, revolutionary in the creation of modern television.

    Man with the plan: Tony Hale, 51, donned thick framed plastic eyeglasses while holding onto a leather notebook as he portrays Jessurun James 'Jess' Oppenheimer who was the head writer of I Love Lucy

    Tony Hale as Jessurun James 'Jess' Oppenheimer

    Tony Hale, 51, donned thick framed plastic eyeglasses while holding onto a leather notebook as he portrays Jessurun James 'Jess' Oppenheimer who was the head writer of I Love Lucy. 

    Oppenheimer was an American radio and television writer, producer, and director. 

    He was the producer and head writer of the CBS sitcom I Love Lucy. Lucille Ball called Oppenheimer 'the brains' behind I Love Lucy.

    Stunning: Alia Shawkat, 32, also posed with a notebook along with a swipe of bright red lipstick as she portrayed writer on the sitcom Madelyn Pugh

     Alia Shawkat as Madelyn Pugh

    Alia Shawkat, 32, also posed with a notebook along with a swipe of bright red lipstick as she portrayed writer on the sitcom Madelyn Pugh.

     Pugh was a television writer who became known in the 1950s for her work on the I Love Lucy television series.

    Oh so now you're fine with someone being a writer? Jake Lacy, 36, pensively put a pencil under his bottom lip as he looked off into the distance as he portrayed Bob Carroll Jr. who wrote on the show alongside Pugh and Oppenheimer

    Jake Lacy as Bob Carroll Jr.

    Jake Lacy, 36, pensively put a pencil under his bottom lip as he looked off into the distance as he portrayed Bob Carroll Jr. who wrote on the show alongside Pugh and Oppenheimer.

    Carroll Jr. was an American television writer notable for his creative role in the series I Love Lucy, the first four seasons of which he wrote with his professional partner Madelyn Pugh, and collaborator Jess Oppenheimer.

    The new Amazon Studios movie centers on a single production week of the 1950s sitcom I Love Lucy and focuses on the relationship between Lucille and her co-star Desi — who played Lucy and Ricky Ricardo in the comedy respectively — and Nicole revealed that the project helped her get to know 'the woman behind the lipstick'. 

    The Bombshell actress explained: 'I fell in love with her as I was reading the script. I realized what she was doing (was) trailblazing for women.'

    Javier also revealed that he knew little about his character despite the popularity of I Love Lucy in the actor's native Spain.

    Man with the plan: Being The Ricardos was written and directed by Aaron, who was joined by his daughter Roxy

    Altogether now: Todd Black, Aaron Sorkin, Nicole Kidman, Javier Bardem, Tony Hale, J.K. Simmons, Linda Lavin, Alia Shawkat, and Nina Arianda posed from left to right

    The 52-year-old star shared: 'I was obsessed with him and his skills as a comedian, as a person, as a producer and a musician at that time while being a foreigner in this country.'

    Sorkin recreated an iconic I Love Lucy moment in the film with Ball stomping on grapes during a winemaking scene and Nicole was determined to perform it to perfection.

    She said: 'It was my obsession to get it absolutely accurate. It was his obsession to have this human being portrayed — what's behind the creation of Lucy Ricardo and who is the woman behind this character?' 

    Sorkin recently told The Hollywood Reporter that Ball and Arnaz's daughter Lucie Arnaz urged him not to sugarcoat the story of their marriage and their classic series.

    Monochrome madness: Samantha Harris donned a lacy white top with a black pencil skirt

     Flower power: Blanca Blanco donned a floral midi dress for the occassion

    'We all had lunch together, and she said to me, "I know my mother wasn't an easy woman,"' he recalled. '"Take the gloves off." In other words, you can go for it.' 

    Despite some concerns that Kidman and Bardem didn't look enough like their characters, the writer–director said the film was more about the real-life figures than the characters they played on the TV. 

    The Trial Of The Chicago 7 director told Entertainment Tonight in February: 'I think that the people thought that we've cast them as Lucy and Ricky Ricardo...

    'The film takes place during a production week of I Love Lucy — a Monday table read to Friday audience taping, with a whole bunch of long flashbacks which are telling the story that got us here — and because there's a table read, there's rehearsals and then there's tape night, we do see moments of them being Lucy and Ricky Ricardo.' 

    Being The Ricardos is to premiere this Friday in the US, before streaming on Amazon Prime from December 21.
