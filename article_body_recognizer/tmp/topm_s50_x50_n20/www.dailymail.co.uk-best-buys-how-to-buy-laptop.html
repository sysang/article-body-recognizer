A buyer’s guide: What to look for when buying a laptop

Unless you trade in PCs by the year, chances are there’s a lot that’s changed since you bought your last laptop. But don’t stress! We’re walking you through how to buy your next computer.

Written by:

Caitlin Willow Greyling

Published: 14 Jun 2021
Last updated: 07 Sep 2021
Share Article
Link copied to clipboard.

For the less tech-savvy, all laptops might seem the same, but they really aren’t. Equally, if you’re on the search for a new business or gaming laptop, you might be wondering what you should consider.

In today’s market, there are seemingly endless options, each of which has its own merits. So, pinpointing which best suits your needs can be hard. That’s why we’ve compiled the ultimate laptop buying guide, so you feel like a pro as you search for your new PC.

In this article, we’ll cover the types of laptops on the market as well as their features, designs, operating systems, storage space, functionality, price range and battery life. We’ve also got your FAQs sorted. So, read on to get the down-low on how to find the perfect laptop for your needs!

And once you've got your laptop covered, you can start think about add-ons; check out our round-up of the best laptop accessories.

Important features to consider before buying a new laptop

Here are some of the most important features to consider before buying a laptop.

RAM and storage space

RAM (random access memory) is the data storage space that your PC temporarily accesses to run processes. The higher RAM, the more demanding tasks, such as gaming, design and editing a laptop can handle. Storage space also helps with performance but it is mainly where your permanent files get stored.

Laptop size

A smaller, notebook-sized laptop is ideal for travel as it’s compact and lightweight. In contrast, a larger laptop will have a bigger display but may be a bit less portable. So, it’s good to consider how you might use your computer as this might dictate the size of your next laptop.

Display

Besides screen size, there are various screen resolutions, including the standard HD 720p, mid-range HD 1080p, and top-of-the-line Ultra HD (4K). The higher the resolution, the more pixels, giving you a clearer, more distinct display.

Accessories

When looking for a laptop, take a look at which accessories come with it and are built into it, such as a webcam, mouse or laptop case. Also consider what accessories you want to use with your laptop and if they’re compatible with it.

Laptop type

These days, there are some exciting new laptops on the market, including convertibles with detachable keyboards and flip-cover laptops.

Battery life

Longer battery life is always a bonus when using a laptop, especially for travel!

Ports, drives and slots

Which ports are crucial to your day-to-day laptop use? These could include: an audio jack, USB, MicroUSB, SD, microSD, ethernet, HDMI, DisplayPort, DVI, Thunderbolt, and VGA ports and slots. All are useful, but most laptops today won’t have all of these.

Security features

Newer laptops now have some exciting high-tech security features, such as facial recognition, fingerprint readers, and webcam and microphone kill switches.

Design

When looking at laptops, the first thing that comes to your attention is likely its look. Be it modern and sleek, utilitarian, or funky and colourful, we all have our style preferences when it comes to our laptop.

Though the design of a laptop may seem like a purely aesthetic, there’s actually a functional side to a laptop's design. Better materials and slimmer designs not only make a laptop attractive, they have made today’s computers more lightweight, portable and durable in comparison to older models.

Speaking of design, there are now some new, unique types of laptops on the market. Convertible, or 2-in-1 laptops as they are sometimes called, have a detachable keyboard that you can remove to convert them into a tablet. Equally, there are also laptops today with flip covers and touch screens revolutionising how you can interact with your device.

Laptop design at a glance

  • Modern laptops are slimmer and more lightweight than ever.
  • Convertible laptops bridge the gap between laptops and tablets.
  • There are a wide variety of laptop designs available, from sleek to utilitarian, funky to understated.
  • Choose from small 10-inch notebooks up to larger 17-inch laptop models.

Operating System OS

One of the most important aspects of what to look for when buying a laptop is the operating system it runs. Why? Because, unlike most other hardware or software, it's almost impossible to change the operating system later on. The most common operating systems are Microsoft Windows, macOS/Mac OS, Linux and Chrome OS.

Each operating system has its own look and capabilities. What’s more though, your operating system also dictates what software you can or cannot install on the computer. For example, Windows software often won’t work on Mac OS/macOS, Chrome OS or Linux laptops, and vice versa.

Though most common, Microsoft Windows is definitely not your only option, either. All Apple computers, including the iMac all-in-one’s, Mac desktop computers and Macbook notebooks and laptops, run on either the older Mac OS or newer macOS operating systems. Linux is another operating system that actually powers the Android platform - though Linux laptops are less common and often more pricey!

Chrome OS is another popular operating system that’s built around Google’s Chrome browser which lends itself to the common name given to these laptops: Chromebook. Of all operating systems, Chromebooks do have more limited functionality - especially when it comes to installing any type of software.

OS takeaways at a glance

  • Choose software, such as design programmes and games, that are compatible with your operating system or vice versa.
  • If changing to a different operating system, you may need to buy new software versions for your new laptop.
  • Select from laptops running the Microsoft Windows, Mac OS/macOS, Linux or Chrome OS operating systems.
  • Chromebooks running Chrome OS are very affordable if limited in their capabilities.

Storage

The storage space and RAM on a laptop are key to ensuring it performs tasks quickly and effectively. There are two critical types of computer storage: RAM and the laptop's internal storage space or hard disk. As mentioned before, RAM is used for temporary processes, while the other storage space is for your permanent files.

RAM comes in various sizes, usually starting from around 2GB up to as much as 32GB. The average modern laptop has around four or 8GB of RAM. If you’re just using your computer to browse, edit documents, work or do basic computing tasks, 4GB is more than sufficient. However, for gaming, editing or design work, you’ll need at least 8GB if not more. The higher-spec your software or programmes are, the more RAM you’ll require.

Storage space is also an important facet of what to look for when buying a laptop. For most, 256GB of space is enough but, you can also get laptops with as low as 128GB and as high as 1TB of space.

How much storage space you need really depends on how much space your permanent files, such as videos, photos and data, take up. Do also bear in mind that storage types differ. Chromebooks usually utilize smaller 16 to 64GB eMMC cards, many older laptops utilize HDD drives, and newer models often come with SSD drives.

Laptop storage at a glance

  • SSD drives access data faster, thereby improving the speed of programmes, aren’t affected by magnetism, use less power than a standard HDD and are smaller, freeing up internal space in a laptop.
  • HDD drives are cheaper than SSD drives per GB and also come in bigger sizes.
  • Chromebooks are very affordable thanks to their low-cost eMMC storage cards, which are smaller in size, though generally aren’t upgradeable.
  • You can also extend your laptop's storage space using an external hard drive.

Functionality and features

While laptops have a lot of similarities - they’re portable, lightweight and fold flat - each comes with its own unique set of features and functionalities.

A new laptop can come with exciting, new tech, such as touch screens, fingerprint scanners, webcam kill switches or shutters, gaming-level graphics cards and even facial recognition. USB, Thunderbolt, microSD or other ports and slots are another important feature to look for in a laptop, depending on which or how many devices you wish to connect to it.

Security features such as kill switches, webcam shutters, fingerprint scanners, and facial recognition are useful for business laptops, where sensitive documents and files may be stored. Whereas a powerful graphics card will serve you well if you’re planning to use the laptop to run demanding editing, gaming, or design programs.

Functionality and features at a glance

  • New laptops can have high-tech security features such as facial recognition, kill switches, webcam shutters and fingerprint scanners to keep your PC safe.
  • Gaming laptops today are more powerful than ever.
  • You can run demanding editing and design programmes on laptops thanks to powerful graphics cards.
  • Touch screen laptops with pens and styluses are also available.

Battery life

Battery life is another important feature on the list of what to look for when buying a laptop. A longer battery life is ideal, particularly if you plan to use your laptop while traveling or when you’re away from home. The longer the battery life, the longer you can use it without a charge or a power outlet nearby.

While many older laptops could last only four to six hours without charging, most newer models offer much more - around seven to 14 hours, to be exact! However, do check each laptop's specs to see just how much juice it has before you buy, as figures vary. Remember that the battery life of a laptop does decrease over time from use, too.

Battery life at a glance

  • Laptops can now offer up to 14 hours of battery life.
  • A longer battery life allows you to use your laptop for longer periods while away from home, not near a power outlet or in the event of a power outage.
  • You can replace your battery when its battery life is below average or what you require.

Purpose (what you’ll be using the laptop for)

There are a variety of purposes for which you may require a laptop. Perhaps you’re a businessperson who travels frequently. Maybe you’re a creative professional or digital nomad who works from home, your garden, the hotel or wherever else you feel like it.

Or, maybe you’re an avid gamer who wants to be able to LAN or play games at a friend’s house? Perhaps you're none of these - just someone who needs a new PC for basic use, such as browsing the internet, chatting to friends and family or answering emails.

No matter which category you fall into, your reason is reason enough for a new laptop. How you plan to use your laptop will ultimately impact the decision you make. There’s no need to overspend if you rarely surf the web. Equally, if you go for a lower level model, you might not be able to run bigger programmes or games.

The part laptop purpose plays at a glance

  • Business laptops offer a premium look alongside advanced security and privacy features.
  • Gaming laptops are ideal for gaming and usually look cool, too!
  • High-performance laptops can run demanding graphics, editing and design programmes.
  • Chromebooks make an excellent choice for basic use and those who are more familiar with using a tablet or smartphone rather than a PC.
  • There are some excellent, slim, small and ultra-lightweight laptops on the market that are ideal for traveling.
  • Convertible and flip-cover laptops are a fun and versatile choice for home use.

Price

Laptops vary greatly in price, from as little as £100 up to £2000 or more! Business and gaming laptops tend to set you back the most, whereas your standard laptop with integrated graphics, a Chromebook or notebook will likely be more affordable. For the most part, though, a laptop’s features, software and hardware are the key components that affect its price.

However, these are important aspects of what to look for when buying a laptop, as these also affect what the laptop can and cannot do and which programmes it can handle. For example, a more powerful CPU, such as one of the latest 10th Gen Intel CPU’s make the PC run faster. A gaming-grade graphics card lets the laptop run demanding programmes and games that it otherwise wouldn’t be able to. And, more RAM and storage space helps the laptop to perform faster and smoother. All of these features can add up, but depending on your needs, they’re well worth the investment.

Set a budget, but do also take into consideration the specs you actually need in a laptop. Checking the requirements of whichever games or software, such as Office 365, Adobe or other programmes you plan to run on the laptop beforehand is the best way to ensure you buy the right laptop!

Laptop pricing at a glance

  • Business laptops will set you back, on average, £500 to £2000.
  • A standard Chromebook will cost, on average, £100 to £250.
  • Premium Chromebooks will set you back, on average, £250 to £1500.
  • Generally, gaming laptops will set you back £1000 or more.
  • A standard laptop with integrated graphics can cost around £400.

FAQs about buying a new laptop

What are the most affordable types of laptops?

Chromebooks, standard laptops with integrated graphics cards and notebooks are among the most affordable types of laptops.

What is a Chromebook?

A Chromebook is a unique type of laptop that runs on the Chrome OS operating system. They’re limited in function compared to other laptops, though. They operate much more like a tablet in that you download apps from the Google Play and Chrome Web Stores to use on them.

Browse all Chromebooks here

What should I look for in a gaming laptop?

A powerful gaming graphics card, CPU, 8GB or more of RAM and additional cooling fans are all important features to look for in a gaming laptop. Gaming laptops are usually a bit more stylish than your average laptop, too likely featuring a unique gaming-style chassis.

What should I look for in a business laptop?

A business laptop should have high-tech security and privacy features to keep your data safe.

What should I look for in a laptop if I want to use it for editing or design?

A high-performance laptop with similar specs to a gaming laptop, such as more RAM (at least eight to 16 GB) and a powerful CPU and graphics card, is also best for demanding graphics and editing tasks. Do check the actual requirements of the specific editing or design programmes you wish to run beforehand to get an idea of what specs you need, though.

How to choose the right laptop for your lifestyle

Whether or not a laptop fits into your lifestyle is one of the most important aspects of what to look for when buying a laptop. A slim, compact and lightweight laptop that seamlessly fits into your carry-on at the airport may suit you if you’re a frequent traveller.

However, if you need the extra specs and power to run demanding programmes and software, a high-performance or gaming laptop with a powerful graphics card, bigger screen and plenty of cooling fans is best, even if a bit bulkier.

Business laptops may not always offer the best graphics. However, they do generally come with the advanced security and privacy features you may need. So, you can keep those sensitive files safe and secure! If your wants are more basic, you’re shopping for a child’s laptop, a student, retiree or stay-at-home user, opt for a basic, lower-cost laptop or Chromebook!
