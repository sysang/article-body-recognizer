The future has arrived: Why Expo 2020 Dubai heralds the start of a glittering new chapter of global innovation

Ad Feature by Expo 2020 Dubai

By Sarah Gordon

Published: 15:41 GMT, 1 December 2021 | Updated: 17:01 GMT, 6 December 2021

  • e-mail

    • Step into the future at Dubai’s most exciting cultural event... you could even win a five-night holiday there for FREE!

    Ever since the Great Exhibition was held in London in 1851, World Expos have been the place to bring the world together and showcase global innovations.

    At that first expo, London showed off revolvers, diamonds, and leech-powered barometers, but today in Dubai you’re more likely to explore space, be served lunch by a robot and discover the future of AI smart cities.

    After great anticipation Expo 2020 Dubai has flung open its doors and is welcoming the world to explore everything from the future of travel to how the way we eat will change, as well as sustainable farming and ambitious Mars missions. Running until March 2022, it is the first World Expo to be hosted in the Middle East and is celebrating a theme of Connecting Minds, Creating the Future.

    Step into the future at Dubai’s most exciting cultural event... you could even win a five-night holiday there for FREE! Pictured: Crowds at the Sustainability District

    With 192 countries taking part, each showcasing dazzling innovations and inspired ideas, it is a chance to hear from the world’s greatest minds, catch breathtaking shows, immerse yourself in the future of science and explore different cultures.

    The city of the future

    Expo 2020 Dubai is ambitious both in the scope of its ideas and the magnitude of its design. Located near Al Maktoum International Airport, the site covers an area larger than the Principality of Monaco.

    Countries from across the globe have travelled to Dubai to create impressive pavilions designed by the world’s leading architects. Santiago Calatrava’s UAE pavilion is inspired by the revered falcon; Singapore has brought a rainforest to the desert; while the UK pavilion is an interactive structure inspired by Stephen Hawking’s idea of sending a ‘Breakthrough message’ to space, beaming out messages from visitors with an AI algorithm.

    There are three distinct districts that make up the Expo. Visit the Sustainability District and you’ll witness first-hand some of the globe’s amazing advances in creating a balance between technology and nature.

    Then there’s the Opportunity District, which celebrates social innovation and promotes youth empowerment. The goal is to galvanise individuals to take small actions that can become a collective force for positive change in the world.

    In the Mobility District, you'll see how we’ll connect in the future. You can catch a ride in an autonomous vehicle, explore space and the Emirates Mars Mission and even have a chance to design your own plane.

    Once Expo 2020 Dubai is over, these futuristic pavilions and designs will create District 2020, a new smart city welcoming business from around the world to set up headquarters and create a forward-looking, human-centric hub where sustainability is key.

    Visitors at the Mobility Pavilion

    Technology that dazzles

    Travel to the stars with exhibits showcasing new frontiers in space research, and meet astronauts to learn about transformative space technology that can be used to fight climate change. Then venture even further and see how the exploration of Mars and beyond can help us better understand life here on Earth.

    As you stroll among the pavilions, you may spot the odd robot wheeling by. These artificial employees are helping with everything from information and outreach to security and logistics. Need to find a certain pavilion? A friendly android will point the way.

    Autonomous vehicles will be delivering food from the talabat kitchen, a robot can whip you up an ice cream, while the centrepiece Al Wasl Dome comes alive every night with light and colour as the world’s largest 360-degree projection dome, hosting awe-inspiring shows and performances.

    Visitors interacting with robots at Expo 2020

    Where innovation meets social development

    With a large focus on education, development, and social mobility, Expo 2020 Dubai is bringing together leading minds from across the planet to explore how innovation can change the world for the better.

    With theme weeks exploring topics as diverse as climate and biodiversity to space, rural development and protecting water, the ambitious Programme for People and Planet highlights people and organisations achieving amazing things. Meet the minds behind ambitious social initiatives and hear the inventive ideas being brought to life around the world.

    The Good Place Pavilion, by Expo Live

    Participating countries are also showing off other innovations in global sustainability. In the Singapore pavilion, you’ll find an urban rainforest grown and powered by net-zero energy, while the Dutch pavilion has created an integrated climate system that produces food and energy through vertical farming.

    Brazil has recreated the sights, sounds and sensations of the Amazon basin, while Tanzania transports you on safari. Morocco shows how traditional construction techniques can influence contemporary, sustainable design, and Germany is showcasing its future-city lab and innovations such as solar-powered concrete.

    Interior of the Singapore Pavilion

    Tuck into the future

    With so many pavilions to discover and more than 60 events taking place each day, there is a lot to do at Expo 2020 Dubai. Even mealtimes are an innovative experience. You’ll find eateries by award-winning chefs and a vast African food hall showcasing the continent’s most mouthwatering flavours.

    At the Sustainability Hub, you can see and taste innovations in plant-based cuisine, order a cheesy pizza or savoury kebab all made from plant-based ingredients, and dine in a state-of-the-art cloud kitchen as robots prepare your meal. 

    Chef Coco Reinharz at Chroma Restaurant in the Alkebulan Food Hall

    A catalyst for change

    For six months, the world is gathering in Dubai and you’re invited. Global innovators are bringing together groundbreaking technology and ambitious ideas, ready to start a new chapter in world history.

    Combine a winter sun break to dynamic Dubai with an inspiring cultural show, immersive experiences and the chance to step into the future. Expo 2020 Dubai is all about inspiring the world to leave a legacy of change.

    Visitors walk from Al Wasl to the UAE Pavilion

    To find out more or book your tickets, visit expo2020dubai.com
