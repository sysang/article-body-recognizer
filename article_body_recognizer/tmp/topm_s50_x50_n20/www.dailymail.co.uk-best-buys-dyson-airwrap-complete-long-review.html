Dyson Airwrap Long: A complete review

You’ve heard of the classic Dyson Airwrap, but its big sister promises the same salon-worthy results with new longer barrels that are specifically targeted to those of us with long hair. But is it worth it?

Written by:

Katia Damborsky

Published: 17 Aug 2021
Last updated: 03 Dec 2021
Share Article
Link copied to clipboard.

Building on the success of an already market-leading product is no easy feat, but Dyson has managed to take everything that makes the original tool so stellar and package it up in the Dyson Airwrap Complete Long.

The new(ish) product is tailored towards people with hair that’s slightly longer than shoulder length.

But if you’re thinking about taking the purchasing plunge, is this new high-tech hair styling tool worth it? Let our honest review of the Dyson Airwrap Complete Long help you make that decision.

What comes with the Dyson Airwrap Complete Long?

As you might expect from the name, the complete package contains everything you’ll need for a range of different hair styles. Here’s everything you get:

30mm Long Airwrap™ barrels (clockwise and anti-clockwise) Good for: Tight curls, S-shaped waves.

40mm Long Airwrap™ barrels (clockwise and anti-clockwise) Good for: Loose curls, beachy waves, blowout-style curls.

Soft smoothing brush Good for: Smoothing down the hair pre-styling, taming frizz and adding smoothness.

Firm smoothing brush Good for: Ironing out minor kinks before styling, controlling frizz and flyaways.

Round volumising brush Good for: Creating body, movement, volume and shape.

Pre-styling dryer Good for: Taking hair from wet to damp ready for styling.

Storage case Good for: Storing and protecting your Airwrap Long, as well as all the different attachments.

How does the Dyson Airwrap work?

The Dyson Airwrap is groundbreaking in its ability to dry and curl hair at the same time. When it comes to styling, it harnesses pioneering Coanda technology that works by creating a spinning vortex of air around the tool which the hair is then ‘sucked’ into.

Unlike traditional curling tools, which work by heating a hot metal surface that the hair is moulded and shape around, the Airwrap primarily uses air to get the same effect- making it less damaging on your hair.

It also claims to be lighter, quieter and much faster than other types of electric hair styling tools.

How curly can you get your hair with the Dyson Airwrap Long?

From personal experience, we know it varies a lot depending on how you use the styler. Bear in mind that the two curling attachments will give you different results based on how damp your hair is when you begin. You’ll also get a slightly different outcome depending on how tightly the hair is wrapped around the barrel and how long you hold it there.

Generally speaking; the dryer the hair, the tighter it’s wrapped around the barrel and the longer you hold it there, the more you’ll get a defined, ringlet-style hair curl. If you pin and hairspray it (and your hair’s not too thick) it’ll keep that definition. If you leave it loose, it’ll fall into a more lived-in wave.

Here’s how to get beautiful curls using the Dyson Airwrap:

  1. Dry your hair using the dryer attachment until it’s about 70% dry- paying particular attention to the root.
  2. Brush using the firm brush to smooth down any frizz. Your hair should be around 80% dry at this point.
  3. Attach whichever curling attachment you want. Our Editor Katia prefers the smaller one because the curl falls out a lot on thick hair, so the thinner barrel keeps the curls a little more defined throughout the day.
  4. When choosing your curl attachment, work out which barrel is clockwise vs. which is anti-clockwise. When you section off the hair, you want to try and make sure the curls are framed away from your face.
  5. Choose whichever heat and power setting takes your fancy. Again, Katia goes for high heat and high power.
  6. Grab a section of hair about an inch thick and switch on the tool. Hold the section of hair out in front of the Airwrap, let it go, and the air should pull it around the barrel. Without twisting the Airwrap, bring it straight up towards the root. Watch a YouTube video if you’re still confused, but after a few tries this should become second nature to you- remember that the Dyson Airwrap is designed to be super straightforward and simple to use.
  7. Before releasing the hair, use the ‘Cool Shot’ to set the hair in place.
  8. Release the hair. If you have time, curl up the hair into a little ‘O’ shape close to your scalp using your fingers. Secure to your scalp with a bobby pin. Repeat, and then mist your finished sections with hairspray.

Can the Dyson Airwrap Long give you a bouncy blow-dry with lots of volume and body?

Yes- and we love that all the attachments make it super easy to achieve this look with minimal hassle. Just switch out the attachments as you go.

  1. To get super bouncy hair with lots of volume at the root, start with the dryer attachment and dry your hair upside down. Do this until it’s about 70% dry, leaving the root a bit damper than the lengths.
  2. Use the soft or firm styling brush to iron out any kinks and tame down frizz and flyaways.
  3. Grab the round volumising brush and section your hair into thirds. Take a section about an inch thick and begin wrapping around the brush.
  4. You want to style your hair by wrapping the sections around the brush and holding at the root to give you the most volume and movement. Use the ‘Cool Shot’ to set the hair after you’ve completed a section, then let loose.
  5. As above, you can do the pinning and hairspray method if you want the hair to hold more of that shape.

Where is the Dyson Airwrap Complete Long in stock in the UK?

You can usually find the Dyson Airwrap Complete Long in stock at most major retailers, with Dyson itself usually having plenty of availability. John Lewis, Boots and Curry also stock the full range of Dyson hair tools, including the Airwrap Long. There’s little price discrepancy between retailers, with the pricetag usually coming out at a pinch under £500.

Do you need the Dyson Airwrap Complete Long if you already have the original Airwrap?

Honestly, we’re on the fence with this. Our Editor Katia has pretty long, thick hair- about mid-chest length- and the original Airwrap works fine. However, that’s not to say that the Airwrap Long doesn’t make the process easier and faster. But at £499.99 a pop, this is a pretty pricey piece of kit.

Is the Dyson Airwrap Complete Long worth it?

Yes, if you need an all-in-one styling tool with all the bells and whistles. No, if you already have a hairdryer, hair curler and hot air brush that you’re happy with.

You should consider buying the Dyson Airwrap Long if you:

  • Have hair that’s past your shoulders
  • Get annoyed by heavy styling tools
  • Want a faster way to dry your hair
  • Like to switch up your hair style often
  • Aren’t happy with your current selection of hair styling tools and want more diversity
  • Are worried about heat damage
  • Travel often and need an all-in-one styler

You shouldn’t consider buying the Dyson Airwrap Long if you:

  • Don’t have the patience to learn to use some of the trickier styling attachments
  • Are on a budget
  • Have hair that’s generally quite well-behaved and easy to manage
