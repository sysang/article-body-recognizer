New Lindt hot chocolate powder has landed- here’s what we like and don't like

We consider ourselves chocolate connoisseurs here at Daily Mail Best Buys, so we’ve been very excited to put the new Lindt hot chocolate powder to the test. But does it live up to the hype?

Written by:

Katia Damborsky

Published: 09 Nov 2021
Last updated: 02 Dec 2021
Share Article
Link copied to clipboard.

Promising a creamy texture, rich flavour and a taste that captures everything we love about decadent Swiss Lindt chocolate, the new hot chocolate powder has pretty big claims to live up to- especially since we’re fast approaching hot cocoa season.

But does it live up to the hype? With some mixed reviews online, it made sense to get our hands on it and put it to the test ourselves.

Read more: Hotel Chocolat Velvetiser review

Lindt Hot Chocolate Powder review

Available at Tesco, Amazon and a handful of other UK supermarkets, the new hot chocolate beverage retails at a very reasonable £4.

You can use it hot or cold, and Lindt recommends using three heaped teaspoons of the powder added to milk.

Here’s what we like about Lindt’s hot chocolate powder:

  • Mixes well with minimal sludgy residue
  • Creates a thick, creamy drink
  • Great chocolate taste
  • Not too sweet
  • Rich flavour
  • Reasonably priced

Here's what we don't like about Lindt hot chocolate powder:

  • Doesn’t taste exactly like Lindt
  • Mixed claims about whether or not it’s vegan
  • Tastes watery without being used with milk
  • Tastes best when used with a milk frother

Is Lindt hot chocolate powder vegan?

The hot chocolate powder does not list milk as an ingredient, which is great news for vegans and people with non-dairy dietary requirements who are looking for a milk chocolate kick. However, the description states the item may contain milk- therefore Lindt’s hot chocolate powder is not vegan certified.
