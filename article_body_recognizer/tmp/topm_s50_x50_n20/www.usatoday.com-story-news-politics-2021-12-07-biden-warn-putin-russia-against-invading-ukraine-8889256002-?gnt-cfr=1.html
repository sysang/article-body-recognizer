Video diplomacy: Biden to warn Putin against Ukraine invasion in high-stakes call

Matthew Brown Michael Collins
USA TODAY
  • A Tuesday call between Biden and Putin is an attempt to defuse the conflict over Ukraine.
  • US intelligence reports a buildup of Russian troops on Ukraine's border.
  • The two leaders also plan to discuss other issues, including cybersecurity and Iran.

WASHINGTON – President Joe Biden will emphasize his desire for a diplomatic solution to the U.S. conflict with Russia over Ukraine but will warn Russian President Vladimir Putin in a call on Tuesday that Russia’s continued aggression against its neighbor will come with costs.

Biden’s high-stakes video call with Putin comes as U.S. intelligence reports a buildup of Russian troops on Ukraine’s border, raising alarms that an invasion may be imminent.
