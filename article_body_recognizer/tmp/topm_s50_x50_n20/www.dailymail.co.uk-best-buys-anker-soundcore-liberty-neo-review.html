Anker Soundcore Liberty Neo review: What to know before you buy these popular earbuds

Are you a budget-conscious shopper with an unyielding taste for the high-life? Or, did your dog just eat your beloved pair of fancy earbuds? Either way, you’re likely on the look-out for a worthwhile set of buds. Perhaps the Anker Soundcore Liberty Neo’s are just the thing.

Written by:

Caitlin Willow Greyling

Published: 26 Feb 2021
Last updated: 07 Sep 2021
Share Article
Link copied to clipboard.

The rule of thumb “You get what you pay for” isn’t steadfast, especially when it comes to electronic purchases. With this in mind, it’s always best to keep an open mind about budget buys -- the Anker Soundcore Liberty Neo earbuds being one of them.

It seems that these affordable true wireless earbuds have amassed quite a following. And in this Anker Soundcore Liberty Neo Review, we aim to find out exactly what has made this set so popular.

Buy now for £42.99 on Amazon

What we like about Anker Soundcore Liberty Neo:

Can one really be too picky when buying a pair of budget earbuds? After all, you still deserve the best value for your money, especially when it comes to devices you’ll use day in and day out. In the case of these earbuds, it may be low-cost, but it is still at the top-end of the headphone spectrum. As such, we had high expectations for this design.

What we like about the Anker Soundcore Liberty Neo earbuds is that they compare very well against the competition. In fact, it’s undoubtedly one of the top ten earbud sets within this price range.Easy pairing, quick charging, and a near-perfect fit are just some of the perks you can expect from a pair.

A lot of work has gone into optimising the upgraded Liberty Neo's, and it shows. The ergonomic fit is a result of extensive testing on multiple users. And, the balanced, clear sound is a product of the finely tuned graphene drivers.

Apart from all this, we appreciate the earbuds amped-up bass and the fact the set is IPX7 waterproof. In fact, they’re resistant to sweat, rain, and water, making it an excellent choice for active and outdoorsy folks, too!

What we don’t like about Anker Soundcore Liberty Neo:

If you happen to be a music connoisseur with a taste for premium sound, these earbuds might not cut it. Though the set features boosted bass and clearer sound, it’s still not on par with more premium earbuds. During our testing, we noticed that the top volume was a teeny bit lacking. Of course, it would be unreasonable to expect them to outperform higher-priced options, considering its price tag.

As mentioned in the Liberty Neo's description, it is a noise-cancelling set of earbuds. However, if you’re expecting this set to perform miracles in this department (spoiler alert) it won’t. The noise-cancellation seems to work better with audio than calls. The microphone picks up plenty of ambient sounds and lets whoever you’re chatting to know about it.

We also found it inconvenient that you can’t adjust the volume directly with the device. Instead, you’ll have to use your paired smartphone to control the sounds.

Even with all of these shortcomings, the Anker Soundcore Liberty Neo works well. For the price, you will be quite satisfied with them, and be definitely better off than if you were to settle for another £40 option.

Main characteristics of Anker Soundcore Liberty Neo

Design

The look of the Anker Soundcore Liberty Neo earbuds is nothing special as the buds are understated and fly under the radar. This is to be expected, though, as they are a less expensive option. Overall, the earbuds look is simplistic and utilitarian, if a bit bulky. These buds also come in two colours - black and white. The black earbuds are available with either a sparkly black or a metallic blue or red charging case. However, the pearl white charging case only accompanies the white earbuds.

Users will like that the case is a small pill-box shape featuring smooth corners, making it easy to slip into pockets, rest in the palm of your hand or stow in your handbag easily. Equally attractive is the smooth way that the earbuds slip into their case. And unlike the buds, the case isn’t bulky.

One of the best aspects of the set’s design is the fact the earbuds are rain-, sweat-, and waterproof. Yes, the IPX7-graded buds are officially waterproof, not water-resistant! You can submerge them up to one meter for half an hour without issues.

Sound quality

Though not as good as some premium earbuds, it's still quite a spectacular set for this price. The boosted bass and clarity it offers are all that a casual music listener really needs.

If you happen to be upgrading from a very cheap pair of earbuds, then the Neo’s are heaven! However, if you’re specifically looking for premium sound or highly-effective noise-cancelling earbuds that cost more than £40, it is worth looking elsewhere (a good starting point is our in-ear headphone buying guide). Though these do block out some sound, they’re not on par with many other premium noise-cancelling sets. We can’t stress this enough: This is to be expected as they are a lower-priced option.

A few annoying aspects of this set are the volume control and single-use options. If you want to adjust the volume, you have to do so via your paired device, not the earbuds. Additionally, you can only use the right earbud by itself.

Making calls is not one of the highlights of using this set, either. In normal, quiet conditions with minimal sharp noises, calls will sound fine. However, the mic picks up way too much sound, specifically wind and voices in noisy environments. Overall, we found that calling with the headphones a relatively average user experience.

Connectivity

In terms of pairing, these earbuds excel. Pop them out of their case, and they’ll automatically connect to the last paired device. Setting things up is equally as easy. With Bluetooth 5.0 and a custom antenna, the set offers some of the best connectivity around for similarly-priced earbuds. While we had no issues with connection or pairing the earbuds to a device during our testing, some users have experienced connection issues. This often seemed to boil down to the type of device paired or software issues.

Good news is that if you have any problems, you’re free to contact Anker’s excellent support team who can troubleshoot on the fly. In fact, Anker offers free lifetime support with your Liberty Neo purchase, as well as a money-back guarantee and warranty. So, you’re covered in the event your pair malfunctions. Just make sure to buy direct from Anker or another reputable seller offering the same warranty and returns policy.

Comfort

Thanks to testing the buds on well over a thousand ears, Anker has managed to ensure that the Neo’s offer the perfect, ergonomic fit.

Apart from this, the set also comes with four sets of ear tips and ear wings for you to fine-tune your fit. As such, we found these buds very enjoyable to wear while relaxing, walking, running, and exercising, too.

One small gripe was the size of the buds - the set is a little bulky for our liking. Though great for most activities, its size makes them not an ideal choice to wear when lying down or sleeping.

We also wouldn’t advise using them during extremely vigorous exercise or swimming, either. Depending on how well they fit you, they may or may not fall out. So, play it by ear and see how comfortable you feel wearing them during these activities before you lose one.

Overall, we felt it was easy to use them, pair them, put them in, and wear them, with overall comfort being one of the sets' strong-suits!

Battery life

In terms of battery life, we have to give a lack-lustre Anker Soundcore Liberty Neo review. The charge is sufficient, but you can get buds with a better charge for this price - or even less - on the market.

The Liberty Neo’s are listed as having a charge of 5 hours, which we found to vary between 3 to 4.5 hours playtime depending on use. The charging case comes packed with up to 20 hours worth of charges, too - four individual charges, to be exact. So, as long as you don’t forget to bring your case with wherever you go, you’ll be fine. Altogether, the case and buds hold up to 25 hours worth of charge. So, you could likely go a day or even a few days without needing to charge the case.

We liked the fact that the charging case has three indicator lights on the front to show you how many charges you have left. This is quite a useful feature, making it that much easier to avoid an accidental and disastrous low battery situation.

Price

Undoubtedly one of the best features of this set of earbuds is its price. The set comes with a pair of buds, charging case, and charging cable, as well as four ear tips and four ear wings. You’ll feel as if you got your money’s worth from the Neo’s, plus a little extra!

Overall, the buds’ sound, noise-cancellation, playback time, look, and performance is above average in this price range. Of course, if you can spare an extra few quid, I think you can find better options within the Soundcore range.

Being that these earbuds aren’t brand-new (this upgraded version got released mid-2019), there are now a number of equally-good or better options currently on the market, most of which happen to come in at a lower price range. For the best alternatives, keep scrolling!

How does the Anker Soundcore Liberty Neo stack up?

If you’re looking for affordable replacement buds or an upgrade from your cheap earphones, then these are an excellent option. They offer the quality, sound, and features you need or may not yet have experienced. You’ll enjoy using them to casually listen to music, podcasts, audiobooks and other audio content.

Being IPX7-graded, it is also perfect for sports, exercise, and outdoor activities. You can safely wear them in averagely damp, sweaty, rainy, misty, or otherwise wet conditions. However, being unable to adjust the volume directly on the earbuds might bug you during these activities.

If you’re looking for excellent noise-cancelling buds or ones for work calls, we wouldn’t recommend them, either. The mic isn’t top-notch at noise-cancellation or capturing your voice with crystal clarity, both of which are vital when talking with current or potential clients.

Our overall Anker Soundcore Liberty Neo review rates the buds highly for casual listeners, average for active folks, and low for business people and commuters.

Are these headphones worth the money?

So, are the Neo’s worth the £40+ price tag? After all, this is likely one of the main reasons you’re reading this Anker Soundcore Liberty Neo review, isn’t it? For casual users, the simple answer is yes. For true audiophiles or those looking for a business-friendly pair of earbuds, it might be worth considering other worthwhile contenders (we will talk about these later on). Many of these alternatives are newer and feature improved performance, such as extended playtime, a slimmer case, or in-built volume control.

All in all, the Liberty Neo earbuds do sound solid within this price range. And in the case of customer support, it excels with its free, unlimited support as well as a solid warranty.

Is there any product that solves the problem for less money?

Being a bit older of a device, there are already cheaper options available that perform better in most, if not, some areas in comparison to the Anker Soundcore Liberty Neo.

For example, the much more affordable ENACFIRE E60 offer 8 hours of playtime. Plus, the E90 and E18 Plus cases include up to a further 48 hours of charge! The 50% cheaper HolyHigh EA7’s come in a slim case, and you can adjust your volume, answer and hang up calls, and more directly on the buds. And, the Boltune BT-BH021t just under £40.

Other worthy competitors include the IP8 Donerton D-18’s, the popular Mpow M30, as well as the 120-hour playtime IPX8 VANKYO X200 earbuds. All of these are worthy alternatives worth considering, yet cost a quarter or even half the price of the Liberty Neos.
