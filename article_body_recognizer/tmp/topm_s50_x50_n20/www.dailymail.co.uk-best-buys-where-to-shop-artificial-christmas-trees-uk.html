Where to shop for artificial Christmas trees

The days are numbered before Saint Nick comes calling, so make sure your tree is trimmed in time to enjoy its beauty over a cup of hot cocoa. But don’t let your view be spoiled by a tree that’s on its last limb!

Written by:

Kat Miller

Published: 01 Dec 2021
Last updated: 03 Dec 2021
Share Article
Link copied to clipboard.

Once upon a time, fake Christmas trees were either totally lacklustre or over-the-top metallic numbers. Thankfully, times have changed and artificial trees have come a long way. Some of the most stunning trees you’ll see this year will be artificial and if you’re reading this, you’re likely curious about finding the perfect one this year.

Whether you’re looking to forego a real tree for a tree with a longer lifespan or you’re a long-time member of Team Fake Tree and simply need a new one, this is the ultimate 2021 buying guide for artificial Christmas trees in the UK.

Do you usually buy real or artificial Christmas trees?

It’s an age-old and divisive question but in the UK, artificial Christmas trees win out. Each year, roughly 74% of UK households opt for fake trees while 26% stick to the tradition of a real Christmas tree. So if you’re thinking of becoming a fake tree household you aren’t alone. But what should you consider when buying? The Daily Mail Best Buys team recommends honing in on the price, size and lighting options of a tree in addition to its look.

We’ll get to these specs later, but first, let’s look at the best places to shop for artificial Christmas trees in the UK and why each shop makes our list.

John Lewis Partners

Unsurprisingly, John Lewis is a super reliable place to shop for your next fake tree. The UK department store nails Christmas trees as well as it nails its Christmas adverts. At John Lewis, you’ll find a lovely selection of trees that vary in height and width so you can find one that perfectly suits your space. To help you achieve the perfect holiday look, John Lewis offers a variety of tree types from Brunswick Spruce to fully flocked.

Of course, with the trusted name of John Lewis, most of its trees are on the pricier side. But, these trees will likely withstand the years to come. John Lewis plans to have a massive restock of Christmas trees in mid-November.

Order today and enjoy free standard delivery on all orders over £50.

Shop John Lewis Christmas trees

Very

With a great selection of trees in stock at the time of writing, Very is another retailer to turn to this year when buying a new artificial tree. Offering a variety of trees that ooze tradition (in both full and pencil-thin options), Very also recognises that not everyone’s decor choices are the same. We like the retailer's selection of outdoor trees as well as ones with a more modern tone, like this six-foot Multi Function Fibre Optic Tree.

Very offer free Click Collect on orders over £30. Standard delivery is available for £3.99 on all orders with the option to upgrade to quicker delivery times for an added cost.

Find your tree at Very

Dunelm

Our list would be incomplete without giving Dunelm the credit it deserves. Many of us across the UK count on Dunelm to provide decent quality goods at an affordable price and when it comes to a fake Christmas tree, it’s no different. Nearly half of the retailer’s trees are under the £150 price point and incredibly varied. From sweet three-foot trees (we love this Abies Christmas Tree in Burlap Base) to trees that tower to nine feet or more, you’ll likely find what you need at Dunelm.

Dunelm offers free Click and Collect, sometimes with the option to pick up in store in just over an hour, as well as free standard delivery on orders over £49.

Shop Dunelm trees today

Amazon

A simple search for ‘Christmas tree’ on Amazon yields a staggering 10,000+ results, so it’s easy to understand why so many turn to the online retailer to shop fake trees, baubles and more. Of course, with such a large selection it can be difficult to separate the wheat from the chaff. If Amazon is your preferred retailer, the Best Buys team recommends narrowing down your search, perhaps by ratings. Look for trees that have a four-star rating or higher. We also recommend checking out customer reviews; if a deal is too good to be true, it likely is! If you do the appropriate leg-work, you’ll be rewarded with beautiful trees like this Pretex 6ft Christmas Tree (4.3 stars, 1,000+ ratings) or this WeRChristmas Pre-Lit Pencil Christmas Tree (4.5 stars, 500 ratings)

Amazon Prime members benefit from free next-day delivery when shopping with a majority of its sellers.

Find your next tree at Amazon

The Range

Akin to a Christmas tree forest, The Range has an excellent selection of fake trees. What sets this retailer apart from others is that it’s website makes it super easy to find the exact tree you want. Shop by size, aesthetic, lighting options or a number of other categories all on The Range website with ease. If you’re torn between real or fake, The Range also offers a range of real Christmas trees that are brought online as Christmas draws nearer.

The Range offers Click Collect services for just £3.95 on most orders as well as delivery options starting at £3.95 as well.

Shop The Range's tree selection

Argos

From artificial parasol Christmas trees designed specifically to deter curious cats and inquisitive toddlers to trees made to fit against the wall, Argos has a wide array of trees that range from extra traditional to quirky and modern. Moreover, you can find a tree to fit most any budget at the UK retailer.

Argos has streamlined the click and collect process so you can add to basket and pick up your order within hours after shopping or pick it up from the nearest collection point for free. You can also choose home delivery options starting at £6.95.

Buy your next tree at Argos

When should I shop for an artificial Christmas tree?

If you’re after a new tree for Christmas 2021, we recommend shopping sooner rather than later. Ongoing supply chain issues that have been a concern over Black Friday are likely to continue through the holidays. To make sure you don’t get put on the naughty list with your family for not having a tree in time, go ahead and add one to your cart now. You can thank us later!

For those who think that they can eke out one more year with their trusty tree, check out after-Christmas sales to save big on a new artificial Christmas tree. These sales will begin right around Christmas and continue for a week or two after the holiday. By buying at the end of the year, you’ll have extra savings for Christmas presents next year.

What is a good price for a fake Christmas tree?

Many factors affect a Christmas tree’s price tag. The shape, size, material, lighting options and features of a tree all add up, meaning you can truly spend anywhere from around £30 on a tree up to as much as £500 (like this stunner from John Lewis) or more. That being said, the Best Buys team estimates that a good price for a fake Christmas tree falls somewhere between £60 and £150.

On average, a fake tree will set you back more than its natural counterpart. But, the benefit of an artificial tree is that it will be with you for years to come. Therefore you may spend more outright, but if you reuse your artificial Christmas tree for at least five years, you’ll really start to see the return on the investment when it comes to your bank account.

What size artificial Christmas tree should I buy?

There are two ways to think about the size of a Christmas tree; its height and its circumference. When it comes to the height of a tree, a good rule of thumb is to leave at least six inches between the top of the tree and your ceiling. Of course, if you trim your tree with an ornate topper, you may want to consider how much height will be added by that. As for the circumference of your tree, the specifications given are typically for the bottom, or widest point of your tree.

Small spaces may call for a slimmer tree while large rooms can accommodate extra-full tenenbaums. If you’re having trouble visualising just how large the base will be, break out the measuring tape and mark out a rough circle on the floor. This way, you’ll be able to judge if you’ll have some adequate space left around the tree.

What are pre-lit Christmas trees?

A pre-lit tree comes with strands of lights already intertwined into its branches. This is a simple way to cut out what is arguably the most frustrating part of Christmas decorating. Pre-lit artificial trees are typically pricier but they give you flexibility. Today, they come in either white or colour options, sometimes with the option of both so you can switch it up from year to year. Likewise, your tree may have different settings allowing you more control over your Christmas aesthetic. More and more pre-lit trees come with LED lights (saving on the electric bill!) and are clever enough so that if one light bulb dies, the whole tree isn’t out of commission.

What is a flocked Christmas tree?

No birds here! Flocked is simply a word used to describe artificial (or real) Christmas trees that have the appearance of being dusted with snow. In recent years, flocked trees have grown in popularity for the Winter Wonderland vibe they create. A light coating of "snow" also tones down the bold greens introduced to a room by your tree, which some with more subdued home decor might enjoy.
