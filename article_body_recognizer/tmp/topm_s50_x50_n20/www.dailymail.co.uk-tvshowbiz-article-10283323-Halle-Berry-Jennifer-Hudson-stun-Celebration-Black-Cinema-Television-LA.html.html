Halle Berry, Jennifer Hudson and Taraji P. Henson turn heads in fashion-forward dresses at the annual Celebration of Black Cinema Television in LA

  • The leading ladies brought the glamour to the fourth annual Celebration of Black Cinema Television
  • The star-studded event took place at the the Fairmont Century Plaza Hotel on Monday evening in Los Angeles
  •  Berry wore a dramatic red low-cut dress with a striking train as she attended with her beau Van Hunt
  • She was accepting the the Career Achievement Award, while Hudson was honored for her role in Respect 
  • Anthony Anderson took home the Producer Award for his work on Black-ish, Grown-ish and Mixed-ish
  • Emmy Award-winning actress and producer Niecy Nash hosted the show 

By Deirdre Simonds For Dailymail.com

    Halle Berry, Jennifer Hudson and Taraji P. Henson brought the glamour in fashion-forward looks while attending the fourth annual Celebration of Black Cinema Television in Los Angeles, California on Monday evening.

    While arriving to the Fairmont Century Plaza Hotel, the 55-year-old Bruised star wore a dramatic red low-cut dress with a striking train, which showed off her cleavage and enviable curves. 

    She paired the eye-catching gown with a pair of silver heels and opted to style her light brown locks in loose waves as she posed for photographs and mingled with her musician boyfriend Van Hunt, who she began dating last fall. 

    Glamorous ladies: Halle Berry, Jennifer Hudson, Tessa Thompson and Taraji P. Henson brought the glamour in fashion-forward looks while attending the fourth annual Celebration of Black Cinema Television in Los Angeles, California on Monday

    As the couple gazed lovingly at each other, they could be seen holding hands and looking blissful just a month after she gushed she 'never had a man that has lifted' her up like him at the Elle Women in Hollywood Awards.

    Celebration of Black Cinema Television 2021 HONOREES 

    Halle Berry with The Career Achievement Award 

    Ava DuVernay with the inaugural Melvin Van Peebles Trailblazer Award

    Anthony Anderson with the Producer Award for Television

    Jennifer Hudson with the Actress Award for Film

    Barry Jenkins with the Director Award for Television

    Will Smith with the Actor Award for Film for his work in "King Richard"

    Antoine Fuqua with the Director Award for Film

    The cast of "The Harder They Fall," including Jonathan Majors, Idris Elba, Zazie Beetz, Regina King, Delroy Lindo, LaKeith Stanfield, Deon Cole, R.J. Cyler, Edi Gathegi and Danielle Deadwyler, with the Ensemble Award

    Actress Danielle Brooks with the Actress Award for Television for her work in the Lifetime movie "Robin Roberts Presents: Mahalia"

    French actor Omar Sy with the Actor Award for Television for his work in "Lupin"

    Robin Thede with the Showrunner Award for HBO's "A Black Lady Sketch Show"

    Toheeb Jimoh with the Breakthrough Award for "Ted Lasso"

    Kenan Thompson with a Special Honoree Award for "Saturday Night Live" and "Kenan"

    Patina Miller with a Special Honoree Award for the Starz series "Power Book III: Raising Kanan"

    Natasha Rothwell with a Special Honoree Award for HBO's "The White Lotus."

    Speaking of Berry being honored with the Career Achievement Award, Shawn Edwards, executive producer of the Celebration of Black Cinema Television and a CCA board member 'praised her iconic performances' and being a trailblazer 'for Black performers who have come after her' in a statement to Variety earlier this year. 

    'She has become the personification of excellence as she transitions from being in front of the camera to sitting in the director’s chair,' he explained.
