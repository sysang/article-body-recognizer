        Data Entry FAQs

        • What is data entry?

          Data entry is the process of entering data or information into a computer, typically using a keyboard or voice recognition. It involves inputting data into a computer from forms or other sources. Today, data entry usually entails inputting data into an online database.

        • What are the top data entry skills?

          Data entry may seem like a mundane and easy task, but it requires some specific skills. Some key data entry skills include a high typing speed, good communication skills, basic knowledge of software like word processors, spreadsheets and database software, great attention to detail, and some basic research and data-collection skills.

        • What types of data entry services can be outsourced?

          There are several types of data entry services, for instance: data mining and entry, data cleansing and verification, data collection, typing or filling up documents.

        • What is a data entry clerk?

          A data entry clerk is an employee or a contract worker employed to enter or update data into a computer system, typically a database. The data is often copied from paper documents like forms or bills, using a keyboard. Looking to outsource your data entry? Hire a data entry clerk to do the job.

        • What is 10-key data entry?

          10-key data entry uses the numeric keypad on the right side of a computer keyboard, a calculator, or a 10-key pad to enter numeric data into a computer system or database. The name comes from the 10 numeric keys (0 to 9). 10-key data entry makes it easier and less tedious to enter numeric data. It is a widely-used data-entry technique, used to enter financial, coding, or number-based information.

        • Is data entry jobs legit?

          There are many online companies that provide data entry jobs, and a majority of them are legit. Legitimate data entry companies put you through a proper job interview and training, and will not ask for money to train or certify you. If a company asks you for irrelevant personal information like your Social Security number or provides vague payment details, it’s best to avoid them.
