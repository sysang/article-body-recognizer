Peppermint sugar scrub is a great DIY gift that you can make for your friends and family around the holidays. Since it only takes a little bit of time and a few ingredients, you can throw together a batch in a single afternoon for a quick, thoughtful gift. Try adding twine or a ribbon to your jar of peppermint scrub to dress it up and make it look adorable.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups (256 g) of granulated sugar
  • 1⁄2 c (120 mL) of melted coconut oil
  • 15 to 20 drops of peppermint essential oil
  • 2 drops of red food coloring

Yields about 2 cups (256 g) of peppermint sugar scrub.

    • The sugar is the main ingredient in your scrub, which is why you need so much.
  2. 2
    Mix in 1⁄2 c (120 mL) of melted coconut oil. Scoop 1⁄2 c (120 mL) of coconut oil into a microwave-safe bowl, then microwave it on high for about 15 seconds. Pour the oil into the sugar, then mix it in with a wooden spoon.[2] X Research source
    • You can also use fractionated coconut oil since it’s already melted.
    • If you need to, you can add more coconut oil to get the scrub to the right consistency.
    • Coconut oil is the best carrier oil to use for a sugar scrub since it’s moisturizing.
    • If you don’t like coconut oil, try using jojoba oil or almond oil instead.

  3. 3
    Drop 15 to 20 drops of peppermint essential oil into the mixture. Open up the lid of your essential oil and carefully use the dropper to drop it in. Add a maximum of 20 drops for an extra peppermint smell, or stick with 10 for a more mild scrub.[3] X Research source
    • Use caution around essential oils, and keep them away from children and pets.[4] X Research source
    • Never put essential oils on your skin without diluting them first.[5] X Research source
  4. 4
    Divide the scrub into 2 equal parts. Grab a second bowl and scoop out about half of your sugar scrub. You don’t have to be precise with your measurements, but get the bowls to roughly the same amount.[6] X Research source
    • There should be about 1 cup (128 g) of scrub in each bowl.
  5. 5
    Mix in 2 drops of red food coloring into 1 part of the sugar mix. Grab a bottle of red food coloring and carefully drop about 2 drops into the mixture. Use a metal spoon to stir the coloring into the scrub to make it a bright red.[7] X Research source
    • Try not to use too much food coloring to avoid staining your skin when you use the scrub.

Part 2 of 2:
Storing the Peppermint Scrub

  1. 1
    Add alternating layers of white and red scrub to a glass jar. Grab a tall glass jar and start adding spoonfuls of your scrub, alternating between white and red. Keep going until you fill the entire jar to make a cute candy cane design.[8] X Research source
    • The red may bleed a little bit into the white layers, but they’ll just turn slightly pink for an even cuter candy cane.
    • Depending on how large your jar is, your mixture could fill 1 to 3 jars.
    • A regular 16-ounce mason jar can hold all of the sugar scrub, and it looks super cute too.
  2. 2
    Press down on the sugar scrub with a spoon to compact it. Once you’ve filled your jar, press down on the top with the back of a metal spoon. This will push down the top layer of the scrub to make it easy for the lid to go on.[9] X Research source
    • If you need to, you can scrape off a little bit of the top layer to make it flat.
  3. 3
    Seal the jar with a lid to keep it airtight. To keep your scrub from drying out, screw the lid onto your jar tightly. If you use your scrub, make sure to seal it back up before you store it.[10] X Research source
    • Try to use your sugar scrub within 6 months.
    • You can use a sugar scrub to exfoliate your legs, hands, and feet and remove dry, flaky skin.

Article SummaryX

To make an easy DIY peppermint sugar scrub, first add 2 cups of granulated sugar to a bowl. Mix in 1/2 cup of melted coconut oil and 15-20 drops of peppermint essential oil. Then, divide the mixture into two separate bowls. Dye one half of the mixture with 2 drops of red food coloring and leave the other half white. Finally, add the scrub to a clean jar in alternating red and white layers. Your peppermint sugar scrub will stay good for up to 6 months. For tips on the best way to use sugar scrubs, check out the rest of the article!
