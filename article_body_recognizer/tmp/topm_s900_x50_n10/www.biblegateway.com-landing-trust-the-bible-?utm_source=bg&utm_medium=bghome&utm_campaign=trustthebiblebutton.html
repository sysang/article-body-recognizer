Looking for reasons to put your confidence in Scripture?

At some point in our lives it’s common to wonder whether the Bible is truly trustworthy. Since the Bible describes God’s character, commands, and plans for humanity, the stakes are high if the Bible is not what it claims to be.

Scripture itself declares it's God’s Word (1 Corinthians 2:12-16; cf. Romans 8:16; John 10:27). But we can also benefit from other evidence that supports our belief in the Bible’s authority. As we become aware of this evidence, our confidence in Scripture grows, and we also grow in our ability to share the gospel with people who typically ask why they should believe the Bible. As Peter exhorts, we should aim to “always be prepared to give an answer to everyone who asks you to give the reason for the hope that you have” (1 Peter 3:15).
