Welcome back to What I Rent, our weekly series that goes inside people’s rented homes and has a proper nose around.

Why? Because we want to explore the reality of renting, and see exactly what your money can get you.

Last Tuesday we were in Brighton with Laura, who pays £875 a month for a one-bedroom flat.

This time around, we’re up in Manchester with Jo, 29.

Jo works in digital marketing alongside running her own blog about vintage fashion, and an athleisure brand, Espoir.

She has dual nationality, as her mum is French and her dad was Welsh, and moved over to Manchester at the end of April – later than planned, due to lockdowns.

Here’s what she rents.

Hey, Jo! How much do you pay to live here?

I pay £750 a month – this doesn’t include council tax, water and electricity.

My bills are generally quite low as I live alone, so I don’t spend too much. I spend on average £200 a month on all my bills, which includes my phone, internet etc.

And what do you get for what you pay?

The flat is a one bed apartment and one bathroom and I live alone.

Do you feel like you have a good deal?

I think I found a great deal in Manchester as I’m lucky to live in an area that has so much culture and is a safe place to live.

I also have a modern apartment that has some Scandinavian-like vibes and was mostly furnished so I didn’t have to buy new furniture for my move. However, I’ve made it more my home by investing in prints, art and homeware accessories ( he black pixar style lamp, fan, record player etc) which share what I love when it comes to music and fashion.

I have a gym at the bottom of the block, which is free for all tenants to use 24/7 a day. I also live close to a pure gym and a cross fit gym so I can switch up my routine.

What do you think of the area?

I moved to Manchester for work but also for a new scene – I fell in love with the place after landing my dream job.

I love Manchester as it has so much personality and it’s in the hub spot for brunch, vintage shopping, gyms and some great bars and gig spots, so super practical for my lifestyle.

How have you made the flat feel like home?

Bringing pictures, prints and unusual items like the cow teddy, which my sister and her boyfriend made me as a joke present, help remind me of family.

I also have a cute print in my kitchen of Strasbourg which reminds me of my family back in France, and have some favourite prints in the lounge, such as one of All The Small Things (which is always a discussion amongst visitors as it tests whether or not people know the lyrics). It also fits the room well as you want to ‘turn the lights off’ ready for bed.. or need to be ‘carried home’ if you’ve drank too much.

I’ve also added my own art to the wall and some pieces from local artists to help motivate me and put some positivity in the place.

I’ve got some polaroids by my TV which are of my family, best friends and work colleagues, too.

Have you found it tricky to decorate while renting?

When you’re renting you’ve got to be respectful and mindful of what you are allowed to do to the property and I’ve made sure that any art work I put up is put with command strips, as it avoids putting holes in the wall.

I’ve also given the space some character by adding other home accessories that have helped add colour and culture to my home.

Do you feel like you have enough space?

I’ve got enough space as I live alone, so it’s fine for one person.

It’s also spacious enough to host when I’ve had friends over. I can easily move the furniture to pop a blow-up bed down for when friends stay over when they’d like to come visit my place in Manchester.

Are there any issues with the flat?

No problems.

Do you have plans to move again any time soon?

I’m actually in the process of looking for a flat to buy in the new year, so I’m renting until I find my next home.

I really love this flat and it’s been a fantastic experience to do this on my own as it’s not often that women get to share their experiences of what it’s like living alone in their late 20’s. This was a massive achievement for me and for anyone renting alone, it shows how hard you’ve worked and how driven you are to focus on your career as you’re fully invested in yourself.

Also living alone has shown me how I cope with situations on my own and how it has allowed me to meet some amazing people in Manchester.

Great stuff. Shall we take a look around?
