Arts and Culture

As part of our goal to help people feel a sense of belonging in their communities, TD supports Arts and Culture events, initiatives and organizations across North America that amplify diverse voices.

In that spirit, we are proud to support Asian Heritage Month, Indigenous National History Month, and Black History Month programming that features individuals and initiatives that contribute to the fabric of Canada where we live, work, and play. We celebrate the achievements of those that shape our story as Canadians and inspire the next generation of future leaders. You can find events, programs, and initiatives supported by TD through the TD Ready Commitment on our Community Events Calendar.

How we support Arts and Culture

  • TD supports a range of Pan-Asian community organizations, like Small World Music

    By working together with Pan-Asian community organizations, initiatives like Small World Music's 2021 Asian Music Series can help showcase the cultural diversity of underrepresented and newcomer artists and celebrate their creativity.

  • How one theatre production is exploring the question 'What is the future of Blackness?'

    Obsidian Theatre Company commissioned 21 Black playwrights from across Canada to produce plays that explore their unique visions of the future of Black experiences.

  • TD helps recent grads and emerging curators gain hands-on experience

    Initiatives like the TD Curator of Education and Outreach Fellowship Program help up-and-coming curators get their break in the world of museums.

  • TD sponsors blockbuster Mandela exhibition in Winnipeg

    TD supports the Canadian Museum for Human Rights in Winnipeg by sponsoring the Mandela: Struggle for Freedom exhibition.
