Colorado wildfire injures at least seven people and scorches up to 1,000 homes

A massive wildfire in Colorado has injured at least seven people and destroyed up to 1,000 homes as it continued to burn on Friday.

Among the people injured in the blaze that swept through parts of Boulder County beginning on Thursday was a first responder.

During a press conference on Friday, Colorado Governor Jared Polis said no deaths have yet been reported. A person who was reported missing has been found.

‘If we’re granted this miracle of no loss of life, it truly will be a miraculous start to the new year,’ Polis said.

Boulder County Sheriff Joe Pelle also said he was hoping for a ‘miracle’.

‘It’s unbelievable when you look at the devastation that we don’t have a hundred missing persons, but we don’t,’ Pelle said. ‘I again, am hoping that’s a miracle, because it would be given the circumstances.’ 

Pelle said that deaths and more injuries could arise given the intensity of the blaze, which scorched about 6,000 acres. Firefighters have contained the fire and do not expect it to continue spreading.

‘This is the kind of fire we can’t fight head-on,’ Pelle said. ‘We actually had deputy sheriffs and firefighters in areas that had to pull out because they just got overrun.’ 

But up to 1,000 homes could be wrecked by the worst fire in the state’s history, he said, up from initial reports of at least 600 homes ravaged.

More than 30,000 people residing in the city of Louisville and town of Superior were asked to evacuate on Thursday. In Superior, some families escaped a Chuck E Cheeses that was burning. A video captured frightening moments like children against the glass doors of the restaurant as a woman pushed the door open against 115 mile-per-hour winds.

‘It’s OK, it’s OK,’ a few mothers could be heard saying and trying to calm the children.

The Marshall Fire has already destroyed more property than any other fire in Colorado’s history.
