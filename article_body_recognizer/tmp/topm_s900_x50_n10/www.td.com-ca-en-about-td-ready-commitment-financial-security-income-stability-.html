Income Stability

Achieving Income Stability is one of the first steps on the road to Financial Security. We’re committed to helping individuals prepare for the jobs of tomorrow in communities across North America. That’s why TD continues to invest in training, entrepreneurship, small business and other initiatives.

Nikki and her team help Canadians with disabilities by empowering them with assistive technology

Nikki works at the Neil Squire Society, where she helps Canadians with disabilities find meaningful employment. With support from TD through the TD Ready Commitment, the Neil Squire Society is helping Canadians with disabilities rewrite their stories.

Hear Vahid’s story of how he became his own boss

Vahid wanted to build new career skills so he enrolled in the Career Recharge Program at Seneca HELIX. See how Vahid rewrote his story and achieved his goal of owning his own business.

Meet Claudia, who is helping many new Canadians achieve success

How we support Income Stability

  • $10 million given to grant recipients for Income Stability innovations

    10 organizations received funding to develop their Income Stability projects through the 2018 TD Ready Challenge.

  • How growing income volatility affects Canadians

    TD Economics published a report on how growing income volatility affects the finances and savings of Canadians.

  • TD and Ryerson University help adult gain higher education

    The TD Bank Road to Success helps adult learners on a pathway from precarious work to promising career opportunities.

Our commitment in action

Through TD Ready Challenge, TD awarded $10 million in grants for innovative solutions that align with the TD Ready Commitment.

Transcript

Text: 4 in 10 Canadians face income instability.

We believe with a little help they can rewrite their story.

Claudia: My name is Claudia Hepburn and I am the CEO of Windmill Microlending.

Windmill Microlending empowers skilled immigrants to achieve economic prosperity through microloans and support. When immigrants arrive in Canada, many of them bring a small amount of savings that they think is going to enable them to get on their feet. But in reality, because they don't have a credit history in Canada, they can't borrow at reasonable rates. We provide low interest loans to take exams that they need to restart their careers in Canada. And as a result, they triple their income and start on a career pathway that is onwards and upwards from there.

We had one client who was an engineer from the Middle East. And so when he got his Windmill loan and he succeeded with his exams and got his first job as an engineer. He brought his first pay cheque home to his family. And when he showed his wife and his children pay cheque, they all cried!

One of the things I love about my job is coming to work each day to help people get out of that rut and give them a small leg up by giving them some advice and some affordable credit to build their credit history. We unlock that potential and allow them to put their skills to work in engineering, in healthcare and supply chain management. So many areas that in particular during the pandemic were experiencing a quiet labour market shortages in.

So when we think about our successful clients, it's wonderful to see their transitions from the despair that they felt when they applied for the loan to the sense of satisfaction and success that they feel where they are contributing to the mosaic of Canadian diversity.

TD understands so well the problem of income insecurity, particularly for new Canadians. And they have been a fantastic supporter for Windmill Microlending, helping us grow from 400 new loans a year to a 1,000 new loans a year. And we really hope and envision them supporting us in our next stage of growth from a 1,000 new loans a year to 4,000 new loans a year.

Text: Through the TD Ready Commitment, TD is proud to support organizations like Windmill Microlending.
