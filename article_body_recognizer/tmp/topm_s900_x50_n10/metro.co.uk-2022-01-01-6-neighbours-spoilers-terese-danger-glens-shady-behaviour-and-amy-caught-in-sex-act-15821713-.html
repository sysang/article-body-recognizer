A new year for the Neighbours, a whole load of new drama. After the literal cliff hanger of Terese hanging off a building, things aren’t about to get any less stressful for Ramsay Street.

Paul (Stefan Dennis) arrives in time to find his wife dangling above the jaws of death. Can he save her, and their marriage?

Craziness clearly runs in the Robinson family as not only is Glen (Richard Huggett) acting as shifty as heck, but Harlow (Jemma Donovan) is also raising a few eyebrows. What does Glen have to hide? Is Harlow turning into her dad? Terese (Rebekah Elmaloglou) might be missing her husband, but maybe she’s better off staying well away.

But speaking off Terese, she has her own deal going on. While everyone’s busy worrying about Glen (Glen Huggett) and Harlow, they miss that Terese has got herself into another dangerous situation. Who’ll help her this time?

Elsewhere on the street there is some wedding planning going on, but it’s well and truly going awry for both the bride and the groom. Can they get things back on track, or is there about to be a human spanner in the works?

And Amy (Jacinta Stapleton) is caught with her pants down – by her daughter. All kinds of awkward. How will she explain her way out of this one?

Here’s what’s ahead on Neighbours.

Glen’s shady behaviour

Glen is proving to be quite the puzzle to the Neighbours and Harlow, for one, is determined to get to the bottom of it. Harlow is angry and curious as to why Glen is acting like Terese’s personal security detail. She knows there must be dirt on him and goes looking for it – will she find Terese’s ring? He’s being particularly cagey about the safe that’s in his room. Harlow has struck gold, but now she just needs to find a way to access it. Will she uncover Glen’s secret? 

Kyle’s wedding mistake 

Kyle makes a pretty bad decision as far as Sheila and Roxy are concerned. He unwittingly hires Mike as the wedding planner. Whoopsie. But can Mike pull it out of the bag? He’s ready to wow Roxy with his ideas, but can he get her on side? 

 Terese in dangerous situation 

As if hanging off a roof wasn’t enough, Terese’s dicing with danger still isn’t done. She’s fed up of her support network babysitting her, so she cunningly gets them to back off. Sadly for her, Glen is too savvy for that and he interrupts her, discovering her dangerous secret. Terese is mortified and is ready for Glen to pile on the judgement. When it doesn’t come, she’s surprised, instead he shows compassion and they spend the evening playing cards. There’s chemistry here, is the dangerous situation transforming into one where they need to be afraid of Paul? 

Amy caught in sex act by daughter 

Amy’s daughter Zara comes to pay her a visit. Unfortunately for Amy, she pays said visit right when Amy is getting jiggy with Levi and Ned. This is a pretty horrific thing for a child to discover of a parent, and Amy desperately tries to convince Zara the two boys are just friends who like to sleep in her bed. But Amy quickly realises she needs to be honest with her daughter, how will Zara take it when she learns her mum is polyamorous? 

 Harlow turns psycho? 

Harlow’s odd behaviour continues and with the discovery that she’s been seeing her dad it seems like she’s going down a dark path. This concern is compounded for Roxy, Paul and David when Harlow fails to show any sympathy for Terese after she narrowly escaped death. Is she really becoming a psychopath like her dad? Or does an idea David has about what’s going on hold more water? 

Roxy’s wedding woes 

Roxy is in a pickle – she still hasn’t chosen her bridesmaids. She’s left gutted when she doesn’t manage to get Terese or Harlow to agree to be part of her entourage. Will she be a bride without a bridal party? Or will there even be a wedding at all – Roxy’s mum comes to town and she’s not as supportive as Roxy would like her to be. Kyle tries his hardest at impressing her, but it’s not enough. Could Gemma put a spanner in the works? 

 Scenes air from Monday 3rd January on Channel 5.
