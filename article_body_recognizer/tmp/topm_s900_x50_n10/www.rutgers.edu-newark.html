Discover Rutgers University-Newark

City Learning

Just minutes from New York City, the city of Newark is a cornerstone of New Jersey with a strong sense of community that Rutgers–Newark is focused on supporting.

Rutgers–Newark, recognized as an anchor institution in the city, is routinely ranked among the most diverse college campuses in the U.S. and is committed to boundary-crossing scholarship, diverse talent cultivation, and civic engagement.

Chancellor Nancy Cantor

Nancy Cantor is chancellor of Rutgers University–Newark. A distinguished leader in higher education, Cantor is recognized nationally and internationally as an advocate for leveraging diversity in all its dimensions; re-emphasizing the public mission of colleges and universities as engines of discovery, innovation, and social mobility; and achieving the full potential of universities as anchor institutions that collaborate with partners from sectors to help their communities thrive.

  • Read more about Chancellor Cantor and her office

Education

Rutgers University–Newark is a gateway to educational opportunity and an engine of social mobility for New Jersey’s diverse population.

Environment

The Rutgers–Newark campus is in an urban setting that is easily accessible via bus, train, or roadway, serving both residential and commuter students.

Community

An energetic community environment where a cross-pollination of cultures, interests, ideas, and backgrounds enriches the student experience, both inside and outside of the classroom.

Choosing Rutgers–Newark

Delivering exceptional education, a reputation that opens doors, and a lively, diverse campus environment. It’s no wonder why so many choose Rutgers University–Newark. 

Undergraduate Admissions

Students choose Rutgers–Newark for its outstanding reputation in academics, civic engagement, renowned faculty, and student success.

Tuition and Fees

More than three-quarters of students receive financial aid, making Rutgers–Newark an affordable choice before and after graduation.

Virtual Tour

From state-of-the-art classroom facilities to great places to socialize and study on campus, there is plenty to explore at Rutgers–Newark.

Graduate Admissions

The Graduate School–Newark supports scholarly inquiry and intellectual growth across 25 disciplines such as the arts, humanities, sciences, business, and urban and global studies.

Honors College

The Honors College seeks curious and creative undergraduate students who are eager to develop themselves further by exploring an idea or passion and working with faculty to conduct original research and explore career pathways.

Community Engagement

As an anchor institution in Newark, Rutgers is committed to the city’s people and places.

Student Life

Make friends, get involved, get fit, try something new. At Rutgers–Newark you’ll find tons of student activities and events to connect you to the campus and the community. Discover student organizations where you can try something you’ve always wanted to, or start your own organization surrounded by new friends with similar interests.

A transformational college access program, the Honors Living-Learning Community (HLLC) is focused on identifying promising talent and broadening opportunities for those who have been impacted by systematic inequality.
