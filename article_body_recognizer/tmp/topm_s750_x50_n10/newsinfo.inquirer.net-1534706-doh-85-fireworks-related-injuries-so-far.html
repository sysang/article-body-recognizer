DOH: 85 fireworks – related injuries, so far

By: Cathrine Gonzales - Reporter / @cgonzalesINQ
INQUIRER.net / 12:26 PM January 01, 2022

(AFP file photo / TED ALJIBE)

MANILA, Philippines — The Department of Health (DOH) reported on Saturday 85 fireworks and firecrackers-related injuries as of 6 a.m. as the nation ushered in 2022.

Health Secretary Francisco Duque III said in a press briefing at the East Avenue Medical Center that this the number of injuries is 11 percent lower compared to 96 cases reported and recorded in 2020.

ADVERTISEMENT

“That is a bit of improvement with less firework related injuries. Ngunit ang mga bilang ng kaso ay maaaring tumaas sa mga susunod na araw dahil sa mga huling reports at consultations (But the number of cases may increase in the coming days due to late reports and consultations),” said Duque.

Meanwhile, Duque also noted that the 85 injuries were 75 percent lower than the average of 336 injuries in the last five years.

FEATURED STORIES

He added that Metro Manila recorded the most number of fireworks-related injuries at 36 percent.

“Karamihan ng mga nasugatan ay mga passing or bystanders lamang sa 58 percent. Nasa 38 percent ng mga kaso ay mula sa mga ipinagbabawal na paputok at nangunguna dito ang boga, kasunod ang five star at piccolo,” said Duque.

(Most of those injured were only bystanders at 58 percent. Thirty-eight percent of cases involved illegal firecrackers.)

There were no cases of fireworks ingestion, stray bullet injury, or death reported, so far, according to the report from the DOH’s firework-related injury surveillance as of Saturday morning.
