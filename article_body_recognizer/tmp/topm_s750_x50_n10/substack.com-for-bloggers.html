Great bloggers are on Substack.
From food and faith to politics and economics, Substack is the new home for the world's best bloggers.
Alicia Kennedy
Alicia Kennedy has been covering Puerto Rico’s culinary scene and foodways since 2015. As of 2021, more than 10,000 people have signed up to read her Substack.
Matt Yglesias
Matthew Yglesias, co-founder of Vox, started Slow Boring on Substack in 2020. Slow Boring counts more than 9,000 paying subscribers, generating more than $70,000 a month.
Luke O’Neil
Luke O’Neil earns six figures annually by publishing Hell World, a publication about despair and beauty in today’s American, featuring poetic essays and interviews.
Jenny Rosenstrach
Jenny Rosenstrach has written Dinner: A Love Story for more than a decade. She moved her blog and its 1,500-post archive to Substack in January 2021.
Courtney Martin
Courtney had a weekly column at the On Being Project, and transferred her readers to Substack in 2019. She was “amazed at the response" from readers eager to fund her.
Judd Legum
Judd Legum, founder of political blog ThinkProgress, launched Popular Information on Substack in July 2018. Popular Information now has more than 100,000 signups.
