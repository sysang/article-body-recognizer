Privacy Policy

This Privacy Policy was last updated on March 4, 2020.

The Globe and Mail Inc. (“The Globe”) recognizes the importance of protecting the privacy of your personal information. We have prepared this Privacy Policy to provide you with information about our privacy practices. Certain personal information that falls outside the scope of applicable privacy legislation is not subject to this Privacy Policy. For example, personal information collected and used for journalistic purposes is excluded from this policy.

The Globe may update this policy from time to time to reflect changes in our personal information practices. If you continue to use our Services after we make changes you are consenting to the updated policy. The Globe will provide notice of significant changes to the Privacy Policy.

1. Information we may collect

The Globe collects personal information from you in several ways. For example, we collect information that you provide to us, through your use of the Services, as well as information collected from publicly available sources or third parties.

Information that you provide

When you subscribe, register or otherwise interact with our Services, you may be asked to provide certain personal information.

For example, when you register or inquire about our Services, you will be asked for your contact information. For paid Services, we will require payment information such as your credit card information. For services that have age restrictions, such as age-restricted content or events, your date of birth may be required to access those services. You may choose to provide supplementary information about yourself when you register for our Services, such as your birth date, gender, income range, interests, the industry you work in, current position, and other demographic information. We collect personal information that you provide when you participate in discussions using our comment feature, engage with us on social media, post an online ad, enter a contest, participate in a promotion, use our event space or use our tools, such as when you create an investment watchlist through The Globe’s portfolio tool.

Information collected through your use of the Services

As you use our Services, we collect usage information such as the pages you view, product and service preferences, purchase history, and other customer engagement information. Some information is collected automatically when you visit our websites or apps through the use of cookies and similar technologies; to learn about the types of automated information we collect and how we use it, see “Information collected and used when you visit our Sites and Apps”.

If you visit The Globe’s offices, you may be recorded on video for security purposes. If you speak with one of our representatives on the telephone, the call may be recorded for quality assurance and training purposes. If you do not want your call recorded, please contact us via our support website.

Information that we collect from publicly available sources or third parties

The Globe may receive personal information about you from third party sources, as permitted by law, such as marketing and data analytics databases. Third party sources provide us with survey data and publicly available data that may include information such as your name, mailing address, phone number, contact information, other demographics and interests.

When you register for our Services using a third party platform (e.g. using your Google, Facebook or LinkedIn account), the third party platform may provide us with your social media identifier or handle and any additional information you authorize that third party platform to share, such as your email address or contact information. If you would like The Globe to delete all or some of the Personal Information that you provide us to when you access our Services through a third party platform, please email privacy@globeandmail.com or call Customer Service at 1-800-387-5400. Please note, The Globe may retain any Personal Information needed to comply with legal and regulatory requirements or to continue to provide Services to you.

We collect certain information automatically when you visit our websites using cookies and other similar technologies such as: your IP address, usage information such as page requests, browser type, and operating system. When you use our mobile app we collect your device hardware type and operating system, unique device identiﬁer, IP address, language settings, and the date and time the app accesses our servers. More Information about Cookies and Other Similar Technologies:

The Globe uses cookies and other commonly used technologies such as JavaScript, web beacons, device identifiers, and pixel tags to collect automated information. Cookies are small text files containing a unique identifier that are placed in your browser in order to store information about your web browsing and usage of our sites. 5. Interest Based Advertising

The Globe may deliver tailored advertising on and off our Services based on your activity across different sites on the Internet and apps known as interest-based advertising (also referred to as personalized or online behavioural advertising).To learn more on how to opt out of interest based advertising see “Opting-Out of Interest-Based Advertising”

The Globe and third party advertising companies, such as ad networks, may use cookies, pixels and other technologies to collect information about your activity on our sites or apps and on third-party sites and apps (such as web pages you visit and your interaction with our advertising and other communications) and use this information to make predictions about your preferences, develop personalized content and deliver ads that are more relevant to you on our sites and apps, third party sites and across your devices. This information may also be used to evaluate the effectiveness of our online advertising campaigns.

Opting-Out of Interest-Based Advertising

As part of The Globe’s commitment to fair information practice, The Globe is a participant of the Digital Advertising Alliance Canada's (DAAC) AdChoices program and adheres to its principles. The DAAC’s AdChoices initiative allows you to opt-out of the use of information for interest-based advertising. For more information on this program, see https://youradchoices.ca/

Web and Mobile Browser: At any time you can opt-out of receiving interest-based ads on your desktop or mobile browser by clicking on the “AdChoices” logo on the top right corner of our site. An “Interest Based Advertising” screen will appear providing you with a link to the DAAC tool that generates a list of third-party advertising companies participating in the DAAC’s Self-Regulatory Program for Online Interest-Based Advertising and who are currently collecting data from your browser for the purposes of targeted advertising. You can also access the tool here

You may also see the AdChoices icon in or around advertisements on a website of The Globe. These advertisements are tailored for you through interest based advertising. You can opt out of targeted advertising from that advertiser by clicking the "AdChoices" logo within the ad.

Mobile App: You can opt out of any interest-based advertising on your mobile app through your mobile device settings, which vary by device. More information on how to do this can be found here: https://www.networkadvertising.org/mobile-choices. DAAC also provides a tool called AppChoices to help you opt-out that can be downloaded here: https://youradchoices.ca/en/tools/ .

Important Notes About Your Opt-Outs:

  • If you opt-out of interest-based advertising by us or a third party, these tracking technologies may still collect data for other purposes including analytics and you will still see ads from us across the Internet, but the ads will not be targeted based on behavioural information and may therefore be less relevant to you and your interests.
  • On web browsers, interest-based advertising opt-outs work by saving cookies in your browser that tells the various ad partners that you have opted-out. For mobile devices, interest-based advertising opt-outs work by sending an opt out signal that tell the various ad partners that you have opted-out on a particular device. You must opt out separately on each of your browsers and mobile devices from which you do not want to receive interest-based advertising to ensure that information collected on a particular browser or device is not used on another browser or device.
  • If you delete or clear your browser’s cookies, you will need to opt-out again.

For more information about interest-based advertising on your desktop, mobile browser or apps please visit the DAAC website: youradchoices.ca.
