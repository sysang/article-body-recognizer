    Long Beach City GuidePopular neighborhoods in Long Beach, CA

    Los Altos - Los Altos offers a wide selection of green spaces to enjoy, despite its sometimes busy feel. There are a few green spaces close by for residents to explore, such as Whaley Park, and they are especially well-spread out, which results in them being easy to reach from most locations within the neighborhood. Lastly, as there are few people out and about on the streets, this area is relatively quiet; however those who are near Interstate 405 or San Diego Freeway may experience more noise.

    Naples - The character of Naples is exemplified by its relaxed ambience. There are a few green spaces nearby for residents to discover, such as Marine Park, and they are very well-spread out, which makes it very easy to get to them. This area is reasonably quiet overall, as there tend to be low levels of noise from traffic.

    Bixby Knolls - The character of Bixby Knolls is exemplified by its slower-paced ambience. This part of Long Beach is fairly good for those who need a quiet atmosphere, as the streets are usually quite tranquil. Lastly, public green spaces are easy to reach since there are a few of them nearby for residents to visit.

    Downtown - The character of Downtown is exemplified by its slower-paced ambience. It is very easy to access green spaces in the neighborhood since there are approximately 20 of them nearby for residents to visit. Most areas in this part of the city are reasonably quiet, as the streets are reasonably calm - however noise can be an issue near Long Beach Freeway.

    Rancho Estates - People who prefer a calm atmosphere will enjoy Rancho Estates. Most areas in this part of the city are reasonably quiet, as there are generally low levels of noise from traffic. Lastly, it is very easy to access green spaces from a lot of locations within the neighborhood, since there are a few of them close by for residents to explore and getting to the closest park is easy from most properties.
