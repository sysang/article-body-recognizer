    Denver City GuidePopular neighborhoods in Denver, CO

    Montbello - Montbello is a good neighborhood to rent a home in for those who prefer a calm ambience. Most areas in this part of Denver are quiet, as there are generally low levels of noise from traffic - however some parts can be quite noisy, especially near Interstate 70, US Highway 36 or the railway line. Finally, most locations within the neighborhood have reasonably good access to green spaces, like Montbello Central Park and Chambers Rd, since there are around 20 of them close by for residents to discover and most rental properties have a park a short walk away.

    Windsor - Windsor offers a slower-paced ambience and will appeal to those who enjoy spending time outdoors. There are a few parks close by for residents to unwind in, like Highline Canal, and they are especially well-located, making them easy to get to. This neighborhood is quiet overall, as noise from streets and other parts of the city is rarely an issue.

    Fort Logan - Fort Logan is a reasonably good neighborhood to rent a home in for those who prefer a calm environment. Most areas in this part of the city are quiet, as the streets are usually quite peaceful. Lastly, it is very easy to get to parks from the majority of locations within the neighborhood since there are a few of them close by for residents to visit.

    Speer - No matter what type of character renters prefer in a neighborhood, Speer is a fairly good option. The noise levels in Speer are low, as the streets tend to be reasonably calm. Public green spaces, like Cherry Creek, are very well-situated and there are around 10 of them close by for residents to relax in, resulting in them being easy to reach from most locations within the neighborhood. Lastly, finding dynamic streets in this part of the city is very easy. Despite the fact that few entertainment venues can be found nearby, there are often lots of people around, and places to go out at night are well-distributed throughout the area.

    Berkeley - Berkeley is a reasonably good neighborhood to rent a home in for those who prefer a relaxed environment. There are a few green spaces close by for residents to explore, like Berkeley Park and Rocky Mountain Lake Park, and they are very well-located, which makes it easy to access them. This part of Denver is quiet overall, as there tend to be low levels of noise from traffic - although that is not the case around Interstate 70.
