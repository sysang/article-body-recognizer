    New York City GuidePopular neighborhoods in New York, NY

    Arverne - People who prefer a calm environment will enjoy Arverne. This area features not much greenery; although, it is very easy to get to public green spaces from most locations within the neighborhood, since there are a few of them close by for residents to explore and reaching a park is very easy from the majority of houses. This part of New York is reasonably quiet overall, as there isn't a lot of street noise or city clamour - although that is not the case around the railway line or Rockaway Freeway.

    Dyker Heights - No matter what type of character renters prefer in a neighborhood, Dyker Heights is a fairly good option. There are numerous things to do in this neighborhood to enjoy. Even though this area is not known for its nightlife, the streets are usually busy with people. Fort Hamilton Parkway is among the most active streets in this neighborhood. Dyker Heights is quiet overall, as there isn't a lot of street noise or city clamour - although there are still several noisier areas, especially around Gowanus Expressway. There is a good amount of greenery in this part of the city as many tree-lined streets are present. There are about 10 green spaces nearby for residents to visit, such as Leif Ericson Park and Dyker Beach Park, which makes it very easy to get to them.

    Co-op City - Despite the somewhat busy feel, there are a lot of green spaces to enjoy in Co-op City. To be more specific, it is very easy to get to green spaces from the majority of locations within the neighborhood, since there are around 10 of them nearby for residents to enjoy, including Pelham Bay Park. This part of New York is a fairly good area for those who aren't bothered by some noise, as traffic can occasionally be loud during busier hours of the day, and there can also be much noise from businesses, although there are still several quieter pockets.

    Old Town - Old Town offers a slower-paced atmosphere and will appeal to home renters who enjoy spending time outdoors. This neighborhood is quiet overall, as the streets are usually tranquil. Finally, there is a fair amount of greenery in this part of New York as there are numerous tree-lined streets. There are a few parks close by for residents to relax in, making them very easy to access.

    Tribeca - Renters who enjoy a vibrant atmosphere and spending time outdoors will feel at home in Tribeca. Finding dynamic streets is especially easy in this neighborhood. There are many nearby entertainment venues, there are usually crowds of people around, and finding one of the around 30 bars is very easy since they are generally situated in very convenient locations. Harrison Street in particular offers an extensive selection of places to go out at night. There is not that much greenery in this neighborhood; to be more specific, residents will find that few tree-lined streets are around. Nonetheless, there are around 10 public green spaces nearby for residents to check out, including Hudson River Park and City Hall Park, which makes it very easy to get to them.
