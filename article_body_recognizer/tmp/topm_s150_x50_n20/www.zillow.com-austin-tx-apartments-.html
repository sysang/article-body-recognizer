    Austin City GuidePopular neighborhoods in Austin, TX

    Wells Branch - Wells Branch is quiet overall, as the streets are generally peaceful. The greenery in this neighborhood is not particularly prominent; there are a few parks nearby for residents to explore.

    Central East Austin - No matter what type of character home renters prefer in a neighborhood, Central East Austin is a good option. Even though the ambience is not the liveliest in this neighborhood with its a few nightlife venues, there are options for nighttime activities. Central East Austin is good for those who need a quiet atmosphere, as there are generally low levels of noise from traffic, however some areas can be quite noisy, especially near the railway line, US Highway 290 or Interstate 35. Greenery is prominent in this part of the city as the tree canopy coverage for most streets is good. Accessing green spaces, such as Boggy Creek Greenbelt, is easy from most locations within the neighborhood since there are a few of them close by for residents to explore.

    Galindo - People who prefer a slower-paced environment will enjoy Galindo. This part of the city is quiet, as the streets tend to be reasonably tranquil - although there are still several noisier sections, especially closer to the railway line. Lastly, this neighborhood also features a good amount of greenery; it is easy to get to public green spaces from most locations within the neighborhood since there are a few of them nearby for residents to relax in. In addition, residents will find that there are many tree-lined streets.

    Garrison Park - Garrison Park is a very good neighborhood to rent a home in for those who prefer a slower-paced ambience. The greenery in this area is reasonably prominent as many tree-lined streets are around. There are a few green spaces close by for residents to visit, such as Garrison Park, and they are very well-spread out, making them easy to access. This neighborhood is also very good for those who prefer a quiet atmosphere, as noise from the streets and other parts of the city is rarely an issue.

    St. Johns - There are a lot of green spaces to enjoy in St. Johns, despite its sometimes busy ambience. More specifically, there are a few public green spaces nearby for residents to discover, like Buttermilk Neighborhood Park, and they are especially well-distributed, which makes them easy to reach from numerous locations in this area. At the same time, a large number of streets have quite bad tree coverage. Lastly, this neighborhood is relatively quiet, as there are few pedestrians, although those who are closer to Interstate 35 may experience higher levels of noise.
