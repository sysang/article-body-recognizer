By: Trevor Noah

Publisher's Summary

One of the most dynamic and globally recognized entertainment forces of our time opens up fully about his life, in a brave and inspiring book that traces his learning curve to a place where outer success, inner happiness, and human connection are aligned. Along the way, Will tells the story in full of one of the most amazing rides through the worlds of music and film that anyone has ever had.

Will Smith’s transformation from a West Philadelphia kid to one of the biggest rap stars of his era, and then one of the biggest movie stars in Hollywood history, is an epic tale — but it’s only half the story.

Will Smith thought, with good reason, that he had won at life: Not only was his own success unparalleled, his whole family was at the pinnacle of the entertainment world. Only they didn't see it that way: They felt more like star performers in his circus, a seven-days-a-week job they hadn't signed up for. It turned out Will Smith's education wasn't nearly over. 

This memoir is the product of a profound journey of self-knowledge, a reckoning with all that your will can get you and all that it can leave behind. Written with the help of Mark Manson, author of the multi-million-copy best seller The Subtle Art of Not Giving a F*ck, Will is the story of how one person mastered his own emotions, written in a way that can help everyone else do the same. Few of us will know the pressure of performing on the world's biggest stages for the highest of stakes, but we can all understand that the fuel that works for one stage of our journey might have to be changed if we want to make it all the way home. The combination of genuine wisdom of universal value and a life story that is preposterously entertaining, even astonishing, puts Will the book, like its author, in a category by itself.

A Memoir of Self-Awareness and Self Psychoanalysis

Will Smith is very intelligent, talented, and funny. A true performer who will always leave his audience laughing, crying, or with something to think deeply about. For that, I will always enjoy him as an entertainer, and the most charismatic guy in Hollywood. This book starts off like a memoir, where Will invites us into his childhood and devulges his childhood fears, likes, and dislikes. We also get to know his family. His "Mom-Mom" an educated woman, grandmother "Gi-Gi" a spiritual woman, and his father, who is a strong-armed entrepreneur, but a thorn in the Smith Family household. During the early years, the stoic Will Smith Sr. inculcates the importance of hardwork with his boys. He has a BIG personality, much like Will's today, except his bullying came in physical form, leaving the Smith family with wounds to recover from for many years to come. The second half of the book was part memoir, part self-awareness and psychoanalysis. I enjoyed the beginning of the book far more than the latter parts because I could feel the intimacy and connection to Will's childhood. It also felt more personable. If I didn't know any better, I would think Will wrote the first half and the second half was co-authored with a therapist. The psychoanalytic of everything that was happening in Will's life after his stardom was informative and enlightning at first, but then it becomes excessively preachy and wordy. In particular, when he discusses feelings and emotions and how we interpret them. I also felt within this self-analysis of himself that there was a slight overcast of him judging and excusing his behavior before the reader could reach their own conclusion and or interpretation. I believe more personal information could have been shared in the latter chapters instead of the expansion on human psyche. With this knowledge and self-awareness, I'm still wondering if Will has actually learned the lessons or does he simply understand the "Why" and "How" things happened? Because knowing is half the battle. I would have liked to have seen growth, not just a keen sense of self-awareness. "The Jump," is the final chapter that brings it all together. It's a reminder that Will Smith will always be adventurous, odd, daring, selfish, yes, even crazy at times! Yet, he will leave you with a thought-provoking question, and that is, you only have one life to live so will you live it "Big Willie" style? or Will you live it on a more balanced scale? For all the world is a stage, but in life, we are still left to deal with ourselves when the curtains close. 3.5 stars
