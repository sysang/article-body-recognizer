How Covid will impact New Year’s Eve celebrations in your state

Here are the highlights of what is happening in your state for New Year’s Eve and how Covid-19 will impact on celebrations.

The threat of the Covid-19 Omicron strain will impact how people celebrate New Year’s Eve in Australia, with some jurisdictions imposing restrictions and others warning people to take responsibility for themselves.

Prime Minister Scott Morrison has encouraged people to “enjoy the evening” and look forward to next year, but also warned revellers to “exercise common sense and follow all the normal precautions”.

Here is what is happening in each state on New Year’s Eve and how Covid-19 will impact on celebrations.

NSW

Celebrations in NSW will still include Sydney’s famous fireworks but it appears there will be far fewer spectators watching the show.

Some events have been cancelled, others are strictly ticketed and some businesses have shut completely, but police will still be out in force.

NSW chief health officer Kerry Chant said it was important to celebrate in a way that kept people safe and minimised the risk of transmission, such as being outdoors.

“We know it’s been a challenging year for everyone ... but it’s important to remember that Covid-19 is active in our communities and people are at an increased risk of contracting Covid-19, particularly in indoor environments,” Dr Chant said.

“We’re urging people to avoid mingling in large groups where possible and hold your events outside if you can.

“If you’re inside, keep your gathering small and make sure the area is well ventilated by opening doors and windows.”

People are also urged to use the Service NSW check-in app where available, wear a mask and practise physical distancing.

It will be a pleasant 29 degrees on New Year’s Day.

VICTORIA

Rooftop fireworks will not form part of the city celebrations this year, with fireworks only visible from the ticketed celebration zones.

The planned drone swarm at the Docklands zone will also not go ahead due to Covid-19.

Celebration zone attendees must be fully vaccinated and wearing a mask is highly recommended.

New Year’s Day in Melbourne will be a hot 37 degrees.

QUEENSLAND

Fireworks will light up the river at South Bank for Brisbane’s official celebration for the first time since 2019.

There will be two fireworks shows — one at 8.30pm for families and another at midnight for late-night revellers.

Thousands of people are expected to attend events across the city and people are urged to wear masks if they cannot physically distance.

There will be a shower or two in Brisbane on New Year’s Day, with the temperature expected to reach 28 degrees.

WESTERN AUSTRALIA

Fireworks displays are planned for the Swan River, Rottnest, Fremantle, Rockingham, Mandurah, City Beach, Mindarie and several regional locations.

Nightclubs remain closed following an outbreak of the Delta variant via a French backpacker who arrived from Queensland, but the Perth Cup will be held on New Year’s Day.

Police Minister Paul Papalia said there would be more officers on the beat to stamp out any anti-social behaviour.

“With the free public transport, there really is no excuse for drinking and driving,” he said.

“Make New Year’s Eve a night to remember for all the right reasons.

“Get home safely and spare a thought for our hardworking police and emergency service workers who will be on duty looking after you.”

After a heatwave in Perth during Christmas, New Year’s Eve will be a little cooler, with January 1 forecast to reach 29 degrees.

SOUTH AUSTRALIA

Premier Steven Marshall said it would be a “very different” New Year’s Eve “compared to virtually every other year”.

“My strong message is you can still have fun but do it in a safe way. 2021 has had some highs and certainly had some lows,” he told reporters on Friday.

“I appreciate the situation has been very, very tough for many people, but we still do live in a very safe place in SA.

“We are confronted with a mighty challenge with Omicron at the moment but I’m confident that we’ll be able to rise to this challenge, just as we’ve been able to rise to the previous waves that affected the world so disastrously.”

Chief health officer Nicola Spurrier was slammed this week after she urged people to have the “quietest New Year’s Eve they’ve ever had”.

Party plans have been scaled back due to Covid-19 but revellers can attend the Light Up family event at Rymill Park.

There will be two fireworks shows at 9.30pm and midnight. It has a capacity of 5000 people.

There will also be five new “Midnight Moments” spread across the city and North Adelaide, with each having live music and within proximity of the fireworks.

“Following last year’s cancellation, we had to find ways to adapt and spread the large crowds across multiple areas of the city and North Adelaide, and I’m genuinely excited about what’s planned,” Lord Mayor Sandy Verschoor said.

It will be a warm night in Adelaide and New Year’s Day is tipped to reach 37 degrees.

ACT

There will be fireworks around Lake Burley Griffin at 9pm and midnight.

It is recommended that people wear a mask when they cannot physically distance from others, such as near food vans or toilets.

Canberra had gone without fireworks for the past two years, due to bushfires and then Covid-19.

It will be mostly sunny and 31 degrees on January 1 in Canberra.

TASMANIA

Hobart will have fireworks at 9.30pm and midnight over the water.

The main viewing point will be from the Taste of Summer New Year’s Eve event, which is ticketed.

New Year’s Day will be partly cloudy and 28 degrees.

NORTHERN TERRITORY

Chief Minister Michael Gunner cracked up laughing this week after being asked about his health advice for people keen for a New Year’s Eve kiss.

Mr Gunner gave the go ahead to pash people you know, but said he was sticking to his pre-pandemic rules about smooching strangers.

“Be careful who you pash at midnight, you should know them, and if you know them, pash them,” he told reporters.

“But if you don’t know them, there’s probably a few things you’re going to maybe ask them about first.”

Darwin will have two fireworks shows at 9pm and midnight as part of an event at the Darwin Waterfront.

The line-up includes Amy Shark and Conrad Sewell, but The Veronicas had to pull out due to “illness within the touring party”.

There will be a possible shower or storm in Darwin on January 1, with the temperature likely to hit 33 degrees.
