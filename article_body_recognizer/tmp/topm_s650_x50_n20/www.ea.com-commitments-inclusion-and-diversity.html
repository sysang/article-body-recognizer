Diversity, Equity and Inclusion at Electronic Arts

We believe in the potential of every human being.

We celebrate diversity of thought, cultural differences, lifestyle, age, background, experience, religion, economic and social status, gender identity and marital status, among other aspects. 

We believe that building a culture of inclusion enables more positive experiences for our people, players, and community.

We are working to embed inclusion across our systems, processes, and culture to:

Attract, develop, and retain great talent that can thrive and do their best work.

Develop relatable content and games.

Enable expansion into new and diverse markets.

Bridge to better representation that authentically reflects players around the world.

Diverse Teams – An Imperative

Amazing games and services start with teams that are diverse as the players and communities we serve. Our investments in diversity, equity, and inclusion empower our people, celebrate diversity, and actively foster inclusion, shaping the future of Electronic Arts along the way.

Our Commitment to Inclusive Hiring

To create games and experiences that resonate with a wide base of players, we must cultivate a workforce that reflects the diverse player community. We focus on including diverse candidates throughout the hiring process. Inclusive hiring is a long-term commitment for Electronic Arts, and we continually improve and broaden our approach to promote the growth of underrepresented talent.

Our Commitment to Pay Equity

To us, pay equity means that employees are paid equitably for their work, regardless of their gender, race/ethnicity, or other characteristics not relevant to their role or performance in it.

Diversity Hiring Practices Page

We know that diversity strengthens our teams, and our global reach requires a workforce that reflects and respects the full range of identities and experiences we see in our players. Substantially all of our hiring includes diverse candidates in the initial pool.

We also focus-in on two later stages of the hiring process that our research shows best drives hiring outcomes:

  1. Recruiter Review: The stage at which our talent acquisition team reviews and assesses candidates and moves them forward to the hiring manager for consideration.
  2. Hiring Manager Interview: The slate of candidates that the hiring manager interviews.

Business unit leaders and talent acquisition leadership are held accountable for these two stages of the hiring process.

We have seen year-over-year increases in the percentage of underrepresented talent at these critical stages. In addition, we’ve seen positive hiring outcomes at all levels of the company, including the executive level. Since 2019, we’ve experienced year-over-year increases in open positions filled with underrepresented talent and we have consistently hired at rates above our current representation rates.

Equality Town Halls

Electronic Arts brings the company together to discuss topics centered around equality and its connection with our business strategy and culture. The Equality Town Halls aim to create a safe environment where employees and leaders can engage in meaningful conversations about social injustice and systems of inequity to promote a new level of awareness for the role that every person plays.

Inclusion-based Partnerships and Events

Inclusion-based strategic partnerships, events and conference sponsorships and participation helps us engage diverse talent inside and outside our organization.

Next-Generation Development and Recruitment

We engage a diverse community of next-generation leaders in our local communities to create more equitable opportunities for underrepresented talent. Our outreach includes Historically Black Colleges and Universities, Hispanic Serving Institutions, and partnerships with organizations such as the Jackie Robinson Foundation and the Hispanic Scholarship Fund.

Creating Inclusive Games

We believe that games have the power to transcend society, and that the characters we create and the stories we tell can positively impact the world around us. To inspire the world to play, we design worlds, stories, characters, experiences and services that resonate with diverse players globally and positively impact how we treat one another. We consider cultural, social, and other needs such as usability, accessibility, and clear and non-exclusionary language throughout our game design and development process.

We incorporate three core principles into our game design:

  • Our games represent believable experiences that are reflective of the diverse communities we serve
  • Our games can be enjoyed and are playable by gamers of all skill levels and abilities
  • Our games are welcoming of all people in ways that are meaningful to them

Supporting Inclusion in Our Communities

Electronic Arts community programs aim to help strengthen underrepresented communities. We contribute to non-profit organizations that are focused on equality and inclusion across science, technology, engineering, arts, and math (STEAM) education. We consider social justice an important component of the well-being of our communities and contribute to organizations working to end systemic racial injustice, fight discrimination and protect human rights around the world.
