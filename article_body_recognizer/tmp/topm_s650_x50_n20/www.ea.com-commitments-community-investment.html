Our Commitment to Community Investment

Electronic Arts is committed to creating positive impact in the communities in which we live, play and work. We are committed to social justice and racial equity, investing in the future of the next generation of playmakers, and responding to our communities’ essential needs.

EA’s community investments help to strengthen underrepresented communities. Our contributions to non-profit organizations are focused on equality and inclusion across science, technology, engineering, arts and math (STEAM) education. 

During this extraordinary time, we supported in providing essential needs impacted by the pandemic and increased our investment in nonprofits who are working toward greater social justice in our communities. In total, we donated $8 million through company grants, EA’s volunteerism grants and employee gift matching programs, a 122% giving increase over last year.

Organizations Electronic Arts is Proud to Support Across Education and Equality

Global Impact by the Numbers Fiscal Year 2021

Supporting Our Global Communities through the Pandemic

EA’s response to the COVID-19 pandemic is focused on caring for our communities. This includes more than $3 million in contributions to nonprofits working on global response including Global Giving and Give2Asia, as well as providing our local communities with food, supplies, game codes and more.

Supporting Social and Racial Justice

We consider social justice an important component of the well-being of our communities. We contributed more than $1.4 million in combined donations and giving programs to organizations working to end systemic racial injustice, fight discrimination and protect human rights in the U.S. and around the world.

EA Partnerships Having Impact

Jackie Robinson Foundation

The Jackie Robinson Foundation Scholarship program addresses the achievement gap in higher education by providing scholarships, mentoring and support services for talented people from underrepresented communities. Electronic Arts has sponsored students affiliated with the Jackie Robinson Foundation and provided multiple internship opportunities each year since 2012.

SpecialEffect

SpecialEffect modifies and creates personalized gaming controls for individuals living with disabilities. Our UK, Sweden and Ireland offices team up with SpecialEffect to raise funds and engage our player community to support their mission.

Girls Who Code

Girls Who Code strives to close the gender gap in technology and to change the image of what a programmer looks like and does. To date, Electronic Arts has:

  • Hosted seven Summer Immersion Programs
  • Helped introduce more than 600 girls to coding
  • Logged more than 2,000 employee volunteer hours. 

Electronic Arts signed the Girls Who Code’s #HireMe pledge promoting career opportunities to its alumni network. To date, eleven alumni have joined EA as interns, and one as a full-time software engineer.

Giving Back to our Communities

We have a bias for action at Electronic Arts, and provide our people with a variety of opportunities to give back to their communities and the organizations that they are passionate about, including:

  • 100% match to any full-time employee’s donation to a charitable organization, up to $5,000 USD (or equivalent)
  • Eight hours of paid volunteer time off each year to qualified employees 
  • A $1,000 USD donation to a nonprofit of choice (up to $2,000 every fiscal year), where an employee has logged 40 volunteer hours over the course of the year
