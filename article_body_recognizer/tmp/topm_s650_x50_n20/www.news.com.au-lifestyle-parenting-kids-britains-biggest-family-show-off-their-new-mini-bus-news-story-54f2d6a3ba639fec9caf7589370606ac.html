Britain’s biggest family show off their new ‘mini bus’

If you think you have a busy household, imagine having 22 kids to ferry around – it’s why this mum now rides around in a “mini bus”.

Martha Cliff, The Sun

December 31, 2021 - 10:28AM

With 22 kids in tow, Sue and Noel Radford no doubt struggle to ferry their bumper brood from A to B.

But the mum of Britain’s biggest family has today revealed that she picked up a late Christmas present – a mini bus.

Sue shared a snap of herself holding the keys to the bus on the family Instagram account with the caption, “Just picked up our new family minibus.”

It is believed that the couple have purchased the nine-seater Renault Trafic, which retails at £34,850 ($A65,000), The Sun reports.

It comes just months after Sue was treated to a swanky new Range Rover for her 46th birthday from husband Noel in March and shared snaps of the black car, which cost upwards of £30,000 ($A55,000).

The excited mum wrote on Instagram: “Happy birthday to me. I’m so incredibly lucky to have such an amazing hubby who works so hard for us all.

“I love you @noelradford.”

Noel then revealed in August that the family changed their Range Rover Discovery for something “greener”.

He confirmed on Instagram that they now own a Porsche Cayenne E-Hybrid Quarzite, which can cost upwards of £80,000 ($A148,000).

In September, Sue revealed how she manages the kids’ school pick up and drops offs.

With eight of her kids attending four different schools, the mum said she has no choice but to prepare their morning routines in advance.

On their Instagram account ‘theradfordfamily’, the two parents give an insight into their busy schedule, and how they keep organised.

In one of their latest posts, the couple uploaded a photo of folded school uniforms, school shoes and drinking bottles.

“And just like that, the summer holidays are over. So sad they are going back to school tomorrow but we’ve had the best summer holidays,” Sue captioned the post.

In the Channel 4 documentary 22 kids and counting, Sue and Noel also open up about how busy life can be being Britain’s largest family.

“We’ve got the uniforms to sort out which we get once a year and the dreaded shoe bill,” Sue said.

“Sometimes you can reuse a PE kit because it still fits them from the year before but definitely not the uniforms, they just get a bit shabby.”

The uniform bill sets the couple back about £418 ($A780) a year. Despite the cost, and chaos of two school runs every morning, Sue is happy when her children are at school.

“I think it’s nice for them to get the normality back into their lives,” she says.

“They need their friends and their schooling, they need their routine back.”

The Radfords, who live in a 10-bed former care home, often show off their millionaire lifestyle on Instagram.

This includes having an outdoor cinema, TV bed, and plenty of family get aways.

In the show, Sue also revealed they have spent £1 million ($A1.8 million) raising their large brood and she has been pregnant for 16-and-a-half years.

Sue and Noel are parents to Chris, 32, Sophie, 28, Chloe, 26, Jack, 24, Daniel, 22, Luke, 21, Millie, 20, Katie, 19, James, 18, Ellie, 16, Aimee, 15, Josh, 14, Max, 13, Tillie, 11, Oscar, 10, Casper, 9, Hallie, 6, Phoebe, 5, Archie, 4, Bonnie, 3, and Heidie, 1.
