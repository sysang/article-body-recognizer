2021: The PlayStation ultimate review

Discover the games, characters, memories and moments that defined 2021 on PlayStation.

Outstanding newcomers

The embattled duo you rooted for, the wise-cracking tough guy you got to know or the lost astronaut you tried to rescue - who was your favourite new face this year?

Selene Vassos | Returnal

Trapped in a perilous time-loop, deep space Astra scout Selene faced her own complex history in action-driven roguelike Returnal. Taking full advantage of the PS5 console's unique hardware features, Housemarque delivered one of the year's most intense and immersive gaming experiences.
Find out more

Colt Vahn | DEATHLOOP

Beleaguered assassin Colt found himself sequestered to a single recurring day on the island of Blackreef in acclaimed developer Arkane Lyon's time loop-driven first-person adventure. Find a way to break the cycle, outwit your sworn nemesis Julianna and pull off the most ambitious series of assassinations ever.
Find out more

May and Cody | It Takes Two

Back in March, Hazelight Studio returned with a dedicated multiplayer experience that combined a whole range of different mechanics and genres to deliver a truly unique adventure that forced an embattled couple to confront the complexities of their failing marriage.

Returning heroes

Old friends on new adventures, familiar faces with fresh new features and a well-known worlds crammed with new things to do.

Ratchet and Clank | Ratchet Clank: Rift Apart

Ratchet and Clank returned in June with a few new friends in tow, including Rivet - a Lombax from another dimension. The brand-new, galaxy-hopping adventure leverages some of the PlayStation 5 console's most innovative features to deliver one of the year's most cutting-edge adventures.
Find out more

Sam Porter Bridges | DEATH STRANDING DIRECTOR'S CUT

Gaming legend Hideo Kojima's unique open-world adventure made the jump to PlayStation 5 this year, expanding Sam Porter Bridges' surreal quest across a shattered America with new tools, story content and features, alongside a swathe of technical upgrades.

Agent 47 | HITMAN 3

I/O Interactive concluded their critically lauded 'World of Assassination' trilogy in January with the return of legendary contract killer Agent 47. Hop the globe as you hunt down high-profile targets, expand your arsenal and look for ever more creative ways to dispatch your marks.

Unforgettable villains

They hunted you, they haunted you and they mocked you - this year's biggest villains were, in some cases, really big.

Lady Dimitrescu | Resident Evil Village

Few villains were more eye-catching or intimidating this year than Resident Evil Village's Lady Dimitrescu. A towering and terrifying vision of 1920s elegance, she stalked the halls of Castle Dimitrescu wielding razor claws.
Find out more

Antón Castillo | Far Cry 6

Celebrated actor Giancarlo Esposito lent both his voice and likeness to the face of Yara's imposing and imperious dictator Antón Castillo in October's Far Cry 6, setting the stage for thrilling turns of corruption, espionage and revolution.
Find out more

IXION | Returnal

Selene's second boss encounter on Atropos, the demonic bird-like creature IXION, was one of the game's first major trials, challenging players to draw on everything they'd learnt so far in a gruelling three-phase showdown.
Find out more

Multiplayer moments

From charming co-operative adventures to gruelling team shoot outs, there have been more than enough reasons to play together this year.

Back 4 Blood

Left 4 Dead developer Turtle Rock Studios set out to perfect and expand its winning formula with Back 4 Blood, which pits a group of plucky survivors against the ravenous hordes of the Ridden - a plague of mutated humanoids that have overrun the Earth.

Riders Republic

Building on the success of open world extreme sports hit Steep, Ubisoft returned in November with an expanded vision, featuring high-flying snowboards, immense downhill cycle routes and blistering wing suit challenges.
