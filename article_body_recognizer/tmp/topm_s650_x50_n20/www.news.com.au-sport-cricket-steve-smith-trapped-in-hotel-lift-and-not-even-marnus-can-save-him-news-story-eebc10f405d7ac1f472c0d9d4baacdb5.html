Steve Smith trapped in hotel lift and not even Marnus can save him

The horror show we call 2021 threw another curve ball Steve Smith’s way when he finally thought he was done with this dog of a year.

This year is insisting on being an absolute trainwreck right up until the very end.

Australia’s cricketers signed off from 2021 on a high by thrashing England in the Boxing Day Test to go up 3-0 in the Ashes and retain the urn but Steve Smith’s final days before saying hello to 2022 haven’t gone entirely to plan.

The Aussie batter documented his difficult Thursday night on social media, revealing he was trapped in a lift for an hour at the team hotel in Melbourne.

“I’m on my floor, I’m staying on level 20 but the doors won’t open,” Smith said as part of a series of clips uploaded to his Instagram story. “Out of service up there apparently. I’m stuck in here for now.

“I’ve tried to get the door open; I’ve got this side open, Marnus (Labuschagne) is on the other side trying to open it up to not much avail.

“Not quite the evening I had planned, let’s be honest.

“Well, I’m just taking a seat. What else do you do when you’re stuck in a lift? I could be here for a while. Anyone got any suggestions for what I can do?”

Marnus Labuschagne has followed Smith around like a puppy dog ever since rejoining the Test side in 2019 and he was helping his good mate out in this time of need, feeding the ex-Aussie captain some MMs through a tiny opening to get him through the night.

“Oh what have you got? Some food for me?” Smith said. “What have we got — a few MMs? That’s naughty. That’ll do. Keep me going in here — I need it.”

Unfortunately, Labuschagne’s efforts to break into the lift weren’t successful so MMs were all he could offer.

After nearly an hour inside, Smith was given hope he’d only be stuck for 10 more minutes, adding: “Not much fun though. Not really enjoying it in here.”

Thankfully, the cavalry came to the rescue. Labuschagne filmed the moment Smith tasted freedom as a technician managed to get the doors open to a smattering of applause.

England was probably hoping Smith was still stuck in there for the fourth Test, which gets underway in Sydney on January 5.
