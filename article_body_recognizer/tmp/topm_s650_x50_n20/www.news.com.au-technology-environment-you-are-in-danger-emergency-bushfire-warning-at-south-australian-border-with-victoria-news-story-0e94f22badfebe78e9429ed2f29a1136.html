‘You are in danger’: Emergency bushfire warning at South Australian border with Victoria

An out-of-control bushfire is burning at the border between South Australia and Victoria, with people warned their lives are in danger.

A bushfire is burning out of control at the border between South Australia and Victoria.

“You are in danger. Act now to protect yourself. It is too late to leave,” the Victorian Country Fire Authority warned on Friday.

“The safest option is to take shelter indoors immediately.”

An emergency warning has been issued for Dergholm, Dorodong, Langkoop, Poolaijelo, Powers Creek and Wrattonbully.

“There is a grassfire at Fire-Wrattonbully-Edenhope Rd, Langkoop that is not yet under control,” the CFA said.

“The grassfire is travelling in a south-easterly direction from Edenhope Rd, near the South Australian border, towards Fullartons Rd and Poolaijelo.

“A wind change is expected between 3pm and 4pm, which will cause the fire to change direction easterly towards Poolaijelo and Casterton-Apsley Rd.

“Conditions can become very dangerous and unpredictable.”

South Australia’s CFA said 20 trucks were in attendance.
