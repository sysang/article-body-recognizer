Why UNICEF?

For the past 75 years, UNICEF has stared down some of the biggest crises our world has faced. We’ve been on the front lines of every pandemic, epidemic, famine and conflict since World War II and we’re the largest distributor of vaccines in the world. History shows us we have the experience, tools and partners to not only recover but to build back better.

UNICEF is the largest buyer of vaccines in the world and has the systems and experience to reach families everywhere. Right now, you can help UNICEF distribute two billion vaccines, syringes, logistical equipment and train health care workers to safely distribute vaccines and protect families everywhere.

COVAX is a global initiative to ensure a fair distribution of the COVID-19 vaccine. Through the COVAX Facility (co-led by WHO, UNICEF, Gavi the Vaccine Alliance and CEPI, the Coalition for Epidemic Preparedness Innovations), a total of 92 countries, known as the AMC923 countries (classified by the World Bank as countries eligible for International Development assistance), as well as more than 90 self-financing countries (comprised of World Bank classified upper-middle-income and high-income countries) will be able to access COVID-19 vaccine doses.
