Patriots, with just three pass attempts, grind out a big win over the Bills

By Mark Maske
Today at 8:49 p.m. EST|Updated today at 11:18 p.m. EST

The New England Patriots ran right over the Buffalo Bills, literally.

On a breezy and blustery night in Orchard Park, N.Y., the Patriots allowed rookie quarterback Mac Jones to throw only three passes, two of which came during the fourth quarter. They relied on their running game and defense to extend their winning streak to seven games by beating the Buffalo Bills, 14-10.

The Patriots improved their record to 9-4. They retained first place in the AFC East and maintained their hold on the No. 1 playoff seed in the AFC.

The New England Patriots ran right over the Buffalo Bills, literally.

On a breezy and blustery night in Orchard Park, N.Y., the Patriots allowed rookie quarterback Mac Jones to throw only three passes, two of which came during the fourth quarter. They relied on their running game and defense to extend their winning streak to seven games by beating the Buffalo Bills, 14-10.

The Patriots improved their record to 9-4. They retained first place in the AFC East and maintained their hold on the No. 1 playoff seed in the AFC.

With the wind gusting to 50 mph and above, the Patriots amassed 222 rushing yards on 46 attempts. Tailback Damien Harris had a 64-yard touchdown run in the first quarter. The Patriots followed that with a two-point conversion, not even daring to try an extra point into the wind. Kicker Nick Folk added a pair of field goals with the wind at his back.

Jones completed two of three passes for 19 yards on the night.

The New England defense did the rest. The Bills scored a first-quarter touchdown on a 14-yard pass from quarterback Josh Allen to wide receiver Gabriel Davis following a muffed punt by the Patriots. Kicker Tyler Bass had a third-quarter field goal.

But Bass missed a fourth-quarter field goal into the wind and Allen threw incomplete on a fourth-and-14 play from the New England 18-yard line with just less than two minutes to play. The Bills fell to 7-5 with the loss. (Final score: Patriots 14, Bills 10)

Pats throw one pass in windy Buffalo, but lead Bills at half

The Patriots, trying to extend their winning streak to seven games and maintain their hold on the No. 1 seed in the AFC, threw only one — one! — pass in the first half in blustery conditions Monday night in Orchard Park, N.Y.

And yet they still lead the Bills, 11-7.

Rookie quarterback Mac Jones completed that lone pass for a 12-yard gain. The Patriots had 23 rushing attempts on their 24 offensive plays. They amassed 149 rushing yards — 64 of them on a touchdown run by tailback Damien Harris in the first quarter, when the Patriots were playing into the teeth of a wind gusting to 50 mph and more.

The Patriots added a successful two-point conversion after the touchdown, not even trying an extra point into the wind. Kicker Nick Folk had a 41-yard field goal in the second quarter, with the wind at his back.

The Bills had only 74 yards of total offense in the half. Quarterback Josh Allen completed six of 10 passes for 46 yards.

Allen threw a 14-yard touchdown pass to wide receiver Gabriel Davis in the first quarter after the Bills recovered a muffed punt by the Patriots’ N’Keal Harry. The ball grazed Harry’s helmet after bouncing off the turf, and the Bills recovered the ball and were awarded possession on a replay reversal. The on-field officials initially had ruled that Harry did not touch the ball.

The Bills failed to capitalize on a 15-yard punt by the Patriots into the wind in the opening quarter. They took possession on the New England 40-yard line but lost a fumble on a botched handoff. (Patriots 11, Bills 7)

Bills get Josh Allen-to-Gabriel Davis TD after muffed punt, replay reversal

The Bills took advantage of a muffed punt by the Patriots, courtesy of an instant replay reversal, and immediately reached the end zone with a 14-yard touchdown pass from Josh Allen to wide receiver Gabriel Davis.

Davis got open against cornerback Jalen Mills on a slant pattern and caught Allen’s on-target throw.

The touchdown came on the first play after the Bills recovered the muff on the punt return by the Patriots’ N’Keal Harry.

Harry initially avoided touching the ball. But the ball bounced off the turf and just grazed his helmet. The Bills grabbed the loose ball at the New England 14-yard line and immediately believed the ball was theirs.

The on-field officials did not agree, ruling that Harry had not touched the ball and possession belonged to the Patriots. But that ruling was quickly overturned by the replay official — correctly — without the Bills having to challenge.

The Bills, with the wind at their back, settled for an extra point. (Patriots 8, Bills 7)

The weather is an issue

The weather will be a factor in Monday night’s Patriots-Bills game, with the Buffalo-area forecast calling for a chance of snow and wind gusts of 50 mph or greater.

Passing and kicking will be particularly affected.

It’s unclear which team that favors. The Bills rely heavily on their quarterback, Josh Allen. But Allen also is a capable runner.

The Bills are ranked 11th in the NFL in rushing offense, at 118.3 yards per game. The Patriots are 13th, at 115.3 yards per game.

Both teams have powerful defenses. But the Bills have the advantage in rushing defense. They’re sixth in the league in that category, while the Patriots are 20th.
