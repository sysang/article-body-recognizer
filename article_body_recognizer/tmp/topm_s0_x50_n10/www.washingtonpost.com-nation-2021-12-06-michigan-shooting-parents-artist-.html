Detectives on Monday interviewed a Detroit-area artist connected to the building where the parents of the 15-year-old suspect in the Michigan school shooting last week were found after an extensive search by law enforcement agencies, according to the Oakland County Sheriff’s Office.

James and Jennifer Crumbley, the parents of Ethan Crumbley, had disappeared in the hours after prosecutors announced that the pair would be charged with four counts of involuntary manslaughter after their son was alleged to have opened fire at Oxford High School, killing four and wounding several. Police arrested the parents early Saturday after they were found in a commercial building on Detroit’s east side, a space the sheriff’s office said is linked to 65-year-old artist Andrzej Sikora.

The weekend’s events unfolded as authorities continued to investigate what led up to the country’s deadliest school shooting in more than three years.

The Oakland County Sheriff’s Office said Monday night that it had interviewed Sikora for about two hours that day.

“Immediately after the questioning, Sheriff’s Detectives went to Sikora’s Troy home and executed a search warrant, seizing several digital devices for examination,” the office said in a news release. “The information will then be presented to prosecutors to determine if charges are appropriate.”

After the interview, Sikora’s attorney, Clarence Dass, said that his client met with law enforcement officials “to assist in their investigation of the tragedy at Oxford High School” and that Sikora “fully cooperated.”

“Mr. Sikora did not assist the Crumbleys in evading law enforcement, did not know there was a warrant for their arrest, and did not know they were at his art studio at the time of their arrest,” the attorney said in a statement. “He has not been charged with any crime, and will continue to assist members law enforcement in their pursuit of justice.”

Oakland County Sheriff Michael Bouchard said over the weekend that the parents were “assisted” in getting into the building and that charges could be filed against the person who helped them.

A day before James and Jennifer Crumbley were taken into custody, prosecutors charged them with four counts of involuntary manslaughter in an unusual effort to hold parents accountable for violence attributed to their child.

Prosecutors say the Crumbleys provided the handgun that the 15-year-old allegedly used to carry out the attack and ignored warning signs about their son’s potential for violence.

In the hours before the shooting, the high school sophomore also had conversations with school counselors, the superintendent of Oxford Community Schools told parents in a weekend note. It shared details about how school officials handled the situation and pledged that those interactions would be part of an independent investigation.

“I’ve asked for a third-party investigation to be conducted so we leave no stone unturned, including any and all interaction the student had with staff and students,” Superintendent Tim Throne said in the lengthy note, which was sent to families Saturday.

In response, Michigan Attorney General Dana Nessel said her office has offered to conduct a “full and comprehensive” review of the shooting and events leading up to it.

“Our attorneys and special agents are uniquely qualified to perform an investigation of this magnitude and are prepared to perform an extensive investigation and inquiry to answer the many questions the community has regarding this tragedy,” Nessel tweeted Sunday.
