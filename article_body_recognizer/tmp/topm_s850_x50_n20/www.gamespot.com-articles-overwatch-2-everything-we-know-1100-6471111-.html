Overwatch 2: Everything We Know

Overwatch 2 will boast new modes, maps, heroes, PvE content, and more, but it also means new stuff for the original game too.

By GameSpot Staff on December 23, 2021 at 10:46AM PST

The highly-anticipated Overwatch 2 may still be a long way off, but a detailed picture of the game is slowly coming together. First announced at Blizzcon 2019, the upcoming sequel to Blizzard’s popular team-based first-person hero shooter from 2016 will have fresh things to offer players old and new, including story missions, hero redesigns, and new maps. We learned a lot about Overwatch 2 in 2021, with Blizzard providing new details showing how you'll fight, where, and with what heroes when the game eventually launches.

To help you get up to speed about everything there is to know about Overwatch 2, we've compiled this feature detailing all the essential details. For a specific topic, use the table of contents above, otherwise just scroll through for all the info, trailers, and more.

Release window

Overwatch 2 doesn't have a confirmed release timing, and 2021 slipped by without too much additional information. We do know the wait is still going to be a while--during a November 2021 earnings call, Activision Blizzard said it was delaying Overwatch 2 even further, without giving a sense of when it was being delayed to. So, it seems the soonest you'll be able to play Overwatch 2 is sometime in 2022. However, Activision Blizzard's discussion of the situation suggested it doesn't expect to see the sales bump it would receive from Overwatch 2 or another big title on its docket, Diablo 4, during the next fiscal year. That could mean that Overwatch 2 won't see release until April 2023 at the earliest, but this is still somewhat unclear.

We do know, however, that the Overwatch League will still play an early build of Overwatch 2 in early 2022, according to the investor call.

Platforms

At BlizzCon 2019, Blizzard confirmed that Overwatch 2 would come to all platforms that currently support the first Overwatch, including the Nintendo Switch. As of writing, it's uncertain whether or not it'll also come to PS5 and Xbox Series X|S.

  • Release date: N/A
  • Platforms: PC (Battle.net), Switch, PS4, Xbox One
  • Genre: First-person shooter

Director Jeff Kaplan steps down

After nearly 20 years with Blizzard, Overwatch director Jeff Kaplan announced in April 2021 he was leaving the company. In addition to working on a number of beloved Blizzard titles during his tenure, including World of Warcraft and Warcraft 3, Kaplan has essentially been the public face of Overwatch since before the first game's release in 2016, and his departure is a major shakeup for Overwatch and its sequel.
