Tsinghua–Science Workshops: Epigenetics Structure and Function (Advertorial series - Part 4)

  • 24 Dec 2021
This Advertorial is brought to you by the Science/AAAS Custom Publishing Office.
Part 4: Epigenetics structure and function

Tsinghua University and Science/AAAS are collaborating to present the latest breakthroughs and advances in a range of important research areas. Internationally renowned experts were invited to present their work in a series of lectures that ran through 2021. In the first lecture series, the speakers sought to broaden our understanding of spliceosomes and RNA, particularly in disease and viral pathogenesis. The second series focused on how molecular structures impact drug design, while the third examined computational structural biology, including the use of artificial intelligence for predicting the shapes of proteins. The fourth and final series covers how epigenetics can impact gene regulation, disease, and more. Modification of DNA and its associated histone proteins can have profound effects on numerous biological processes and elucidating these pathways can assist in developing new therapies for a range of diseases. You can find other advertorials in this series here: Part 1, Part 2, and Part 3.
