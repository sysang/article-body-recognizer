We encourage our staff to share their knowledge with the public through a number of avenues, including publishing papers and speaking at conferences and symposia. You can find technical reports, white papers, and other related publications on a wide variety of topics on this site.

To learn more about how we support our sponsors' missions, we also feature a collection of Project Stories, which highlight work programs from the last several years. To download recent MITRE Annual Reports, please select them using the Publications list.

Publications

Project Story | December 2021 Know Your Weakest Link: Exposing Weaknesses to Eliminate Cyber Flaws Mistakes in implementation, code, design, or architecture leave products and systems open to cyber attacks. Since spotting common software weaknesses helps eliminate them early, MITRE created a common list of weakness types used worldwide.
Technical Paper | December 2021 Federal Identity Learning Agenda: Outcomes from the 2021 FedID Conference This document, developed from presentations at the federal government’s 2021 FedID conference, highlights three areas of primary concern for the community to address in the future.
Project Story | December 2021 National Publications Highlight Leadership Diversity at MITRE As a company built on problem-solving and innovation, we know diverse perspectives are essential to our success. Our distinguished Board of Trustees is just one example of our commitment to cultivating an inclusive environment where everyone can thrive.
Project Story | November 2021 A Pivotal Year: Transforming Transportation in 2021
Technical Paper | November 2021 Achieving Global Safety Data Aggregation and Processing: The Role of Standards and Data Sharing Platforms Are we ready on a global scale to monitor flight safety, integrate UAS and other emerging entrants, and provide actionable safety intelligence? Currently, the answer is no, so there is much work to be done.
Technical Paper | November 2021 Playbook for Threat Modeling Medical Devices The “Playbook for Threat Modeling Medical Devices” was developed to increase knowledge of threat modeling throughout the medical device ecosystem in order to further strengthen the cybersecurity and safety of medical devices.
Fact Sheet | November 2021 Integration Demonstration and Experimentation for Aeronautics (IDEA) Lab IDEA Lab Corporate Fact Sheet
Project Story | November 2021 Veterans at MITRE: Still Serving U.S. veterans comprise a large segment of MITRE staff. They work across domains, including defense, health, and cyber. As service members close out their military careers, we connect their expertise to prime opportunities to continue serving the nation.
Technical Paper | October 2021 Eight Recommendations for Congress to Improve Federal Cybersecurity Eight recommendations for congressional action to improve federal agency cybersecurity while increasing the efficiency and effectiveness of oversight.
Project Story | October 2021 Pioneering New Ways to Protect Our Nation From the tragic events of 9/11 to countering today’s complex threats, protecting our country requires a holistic approach. We collaborate across multiple government agencies working in various domains—land, sea, air, and cyber—to safeguard the U.S.
Project Story | October 2021 Groundbreaking Signal Processing Technology Broadens View of the World MITRE partners with the U.S. Army to transform signals intelligence and electronic warfare through Photon™, bringing enhanced digital signal processing to the warfighter—and beyond.
Technical Paper | October 2021 Accelerating Innovation for Better Health: A MITRE Framework Supporting the Proposed Advanced Research Projects Agency for Health (ARPA-H) Policymakers propose a new agency, ARPA-H, to speed up breakthroughs that will improve health. MITRE’s framework for measuring innovation gaps, prioritizing societal needs, and attracting private capital with public funding offers a roadmap.
Technical Paper | September 2021 Reverse Innovation in Defense The Department of Defense and U.S. allies have an opportunity to apply reverse innovation strategies to design novel defense capabilities for allies and scaling the most promising solutions for U.S. forces.
Project Story | September 2021 MITRE Partnerships with the Private Sector Deliver Public Benefit MITRE’s partnerships with industry bring the best capabilities, tools, and insights to help solve the toughest national and global problems—and enable us to contribute to a robust technology market.
Fact Sheet | September 2021 Global Health Security The nation will continue to grapple with the threat of biological events such as COVID-19, and MITRE stands ready to partner across sectors to mitigate the consequences of such events to the American people.
Technical Paper | September 2021 Federal Response Team Playbook: Community Activation in a National Public Health Emergency This playbook details how to manage a community activation campaign during a public health emergency. Community activation requires targeted messaging, rapid analysis and translation of data to operations, and a nimble infrastructure.
Project Story | September 2021 In the Community: Bobby Blount’s Lifetime of Volunteerism and Public Service MITRE Austin/San Antonio iHub site leader Bobby Blount believes in the power of opportunity to transform lives. From solar race cars to mentoring to micro-lending initiatives, his longtime volunteerism in and around the city has made a lasting impact.
Fact Sheet | September 2021 MITRE’s Independent Research Development Program MITRE’s Independent Research Development (RD) experts work with government agencies to identify their hardest problems that could be solved through RD.
Technical Paper | August 2021 WebAssembly: Current and Future Applications This report introduces WebAssembly (Wasm) technology, with a goal of educating potential users and motivating further exploration and use.
Technical Paper | August 2021 Charting New Horizons: Technology and U.S. Competitive Success The U.S. is at an inflection point in how we tackle our most critical ST issues. Implementing a 21st Century ST strategy will require not merely developing fancy widgets, but also a focus on legal, workforce, and governance issues.
