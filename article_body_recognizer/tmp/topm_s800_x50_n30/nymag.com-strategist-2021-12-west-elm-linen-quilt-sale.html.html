This Perfectly Minimalist Linen Quilt Is 50 Percent Off

By Katherine Gillespie
Illustration: The Strategist

With temperatures dropping and radiators currently on full blast, a cozy-yet-breathable comforter makes for a far better winter night’s sleep. We don’t think you can go wrong with linen bedding when the weather outside is frightful, but it’s certainly a fiber that tends to come with a high price tag.

Enter this minimalist quilt from West Elm, which is currently 50 percent off in two colorways — neutral flax and a lovely burnt terra-cotta — and also comes with free shipping. Made from a blend of European linen and cotton in an OEKO-TEX Standard 100 factory with high safety and environmental standards, the queen-size quilt is available with two matching shams for $135, or on its own for a very reasonable $100. Upgrade to king size for just a few bucks more. We like that the quilt features contemporary tufting that feels more Scandinavian than cottagecore and that the fill is made from 100 percent recycled polyester. If you prefer the blue or silver colorways, those are almost as deeply discounted; it’s also possible to add on individual shams in contrasting hues for that dreamy layered look.
