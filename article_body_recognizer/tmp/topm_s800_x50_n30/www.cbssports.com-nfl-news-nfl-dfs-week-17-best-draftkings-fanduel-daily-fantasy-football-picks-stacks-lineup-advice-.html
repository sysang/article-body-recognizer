NFL DFS, Week 17: Best DraftKings, FanDuel daily Fantasy football picks, stacks, lineup advice

DFS millionaire Mike McClure shares his top NFL DFS picks for Week 17

By CBS Sports Staff
Dec 31, 2021 at 9:23 am ET 3 min read

With no more byes and no more Thursday Night Football, Sunday's NFL DFS player pool for Week 17 is as stacked as ever. Having so many top players to choose from makes finalizing your NFL DFS picks even more difficult with plenty of tough decisions to make. Add in injured players like Lamar Jackson, Darren Waller and James Conner possibly returning to the field this week and there are plenty of NFL DFS stacks you can targert.

A winning NFL DFS strategy could be one that combines household names with low-cost, high-upside picks like Rex Burkhead and Isaiah McKenzie were in Week 16. Before locking in your Week 17 NFL DFS picks, be sure to check out the NFL DFS advice, strategy and projections from SportsLine daily Fantasy expert Mike McClure.

McClure is a legendary professional DFS player with almost $2 million in career winnings. His methodology has led to enormous cashes on FanDuel and DraftKings. He took down two high-stakes tournaments on FanDuel in 2020 and recorded a pair top-10 finishes in tournaments with over 130,000 entrants on DraftKings. His lineups also finished inside the top 1 percent in more than a dozen other tournaments last season.

He uses a powerful prediction model that simulates every snap of NFL action, taking factors such as matchups, statistical trends and injuries into account. This allows him to find the best DFS values that he shares only with members at SportsLine.

Now, McClure has turned his attention to the Week 17 slate of games and locked in his top daily Fantasy football picks. You can only see them by heading to SportsLine.

Top NFL DFS picks for Week 17

One of McClure's top NFL DFS picks for Week 17 is Chiefs running back Darrel Williams ($5,800 on DraftKings and $6,200 on FanDuel). Starting running back Clyde Edwards-Helaire left last week's game against Pittsburgh after nine carries with a collarbone injury and was unable to return. Reports suggested he could be available to play this week, but with Williams and fellow running back Derrick Gore available, there's no pressure for the Chiefs to rush him back.

Williams started five games earlier this season and finished with 244 yards on 69 carries with three total touchdowns. Those numbers aren't jaw-dropping, but he should be able to make an impact against Cincinnati. Removing last week against a perilously depleted Baltimore team, the Bengals allowed 233 rushing yards in the previous two games to San Francisco and Denver, and the Bengals have allowed the fourth-most receptions to opposing running backs this season.

Part of McClure's optimal NFL DFS strategy includes rostering Buccaneers wide receiver Antonio Brown ($6,100 on DraftKings and $8,500 on FanDuel). With Mike Evans and Chris Godwin out, Brown was the Bucs' featured receiver last week and posted a 10-101 stat line. He was also targeted 15 times by Tom Brady, which is his most since he was a Steeler back in 2018.

Godwin is out for the season, while Evans has a hamstring injury and was placed on the COVID-19/reserve list, so he's unlikely to go on Sunday. Thus, Brown should see a similar number of targets from Brady, who threw just three other passes to all other Bucs' receivers combined in Week 16. If that wasn't enough to sway you in rostering Brown, then the fact that Tampa takes on the Jets' defense should do the trick since New York ranks last in points allowed and yards allowed.
