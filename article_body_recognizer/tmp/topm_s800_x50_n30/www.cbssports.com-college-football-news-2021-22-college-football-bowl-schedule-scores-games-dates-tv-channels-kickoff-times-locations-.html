2021-22 college football bowl schedule, scores, games, dates, TV channels, kickoff times, locations

A detailed look at every college football bowl game set to be played following the 2021 season

By Barrett Sallee
Dec 30, 2021 at 11:33 pm ET 3 min read

The 2021 college football regular season has reached its conclusion, but there's still plenty of action left. Bowl season is right around the corner, culminating in the New Year's Six and College Football Playoff games. The 2021-22 bowl schedule has been announced, and it looks to be a three-and-a-half week extravaganza over the holidays. The postseason will culminate with the College Football Playoff National Championship on Jan. 10, 2022 at Lucas Oil Stadium in Indianapolis.

The CFP semifinals will return to New Year's Eve, with the Cotton Bowl and Orange Bowls serving as this season's locations. No. 1 Alabama will face No. 4 Cincinnati in the Cotton Bowl at 3:30 p.m. ET with No. 2 Michigan vs. No. 3 Georgia in the Orange Bowl following immediately after at 7:30 p.m.

CBS is once again set to broadcast the Tony the Tiger Sun Bowl from El Paso, Texas, on Dec. 31. Kickoff is set for 12:30 p.m. ET.

Below is the entire remaining schedule for bowl games with dates, times and network affiliations. All information regarding bowl games is subject to change. All times are Eastern.
