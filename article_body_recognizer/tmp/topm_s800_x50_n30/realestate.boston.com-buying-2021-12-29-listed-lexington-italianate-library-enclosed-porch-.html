Listed: For $2.95m, Lexington Italianate welcomes with winter comforts

Buying Luxury Lexington

Kelly Garrity -- Boston.com correspondent

December 29, 2021 1:07 pm

A wraparound porch can be the perfect place to enjoy a view, but the cold New England winter tends to drive even the hardiest porch-sitter indoors. At 6 Hayes Ave. in Lexington, inclement weather isn’t an issue. The fully enclosed wraparound porch has wood-paneled walls and a wood bead board ceiling and is lined with multi-muntined windows, letting in lots of light (and not the cold). Just open the French doors and let the heat pour in.

The 6,170-square-foot property hit the market Dec. 10 for $2,950,000. Elizabeth Crampton of Coldwell Banker Realty-Lexington has the listing. “Flooded with warm southwest light, this home is brimming with timeless elegance, quality craftsmanship, and gracious period detail throughout the interior spaces,” the listing reads. 

The Italianate-style home offers five bedrooms, four bathrooms, and one half bathroom. A white porte cochère clad in green trellises leads to a mahogany door.

The door opens into a small foyer, with the sitting room to the right and the living room to the left. Both have wood-burning fireplaces. French doors connect the living room to the porch, where it wraps around the side of the home. The dining room, found toward the back of the home, features white chair rail molding atop white board-and-batten wainscoting. A chandelier hanging from the coffered ceiling adds elegance and light to the room, as does the row of picture windows that look out over the backyard. 

The kitchen glows with all-white cabinets, white marble countertops, a white subway tile backsplash, and white panels on the double-door refrigerator. The sink is built into the island (which also features white marble countertops), and there’s plenty of extra space for a breakfast nook or dining area.

The mudroom and a half bathroom with marble counters and a sleek vessel sink are located down the hall, as is a set of stairs to the unfinished basement. Pillars and crown molding throughout this floor retain the antique character of the home, which was built in 1840. The floors on this level are wood, including in the bathroom. 

The second floor is home to the library, three bedrooms (including the owner suite), and a laundry room. The owner bedroom has two walk-in closets and an en-suite bathroom that offers a vanity with a stone countertop and two sinks, as well as a tiled standalone shower with a built-in bench.

The other two bedrooms come with en-suite bathrooms, and one has a walk-in closet. All three rooms have wood floors. 

The library is located atop the porte cochère and features wood floors, paneled walls, exposed beams, and the same rows of multi-muntined windows as the enclosed porch. 

The home’s final two bedrooms are on the third floor. One has a wood-burning fireplace, a double-door closet, and two window seats – one facing the front yard, the other the back.

The other bedroom also has a window seat flanked by built-in shelves, as well as a walk-in closet and another small private space described on the home’s website as a “Zoom Room.” This bedroom is connected to carpeted, finished attic space, which has a skylight built into the slanted ceiling. 

The third floor is also home to office space and a full bathroom with a combination shower and tub. 

A stone patio in the backyard offers space to sit and enjoy the outdoors when the weather warms up. The home also comes with a detached two-car garage.
