Health Care

Penn State educates future generations of health-care professionals and scientists, conducts biomedical research, and offers a variety of health services and programs for the public as well as its students, faculty, and staff.

Penn State Health

Penn State Health Milton S. Hershey Medical Center offers a wide range of health care services at hospitals and outpatient practice sites across central Pennsylvania and conducts biomedical research in affiliation with Penn State College of Medicine. College of Medicine

The College of Medicine is committed to enhancing the quality of life through improved health, the professional preparation of those who will serve the health needs of others, and the discovery of knowledge that will benefit all. Nese College of Nursing

Through the integrated programs of nursing, education, research, scholarship, and outreach, the Ross and Carol Nese College of Nursing develops qualified nurse leaders at all levels of practice, advances nursing science, and provides care to individuals, families, and communities. Undergraduate Programs Graduate Programs Faculty Center for Nursing Research Center of Geriatric Nursing Excellence

College of Health and Human Development

Dedicated to enriching human well-being and strengthening intellectual, social, physical, economic, and emotional wellness, the College of Health and Human Development provides students with a solid foundation for positively impacting the way people live—both now and far into the future. Departments Graduate Studies Minors Faculty Research Centers
