Is Woody the talking Christmas tree creepy or cute?

Story by CBC Kids News • 2021-12-06 06:00

U.S. talk show host Jimmy Fallon votes creepy, take our poll and tell us

⭐️HERE’S WHAT YOU NEED TO KNOW⭐️

  • After 15 years away, Woody the talking Christmas tree has made a comeback.
  • The robotic tree interacts with shoppers in real time at a mall in Dartmouth, Nova Scotia.
  • With his unique look, Woody has been getting attention all over the world, including on a U.S. talk show.
  • Some find him adorable, others think he’s frightening.
  • What do you think? Keep reading and vote in our poll! ⬇️ ⬇️ ⬇️

A giant talking Christmas tree has returned to a Nova Scotia mall after a 15-year absence — lighting up the internet and capturing the attention of a high-profile U.S. talk show host.

Here’s the question up for debate: Is Woody the talking Christmas tree creepy or cute?

The ﻿﻿15-metre tall robotic tree, which interacts in real time with kids who visit him, was a holiday hallmark for more than 25 years at the Mic Mac Mall in Dartmouth.

The official story is that Woody was retired in 2006 due to maintenance costs, although some people wondered if his look had something to do with his disappearance.

Mall general manager Tamitha Oakley said that isn’t true.

“He did not leave because people thought that he was creepy,” she said.

In fact, a huge crowd was on hand for his unveiling.

Still, when Woody made his triumphant return on Nov. 19, he was sporting a slightly different face.

His fresh look — including rosy cheeks and dimpled smile — sparked reactions on social media.

Some were excited to have Woody back, while others, not so much.

This is the face U.S. talk show host Jimmy Fallon made after showing a clip of Woody the talking Christmas tree on his show on Nov. 23. (Image credit: The Tonight Show Starring Jimmy Fallon/YouTube)

Jimmy Fallon is not a fan

Late-night TV talk show host Jimmy Fallon, who hosts The Tonight Show every weeknight on NBC, seems to have put Woody in the creepy category.

He played a clip of Woody on his show on Nov. 23.

When the clip ended, Fallon looked at the screen with a bewildered expression on his face.

Then he said, “Now we know what the Christmas episode of Squid Game will look like.”

  • Thinking of watching Netflix’s Squid Game? Here’s what you should know

Squid Game is a thriller drama Netflix series rated TV-MA about what happens when children’s games turn deadly.

Some are comparing Woody to the creepy robotic doll in the Netflix thriller Squid Game. (Image credit: Lee Jin-man/The Associated Press) 

The stuff of nightmares

Some people on Twitter blamed Woody for their childhood nightmares.

Others said they aren’t so sure about Woody’s new face, saying it might be even worse than the old one.

Then again, maybe creepy isn’t the right word?

One visitor thought Woody was just plain weird.

Old fans say Woody's a cutie

Not everyone finds Woody unsettling, though.

Oakley said his original fans, many of whom now have kids of their own, “have been the biggest supporters of Woody's return.”

One person said the return of the talking tree sparked tears of joy.

Others said their kids are loving the new and improved Woody.

Hundreds turned out to welcome Woody back to the mall on Nov. 19.

Video of the event shows the crowd chanting “Wake up, Woody!” as the tree opens his eyes for the first time in more than a decade.
