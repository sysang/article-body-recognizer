A Game of Birds and Wolves: The Secret Game That Won the War by Simon Parkin review — the Wrens who beat Germany’s U‑boats

Steven Spielberg has snapped up this tale of a lesser-known battle of wits from the Second World War

Review by Giles Milton
Sunday October 27 2019, 12.01am, The Sunday Times

There has been a deluge of books in recent years about lesser-known missions of the Second World War. The most successful of these was Ben Macintyre’s Operation Mincemeat, with its attention-grabbing subtitle, The True Spy Story That Changed the Course of World War II. Similar books soon followed in its wake: tales of forgotten female agents, eccentric Bletchley boffins and SAS-led adventures in North Africa.

This first history book by Simon Parkin (he writes mostly about video games) sits firmly within this genre and comes with a subtitle that promises much. The game in question was the brainchild of Gilbert Roberts, a veteran naval captain so riddled with tuberculosis that he could no longer put to sea.
