The Times view on New Year’s Eve in the time of Covid-19: Big Night In

The sophisticated option tonight is to stay at home with a few intimates

Thursday December 30 2021, 9.00pm, The Times
People are more likely to celebrate New Year’s Eve with an intimate dinner party this year
GETTY IMAGES

Tonight, according to customer searches recorded by Waitrose, the dinner party with family and friends as opposed to the big bash with strangers is making a comeback. Much has been written about recent parallels with the 1970s, an era of inflation, fuel shortages, and cheesy light entertainment on television. But not all aspects of that much-maligned decade were disastrous.

For starters (prawn cocktail, anyone?) a British woman has won a tennis singles grand slam and Abba are back together. Furthermore, the 1970s were an age that embraced the domestic dinner party and the signs are that the same is happening as 2021 comes to a close. Perhaps Boris Johnson’s one-man campaign to resurrect the cheese and wine party is having some influence.
