Oklahoma doesn’t seem to be taking its breakup with Lincoln Riley well.

It’s just over a week since the former Sooners head coach abruptly left his job for USC and the state took action to rid Riley’s name from a landmark.

Republican Senator Bill Coleman of Ponca City proposed a bill on Tuesday that would rename three inches of a westbound lane of State Highway 325 in the panhandle as the “Lincoln Riley Highway.”

Coleman said in a release he “felt the city of Oklahoma needed an appropriate goodbye” to Riley, “whose sudden departure left many in shock, including a team of young, delisted college players.”

The Senator explained the selection method behind the highway spot.

“I found the tiniest section of our most desolate of highways to pay tribute to Coach Riley’s exit from Oklahoma football and the state,” Coleman said. “This is only fitting as this is the last three inches one sees before leaving our great state heading west.”

Coleman’s made his ire for Riley’s exit clear.

“From all indications, professionalism was pretty much absent in how this departure was executed,” he said.

The bill could become law at a meeting that will take place on Feb. 7, according to the press release.

The news came after some Sooners players took to social media to call out Riley after his departure.

On Nov. 29, the USC football Instagram shared a photo introducing Riley as its new head coach, quoting him as saying, “This is going to be the mecca of college football.” The post caught the attention of Oklahoma players. 

Defensive lineman Isaiah Thomas claimed Riley “told us that last week,” referring to his former players, and Sooners linebacker Nik Bonitto replied to Thomas, “LOL.”

Sunday, Oklahoma hired Clemson defensive coordinator Brent Venables, who was on the Sooners’ staff under Bob Stoops as co-defensive coordinator from 1999 to 2003, and defensive coordinator from 2004 to 2011, as Riley’s replacement.

Venables will be introduced Monday morning on the campus in Norman, the school said.
