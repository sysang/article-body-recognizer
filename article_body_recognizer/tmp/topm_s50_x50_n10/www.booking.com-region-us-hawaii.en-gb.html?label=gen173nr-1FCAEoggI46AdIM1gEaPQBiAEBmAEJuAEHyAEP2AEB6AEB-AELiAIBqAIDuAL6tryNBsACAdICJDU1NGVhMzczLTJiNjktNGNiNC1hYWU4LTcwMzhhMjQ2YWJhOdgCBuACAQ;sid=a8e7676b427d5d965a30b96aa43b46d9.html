Hawaii highlights

  • The Hawai'i Volcanoes National Park

    Explore ancient lava caves and a cluster of volcanos on Big Island that are as much as 60,000 years old.

  • Golf in Hawaii

    Golfers will enjoy the Kohala Coast on Hawaii Island, also known as the Golf Capital of Hawaii.

  • Discover Molokai

    Travel by mule through Molokai's Kalaupapa National Historical Park and enjoy the stunning coastal vistas.

  • Na Pali Coast State Park and snorkelling at Ha’ena State Park

    Discover Ke'e beach with its calm ocean lagoon, then snorkel over stunning coral reefs along the Na Pali coast.

  • Helicopter tour of the Waimea Canyon State Park

    Take a helicopter tour of Waimea Canyon State Park and get a bird's eye view of Hawaii's Grand Canyon.

  • Parker Ranch

    This 160-year-old ranch is home to Spanish and Hawaiian ibex goats, Polynesian boar with 6-inch tusks and other local fauna.

  • Akaka Falls State Park

    Trek through the lush rainforests of Big Island to the 135-metre Akaka Falls, also known as Kahuna Falls.

  • Swim with sea turtles in Honanau Bay

    See the protected honu, or green sea turtles, as they sun themselves on Lanai's north coast.

  • Biking in Hawaii

    Set out and explore the islands, discovering dramatic landscapes like that of Haleakala volcano on Maui.

  • Valley of the Temples Memorial Park

    Home to a replica of an 11th-century Buddhist temple, this park is set in rolling hills near the Koʻolau mountains.

The American Dream

Hawaii is everything you’ve seen in films and more. The USA’s 50th and most exotic state comprises over 19 volcanic islands with diverse landscapes, amazing beaches and rainforest greenery. It’s an exclusive destination, so of all Hawaii hotels, 5 star properties make up a decent proportion.

Home to Pearl Harbour, the capital Honolulu on O’ahu island has many hotels by Waikiki beach, immortalised in song and famous for excellent nightlife and surfing, as anyone familiar with Hawaii-5-0 will know. Northern O’ahu has some of the best winter surfing around.

The nearest island is laidback Molokai and to its south is unspoilt Lanai. East is the island home to most Hawaii hotels – Maui. Hiking to the green peaks of its rugged interior gives a completely different viewpoint. Of course, the beaches are still the main attraction and there is a long line of hotels on the beach on Booking.com, from green Lahaina all the way to beautiful Kapalua. Other fantastic beaches are found at Kihei and neighbouring Wailea.

South-west is Hawai’I (Big Island), dominated by Mauna Loa – one of the world’s largest volcanoes. In its foothills are the joint resorts of Kailua and Kona, while the northern Kohala coast provides spectacular scenery. In the east Hilo curves around a bay and is home to the island’s airport.
