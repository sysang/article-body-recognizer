The Most Popular Bathroom Makeovers of 2021

Gorgeous tiles, custom vanities, chic hardware and clever storage made these the most-read bathroom stories of the year

Bryan Anthony3 days ago
Houzz Editorial Staff; writer, design enthusiast, reader, avid traveler.
More
Print
Embed
Facebook
Twitter
Email
This Trending Now story features the most popular Bathroom of the Week stories shared on Houzz since Jan. 1, 2021.A successful bathroom makeover is the result of numerous decisions about individual design details and features. If you’re considering a bathroom remodel, you can find inspiration for hardworking vanities, stunning shower tile, soothing color palettes and other stylish and functional elements in this countdown of the most-read bathroom makeover stories of 2021 from our Bathroom of the Week series.
Heiser Designs
10. Fresh Style and an Airy LayoutBathroom at a GlanceWho lives here: Mike and Janet BryantLocation: Bellevue, WashingtonSize: 144 square feet (13 square meters)Builder: Alpental ConstructionDesigner: Brittney Heiser of Heiser DesignsAfter 27 years of living with carpet on their primary bathroom floor, these Washington state homeowners had had enough. But carpeting on the floor wasn’t their only complaint. Armed with years of ideas collected from Houzz, the couple hired designer Brittney Heiser and Alpental Construction to create a brighter bathroom with more storage, fresh style and an airy layout. The now spa-like space has a hardworking vanity, freestanding tub, roomy shower, calming color palette and, best of all, no carpet.See more of this bathroom Find a bathroom designer on Houzz
LHK Interiors
9. Breezy and Open With a Navy Blue VanityBathroom at a GlanceWho lives here: A coupleLocation: Modesto, CaliforniaSize: 120 square feet (11 square meters)Designer: Lindsay Kjellberg of LHK InteriorsTired of living with their all-white bathroom for 15 years, this California couple asked designer Lindsay Kjellberg to create a refreshed space that didn’t feel cold and sterile. The designer responded by taking the previous bathroom down to the studs, giving the owners a clean slate from which to add style and increase storage and light. The space now features creamy beige porcelain shower tiles, a wood-look tile floor and a new navy blue vanity with champagne bronze hardware.See more of this bathroomShop for a vanity
Tara Lenney Design
8. Streamlined Layout With a Soothing Spa Feel Bathroom at a GlanceWho lives here: Ken and Nikki Gill and their school-age twinsLocation: DallasSize: 240 square feet (22 square meters)Designer: Tara Lenney DesignThese Dallas homeowners weren’t satisfied with the busy layout of their primary bathroom, which had two single-sink vanities on different walls, a badly positioned water closet and two large closets that took up valuable floor space. They asked designer Tara Lenney to create a more streamlined space that includes a 12½-foot-long custom rift-cut white oak vanity with matte black pulls and a floor-to-ceiling storage tower on one end.See more of this bathroom
Need a pro for your bathroom remodeling project?Let Houzz find the best pros for you
Susan Sutter Interiors
7. ‘Car Wash of Showers’ Inspires a RemodelBathroom of the WeekWho lives here: A couple and their two teenagersLocation: Arlington, VirginiaSize: 180 square feet (17 square meters)Designer: Susan Sutter of Susan Sutter InteriorsBuilder: Denny + GardnerThese Virginia homeowners wanted their en suite bathroom to serve as a retreat from the hustle and bustle of life. Designer Susan Sutter was tasked with creating a clean-lined, moody-yet-calming space with tiled wainscoting, a new freestanding tub and a bright new shower with as many body sprays as possible.See more of this bathroom
Caroline Myers Interior Design
6. Small but Mighty in 60 Square FeetBathroom at a GlanceWho lives here: Nick and Caroline Myers and their two childrenLocation: Oakland, CaliforniaSize: 60 square feet (5.6 square meters)Designer: Caroline Myers Interior DesignFor her own primary bathroom remodel in Oakland, California, designer Caroline Myers wanted to keep the space feeling open and airy with a classic light color scheme that includes various shades of white, different types of marble and shiny polished nickel finishes.Among the notable highlights of the room is the the custom vanity that has six drawers and a cabinet with a pullout inside. Polished nickel knobs add a touch of timeless shine. See more of this bathroom
Thayer Construction LLC
5. New Layout and Clean Look in 52 Square FeetBathroom at a GlanceWho lives here: James Roberts and Sue Theberge, their daughter Olive and their two dogsLocation: Corvallis, OregonSize: 52 square feet (4.8 square meters)Designer: Desia Graybill of atomic * designBuilder: Jared Thayer of Thayer ConstructionThese Oregon homeowners looked to Houzz for bathroom design ideas and then turned to designer Desia Graybill and builder Jared Thayer to create a space that felt contemporary and cool. The refreshed space now includes a slim but efficient double-sink vanity and an alcove for the toilet. White tile walls bounce light around and matte emerald green tile in the shower gives the space a fresh style that nods to the 1966 ranch home’s origins.See more of this bathroom
Ke Design Collective Inc.
4. Big Style in Less Than 43 Square FeetBathroom at a GlanceWho lives here: A coupleLocation: TorontoSize: 42½ square feet (4 square meters); 5 feet by 8½ feetDesigner: Ketty Khuong of Ke Design CollectiveLooking to save this Toronto couple money on their bathroom remodel without sacrificing style, designer Ketty Khuong found a beautiful 36-by-36-inch porcelain tile digitally printed to look like Carrara marble for the shower wall. The custom vanity is another sensible selection. The doors are thermally fused laminate. The wood-based boards are coated with a printed paper soaked in melamine resin. They’re durable and look just like wood. See more of this bathroom
Synergy Design Construction
3. Large Curbless Shower Bathed in SunshineBathroom at a GlanceWho lives here: A professional coupleLocation: Reston, VirginiaSize: 127 square feet (12 square meters)Design: Emily Bickl (layout and cabinet design) and Camille DeLew (interior design) of Synergy Design ConstructionThese Virginia homeowners were more than happy to ditch the shower-tub combo in their main bathroom in favor of a large curbless shower that features textured 12-by-24-inch 3D porcelain tile that covers the wide wall of the shower. Designer Camille DeLew also added a built-in linen storage system with a mix of cabinets, open shelves and drawers. The pulls match those on the new vanity.See more of this bathroom
Rosen Kelly Conway Architecture Design
2. Soft Neutrals With a Nod to HistoryBathroom at a GlanceWho lives here: A family of fiveLocation: Summit, New JerseySize: 120 square feet (11 square meters)Designer: Rosen Kelly Conway Architecture Design (interior design and architecture) Contractor:R. Keller ConstructionThese New Jersey homeowners wanted a bathroom makeover with contemporary updates without losing the historic charm of the original 1920s Colonial Revival home. To blend modern and vintage elements, interior designer Aaron Englishpreserved the original millwork and windows. The light fixtures and polished nickel plumbing fixtures also nod to the home’s era. More contemporary updates include the sleek freestanding tub and the black-framed frosted glass partition that conceals the toilet. See more of this bathroom
Align Design LLC
1. Serene Spa Style in 100 Square FeetBathroom at a GlanceWho lives here: Blake and Lacey Hoyle and their two young sonsLocation: Asheville, North CarolinaSize: 100 square feet (9.3 square meters)Designer: Shawn Merkel of Align DesignThe most-read bathroom story of 2021 features a soothing Scandinavian style with lots of white, wood and light that encourages one to unwind. Designer Shawn Merkel added a new transom window above the concrete-topped vanity to bring in natural light and offer treetop views. Merkel also added a larger picture window in the shower and framed it in light wood. A teak shower stool coordinates with the wood details.See more of this bathroom Tell us: Which bathroom makeover is your favorite?
