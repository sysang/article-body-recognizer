December 22, 2021 | Weekly Brief

Phish kits cut out the hard work for aspiring cyber criminals. A video deep dive on RTF template injection. And sad tidings for yet another crypto platform.

This week on The Threat Hub: Credential theft is a lucrative form of cyber attack, but what if you don’t have the technical know-how to do it yourself? Phish kits have emerged in recent years as an easy solution, allowing low-skilled attackers to launch highly tailored campaigns. Now these kits are gaining in complexity, adding the ability to steal multifactor authentication and OAuth tokens in real time. And with some operators now offering fully outsourced phishing-as-a-service, the barrier to entry for criminals has never been lower. Check out the blog for a rundown on this cheap but dangerous threat.

Following his recent Threat Insight post about RTF template injection attacks, Senior Threat Research Engineer Michael Raggi appeared on the SANS STAR Live Stream to explore this novel technique in detail. You can catch a recording of the interview on the SANS YouTube channel.

And on this week’s Five-Minute Forecast, attackers start to exploit Log4Shell, Anubis banking malware targets Android users, and Threat Researcher Selena Larson talks geofencing, spray-and-pray, and a recent spate of attacks targeting customers of German banks.

About The Threat Research Team

Our threat researchers are responsible for tracking shifts in the cybersecurity landscape, identifying new attacks as they emerge, and monitoring how threat actor tactics, techniques and procedures change over time. The threats they detect and the signatures they write feed into our platforms and are keystones in a system that analyzes more than 2.2 billion emails, 35 billion URLs and 200 million attachments every single day. By studying what cyber criminals are doing now, our threat researchers are better able to anticipate what they’ll do next. Every day, their work keeps our customers protected—not just from today’s attacks, but tomorrow’s threats as they evolve.
