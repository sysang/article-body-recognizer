Scope Mission

Frontiers in Climate publishes rigorously peer-reviewed, cutting-edge research on a broad range of topics that cover the applications of scientific advances in climate research, with a focus on mitigation and adaptation in the Anthropocene (in line with SDGs). In light of the Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change warning of the global challenges facing societies and ecosystems of surviving in a world warmed above pre-industrial levels by 1.5◦C, or beyond, international collaboration across all disciplines is required. 

Our journal’s key sections bring together emissions/mitigation experts, climate modellers, and adaptation experts in one place alongside the latest research into climate policy and economic drivers. Led by an outstanding Editorial Board of international experts, this multidisciplinary open-access journal is at the forefront of disseminating and communicating scientific knowledge and impactful discoveries to researchers, academics and the public worldwide.

We welcome high quality submissions in:

Attribution science, negative emission technologies, greenhouse gas reduction and climate technology, climate modelling and projections, climate risk management, climate services, climate policy and legislation, climate economics, and research advancing climate adaptation, mitigation and resilience strategies and technologies.

Submission

Frontiers in Climate is composed of the following Specialty Sections:

Climate and Decision Making

Climate and Economics

Climate Law and Policy

Climate Risk Management

Climate Services

Climate, Ecology and People

Negative Emission Technologies

Predictions and Projections

The specialty sections of Frontiers in Climate welcome submission of the following article types: Correction, Data Report, Editorial, General Commentary, Original Research, Perspective, Policy and Practice Reviews, Policy Brief, Review, Technology and Code, Case Report, Methods, Opinion, Systematic Review, Community Case Study, Curriculum, Instruction, and Pedagogy, Hypothesis and Theory and Mini Review.

When submitting a manuscript to Frontiers in Climate, authors must submit the material directly to one of the specialty sections. Manuscripts are peer-reviewed by the Associate and Review Editors of the respective specialty section.
