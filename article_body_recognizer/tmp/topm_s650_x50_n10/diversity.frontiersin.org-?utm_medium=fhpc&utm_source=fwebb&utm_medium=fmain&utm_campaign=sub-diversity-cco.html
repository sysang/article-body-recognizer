Frontiers Diversity Equity Statement

The global anti-racism protests have rallied millions to join the long walk towards full inclusivity and equity in society. They have raised a collective voice against social inequalities and injustice in unmistakable terms. They have triggered rigorous debate and a demand for action not only in society at large, but also in science and the institutions associated with science, including in our own organization.

Frontiers stands with our Black colleagues, authors, reviewers, editors and readers, and with all people affected by systemic racism and prejudice. We are listening to their voices and will work as a single community towards the building of an equitable and inclusive society.

Research Topics

GENDER

Sexual and Reproductive Health Among Transgender and Nonbinary Adolescents and Young Adults

This Research Topic aims to highlight emerging research, clinical, educational, and policy approaches to improving sexual and reproductive health (SRH) among transgender and non-binary adolescents and young adults, recognizing the social, economic, and cultural factors that affect access to SRH services. The topic also seeks to support the dissemination of knowledge and experiences from transgender and non-binary adolescents and young adults in low- and middle-income countries.

GENDER

Mind the Backlash: Gender Discrimination and Sexism in Contemporary Societies

Now is a critical time to revisit and broaden our knowledge about gender discrimination, gender equality and sexism. This Research Topic offers a gender perspective on the “backlash" against gender, as observed in various countries around the globe. It presents a gender perspective to illustrate and evaluate the recent illiberal turn in politics in a variety of contexts.

DEVELOPMENTAL PSYCHOLOGY

Positive Youth Development

This collection of articles will bring a truly cross-national perspective on science surrounding the study of adolescence. The vast majority of adolescents live in non-WEIRD (Western, Educated, Industrialized, Rich and Democratic) countries whilst the vast majority of what we know about adolescence comes from studies of teens living in WEIRD countries. The goal of this project is to document how young people make positive and valued contributions to themselves and others.

Participate now

SPORT SCIENCE

Gender inequalities and sport – a changing landscape?

The aim of this Research Topic is to explore Gender inequalities within different sports and across different countries; assess the extent of change, and what further can be done; examine intersecting factors such as ethnicity, disability and sexuality with regards to inequalities and discrimination in sport; and assess initiatives designed to tackle inequality in sport.

Participate now

SCIENCE COMMUNICATION

Inclusive science communication in theory and practice

We seek contributions from practitioners and scholars of science communication that move beyond documenting the barriers to inclusion and diversity that exist within science communication fields, to strategies and solutions that can help increase inclusion, equity, and diversity, and examinations of the impacts when those strategies and solutions are implemented.

Participate now

ORAL HEALTH

Inequities in oral health: from determinants to solutions

Oral diseases are among the most prevalent diseases globally. In this Research Topic, we welcome papers on inequities in oral health that go beyond the description of individual differences between socially relevant groups. We are looking for the role of systemic structural factors, including racism, and social determinants of health on oral health and oral health inequities. »Participate now

WATER QUALITY

Bioremediation of emerging pollutants in aquatic systems

In this Research Topic, we seek manuscripts on the topics of mechanisms and applications of biosorption, bio-uptake, and biotransformation in the in situ removal of emerging contaminants in aquatic systems. Submissions should be focused on the role of in situ bioremediation in attaining SDGs. »Participate now

PUBLIC HEALTH

Racism as a public health crisis

Racial inequality is a persistent force that produces significant disparities in health and health care. Join this opportunity to document the existence of health inequities stemming from structural and institutional racism. »Participate now

Research Journals

SOCIOLOGY

Race and ethnicity

Race and Ethnicity, a new specialty in Frontiers in Sociology, publishes original empirical contributions and analyses on all aspects of race, ethnicity and religion research, across political science, social policy, health, education, labor economics and more. 

Explore

PUBLIC HEALTH

Inequalities in health

Inequalities in Health, a section within Frontiers in Public Health, publishes research at the crossroads of social and biomedical sciences. It covers a wide array of factors driving health inequalities, including social, socio-economic, psychological, psychosocial, gender and economic factors. »Explore

EDUCATION

Language, culture, and diversity

Language, Culture, and Diversity, a new section of Frontiers in Education, publishes studies on practices, techniques, policies, and research on valuing diversity and supporting, developing, and preparing multilingual and multicultural compassionate leaders for a global society. »Explore

HUMAN DYNAMICS

Impacts of global change

Frontiers in Human Dynamics publishes studies on sociological and demographic patterns of resilience and adaptation to our ever-changing societies and environment. This includes population shifts, from displacement of people(s) to inequalities of race, ethnicity, gender and class. »Explore
