Friday, December 17, 2021

The President pro tempore called the Senate to order at 9:30a.m., the the Reverend Lisa Schultz, of the Church of the Advent, in Washington, D.C. offered a prayer, and the President pro tempore led the Senate in reciting the Pledge of Allegiance to the Flag of the United States of America.

The Journal

Pursuant to the order of yesterday, the Journal of the proceedings of the Senate was deemed approved to date.

Certain Procedures Dispensed With

Pursuant to the order of yesterday,the morning hour being deemed expired, and the times for the recognition of the two leaders being reserved.

Transaction of Morning Business

The following transactions of morning business occurred on today.

Legislative Business (Saturday, December 18)

H.R. 1192: A bill to impose requirements on the payment of compensation to professional persons employed in voluntary cases commenced under title III of the Puerto Rico Oversight Management and Economic Stability Act (commonly known as "PROMESA").
-- Passed Senate with an amendment by Unanimous Consent.
H.R. 1664 (Rep. Veasey): A bill to authorize the National Medal of Honor Museum Foundation to establish a commemorative work in the District of Columbia and its environs, and for other purposes.
-- Passed Senate without amendment by Unanimous Consent.
S. 1760 (Sen. Hirono): A bill to designate the community-based outpatientclinic of the Department of Veterans Affairs planned to be built in Oahu, Hawaii, as the "Daniel Kahikina Akaka Department of Veterans Affairs Community-Based Outpatient Clinic".
-- Senate Committee on Veterans' Affairs discharged by Unanimous Consent.
-- Passed Senate without amendment by Unanimous Consent.
S. 2514 (Sen. Lee): A bill to rename the Provo Veterans Center in Orem, Utah, as the "Col. Gail S. Halvorsen 'Candy Bomber' Veterans Center".
-- Passed Senate without amendment by Unanimous Consent.
S. 2551 (Sen. Peters): A bill to require the Director of the Office of Management and Budget to establish or otherwise provide an artificial intelligence training program for the acquisition workforce, and for other purposes.
-- Measure laid before Senate by unanimous consent.
-- Passed Senate with an amendment by Unanimous Consent.
S.Amdt. 4896 (Sen. Peters): In the nature of a substitute.
--Amendment SA 4896 proposed by Senator Schatz for Senator Peters.
--Amendment SA 4896 agreed to in Senate by Unanimous Consent.
S. 3437 (Sen. Durbin): A bill to extend certain COVID-19 bankruptcy relief provisions through March 27, 2022.
-- Introduced in the Senate, read twice, considered, read the third time, and passed without amendment by Voice Vote.
S.Res. 447 (Sen. Stabenow): A resolution celebrating the 125th anniversary of the National Grain and Feed Association and recognizing the Association andits members for transforming the bounty of United States farmers into safe,nutritious, sustainable, and affordable human and animal food.
-- Senate Committee on Agriculture, Nutrition, and Forestry discharged by Unanimous Consent.
-- Resolution agreed to in Senate without amendment and with a preamble by Unanimous Consent.
S.Res. 483 (Sen. Rubio): A resolution remembering the December 6, 2019, terrorist attack at Naval Air Station Pensacola and commemorating those wholost their lives, and those who were injured, in the line of duty.
-- Submitted in the Senate, considered, and agreed to without amendment and with a preamble by Unanimous Consent.
S.Con.Res. 24 (Sen. Reed): A concurrent resolution directing the Secretary ofthe Senate to make corrections in the enrollment of the bill S. 1605.
-- Submitted in the Senate, considered, and agreed to without amendment by Unanimous Consent.
