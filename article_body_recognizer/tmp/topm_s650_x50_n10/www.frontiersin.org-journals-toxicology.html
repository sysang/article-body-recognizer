Scope Mission

Frontiers in Toxicology is an interdisciplinary, open-access journal presenting the latest research on the adverse effects of substances, particles and mixtures on living organisms, from molecular mechanisms to population level effects. The journal publishes rigorously peer-reviewed research, and aims to disseminate scientific knowledge to academic researchers, clinicians, regulatory authorities and industry, providing a platform for exchanges across research areas and between those at the forefront of toxicological research.

The journal will feature research on the advances in risk assessment and regulation, identification of novel endpoints, pathways or mechanisms of toxicity, in silico testing strategies, new or relevant tools and methods for toxicological research as well as review and opinion pieces on state-of-the-art research in toxicological sciences.

Frontiers’ Research Topics are particularly suited to introduce new developments and directions in all aspects of toxicology (www.frontiersin.org/about/RTGuidelines). Frontiers in Toxicology will make use of the unique Frontiers platform for open-access publishing and research networking for scientists, which provides an equal opportunity to seek, share and create knowledge. Articles are peer-reviewed according to the Frontiers review guidelines, which evaluate manuscripts on objective editorial criteria. Please refer to the author guidelines for details on article types and the submission process.

Submission

Frontiers in Toxicology is composed of the following Specialty Sections:

Computational Toxicology and Informatics

Developmental and Reproductive Toxicology

Immunotoxicology

In Vitro Toxicology

Nanotoxicology

Neurotoxicology

Regulatory Toxicology

Toxicogenomics

The specialty sections of Frontiers in Toxicology welcome submission of the following article types: Brief Research Report, Correction, Data Report, Editorial, General Commentary, Hypothesis and Theory, Methods, Mini Review, Opinion, Original Research, Perspective, Policy Brief, Review, Systematic Review and Technology and Code.

When submitting a manuscript to Frontiers in Toxicology, authors must submit the material directly to one of the specialty sections. Manuscripts are peer-reviewed by the Associate and Review Editors of the respective specialty section.
