Scope Mission

Communication systems and networks are becoming the dominant mode of information access and exchange.

Besides allowing developing economies to leap into the information age without necessarily the costly infrastructure of wiring to every home and office, these technologies are also offering people in more developed economies more freedom and flexibility. These technologies also connect everyday objects (household appliances, cars, thermostats, etc.) to the Internet through integrated devices, enabling seamless communications between people, processes, and devices without requiring human-to-human or human-to-computer interaction. Thanks to the combination of data analytics, low-cost computing, and the cloud, with communication and networking technologies, physical objects can share and collect data with minimal human intervention. This leads to a hyper-connected world in which the physical world meets and collaborates with the digital world.

In this context, the objective of Frontiers in Communications and Networks is to publish original, high-level, and timely scientific articles in telecommunication across wireless and wired domains. Papers submitted by researchers from around the world are rigorously reviewed by leading and active international researchers. The journal capitalizes on the distinctive Frontiers digital platform for collaborative review to provide better and fair opportunities to receive and offer information. The ultimate goal is to create new knowledge and improve the quality and readability of the open-access published papers. The journal aims to cover all the critical branches of contemporary telecommunications, ranging from the physical layer to cross-layer and networking design, performance evaluation, and validation. The journal not only welcomes submissions with theoretical contributions but also encourages those that are focusing on applications and experimental demonstrations. As a result, the journal offers a forum for interchanging recent research findings between scientists not only from academia but also from industry. Submissions in all formats/styles, including full research transactions (original reserach/ technology and code), brief research report, perspective/vision papers, tutorials, surveys/reviews, mini riviews, and replies, and comments, are welcomed.

Focal areas of the journal include but are not limited to communication, coding and information theory, signal processing for communication, wireless communications, wireless, wired, and ad-hoc networks, internet of things, sensor networks, data science for communication, secure communications, smart grid communications, optical communications and networks, aerial and space networks, non-conventional (nano, molecular, underwater, underground, etc.) communications and networks, and design and demonstration of system and test-bed. Frontiers in Communications and Networks is a member of the Committee on Publication Ethics.

Submission

Frontiers in Communications and Networks is composed of the following Specialty Sections:

Aerial and Space Networks

Communications Theory

Data Science for Communications

IoT and Sensor Networks

Networks

Non-Conventional Communications and Networks

Optical Communications and Networks

Security, Privacy and Authentication

Smart Grid Communications

System and Test-Bed Design

Wireless Communications

The specialty sections of Frontiers in Communications and Networks welcome submission of the following article types: Brief Research Report, Correction, Data Report, Editorial, General Commentary, Hypothesis and Theory, Methods, Mini Review, Opinion, Original Research, Perspective, Review, Technology and Code, Addendum, Case Report, Classification, Clinical Study Protocol, Clinical Trial, Code, Community Case Study, Conceptual Analysis, Conference Brief Paper, Conference Comments Paper, Conference Full Paper, Core Concept, CPC, Curriculum, Instruction, and Pedagogy, Empirical Study, Erratum, Evaluation, Focused Review, Frontiers Commentary, Highlights, Images in Medicine, Letter to the Editor, Medical Book Review, Medical Case Report, Mini Reviews in Medicine, New Discovery, Original Research in Medicine, Perspectives in Medicine, Policy and Practice Reviews, Policy Brief, Protocols, Registered Report, Research Snapshot, Reviews in Medicine, Study Protocol, Systematic Review and Technology Report.

When submitting a manuscript to Frontiers in Communications and Networks, authors must submit the material directly to one of the specialty sections. Manuscripts are peer-reviewed by the Associate and Review Editors of the respective specialty section.
