Frequently Asked Questions

Can I present my session remotely?

Possibly. All division programs will be in-person and not offered virtually. We will consider division proposals that have up to 25% of the speakers participating remotely. The remaining 75% of the program’s presenters must be present in Minneapolis. 

Due to space and technology limitations, we may not be able to accommodate all sessions with virtual presenters. If we are unable to accommodate your session’s request, you would be given the opportunity to replace the virtual speaker(s) or withdraw your proposal. 

Note: This does not apply to CE Workshops. All CE Workshop presenters must be onsite. 

Can I withdraw my proposal or decline my invitation if I cannot attend?

Yes. We understand that circumstances change, and you may no longer be able to participate in APA 2022. If you would like to withdraw your proposal or decline your invitation, please email convention@apa.org. 

What COVID safety protocols will be in place at APA 2022?

The safety of our speakers, attendees, and employees remains our highest priority and we are in the process of developing our comprehensive safety plan. We are committed to following the health and safety measures in accordance with in-person event guidance provided by the U.S. Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC), the State of Minnesota, and City of Minneapolis. Details about our plan will be shared on this website and via email when available.

Who can answer my other questions?

Division Proposals: Contact the relevant Division Program Chairperson or email convention@apa.org. 

CE Workshop Proposals: Email ekcarney@apa.org.

All Other Questions: Email convention@apa.org. 

How to Submit a Proposal 

All proposals must be submitted online. 

Division Programs

Continuing Education Workshops

Start a Proposal

A MyAPA login account and password are required to access these submissions systems. On-screen instructions will guide you through logging in using your existing MyAPA account, looking up your account and password, or creating an account, if necessary. Note: You do not need to be an APA member to create a MyAPA login.

Once logged in, you can start a new proposal. Online instructions are provided to guide you through the process.

  • For Division Programs, select “Individual Presentation” for posters and “Session Proposal” for all other program types.
  • For CE Workshops, select “Workshop Proposal”.

The proposal does not have to be completed in one sitting — you may start, save, and return to it later.

Remember to save your work as you go. By design, if you stay on the proposal screen for more than 30 minutes without interacting with the web server, the system will time out and all unsaved work will be lost.

Review your proposal thoroughly before final submission. When you have successfully submitted your proposal, a screen will appear acknowledging your submission and providing you with a proposal ID. Take a screenshot or print that screen and save the proposal ID for future reference. If you do not receive a proposal ID at the end of your submission, please retry.

Only Internet Explorer 7.0+, Firefox 3.0+, Safari 3.0+, and Chrome 12.0+ are supported.
