FILM REVIEW

The Electrical Life of Louis Wain review — meows of disapproval for biopic of a naff cat artist

Kevin Maher
Friday December 31 2021, 12.01am, The Times
Kevin Maher
Friday December 31 2021, 12.01am, The Times

★★☆☆☆A busy visual style and a bloody-minded commitment to one-note performances are the most notable elements of this misfired biopic about the Victorian artist and illustrator Louis Wain. Benedict Cumberbatch follows his classy cowboy turn in The Power of the Dog with a maniacal lurch into twitchy, jerky mannerisms, playing the apparently neurodiverse Wain as a nightmarish characterisation of Sherlock Holmes meets Forrest Gump on crystal meth.

This might have been fine, even appropriate, had the film, co-written and directed by Will Sharpe (of TV’s Landscapers) had anything original to say about Wain’s life other than the “world is full of beauty” and it was Wain’s mission to capture that beauty to honour the memory of his dearly and prematurely departed wife Emily
