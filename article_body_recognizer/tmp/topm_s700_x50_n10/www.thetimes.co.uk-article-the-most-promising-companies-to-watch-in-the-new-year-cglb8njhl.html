The most promising companies to watch in the new year

After another volatile year on the stock markets, Emma Powell looks at the thriving businesses which could have plenty to offer for investors

Friday December 31 2021, 12.01am, The Times
Friday December 31 2021, 12.01am, The Times

Associated British Foods It’s not just Primark’s clothing that is going cheap — shares in its parent company Associated British Foods are still valued near their pandemic trough at only 14 times forecast earnings for 2022.

But that rating doesn’t give enough credit to clearer plans to return surplus cash to shareholders, which kicked off with a 13.8p special dividend being announced in November, as well as plans to roll out more Primark stores overseas. The latter could accelerate sales growth for the budget clothing retailer. Group pre-tax profits are forecast by analysts to more than double to £1.47 billion over the 12 months to the end of September, which if achieved would be ahead of the pre-pandemic level of £1.17 billion.
