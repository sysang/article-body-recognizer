How Does Wireless Internet for Home Work?

Once you bring the device home, it functions just like a typical router, but with even easier set-up. Just plug it in and activate your service, and, in minutes, you’ll have a secure, private internet connection for your home that can support up to 64 devices.

Does Wireless Internet Use 5G or 4G LTE for Home Internet?

Wireless home internet is unique because it uses the strongest signal from nearby cell phone towers. If you live in a more rural area where 4G LTE is a widely available, you’ll be covered with a high-speed 4G home internet connection. 4G LTE is great for rural areas because cell towers are often available where a wired connection (like fiber internet) is not. And it’s faster than DSL internet, too.

For now, 5G home internet isn’t available in many places. But as it expands, you’ll be the first to know when you can use 5G internet service.

What’s the Difference Between Wireless Internet and Fiber Internet?

Both types of high-speed internet will get you online, but they serve different purposes. Wireless home internet is a new type of internet connection, with a focus on getting reliable high-speed internet to areas where wired fiber internet (or other high-speed internet options) isn’t available.

If you’re in an area where fiber internet is available, that will always be the best option. But if you’re currently stuck with spotty cable, DSL, or satellite internet, wireless internet is likely a much better choice. It’s more affordable and more reliable than satellite and faster than both satellite and DSL.
