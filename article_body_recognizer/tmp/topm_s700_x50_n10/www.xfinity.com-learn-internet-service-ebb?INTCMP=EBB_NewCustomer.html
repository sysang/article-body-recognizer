Affordable Connectivity Program

You may be eligible for a monthly credit toward your Internet service

As part of our ongoing commitment to keeping you connected, Xfinity is proud to participate in the Affordable Connectivity Program (ACP), which provides eligible households a credit of up to $30/month toward Internet service. We're making it simple for both new and existing customers to apply this credit toward Xfinity Internet services, including Internet Essentials.

What is the Affordable Connectivity Program?

The Affordable Connectivity Program (ACP) is a longer-term replacement for the Emergency Broadband Benefit, designed to help keep families connected to the Internet. Qualified households can receive a monthly credit of up to $30/month (up to $75/month for customers in Tribal lands) toward their Internet service.

Frequently asked questions

The federal Affordable Connectivity Program (ACP) is a longer-term replacement for the federal Emergency Broadband Benefit (EBB). ACP is designed to help lower-income households connect to the Internet and stay connected. The program provides eligible customers of participating broadband providers up to a $30/month ($75/month for Tribal lands) credit toward Internet service for the duration of the program. It will last for as long as the Federal Government funds the Program, which is expected to be several years. The amount of the credit depends on the rate for the Internet service selected.
Customers who enrolled in EBB before December 31, 2021 will continue to receive their EBB credit of up to $50 a month (up to $75 for customers on Tribal Lands) for a 60-day transition, until March 1, 2022. After that, the credit will transition from EBB to ACP with a monthly bill credit toward Internet service of up to $30 (up to $75 for customers on Tribal lands), as long as customers continue to meet the new program’s eligibility requirements. Customers enrolled in EBB will be contacted before the end of February with specific details on transitioning to ACP.

Internet Essentials customers automatically qualify for ACP but still must enroll in ACP. If you are not an Internet Essentials customer, you must confirm eligibility through the National Verifier. To qualify, a member of your household must meet at least one of the following criteria:

  • Qualifies for the federal Lifeline program, including:
  • Medicaid
  • SNAP
  • Supplemental Security Income (SSI)
  • Federal Public Housing Assistance
  • Veterans and Survivors Pension Benefit
  • Tribal Programs for Residents for Qualifying Tribal Lands
  • Receives benefits under the free and reduced-price school lunch program or the school breakfast program, including through the USDA Community Eligibility Provision
  • Has an income that is at or below 200% of the federal poverty guidelines
  • Received a federal Pell Grant in the current award year

In order to confirm your eligibility for ACP, we will have to share certain qualifying information about you with the Federal Government.

The credit will last for as long as the Federal Government provides funding for the Program and is expected to last several years. Enrolled customers will be notified at least 30 days before the program ends and the credit is removed.

ACP applies to monthly charges for broadband Internet service. Qualified households will receive a credit of up to $30/month toward Xfinity Internet service, including Internet Essentials. Qualified households within Tribal lands will receive up to $75/month. The amount of the credit depends on the rate for the Internet service that you choose.
The ACP credit cannot be used for unlimited data, data overage charges, taxes and fees, or any non-Internet Xfinity products or equipment, including Xfinity Mobile and Xfinity Voice. Customers who receive Internet service provided to them by their residential building through Xfinity Communities or Xfinity On Campus and do not have an individual Xfinity account with upgraded Internet services may not enroll in ACP through Xfinity. If your account is in voluntary seasonal suspend status, you must take your account out of seasonal suspend status to enroll in ACP. If your Internet Essentials service is provided through an organization or school sponsored program, you cannot currently enroll in ACP through Xfinity. To enroll in ACP, you would have to cancel your service you receive under the sponsored program, sign up for Xfinity Internet or Internet Essentials, and apply for ACP.
The ACP credit of up to $30/month ($75/month in Tribal lands) can only be applied to the Internet service portion of your bundled Xfinity services. You are still responsible in full for any portion of your bill not covered by ACP.
The ACP credit will never be higher than the total balance for your Xfinity Internet service and leased Xfinity Internet equipment.
