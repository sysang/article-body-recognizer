The best luxury Christmas gifts for 2021, from designer scarves to men's aftershave

From festive hampers to caviar and cashmere, these are the best present ideas to thrill your friends and family this year

By Telegraph Reporters 6 December 2021 • 12:01pm

Hey, Big Spender. Christmas is getting closer by the second and it's time to make sure you've got enough indulgent gifts to place under your tree - and what with all the dinner parties and cocktail soirées you'll be hosting, you may as well treat yourself while you're at it. (Exquisitely-crafted knives, tins of caviar and an elegant spritz of perfume will significantly up the ante). 

What follows are the very most lavish, luxurious Christmas gifts in existence, for fashion lovers, sybaritic homebodies and social kingpins. 

One piece of advice: don't hold back this festive season.

Luxury Christmas gifts for women

1. JW Anderson Wool loafers

£430, Matchesfashion.com

Slip on these wool loafers and swan about the house in style. This pair, in a delightfully festive shade of emerald, are just as good for kicking around the manor as heading out to the farm shop for a pint of milk. 

2. Tom Ford Lost Cherry Eau de Parfum

£343 for 100ml; johnlewis.com

It's not just the bottle that packs a punch. This perfume carries top notes of cherry and bitter almond, with a base scent of cedar, vetiver and sandalwood. Christmas has never smelt better.

3. Loro Piana cashmere jumper

£1,580; net-a-porter.com

Crafted in tonal brown cashmere by Loro Piana's artisans, this thick polo neck will bring warmth to winters days. Layer it over a silk shirt and pair with the matching LP skirt. 

4. Louis Vuitton cashmere scarf

£645; uk.louisvuitton.com

Crafted from pure cashmere, Vuitton's Reykjavik scarf will bat off Icelandic breezes and provide an extra soft buffer around your neck. 

5. The Elder Statesman cashmere and silk blanket

£2,950; brownsfashion.com

Cuddle up with a hot cocoa in this cashmere and silk blanket, handmade in sunny California. The teddy texture is also said to increase emotional wellbeing and boost confidence. 

Luxury Christmas gifts for men

1. Christian Dior silk scarf

£600; dior.com

Crafted from blue silk and showcasing the iconic Dior Oblique motif, this scarf is ideal for any gentlemen heading back into the office after New Year. 

2. Prada shearling boots

£1,020; prada.com

Warm feet, a fire and a bottle of red wine - what could be more festive than that? Keep your toes on trend and happy with Prada shearling boots that are built to withstand even the chilliest British houses...

3. New and Lingwood embroidered dressing gown

£2,750; newandlingwood.com

Who wants to get dressed on those wonderfully lazy days between Christmas and New Year? Instead, waft around the house in this embroidered number made from wool and silk.

4. Cartier Juste un Clou cufflinks

£9,600; cartier.com

These 18-carat white gold cufflinks with 19 diamonds will add sparkle to any outfit – from New Year's Eve and beyond.

5. Maison Francis Kurkdjian Amyris homme extrait de parfum

£245 for 70ml; selfridges.com

Featuring amyris from Jamaica and iris from Florence, this woody, musky scent will evoke memories of heady summer holidays – even during a very chilly, very British Christmas.

Christmas gifts for food lovers

1. Fortnum Mason Beluga 000 caviar

£850 for 125g; fortnumandmason.com

Ideal for indulgent pre-lunch snacks, this generous pot of Beluga straight from the shores of Russia will delight nearly every food-lover you know. Blinis at the ready!

2. Da Vittorio x Annabel's food and drink hamper

£495; annabels.co.uk

Three Michelin starred restaurant Da Vittorio has paired up with legendary Mayfair club Annabel's to create this hamper, brimming with luxurious treats from a bottle of Matteo's Barolo to porcini mushrooms and a decadent panettone. 'Tis the season to feast, after all. 

3. Claridge's Christmas pudding

From £45; shop.claridges.co.uk

Even the most talented chefs can struggle to make a good Christmas pudding – this year, indulge with one of the best in Britain, straight from the kitchens of Claridge's hotel and boxed up in the prettiest packaging.

4. Blenheim Forge knife set of four

£1,180; blenheimforge.co.uk

These classic knives will update any kitchen with their beautiful handstitched leather roll made by local leatherworker Harry Owen. Designed to cover all bases in the kitchen – from fiddly off-board work to vegetable chopping and slicing meat and fish – they are the perfect gift for newlyweds and veteran chefs alike. 

5. Manuka honey UMF 28+

£950 for 250g; fineandwild.com

The apex of the honey-making world, this Manuka honey is carefully matured in temperature-controlled facilities in New Zealand before being individually packed into sustainable jars to preserve all the natural enzymes. Anyone for toast?

Luxury drink gifts

1. Gordon MacPhail Private Collection Showcase

£100,000; register interest at gordonandmacphail.com

Hidden beneath the Gordon MacPhail archives are casks from specific Scottish distilleries that they consider to be particularly outstanding - when individually selected and bottled at natural strength they represent the perfect gift for any lovers of the amber liquid. 

2. Jing rare tea selection

£1,500 for three rare teas; jingtea.com

So often at Christmas people give alcohol as gifts, forgetting about the wonderful selection of teas on the market. Jing Rare Tea brings together the most delicious and sought-after teas in the world in one delightful package. 

3. Lady Gaga Limited Edition Dom Pérignon Rosé Champagne 2006 

£380 for 75cl; harrods.com

Lady Gaga surely knows a thing or two about champagne. Hence her being the perfect person to put her name to a very celebratory limited edition Dom Perignon Rosé. 

4. Hermitage Paradis 1890 Grande Champagne Cognac

£5,820 for 75cl; brandyclassics.com

This remarkable cognac has spent more than 80 years in its oak cask slowly developing rich aromas and complex flavours. Hints of marmalade, honey and much more await the lucky recipient of this gift. 

5. Cambridge Distillery Anty Gin

£220 for 700ml; cambridgedistillery.co.uk

Martini, anyone? Each botanical in this ground-breaking gin is distilled individually to preserve its purity, then blended with the essence of 62 red wood ants. Yes, you read that right. The result is a flavour-filled alcohol that everyone - ants aside - will love.

Luxury home gifts 

1. Versace x Venini Gessato Murano-glass vase

£2,550; versace.com

Recreate the feeling of Venice at Christmas with this Gessato vase, which has been hand blown using coloured Murano glass threads. The golden Medusa accents add high fashion fun to the piece.

2. Molteni C Gio Ponti D.754.1 rug

£4,670; gomodern.co.uk

A great rug updates any room so buy this thick pony-skin design for any newlywed or newly-moved friends – or anyone hoping to add a touch of modern deign and colour to their house. 

3. Colville arrow cotton-jacquard cushion

£550; selfridges.com

Know any fashion lovers re-doing their apartment? Add a splash of Christmas colour to their presents with this Italian-made pure cotton cushion from Colville.

4. Margit Wittig silhouette table lamp

From £1,738; margitwittig.com

This playful, pleasing contemporary table lamp is made from multiple handcrafted resin spheres and was sculpted by Margit Wittig herself, and cast in her London studio

5. Goossens Paris Foliage trinket bowl in brass, natural crystal rock and 24-carat gold

£1,242; theinvisiblecollection.com

Hand-made over a course of four to six weeks and soaked in a bath of 24-carat yellow gold and decorated with foliage, this not-for-cereal bowl is available in either a matt or shiny gold finish – and will brighten up any display cabinet. 

Luxury Christmas hampers

1. Melrose and Morgan's 12 Days of Christmas Hamper 

£595; melroseandmorgan.com

Offering a mix of the very best the UK and Europe has to offer for all things edible, this gourmet hamper brings all the most festive ingredients together in one mouthwatering combination.

2. Brindisa Spanish Christmas feasting hamper

£175; brindisa.com

Recreate the taste of Barcelona in Battersea with the Brindisa hamper – filled with all the cured meats you can imagine plus lots of red wine, truffle crisps and cheese, it sorts out a week of Christmas snacks...

3. Angela Hartnett's foodie hamper

£220; cafemurano.co.uk

Chef Angela Hartnett’s restaurant Murano serves some of the best Italian fare in London. Bring a bit of her magic into your own home or someone else's with her favourite balsamic vinegar, pear mustard, grissini breadsticks and homemade pasta. La Dolce Vita, indeed. 

4. Royal Collection luxury Christmas hamper

£200; royalcollectionshop.co.uk

The most traditional of all the hampers – and the most sugary – this is the ideal gift for anyone who loves all the yuletide classics, and includes every type of biscuit you could wish for, Christmas pudding, strawberry and apple sweets, salted caramels and much more. Ideal for Boxing Day feasting. 

5. Ultimate Aegle Hamper 

£100; meetaegle.com

This hamper contains all the delicious extras you might not buy yourself, but which will bring you so much pleasure over a year of cooking. With the very best pots of mustard, honey, cashew nuts, tea, truffles and more, it is designed to make cooking that bit more fun.

Some gifts should be luxurious, but perhaps not all. For further inspiration of a more affordable kind, read The Telegraph’s guides to the best Christmas gifts, gifts for him, gifts for her and letterbox gifts.
