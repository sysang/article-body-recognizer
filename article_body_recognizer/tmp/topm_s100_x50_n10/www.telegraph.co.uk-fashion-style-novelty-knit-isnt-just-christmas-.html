The best novelty knits to wear for Christmas Jumper Day - and beyond

Forget flashing lights and scratchy fabrics; an altogether classier category of festive jumper will see you into January, too

By Bethan Holt, Fashion news and features director 6 December 2021 • 10:12am

’Tis the season to wear a Christmas jumper – the shops are crammed with bargain ‘Ho Ho Ho’ designs, some complete with flashing red Rudolph noses, most of them made from scratchy polyester and destined to languish at the back of your wardrobe come 1 January.

So, this year, I’m making the case for a novelty knit – a more luxurious, timeless cousin of the Christmas jumper. The right novelty knit will convey festive cheer in the coming weeks, but, more importantly, can carry on making you happy in the dark and dreary first months of the new year, too.

It comes in jolly colours with an uplifting pattern, but there’s not a light-up Santa Claus in sight. There’s a certain chicness to these jumpers, so if you’re not sure who in the office is getting involved in Christmas Jumper Day (Save the Children’s annual event, happening on 10 December this year), then you can hedge your bets with a novelty knit and blend in either way.

Bethan wears a recycled hand-knit jumper, £845, Joseph; maternity jeans, £200, 7 For All Mankind; boots and shirt, Bethan’s own

Here, I’m wearing the ultimate-indulgence version of the novelty knit from Joseph’s The Waste Project collection, which has been hand-knitted using 20 different yarns that would otherwise have been thrown away. 

Emblazoned with kooky hearts (perfectly placed for my baby bump) and with tactile fringing, it’s the sort of thing you’d dream of a particularly talented granny knitting for you, but the muted palette means it doesn’t veer into ‘turkey curry at Bridget Jones’s mum’s house’ territory. Expensive, yes, but a true fashion heirloom.

There are so many wonderful small luxury knitwear labels around now who are doing great things with jumpers, and if you’re going to invest anywhere in your wardrobe, this is one category guaranteed never to go out of fashion.

Inacio Ribeiro has revived the brand he began in the 1990s with his wife, Suzanne Clements, as a knitwear capsule with a gloriously bright palette – think magenta and clementine cardigans, and cyan and yellow co-ords, as well as oatmeal, grey, cream and black hoodies. 

Better sweaters, from left to right: Princess Diana in Warm Wonderful; influencer Alba Garavito Torre in Lollys Laundry; fashion editor Annette Weber in Tory Burch; and model Marlies-Pia Pfeifhofer wears Mother Denim

This zesty striped knit is one of the very few things that meets on the Venn diagram of ‘suitable for a festive drinks soirée’ and ‘capable of getting you out of a funk on a dark January morning’ – and many of the pieces are made at Chanel’s Barrie knitwear factory in Hawick, Scotland.

Alex Gore Browne is another designer whose knits are firmly in the elegant but happy-making camp – bows, smiley faces and heart motifs are among the delicate embroideries she offers, with customisable options available, which would make great gifts, whether for someone else or yourself.

Several high-street stores are offering more sustainable takes on the festive knit. MS is a standout with a great range of long-lasting statement pieces, from multicoloured ‘Fabulous!’ slogans to cheering Fair Isles and starry cashmere crew necks. With jumpers like this, the novelty will never wear off.
