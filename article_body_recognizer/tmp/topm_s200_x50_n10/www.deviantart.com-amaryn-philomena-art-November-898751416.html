Literature Text

Fallen petals are nature’s confetti

The wilted remnants of summer celebration

Stiff stems blackened by the sun

Are the used wicks of fireworks

The colors that blazed and inspired awe

Have long faded now

As the nights grow longer

In Praise of Penguins

Praising prideful penguins Prizing perfect plumes Professionally plunging To their icy doom Risking being ripped By lion seals’ deadly jaws Latching onto shining fish With eager beaks and claws As mates await anxiously For their victorious return And slumbering, sweet, downy chicks Nestle under fathers firm As humans, watching, yearn To close them in a reserve To give the penguin mothers rest Humans think that they deserve Ignoring the nobility Beating in the penguin’s chest Thinking, always, eternally That they alone know best.
