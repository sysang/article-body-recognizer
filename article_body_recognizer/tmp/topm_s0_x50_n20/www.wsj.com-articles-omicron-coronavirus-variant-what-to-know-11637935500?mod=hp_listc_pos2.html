Omicron, the New Covid-19 Variant: What to Know

Scientists are racing to determine how readily this variant, also known as B.1.1.529, can sidestep vaccines, treatments and past infections

The Omicron variant has been identified in more than 30 countries so far. Matt Dunham/Associated Press

Since its discovery was announced at the end of November, Omicron has rattled markets, closed borders and sent scientists hunting for how it differs from other variants. They want to know how fast it spreads and whether it can sidestep vaccines, treatments and past infection more readily than the variants that came before it. Omicron has now been identified in more than 30 countries, including the U.S. The World Health Organization has declared the variant, which is also known as B.1.1.529, a variant of concern, indicating that it carries higher risks than other coronavirus strains. Here is what we know so far.

Where has Omicron been found in the U.S.?

Federal regulators on Sunday said cases have been identified in 16 states. Cases span the country from Hawaii to California and New York.
