Honoring Academic Integrity

Chegg is committed to students and their learning outcomes. We believe academic integrity is a fundamental part of the learning process and we work to preserve it.

Our services are designed to support learning, not replace it. Our chat-based learning tutors and subject matter experts work hard to supplement traditional instruction with a number of different tools delivered on demand, 24/7 when students need them. We offer step by step explanations, chat-based tutoring support, and learning support tools like flashcards, practice tests, writing and citation tools and many other services, including textbook rentals, to support students wherever and whenever they need it.

Our products and services should never be used by you for any sort of cheating or fraud – like asking for answers to an active test or exam, or copying answers found online and submitting them as your own. These actions defeat the purpose of learning and are not fair to anyone.

We expect Chegg students to use our services only to help them learn and understand. We don’t tolerate abuse of our platform or services. Dishonest behavior can damage a student’s reputation and record. It’s also unfair to other students, and it makes it difficult for educators to assess student progress.

Misuse of Chegg’s services can have serious consequences, including without limitation being banned from our platforms or having an investigation opened by your institution, which investigation could lead to possible consequences including, without limitation, academic action, expulsion, civil or criminal liability.

If it’s not clear, please ask! An open and honest conversation can save time, energy and heartache about appropriate use. For example, one common instruction is “open book” – but while some use that to mean all outside materials are fair game, others very much mean only the assigned textbook. Get clear - it’s better to have a clear conversation than have a miscommunication that ends up in an academic integrity board hearing.

If we learn that Chegg services have been misused, we may take any action necessary to maintain the integrity of our services and our community. This may be simply removing offending materials; it may also mean terminating the accounts of users involved with misusing our platform, or helping an institution determine the nature of the misuse and the identities of those involved in committing such fraud. For further information, please look at the SAT Frequently Asked Questions page.

Honoring Intellectual Property Rights

We respect the intellectual property rights of others, and we expect our users to do the same. You should only upload content to our website that is yours or that you’re otherwise authorized to post. In keeping with the Digital Millennium Copyright Act (DMCA) and other applicable copyright laws in other jurisdictions in which we offer our services, we will remove content that’s identified to us by the copyright owner through a valid notice of copyright infringement. We will also terminate the accounts of users who repeatedly engage in copyright infringement. For more information, please look at our Copyright Policy.

If you are a copyright owner and have a good faith belief that your copyrighted material has been uploaded to our website, we will remove the material upon submission of the information and sworn statements set out in the DMCA.

Investigating Academic Integrity

While the first step should be a conversation between instructor and student, if you are the instructor and cannot resolve your concerns directly, please use the appropriate form below to request information. While misuse of our platform represents a very small portion of the activity on our platform, we understand how frustrating it can be. We also know how disappointing it is to confront, or be accused of, academic dishonesty. We are constantly working to improve our abilities to detect and respond to issues around both copyright and academic integrity. We take both of these situations very seriously, and we will respond as quickly as possible.

Open an Honor Code Investigation

If you are an instructor, you can request an honor code investigation to access information on what accounts posted or accessed specific questions and solutions. To initiate an honor code investigation and request our assistance in identifying the nature of misuse or any information about users, you must be working with the office of the dean or the body in charge of handling matters of academic integrity at your university or institution. You will need to submit a signed copy of your request on official institution letterhead. You agree that your institution requires the personal information requested for the purposes of (i) investigating a breach of an agreement or a contravention of the laws of Canada or a province that has been, if your institution is located in Canada, is being or is about to be committed and the institution is concerned that if Chegg were to seek consent of the individual(s) involved, this would compromise the institution’s investigation and/or (ii) detecting or suppressing fraud or of preventing fraud that is likely to be committed and the institution is concerned that if Chegg were to seek consent of the individual(s) involved, this would compromise the institution’s ability to prevent, detect or suppress the fraud. This step cannot be waived or circumvented, as it is part of Chegg’s commitment to student privacy.

Honor Shield™ Proctoring Tool Terms

The Chegg.com Terms of Use and these Additional Terms apply to the Honor Shield™ Proctoring Support Tool. To the extent there is a conflict between these Additional Terms and the Chegg.com Terms of Use, the Additional Terms will control. These terms apply to you if you are an instructor; the use of the Tool is restricted to use by instructors at higher education institutions in the U.S. with verified academic credentials.

Purpose of the Honor Shield™ Proctoring Support Tool (“Tool”)

Instructors (“you” or “your”) can use the Tool to confidentially upload exam content (“Exam Content”) at least 48 hours prior to an examination (“Exam”); provided that you allow for up to five (5) days prior to your first Exam for first time account set up and validation of Instructor credentials.

The purpose of the Tool is deter cheating on Exams. The Tool prevents your Exam questions from being answered by Chegg Experts for the duration of the Exam (“Exam Timeframe”).

Chegg will not post your uploaded Exam Content or use it for any purpose other than as described in these terms. Chegg may respond to your inquiries regarding your Exam with metrics related to your Exam. Further, Chegg may use aggregated anonymized data related to the usage of the Tool to provide feedback and to improve the Tool. Chegg deletes your uploaded Exam Content on a monthly basis.

There is no charge to use the Tool. Chegg may modify the Tool, including to add features or improve existing features, at any time without notice.

Your Content

Exam Content that you upload to the Tool is and remains your content. Chegg does not claim any intellectual property rights over the materials you upload to the Tool. By uploading your content to the Tool, you agree that Chegg may review your Exam Content for any reason, including for compliance with our prohibited content requirements, but Chegg has no obligation to review anything that you upload. You agree that you are uploading your Exam Content to the Tool at your direction and Chegg does not in any way certify, provide approval or permission prior to you uploading your Exam Content

Limitations of the Tool

The Tool is not perfect; Chegg is still working out the kinks. Please be patient. It is Chegg’s intention to iterate on and improve the Tool and we hope to provide increased accuracy and functionality over time

Here are some of its limitations:

  • If an Exam question is independently available in our Textbook Solutions product, the Tool will not to be able to block the corresponding answer during the Exam Timeframe.

  • If a user posts an Exam question outside of the Exam Timeframe, it will not be blocked. In accordance with Chegg’s Copyright Policy, copyright owners may submit a DMCA takedown request to remove copyrighted material.

  • The Tool currently only accepts pdf and word (docx) files that are no more than 10MB in size.

  • You must have established an Instructor account at least five (5) days prior to their first Exam.

  • You must upload the Exam Content at least 48 hours prior to the start of the Exam Timeframe, which is limited to six (6) hours. If there are more than 48 hours remaining prior to the start of the Exam Timeframe, you have the ability to edit your Exam Content or upload a new Exam file altogether. If there are less than 48 hours remaining prior to the start of the Timeframe, you have the ability to edit the title of the Exam or cancel the Exam. You will not be able to edit the questions or upload a new file.

  • We will not be able to provide any information regarding users who attempt to post a blocked Exam question during the Exam Timeframe.

Right to Refuse Service

Chegg reserves the right to refuse to process any request received via the Tool. Some reasons that we may not process Tool requests may be: (i) the request was made less than forty-eight (48) hours from the start of the Exam Timeframe; (ii) the volume of requests received by our team becomes too great to timely process, (iii) we believe that requesters are not or are no longer educators, or (iv) an individual abuses the system, for example, by submitting an excessive number of requests. Those who, in our sole determination, abuse the Tool or take any action hindering our ability to operate the Tool for others, may be refused service and their account terminated.
