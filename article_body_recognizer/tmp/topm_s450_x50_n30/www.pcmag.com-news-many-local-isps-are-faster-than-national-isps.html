Many Local ISPs Are Faster Than National ISPs

19 states have better internet provider options than behemoths ATT, Comcast, and Verizon. Is yours one of them?

The largest internet service providers in the United States may dominate the market on a national level, but that doesn't mean they're the best option everywhere in the country.

According to Ookla's Speedtest, smaller ISPs that serve local populations have proven to be faster than the big conglomerates in certain locations. While ATT, Comcast, and Verizon may dominate the coastal markets, the data for Q3 2021 shows that smaller providers are faster in the Midwest and parts of the South.

The Speedtest data shows that a local ISP proved to be the fastest option in 19 states. Google Fiber was the fastest ISP in Alabama, Kansas, Missouri, North Carolina, and Utah. MetroNet proved to be fastest in Indiana, Iowa, and Kentucky. Sparklight was fastest in New Mexico and North Dakota.

Other local options that proved to be faster than their national counterparts include Allo in Nebraska, C Spire in Mississippi, EPB in Tennessee, GCI in Alaska, HTC in South Carolina, Midco in South Dakota, OzarksGo in Arkansas, RCN in Illinois, and USI in Minnesota.

In our annual survey, we too found that the 10 fastest ISPs for 2021 were all local providers. Google Fiber ranked second overall (and was this year's Readers' Choice winner for local ISPs), EPB ranked sixth, and Allo ranked eighth. Sparklight and RCN, meanwhile, were rated the third- and fourth-fastest of all major ISPs, behind FiOS and Xfinity.
