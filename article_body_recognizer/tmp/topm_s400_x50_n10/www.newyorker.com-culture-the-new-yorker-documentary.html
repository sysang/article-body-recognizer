The New Yorker Documentary

  • When a Memorial Is Placed Out of Sight

    Güzin Kar’s documentary “Your Street” addresses a deceased victim of racist violence in post-reunification Germany and asks what places a society should allot to the shameful parts of its past.

    By Fabrice Robinet

    December 8, 2021

  • Andrew Yang and the Weird Arcs of Political Fame

    “The Andrew Yang Show” dissects what propelled the candidate to the top of New York City’s mayoral race—and what brought him down.

    By Sumit Poudyal

    December 1, 2021

  • Politics and Resilience in a Tunis Salon

    In Sarra El Abed’s documentary short “Ain’t No Time for Women,” a group of beauty-shop regulars process political upheaval through lighthearted chat.

    By Yasmine Al-Sayyad

    November 24, 2021

  • How Former Prisoners View the Parole System

    “The Interview,” by Jon Miller and Zach Russo, shines a light on the intricacies of parole in New York State from the perspective of those who were eventually granted it.

    By Sumit Poudyal

    November 17, 2021

  • A Choir at the Crossroads of Boyhood and Manhood

    The documentary “The Voice Break Choir” features a Swedish ensemble that lets teen-age boys keep singing as their voices—and lives—change.

    By Dan Stahl

    November 10, 2021

  • A Celebrity Chef’s Burnout and Pivot to Resiliency

    Edward Columbia’s documentary “Little Fish” follows the sustainable-food pioneer Bun Lai as he closes his successful restaurant and pursues a new approach to life.

    By David Kortava

    November 3, 2021

  • A Texan Housepainter’s Journey to Gender Confidence

    In Jessica Wolfson and Jessie Auritt’s short film “The Paint Wizzard,” Millicent McCrory’s flamboyant look generates questions.

    By Coralie Kraft

    October 27, 2021

  • Healing Dual Traumas at a Congolese Wildlife Sanctuary

    In “Mama,” a survivor of sexual abuse finds an unusual place of refuge.

    By Anna Boots

    October 20, 2021

  • The Romance of American Activism in “Radical Love”

    William A. Kirkley’s documentary follows a married couple working on behalf of the Weather Underground, the Black Panthers, and others during the nineteen-sixties and seventies.

    By Hannah Aizenman

    October 13, 2021

  • A Cree Skateboarding Legend Grapples with the Trauma of Canada’s Residential Schools

    Through a story of addiction and athleticism, Amar Chebib’s documentary short “Joe Buffalo” shows the effects of the system that separated Native children from their families.

    By Dan Greene

    October 6, 2021
