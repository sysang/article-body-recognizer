Of course, distancing yourself from the comfort of accepting whatever router your ISP rents to you can present its own challenges if you’re unsure of what to look for or how much you should spend. That’s why we’re here with a list of the five best WiFi routers, which includes options that span multiple price points and use cases. 

5 things to know about WiFi routers before making a purchase:

  1. Wireless Standards: Consider the wireless technology of the devices you use daily. WiFi 6 (802.11ax) is gradually becoming the de facto standard, although WiFi 5 (802.11ac) is still quite common.
  2. Bands: Single-band routers are fine if you don’t live in close proximity to your neighbors and their myriad electronics. Otherwise, opt for a dual-band router to limit interference.
  3. Security: Protect your home network from hackers and WiFi squatters by purchasing a router with at least WPA2 data encryption and other safety controls.
  4. Range: The larger your living space, the broader the wireless coverage you’ll need. Mesh WiFi routers are ideal for this. 
  5. Price: Have a budget in mind to avoid buying beyond your means. Most consumers don’t need to purchase a top-of-the-line router.

We believe the best WiFi router overall is the ASUS RT-AX86U, a capable, gaming-forward WiFi 6 router that can also handle the rigor of people streaming Netflix shows while they work from home. If that doesn’t suit your needs, our list also includes models that are more budget-friendly and security-focused, among other superlatives.

Product Snapshot

The TP-Link Archer A6 is proof that you don’t need to spend a fortune on a decent dual-band WiFi router. It may not boast the latest 802.11ax (WiFi 6) standard, but at 802.11ac (WiFi 5), it’s compliant with plenty of current tech. It also has a respectable max speed of 867Mbps at 5GHz. (Conversely, it tops off at 300Mbps at 2.4GHz.)

Then there are its advanced features, which aren’t typical of WiFi routers at this price point. For instance, MU-MIMO technology at 5GHz comes in handy when multiple devices are streaming video at once. It also includes five hardwired ports: a single WAN port, plus four Gigabit LAN ports. As for security, the TP-Link Archer A6 has the latest WPA3 protocol and offers parental controls via the TP-Link Tether mobile app.

The TP-Link Archer A6 doesn’t provide quite enough range for larger homes; several customer reviews have noted drops in connection between floors. But for people who live solo in small spaces — particularly students who need a reliable router for their dorm room — this budget buy should serve you well. Product Snapshot

Cover your home in wall-to-wall WiFi with the scalable Google Nest mesh router. A single Nest router yields a 2,200-square-foot range, which will suit most two-story houses. You can add as many as three Nest routers for up to 6,600 square feet of coverage, which is ideal for sprawling three-story homes. Unlike most WiFi routers, the Google Nest is quite sleek and won’t look terribly out of place in most rooms. However, at just under 4 inches tall, it’s easy to keep it out of sight if you wish. 

Setting up a Google Nest mesh router is as simple as connecting it to a modem and downloading the Google Home app, which will then serve as a hub for network controls and QoS settings. Firmware maintenance is also painless thanks to a built-in security chip that prompts automatic updates. 

That ease of setup is what users love most about the Google Nest. Many buyer reviews also note an improvement in WiFi speeds in areas that would otherwise be dead zones, a testament to the quality of the Nest’s overall coverage. However, a lack of configurability may give you pause if you like to be more hands-on with your wireless setup. But for a quick and simple way to blanket your home in WiFi, the Google Nest is your best bet. Buy the Google Nest now from Amazon.
