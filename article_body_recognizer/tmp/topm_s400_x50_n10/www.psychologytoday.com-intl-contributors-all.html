Azadeh Aalai, Ph.D., is an assistant professor of Psychology at Queensborough Community College in New York.

Mitch Abblett, Ph.D., is a clinical psychologist, consultant, speaker, podcaster, and author of Prizeworthy with Shambhala Publications.

J. Stuart Ablon, Ph.D., the Thomas G. Stemberg Endowed Chair in Child and Adolescent Psychiatry at Harvard Medical School, is the Director of Think:Kids in the Department of Psychiatry at Massachusetts General.

Mitch Abrams, Psy.D. is a clinical sport psychologist specializing in anger management, sexual assault prevention and the treatment of trauma.

Josh Ackerman, Ph.D., is a faculty member with the Sloan School of Management at MIT.

Benjamin van Rooij, Ph.D., is a professor at the University of Amsterdam and the University of California, Irvine. Adam Fine, Ph.D., is an assistant professor at Arizona State University. They are co-authors of The Behavioral Code.

Christine B. L. Adams, MD is a child psychiatrist in private practice in Louisville, KY. She is co-author of the book Living on Automatic: How Emotional Conditioning Shapes Our Lives and Relationships.

Jane Adams, Ph.D., is a social psychologist, writer and speaker. She is the author of several books including I'm Still Your Mother, When Our Grown Kids Disappoint Us and Boundary Issues.

Kathryn Betts Adams, PhD, MSW, is a former associate professor of social work at Case Western Reserve University. Now living near San Diego, she writes and consults about mid-life and aging.

Patti Adler, Ph.D., is Professor of Sociology at the University of Colorado, Boulder. Peter Adler, Ph.D., is Professor of Sociology at the University of Denver.

Marina Adshade, Ph.D., teaches at the Vancouver School of Economics at the University of British Columbia.

Amir A. Afkhami, M.D., Ph.D., is an Assistant Professor of Psychiatry and Behavioral Sciences and of Global Health at the George Washington University.

J. Ibeh Agbanyim, Ph.D., is an industrial-organizational psychology practitioner and the author of The Five Principles of Collaboration.

Leah Aguirre, LCSW, is a psychotherapist based in San Diego, CA. She works primarily with individuals who have experienced complex trauma and struggle with mental health challenges.

Nina Ahuja, M.D., is an ophthalmic surgeon, award-winning medical educator, founder of Docs in Leadership, certified emotional intelligence instructor, and author of Amazon bestseller Stress in Medicine.

Anna Akbari, Ph.D., is a sociologist, writer, and entrepreneur. She is the author of Startup Your Life: Hustle and Hack Your Way To Happiness.

Laith Al-Shawaf, Ph.D., is an associate professor of psychology at the University of Colorado. He studies emotions, cognition, and personality individual differences.

Matt Albert, Ed.D., is the author of An Educated Guess and executive director at the Center for Reflective Communities, a non-profit that aims to enhance relationships between children and caregivers.

Steve Albrecht, D.B.A., holds degrees in English and Psychology, and a doctorate in Business Administration. He is a former police officer and domestic violence investigator with the San Diego Police.

Michael Alcee, Ph.D., is a clinical psychologist, TEDx speaker, and author who specializes in using his background in music, literature, and the arts to showcase the transformative power of psychotherapy.

Steve Alexander, Jr., M.A., Ed.M., is a Licensed Mental Health Counselor in New York who has worked in multiple outpatient clinics, psychiatric settings, and is currently in private practice.

Shainna Ali, Ph.D., is a practitioner, educator, and advocate who is passionate about highlighting the essentiality of mental health in fostering happiness and fulfillment.

David M. Allen, M.D. is a professor of psychiatry at the University of Tennessee and the author of the book Coping with Critical, Demanding, and Dysfunctional Parents.

Jessica Alleva, Ph.D., is an assistant professor of psychology at Maastricht University in the Netherlands, and a Visiting Fellow at the Centre for Appearance Research in the U.K.
