About

Araya Baker is a counselor educator, suicidologist, and policy analyst. Baker holds an Ed.M. in Human Development and Psychology from the Harvard Graduate School of Education and an M.Phil.Ed. in Professional Counseling from the University of Pennsylvania Graduate School of Education.

Prior to graduate school, Baker worked with The National Suicide Prevention Lifeline (1-800-273-TALK), The Trevor Project, and Mental Health America of Greater Houston, where he assisted with authoring the City of Houston’s inaugural city-wide mental health survey. Baker formerly conducted developmental science research with The Center for Autism Research at The Children's Hospital of Philadelphia, The Racial Empowerment Collective, and The Ecological Approaches to Social Emotional Learning (EASEL) Lab. Baker also has served on the advisory boards of NATAL Stories, an award-winning, international podcast about birth equity and reproductive justice, in addition to Pride in the Pews, "a grassroots nationwide campaign aimed at celebrating stories of resilient queer faith in the Black church" that pioneered the first-ever directory of LGBTQ-affirming Black churches.

As a writer and thought leader, Baker has contributed commentary and public scholarship to The New York Times, The Washington Post's The Lily, Huffington Post, Teen Vogue, Education Post, Vice, Buzzfeed, The Mighty, The Tennessean, and other platforms.
