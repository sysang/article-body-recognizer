Joe Root, Dawid Malan lead England fightback against Australia on day three of first Ashes Test in Brisbane

By Jon Healy and Dean Bilton at the Gabba

England has dug deep to create a foothold on day three of the first Test at the Gabba, with Dawid Malan and Joe Root's partnership setting the scene for an intriguing fourth day.

Having lost both openers cheaply again, Root and Malan convened with England in a dire situation, but began to swing the momentum back in the tourists' direction through resolute defence and cautious counter-attack.

Root and Malan were unbroken through the final session of the day, finishing on 86* and 80* respectively. Closing the day at 2-220, England still trails by 58 runs.

Australia started the day with Travis Head still at the crease, and he continued with the fine strokeplay he showcased on Thursday afternoon.

Having passed 150, he was bowled by Mark Wood for 152 as Australia's innings closed at 425.

Fresh off a golden duck from day one, Rory Burns appeared to have suffered a near-identical fate in the first over of England's second innings when he was given out LBW off the bowling of Mitchell Starc.

A successful DRS call saved Burns for the time being, but Pat Cummins soon did for the unorthodox left-hander on 13, catching his glove and presenting Alex Carey an easy catch.

Haseeb Hameed fell to a similar fate, strangled down the leg side off Starc's bowling soon after for 27.

But any thoughts of a quickfire Australian win were extinguished by Root and Malan, who both passed half-centuries and set the table for an extended fightback on day four.

A strong first session of batting on Saturday could make a serious game of things at the Gabba.

STUMPS: England 2-220, trailing by 58 runs (Root 86, Malan 80)

We. Have. A. Test!

Joe Root and Dawid Malan have turned a potential three-day demolition into a proper contest.

With eight wickets in hand and two in-form batters at the crease, day four is going to be veeeeerrrry interesting.

England are still 58 runs behind Australia, so it's not like they're in front of the game or anything, but this partnership has completely changed the complexion of this game. Excellent digs by the pair of them.

And suddenly Australia is the one with the injury woes, with Josh Hazlewood seemingly unable to bowl in the back half of the day, and David Warner off nursing sore ribs for the entirety.

Can't wait for tomorrow.

Time for Carey to step up

Mitchell Johnson has noted that things are getting a bit tough for Australia as England's dogged pair dig in as their partnership passes 140.

He says it's up to the wicketkeeper, debutant Alex Carey, to bring some energy out in the field and get his bowlers in a better space.

"This is where Carey needs to step up," Johnson says.

"I know that it's his first Test, but he needs to make some noise, bring some energy."
