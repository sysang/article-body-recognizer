National top stories

  • 'She must have been in a great deal of terror': Man jailed for setting partner on fire

    A Tasmanian man who set his girlfriend on fire in 2017, leaving her with "catastrophic burns" and lifelong injuries, is sentenced to 18 years in prison for his "cruel" actions.

  • Root and Malan lead dogged England fightback on day three of first Ashes Test

    Determined and classy batting from Joe Root and Dawid Malan gives England a route back into the first Test at the Gabba as Australia's bowlers are defied through the afternoon.

  • Mother who allegedly forced daughter to marry man who later killed her will stand trial

    Shepparton woman Ruqia Haidari allegedly begged her mother to call off her arranged marriage, but her plea was refused. Six weeks after the wedding, the young bride was dead.

  • Vaccines are on the way for kids, so what now?

    The vaccine rollout is going to be expanded again, this time for kids aged five to 11 after they were given a final green light to receive the Pfizer COVID-19 vaccine. Here's what we know about how it's going to work. 

  • Premier could be among thousands of SA government employees affected by cyber attack

    The South Australian government says the personal details of up to 80,000 workers — including potentially the Premier — have been accessed in a cyber attack on an external payroll software provider.

  • 'Today is the day we've been planning for': Gold Coast on COVID alert after six cases detected

    Authorities are assuming there has been COVID-19 transmission happening across the Gold Coast, with six people having been infectious in the community for a number of days.

  • Erika and Daragh climbed the ladder at Coles. Now they can't bear to set foot in one of its stores

    Former Coles managers allege a culture of overwork and underpayment at the supermarket giant as it faces legal action from the Fair Work Ombudsman as well as a class action on behalf of 2,200 workers.

  • Hobart to host fifth Ashes Test — making cricket history for Tasmania

    Hobart will host its first ever Ashes cricket Test next month after fighting off mainland rivals to be awarded the venue rights to the fifth and final match of the series.

  • 'It brings back bad memories': Margaret River community again in the grips of a dangerous bushfire

    As the Margaret River community worriedly watches a dangerous fire razing precious Boranup forest, memories of the devastating blaze destroying 39 homes resurface.

  • Police officer 'disgusted' with himself after propositioning schoolgirl while on duty, court hears

    A Sydney police officer being sentenced for misconduct after having sex with a schoolgirl he approached on duty tells a court he's "disgusted" in himself.

  • Global child abuse ring member jailed for 'depraved' crimes of the 'worst kind'

    A New South Wales man who was a member of an international child abuse network is sentenced to four-and-a-half years' jail for his "abhorrent"crimes. 

  • Two Omicron COVID-19 cases announced in SA, along with changes to quarantine and testing

    South Australia records eight new cases of COVID-19, including two from interstate suspected to be the Omicron variant — the first in the state. Close contacts of people infected with the strain will now have to quarantine for 14 days.

  • A third of big companies paid no tax in 2019-20, this table reveals which ones

    The number of Australian public companies that paid no tax rose during COVID-19 lockdowns, according to the ATO's latest transparency data.

  • Woman dies in floodwaters, NSW south coast towns under siege after 200mm of rainfall overnight

    Flash flooding is occurring at Mogo, south of Batemans Bay, with up to 200 millimetres of rain falling overnight and 100mm more expected today in the region still in crisis after the Black Summer bushfires.

  • Lionel Rose Google doodle brings new generation to iconic fighter

    A whole new generation of Australian sports fans are discovering legendary boxer Lionel Rose after the former world champion was featured as a Google Doodle.

  • Drug-affected driver jailed over hit-and-run death of Melbourne school principal

    Chris Hyslop will spend at least five years in jail after fleeing the scene of an accident that left school principal Debbie Locco fatally injured.

  • Anaesthetist who sexually assaulted unconscious patients and took inappropriate pictures avoids jail time

    A Queensland court hears the 59-year-old doctor committed the crimes against three women while working in Toowoomba on separate occasions between last November and January this year.

  • Donald Trump sued the White House over documents relating to the January 6 riot at the Capitol. He just lost

    A three-judge panel throws out the former US president's claim of executive privilege over diaries, handwritten notes and a draft Executive Order that might throw light on the January 6 election protest. 

  • Former Labor staffer blames technical 'glitch' for watching two child exploitation videos

    A court hears a former South Australian Labor Party staffer blames accessing child exploitation material on a technical "glitch".

  • Denver Broncos Super Bowl winner dies aged 33

    Broncos great Demaryius Thomas, who helped the franchise win Super Bowl 50, has been found dead at his home in Georgia.

  • Trailblazing Fortescue CEO stands down as company reinvents with green energy

    The first female CEO of Andrew Forrest's Fortescue Metals Group will stand down, but remain on the board, as the company transitions away from a pure play iron ore miner. 

  • Tesla criticised for allowing drivers to play video games while on the road

    America's National Highway Transport Safety Authority confirms it is looking into a recently discovered Tesla feature that allows drivers to play video games on the car's dashboard while in motion.

  • 'God will like it': Paedophile priest gets 15 years' jail for terrorising 12 schoolboys

    A priest and former high school teacher will spend at least the next 10 years in jail for sexually abusing 12 students at a NSW school in the 1980s.

  • Major study of PFAS firefighting foam's impact on communities finds limited evidence of disease links

    A major study of toxic chemicals that leaked into the groundwater of regional towns near Australian air force bases has found no conclusive evidence they increase the risk of cancer or disease.

  • The biggest win of Tai Tuivasa's UFC career won't see the pride of western Sydney change his ways

    Decking a former NFL All-Star during one of the biggest UFC cards of the year didn't change Tai Tuivasa — and that's just how the big man likes it as he faces his next bout on Sunday.

  • Anti-domestic violence group wants media to do better
