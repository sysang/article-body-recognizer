Travis Head powers to century after David Warner falls short on day two of the first Ashes Test at the Gabba

By Simon Smale and Dean Bilton

Travis Head has kept Australia's foot on the throat of England, scoring a rapid century on day two of the first Ashes Test at the Gabba to build a 196-run lead

After being rolled for just 147 on day one, things only got worse for England on Thursday, with Head (112) backed up by David Warner (94) and Marnus Labuschagne (74) as Australia reached 7-343 at stumps.

Warner was bowled off a Ben Stokes no ball while on 17, dropped by Rory Burns on 48 and survived a poor Haseeb Hameed run-out effort on 60, before he finally chipped Ollie Robinson to Stokes late in the day to fall just short of a ton.

But Head took over from there, and despite looking very shaky to start, a couple of loose overs from a hobbled Stokes and part-timer Joe Root allowed him to free his arms, and he reached his third Test century of just 85 balls.

While Warner was clearly disappointed with how his innings ended, he lasted a lot longer than Marcus Harris, who will be looking over his shoulder at Usman Khawaja after nicking off for just 3 in the sixth over.

Warner and Marnus Labuschagne (74) powered on from there, and while Warner rode his luck, Labuschagne batted assuredly and seemed destined for another ton on his home ground.

Both batters took a liking to England spinner Jack Leach en route to their half-centuries, but the left-arm finger spinner eventually won the battle when Labuschagne, one ball after belting him for six, cut straight to backward point.

Steve Smith hit his second ball from Leach for four, but only managed 12 before he edged Mark Wood behind, bringing Head to the crease.

He and Warner made it to tea, but Warner was unable to add to his tea-time score, departing in the second over after the break, with Cameron Green chasing him off the park after he was bowled leaving the next delivery.

Wicketkeeper Alex Carey made it through Robinson's hat-trick ball on debut, but also fell for 12 when he pulled Chris Woakes straight to mid-wicket, and captain Pat Cummins scored 12 before edging Root to leg slip.

On an already difficult day for England, apparent injuries to Stokes and Robinson made matters worse.

England bowling coach Jon Lewis said Stokes — who is playing his first competitive match since July — would be monitored ahead of day three.

"Obviously Ben has hurt himself on the field today," Lewis said after stumps on day two.

"We'll assess him overnight and see how he pulls up in the morning."

That's stumps! Australia 7-343 (Head 112*, Warner 94) leads by 196 runs

It was a day that was in the balance on a few occasions, but has ended firmly in Australia's favour thanks to the afternoon exploits of Travis Head. His rapid century looks like it has broken England, whose bowling attack limped to the day two finish line.

The day was set up for Australia by David Warner and Marnus Labuschagne, who both fell short of centuries, but Australia didn't have it all their own way. England bowled well without luck early, and then an arvo flurry of wickets seemed to swing momentum in their favour. But in just a couple of hours, Head undid all that good work.

Australia will go into day three up and about, eyeing off the English batting lineup and hoping to set up a convincing win in this first Test in Brisbane. We'll be back tomorrow morning, so be sure to join us then. In the meantime, keep an eye on abc.net.au/sport for all our wrap up coverage, including analysis from Russell Jackson.
