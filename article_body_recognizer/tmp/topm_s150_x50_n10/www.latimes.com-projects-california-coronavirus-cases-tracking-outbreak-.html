To better understand the COVID-19 pandemic, The Times is following the latest data published by the California Department of Public Health and other government agencies.

What we know

  • Cases Over the past week, the state has averaged 6,295 new cases, a increase of 20.5% compared to two weeks ago.
  • Deaths 553 total deaths have been reported over the last week, 12.1% less than two weeks ago. Learn more about the lives lost with our COVID-19 obituaries collection.
  • Hospitalizations There are 3,214 people hospitalized with a confirmed case statewide, a 1.3% increase from two weeks ago. See the latest figures with The Times’ hospital tracker.
  • Vaccinations 72.6% of the state population has received at least one dose. Follow the rollout with The Times’ vaccine tracker.
  • Variants The Delta variant now accounts for almost all new cases. It's too soon to say if the Omicron variant will spread widely. Follow the latest with The Times’ variant tracker.

The latest totals

About 1 out of every 8 people in the state has tested positive, 5,107,276 in total. Since the start of the pandemic, 74,209 deaths in the state have been linked to the virus.

The case tally includes 261,981 probable cases diagnosed with an antigen test, which return results faster but may be less accurate.

Over the past week, the state has averaged 6,295 new cases and 79 new deaths per day. On Sunday, the state reported 22,406 new cases and 163 deaths.

Experts say the true number of people infected is unknown and likely much higher than official counts.

Seven-day averages offer a more stable view of the trend than daily totals.

An estimated 5 million Californians have recovered from COVID-19 or are no longer infectious, according to a common statistical method applied by The Times. Roughly 70,000 cases remain active.

Mapping the toll

The coronavirus has been found in all 58 counties, from urban Southern California to the state's rural north. After adjusting for population, the highest concentration of cases have been found in Southern California and the Central Valley.

Vaccines

Three COVID-19 vaccines are available in the U.S. Two of them require two doses given a few weeks apart to provide full inoculation.

So far, 72.6% of Californians have received at least one dose, about 28.4 million people.

Among all Californians, 64.9% are fully vaccinated, meaning they have either received both shots of Pfizer or Moderna or the single-dose Johnson Johnson vaccine.

Hospitals and patients

To keep tabs on available beds, officials watch out for rapid increases in the number of patients.

There are now 3,214 hospital patients statewide with a confirmed case, a change of 1% from two weeks ago.

Variants

State officials study a subset of patients to identify mutations in the virus that causes COVID-19. In November, the Delta variant accounted for 99.7% new cases in the state.

The newly identified Omicron variant has been detected in the United States, including here in California. Whether it spreads widely won’t be known until scientists examine new cases in the coming weeks.

Testing

In the last seven days, about 2.2% of the 1,850,747 tests conducted have returned a positive result.

Over the last week, an average of 264,392 tests have been conducted each day.

Wide disparities in age and race

While younger adults make up the majority of positive tests, deaths due to the virus have skewed heavily toward the elderly.

The state has logged the race of the patient in nearly two-thirds of cases.

Latinos and Black people have contracted the virus at a higher rate than white and Asian people. After adjusting for population, Latinos are now 2.1 times more likely to test positive than white people.

Nursing homes

Nursing homes are a tragic focal point of the outbreak. Residents and staff at skilled-nursing facilities have accounted for 2% the state's coronavirus cases, but 13% of its deaths. Early in the pandemic, the share was even higher.

Early in the pandemic, the Department of Corrections and Rehabilitation faced criticism after surges in cases at its 35 facilities across the state.

California in context

To date, the United States has recorded 49,055,945 coronavirus cases and 787,571 deaths. In the last week, the country has averaged 121,710 new cases and 1,612 deaths per day.

California — America’s most populous state — has the nation’s top case count. Home to 12% of the country's population, it has accounted for roughly 10% of cases.

California has accounted for 9% of deaths nationwide.
