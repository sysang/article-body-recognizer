Best Broadband Deals in the UK

The best broadband deal for you will depend on what you need the most from your internet service. If you use the internet for just social media and emails, then a cheap broadband deal could be good for you. If you love to binge-watch Netflix shows and play online games, you should go for deals offering fast download speeds. For people often on the move, your best option may be a no contract internet package. Ultimately, the best broadband deal is one that offers the best services you need for a monthly price you can afford. Picking the Best Broadband Deal for You

Choosing the best internet plan can be easier when you can see and compare all of the packages you could get side by side.

Knowing the important factors upfront like;

  • what your internet usage levels are like
  • what your budget is
  • the broadband speed you need
  • additional services or features you want
  • contract length that works for you 
  • the provider with the best customer service 
  • any added perks, extras, freebies, gifts or vouchers that you can get by switching broadband deal. 
  • ensuring the broadband deal you want is available in your area
  • whether you need new routers or hardware or professional installation and whether this is included in the cost of your broadband. 

Don’t be fooled by providers who claim to have the best deal. Your needs will be very different to anyone else’s because of your personal interests and internet usage requirements. While there is no one size fits all broadband deal that is best for everyone, we’ve compiled a few factors that can help you compare and make an informed decision based on what’s most important to you and your family.

Usage 

This is quite often the main factor in determining which broadband deal is best for you. How often do you need the internet and for what purpose? If you find yourself barely having time to check your email and you haven’t streamed TV in over a week, the best deal for you will be very different from someone who works from home or frequently plays online games. 

Affordability 

What is your budget and does that budget align with your usage? If you’re a high usage customer you’ll want at least a mid-tier package but if you choose the cheapest plan, you’ll likely end up frustrated when your service cannot perform the activities you care about. It’s better to understand what speed you need first, and then compare deals in that speed range to find one that satisfies your budget and lifestyle.

Speed 

The general rule is, that the higher the speed the more the price you have to pay for it. Although it is generally believed that it’s best to have the highest speed possible, it is useless to pay extra money for speed if you won’t end up using it. If your internet usage only involves regular browsing and there are not many users or devices in your house, broadband that offers around the average UK speed of 30Mb or above will be just fine for you and save you quite a lot compared to a high speed plan. This underlines the importance of step 1: it’s extremely important to understand your usage.

Availability 

It is very important to check whether the broadband deal you want is available in your area. If you live in a remote area, you might not be able to enjoy a fibre-optic broadband connection. Similarly, some broadband providers might have better broadband coverage in your area than another one. It’s a good idea to check which providers are available near you early on in the process of shopping for a broadband deal, so you don’t waste time comparing plans that won’t be available to you.

Additional services 

Are you also looking for additional services like phone and call allowances or features like TV bundles and entertainment deals? Instead of finding a separate deal for each of these services, you can find a bundle deal that offers all these services together. Bundling these services always results in a lower price than purchasing them separately, so it is important to know what you want before choosing your broadband deal.

Hardware requirements 

This is often overlooked when shopping for broadband service, but knowing your hardware requirements can save you money in the long run. One provider may have the cheapest deal, but if that deal requires you to purchase all new equipment you may end up spending more than you expected. If you are switching from BT to Sky, you will need a Sky broadband router at your home. If the price of that equipment pushes you over the budget you have set for your broadband, you will likely be better off finding a deal that does not require hardware upgrades.

Length of the contract 

The benefit of a longer contract is that the payment is divided into smaller chunks. On the flip side, longer contracts mean that you are tied to a particular deal for a longer period, and if you want to switch broadband deals to another deal, you will have to wait longer or end the contract by paying contract exit fees which can sometimes equal the amount of money you’d spend by staying with your existing provider. If you move frequently, you could find that the best deal for you is a no contract, or rolling month to month, broadband service. Though it may cost more monthly, you will save by not having to buy out your contract if you need to move. However, if you have a home that you do not intend to move for several years, you can save quite a bit by choosing a lengthier contract. No matter what you choose, it’s important to be aware of your contract end date. Most promotional pricing only lasts until the end of your contract period, and after that date, your broadband provider is free to increase prices as they see fit.

Customer service 

No matter how good your service provider is, you will always have to talk to customer service at some point. Whether it’s your initial setup, paying a bill or dealing with an issue, it’s comforting to know that you’ll be dealing with a friendly, helpful, and efficient customer service team. Talktalk, Vodafone, and Virgin Media have some of the best customer service teams among broadband companies.

Added perks and extras 

Some broadband providers offer freebies to promote their deals. Prepaid cards, cash back, vouchers are relatively common free perks often included in new customer deals. Gadgets, such as laptops or tablets, or combined mobile phone and broadband deals are much more rare but not unheard of and are definitely something to keep an eye out for. Free routers are very common and are included in most packages, but it’s always good to check so you don’t end up with an unexpected fee.

You will want to compare broadband deals that offer free gifts against similar deals that do not offer them, as very often, the cost of the free gift is simply added to the monthly price of your broadband. 

If the equivalent package is cheaper, then consider whether you really need the free gift at all.

Picking the Right Broadband Speed

The speed of your internet is measured in megabits per second. 30 megabits per second means that you will be able to download 30 megabits of data in one second. The speed of the internet affects streaming quality while watching shows and movies, and can result in the dreaded buffer if your speeds aren’t fast enough. Although you always want to have internet service fast enough to keep up with your online activity, it’s not always worth paying the highest price for the fastest package. What you really need to do is match the speed you pay for with your actual usage.

The speed of 30 megabits per second may suit a user who only uses the internet for regular browsing or social media. However, if you stream a lot of videos, are into online gaming, or have a large household with many connected devices, you will need a faster speed.

Having trouble determining what broadband speed you need? We can help. For home broadband, there are various speeds available that you can pick from according to your activities and location.

0-35 Mbps

Best deal for: Checking email, web browsing, social media Devices: 1-2

This speed is suitable for a house with one, maybe two people living in it. It will work for those who don’t have to do much with the internet except for web browsing and social media. Netflix claims you need 25 Mbps to stream 4K content, and only 5 Mbps for streaming HD. However, if you plan on having anything else connected to your broadband at the same time, you’ll likely want to opt for a higher speed. Ideally, this speed is for light internet usage at most.

45-100 Mbps

Best deal for: 4K streaming and online gaming Devices: 3-5

This speed range is generally for households having 3 to 5 connected devices at any time with moderate to high internet usage. If you’re looking to stream 4K video, this will be an ideal range. For fans of online gaming this speed range should keep you playing smoothly without interruptions.

100-920 Mbps

Best deal for: Working from home, downloading large files, live video streaming Devices: 6+ 

100 Mbps to 920 Mbps are the fastest broadband speeds available in the UK currently and provide the best speeds you can get. For those who want the best connections and the fastest download speeds, these are the deals to compare. These deals can handle the heaviest workloads and are perfect for people who work from home and frequently share large files. While some people can save money by selecting a lower speed plan, a 2020 study showed that on average, UK citizens have access to 9 connected devices in their home. Ranging from tablets to headphones and game consoles to speakers, the average person is using their broadband connection on much more than just their computer, smartphone and smart TV. If you fall into this group, it would be worth considering a high speed fibre deal to keep up with your connected lifestyle.

Best Broadband Deals FAQ

Do these broadband deals include VAT line rental? 

The deals shown here all include line rental. Both VAT and line rental are included so, for the term of the offer period the price you see is the price you will pay. 

Once the initial contract term of your broadband offer expires, your price will increase so you will want to switch again to make sure you don’t end up paying more than you have to.

Do these broadband deals include phone calls? 

While many of these deals include a phone line included in the price, they may not include minutes or call time allowances to use your home phone. 

If calling from your home phone is important to you compare broadband and home phone packages to choose a deal that includes landline calls.

Can you get these broadband deals where you live? 

Most areas on the UK mainland can get some form of broadband connection to their homes. To see which deals you can get use our broadband postcode checker, this will refine the list of deals you see and show you only deals from the best providers that can offer broadband to your home.

Can you switch your broadband deal?

Yes, anyone with a broadband deal can switch to another provider or offer at any time. However, if you are within your minimum contract term, you may have to pay an exit or contract termination fee to do so. The best way to check whether you are inside or outside of your broadband contract is by logging into your online account and finding your account details online. Alternatively, you can call your provider or find a paper copy of your bill which might contain contract term information.
