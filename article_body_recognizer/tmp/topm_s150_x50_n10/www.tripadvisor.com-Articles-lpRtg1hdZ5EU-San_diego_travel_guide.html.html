Beaches, Ice Cream, and Animal-Watching: a Weekend in San Diego

By Christine SarkisDec 2, 2021

Why we love San Diego

  • Its sunkissed, outdoorsy approach to winter.
  • Its super relaxed but wow-worthy attractions.
  • Its strong commitment to frozen treats.

Just hypothetically, say you’ve been hunkered down with your immediate family for the better part of two years. You’ve added assistant teacher, tech support, coach, and therapist to your parenting resume. And, you’ve cancelled one (or more) blowout family vacations to make sure everyone stayed safe and healthy.

And now, suddenly, vacation is on the horizon again. What to do? Here’s an idea: Go big—really big—in San Diego, even if it's just for a weekend. AJ Williams, owner of local ice cream shop Hammond’s Gourmet Ice Cream, explains why San Diego shines as a family destination. “There’s so much to do here. We’re 15 minutes from the beach, an hour from the desert and mountains, and there are tons of local places to go like the zoo, SeaWorld, and Legoland.”

It’s not just all the kid-friendly things to do, it’s also the city’s warm and laid-back vibe that sets it apart as a place that welcomes parents, kids, and really any traveler who wants to reconnect with that twirl-until-you-fall-down playground joy. “San Diego is also a great multicultural city with really great friendly people who know how to have a great time and take care of each other,” says Williams.

”Going big” on vacation is about big attractions, big adventures, and big eats—something Hammond’s does every day. The shop rotates through over 300 flavors throughout the year, and it’s famous for its 32-flavor tasting flights. (Definitely worth the brain freeze!) Williams says families come in and bond over choosing flavors, sharing cones, and debating the best flavor. Everyone has a favorite—Williams is partial to Banana Storm, which he says is like having a “Banana Split on a cone” and Vanilla Pandan, a flavor he loves for its “complex flavor of nutty, aromatic, and vanilla goodness.”

All that sugar is just the fuel you need to go on a San Diego spree. With 70 miles of sun-soaked SoCal coast, one of the world’s best zoos, and a bajillion ways to keep kids and adults beaming ear to ear, San Diego brings major vacation vibes to any long weekend.

—Christine Sarkis, SoCal’s Senior WeekEnder Guide

Things to do

San Diego is all about a mellow vibe and wow-worthy activities—just the thing for families looking to vacation big time.

Go wild at the San Diego Zoo

Ready to check some jumbo items off your going-big-in-San-Diego checklist? Then hotfoot it to the San Diego Zoo, where you’ll be greeted by a humongous bronze sculpture, a 27-foot long lion in mid leap. Then make your way through other sizable attractions: Watch giraffes, the world’s tallest land animals, snack and socialize; and marvel at gorillas, the planet’s largest primates, swing and play. Thanks to the zoo’s remarkable education program, visitors come away with plenty of cool facts about wild animals plus loads of appreciation.

Catch a kid-friendly concert at The Rady Shell

What better place to catch a concert than the newly opened Rady Shell at Jacobs Park with its acoustically awesome sound and gorgeous waterfront location. In its first few months, this new outdoor waterfront performance venue has already hosted the greats, including Yo Yo Ma and Gladys Knight. Its kid-friendly holiday lineup includes The Muppet Christmas Carol in concert and a screening of Frozen with music performed by the San Diego Symphony. If you’ve endured your little one shout-singing it off-key, it’s time to turn this over to the pros and hear this music with a full symphony backing it, so you can truly “Let it go.”

Welcome wonder on a San Diego Whale Watching Cruise

Ahoy, matey! Set sail aboard a replica 1851 sailboat in search of your own Moby Dick. San Diego’s whale watching season stretches from mid-December through April, and Next Level Sailing’s San Diego Whale Watching Cruise welcomes kids of all ages (with their adults). Get ready for the incredible experience of gliding alongside some of the world’s largest mammals, including gray whales and dolphins. The company promises both whale sightings and “no seasickness,” so you and the kids can ply the Pacific in comfort.

Add some easy beach time at La Jolla Cove Beach

You want to talk massive? Hit the beach and look out at the endless Pacific. La Jolla Cove Beach has kid-friendly activities plus an inspiring view of the world’s largest ocean. Good visibility and lifeguards make this modestly sized beach a fun stop for wading and snorkeling. Take a stroll to the Children’s Pool to spot seals. (This beach closes next month through mid-spring, but the viewing areas remain open.) And if the tide is right, explore the nearby tidepools for hours of wonder. Plus La Jolla Cove Beach’s proximity to parks and restaurants means you can weave beach time into any day in La Jolla.

Drive a (mini) speed boat with San Diego Harbor Speed Boat Adventures

Zoom your way around San Diego’s waters with your favorite little navigator beside you. On this speed boat tour, you’ll pilot your own F13 mini speed boat on a two-hour guided and narrated tour around San Diego Harbor. Get great views of Coronado Bridge, U.S. Navy ships, and other popular harbor sights. Don’t worry, you’ll be following a guide the whole time (and communicating via two-way radio), and the tour kicks off with a bit of training, so even newbie sailors feel comfortable. You need to be 18 or older to drive a boat, but kids will get a front-row seat to all the speed boat fun.

Where to eat

Making ice cream the main event? You’re right, that’s not a good idea—it’s a great one. Get ready to surf that sugar high, San Diego style.

For families up for a 32-flavor challenge, Hammond’s Gourmet Ice Cream

Single? Double? Think bigger: The kids are sure to crown you Parent of the Year when you order the 32-scoop tasting flight at Hammond’s Gourmet Ice Cream. Taste your way through favorites like orange dreamsicle, brown sugar, and cookies and cream on this unique tiny-cone adventure. With more than enough to share, it’s a bucket-list ice cream experience.

For the ultimate eat-treat-play trio, Corvette Diner

Fuel up the fam with a pit stop at Corvette Diner. First, head off a sugar crash with savory faves like burgers, grilled cheese, and hot dogs. But don’t forget to leave room for the sugary finale. This 1950s-themed diner and arcade deliver with truly over-the-top sweet treats including show-stopping shakes like Marshmallow Cloud and Pineapple Princess, custom ice cream sodas and egg creams, and go-big-or-go-home shareable sundaes. Not enough? Rev up that sugar high with Corvette Diner’s signature tower of cotton candy, then have a family face-off in the diner’s arcade.

For ice cream artists ready to express themselves, The Baked Bear

Sandwiches may be a lunchroom yawn, but ice cream sandwiches shout yay with every bite. And The Baked Bear takes this to the next level with giant ice cream sandwiches you and your kids can design yourselves. Choose your favorite cookie, pair it with the perfect ice cream flavor, and roll it in a crunchy topping for the best sandwich ever.

For an Instagram-ready ruffled dessert, Iceskimo

The delicate ruffles of Taiwanese shaved snow look gorgeous and taste even better, with a texture that’s creamy like ice cream but still manages to be light and refreshing. The shaved snow experts at Iceskimo pair flavors like strawberry, nutella, and horchata with classic toppings including cookie dough, fresh fruit, and popping boba. The shop is also known for its Hong Kong bubble waffles and soft serve combos that level up the traditional ice cream cone experience.

For an all-ages ice cream stop, Salt and Straw

If you’d like to do something a little more sophisticated, but the kids are still craving candy-covered everything, Salt and Straw has you covered. The small West-Coast chain makes sure there’s something for everyone, with everything from kid-pleasers like chocolate gooey brownie to sophisticated delights such as Arbequina olive oil or pear and blue cheese. Pro tip: don’t miss the seasonal flavors like fall’s pumpkin and gingersnap pie.

Places to stay

With extras like waterslides and beach bonfires, these kid-friendly hotels and resorts shine like the California sun.

For kid-approved luxury, Fairmont Grand Del Mar

The Fairmont Grand Del Mar dazzles both kids and adults right from the jump. Upon arrival, little ones are greeted with surprises from a treasure chest of goodies, and families can request kids-sized robes and slippers. The resort’s four heated pools include a children’s pool with white-sand seating areas and fire pits as well as an ultra-chill adults-only pool. (Consider this your refuge.) There are plenty of fun activities like tennis lessons, a junior golf academy, archery, and outdoor family movies. Kids will never want to leave, especially once they discover Grand Sweets, the resort’s indulgent ice cream and candy shop.

For poolside views for miles, Manchester Grand Hyatt

Embrace San Diego’s kid-friendly cosmopolitan side at the Manchester Grand Hyatt. This downtown tower delivers city skyline views plus relaxed family fun. Spacious rooms and suites make it easy to spread out, and the rooftop family pool is surrounded by a large pool deck and a turfed area ready for play. And the family pool is also the place to be after sunset, when the Rooftop Cinema Club screens al fresco hits like Home Alone, Elf, and Harry Potter and the Sorcerer’s Stone. The Embarcadero boardwalk runs right in back of the hotel, making it beyond easy to rent a bike and explore the city. You’re also well positioned for San Diego Bay activities like boating and kayaking.

For a splish-splashin’ good time, Hyatt Regency Mission Bay

Location, location, location. And waterslides. The Hyatt Regency Mission Bay is known for its prime spot on Mission Bay and its three waterslides (open-year around, of course). But there’s so much more for families to love at this 19-acre waterfront resort. Like three pools and direct access to sailing, whale watching, stand-up paddleboard and kayak rentals, and other water sports. The Hyatt offers complimentary weekend yoga (welcoming to both kids and adults), and a deluxe s’mores experience that includes everything you need for a memorable—and lip-smacking—time around the fire pit.

For a blissful beachy break, La Jolla Beach Tennis Club

La Jolla Beach Tennis Club has nailed the perfect vacation formula of sun, sand, and surf. This beachfront resort has been a family fave for more than 80 years, and it’s no secret why. Its beachfront location, soft warm sand, colorful umbrellas, sunset beach barbeques, and wadable waves offer a blissful escape. Its large heated pool is an all-day hangout for kids and adults, and poolside dining makes it even easier to spend the whole afternoon floating in a carefree bubble. Need something a bit more active for your little ones? Tap the resort’s expert team for swim lessons, golf camps, and tennis classes for kids.

For a tropical time out, Catamaran Resort Hotel and Spa

Swaying palm trees, lush grounds, and chatty macaws dial up the tropical vibe at Catamaran Resort Hotel and Spa. Hit up the beach concierge for everything you need for a day on the sand, or while away the hours by the kid-friendly pool (and adjacent arcade). On property, rent everything from powerboats to stand-up paddleboards for family adventures on the water. At the end of the day, book a private bonfire and relax into a fireside evening kitted out with comfy chairs, firewood, and s’mores.

Getting there

The drive from L.A. to San Diego is a straight shot south on I-5. With the usual traffic, you’re looking at a two-and-a-half hour drive, much of it along the scenic coast. For a car-free journey, Amtrak’s Pacific Surfliner offers nearly a dozen round-trips per day, making it easy to ditch the traffic and opt for a scenic ride instead. Flights connecting the cities are an option, but when you add in the travel time to and from the airport, it doesn’t make as much sense as driving or taking the train.

Listen to

The San Diego Zoo’s brand new Amazing Wildlife podcast is the perfect way to prep the fam for your wildlife weekend. Listen for cool facts (did you know a grizzly bear’s bite is strong enough to crush a bowling ball?) and stories plus behind-the-scenes peeks at the ways the zoo is working to preserve the future of animals and plants around the world. It’s a perfect fit for curious kids, budding zoologists, and parents who appreciate a kids podcast made for them too.
