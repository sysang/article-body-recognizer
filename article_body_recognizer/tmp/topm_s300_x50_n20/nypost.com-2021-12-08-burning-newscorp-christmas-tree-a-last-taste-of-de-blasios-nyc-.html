Burning Newscorp Christmas tree a perfectly grim last taste of De Blasio’s NYC

By
Post Editorial Board

Thanks for contacting us. We've received your submission.

Back to Reading
December 8, 2021 6:54pm
Updated
December 9, 2021 2:00am

It sure feels like one last taste of #DeBlasiosNYC to us: A vagrant set our Christmas tree ablaze late Tuesday night.

Craig Tamanaha, a man with a long rap sheet and history of mental issues, stands accused of torching the tree outside the NewsCorp building just after midnight. Police say he climbed up the metal frame and lit a piece of cardboard to start the blaze, possibly using accelerants as well.

Happily, Fox News started replacing the tree immediately, refusing to acquiesce in the decay this mayor seems to actively encourage. It’s a symbol that Fox News and its sister company, which includes The Post and the Wall Street Journal, refuse to surrender to disorder in our streets.

Tamanaha, meanwhile, may be back on the streets by the time you read this, thanks to the state’s disastrous no-bail law.

All too many New Yorkers have suffered far worse from Team de Blasio’s apparent determination to give the mentally ill free run of the city’s streets instead of properly treating them, and from the Legislature’s lunacy in making it near-impossible to jail even clear threats to public safety.

Sadly, this is the grimly perfect summary of the general disorder wrought by Mayor Bill de Blasio’s eight years of misrule. Nothing is sacred, not even Christmas.
