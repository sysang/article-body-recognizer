'A clusterf—k': Disneyland’s Genie Plus has been incredibly glitchy on its first day

Joshua Bote
Dec. 8, 2021
Facebook Twitter Email

The debut of Genie Plus at Disneyland was never going to be well-received. After all, paying for a feature that was built-in to the cost of a ticket just a few years ago is a tough pill to swallow.

So it was perhaps inevitable that Genie Plus was beleaguered with outages when it officially debuted Wednesday at the park, with scores of users griping about the service on Twitter, TikTok and Reddit.

Videos and screenshots show just how arduous the process is to reserve seating on a ride with Genie Plus. Wait times to even use Genie Plus have been reported. Error messages abound, with the Genie Plus app imploring users to refresh the service or telling parkgoers that the app’s baseline features “are unavailable.”

Fans are understandably peeved. “GENIE PLUS IS NOW LIVE AND IS ALREADY PROBLEMATIC,” said one attendee on TikTok.

“They need to address their server issues,” said another Disney fan on Twitter.

A Reddit user griped at how glitchy the service is, considering that it likely uses similar technology to MaxPass, the premium line-cutting feature that operated as a sort-of predecessor to Genie Plus. "How is this such a clusterf—k when Max Pass worked perfectly fine?" the user asked.

It appears that even Disneyland workers are circumventing the buggy service. One user on Twitter reportedly was just let in to the Matterhorn ride — no app reservation required — due to how error-laden the app has been on its first day out.

At least with the old FastPass setup, which used paper passes, Disneyland goers did not have to bend to the whims of an overloaded server.

Only time will tell if Disney will beef up its digital infrastructure to ensure that the benefits that people are ponying up for will work, or if the park will just let users ride it out.
