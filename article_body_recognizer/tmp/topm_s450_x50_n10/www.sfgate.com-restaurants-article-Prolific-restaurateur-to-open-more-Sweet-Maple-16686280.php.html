Prolific restaurateur to open more Sweet Maple, Kitchen Story restaurants around the Bay Area

Susana Guerrero, SFGATE
Dec. 8, 2021Updated: Dec. 8, 2021 4:10 p.m.
Facebook Twitter Email

To say that Hoyul Steven Choi is a brunch fanatic would be a major understatement for the restaurateur who owns popular brunch destinations Sweet Maple and Kitchen Story. As he looks ahead to 2022, the prolific brunch restaurateur has big plans to open five new restaurant locations around the Bay Area.

“We were thinking of expanding before, but it was solidified during the pandemic,” Choi told SFGATE. "Expansion was always on the back of our minds, but there are a couple of things. My team was confident of our brand power, but I was being cautious. Throughout the pandemic we became very busy with takeout and delivery."

So much so that Choi decided the moment was ripe to open Sweet Maple storefronts in Cupertino and Walnut Creek with a targeted opening date of February 2022. A Sweet Maple location that has been in the works in Palo Alto is expected to open later this month. Choi has also secured locations for a combined Kitchen Story and U: Dessert Story outpost in Mountain View, as well as a non-brunch focused restaurant, Noodle Story, in San Francisco that are both slated to open next fall. Noodle Story will serve a mix of Korean, Chinese, Japanese, and Thai-style noodles.

Adding to his brunch empire, Choi expects to debut a Southern California Sweet Maple and U :Dessert Story within one space in Santa Monica. The restaurateur added that he has his eyes set on Texas, Washington, and Arizona as states where he might open more Sweet Maple restaurants in the future.

“National brands are very nice, but they tend to follow the same type of everything,” Choi said. “I think we can bring something new to other states. Opening a restaurant in any other state outside of California is much easier,” he adds with a laugh.

Perhaps the biggest claim to fame that Sweet Maple has is the restaurant’s trademarked dish, millionaire’s bacon, which sells for $11 a plate or $22 for the millionaire’s bacon sampler platter. In 2017, Choi told SFGATE that millionaire’s bacon put Sweet Maple on the map. A year earlier, he trademarked the name which remains active, according to the United States Patent and Trademark Office database.

Opening six new storefronts within the next year is a huge undertaking, but Choi is up for the challenge. Choi, who said he has no investors or franchisers, is confident that his 19 years in the restaurant industry has given him enough experience “to make this happen.” He added that business partners will help him run the new storefronts and help oversee hiring general managers and staff. Choi said that his businesses will remain company-owned, even with the possibility of opening restaurants outside the state.

Choi got his start in the restaurant business in San Francisco’s Taylor Street Coffee Shop, which was previously under different ownership until he took over in 2002. A few years later, he became owner of Fred's Coffee Shop in Sausalito. In addition to Sweet Maple, Choi also owns Blackwood, A Kitchen Story, U: Dessert Story, Berkeley Social Club, Kitchen Sunnyside, and Surisan.
