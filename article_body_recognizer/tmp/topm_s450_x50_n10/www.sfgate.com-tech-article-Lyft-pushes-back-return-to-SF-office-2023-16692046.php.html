San Francisco-based Lyft pushes back its return to office until 2023

Joshua Bote, SFGATE
Dec. 10, 2021
Facebook Twitter Email

Ride-hailing app Lyft is delaying mandatory in-person work, continuing a trend among Silicon Valley tech companies.

CNN Business reports that Lyft won’t require staff to come into its offices, including its San Francisco headquarters, until 2023. A Lyft spokesperson did not immediately respond to a request for comment from SFGATE.

It is not likely tied to any anxieties over the omicron variant and instead falls in line with Silicon Valley’s move to long-term remote work.

"We've heard from our team members that they value continued flexibility in determining where they work and would benefit from additional time to plan,” a Lyft spokesperson told CNN.

And, as with the continued delay of return-to-office over the pandemic so far, Lyft is following the lead of Google, Uber and Microsoft, all of which have delayed their return indefinitely. 

Meta, the newly-minted holding company that owns Facebook and Instagram, is pushing for a rapid return to the workplace but will allow employees to stay remote through the middle of 2022.

Perhaps the most notable exception to the trend is Apple, which has only pushed back its return to in-person office work on a month-to-month basis, moving the date back to February — and only after considerable pushback from employees.
