Someone’s re-selling tickets for Olivia Rodrigo’s San Francisco concert for as high as $9,000

Joshua Bote, SFGATE
Dec. 10, 2021
Facebook Twitter Email

Bay Area Olivia Rodrigo fans, eat your heart out.

Less than an hour after tickets went live for the newly-crowned pop sensation debut tour, resellers posted tickets to the tour’s opening night at San Francisco’s Bill Graham Civic Auditorium on StubHub for as high as $9,000. 

Understandably, the demand for tickets to Olivia Rodrigo’s inaugural tour is staggering. Rodrigo has had an immense year, buoyed by the sheer success of “Driver’s License” and “Good 4 U” and the melodramatic speculation surrounding her and “High School Musical: The Musical: The Series” co-star Joshua Bassett.

But the high prices by resellers is also matched by StubHub itself. To even buy the cheapest tickets available on the platform — currently priced at $755 — the cost of StubHub fees is stunning, at $184. That’s about as much as the original price of Rodrigo’s tickets.

StubHub states that its fees “vary depending on event type, ticket type and location” and can be changed “in its sole and absolute discretion.” And previously, it has told SFGATE that the fees include “a service fee and a fulfillment fee.”

“The fulfillment fee is a fixed amount based on the type of ticket purchased. The service fee is a percentage of the sale price set by the ticket seller and can vary depending on several factors including the type of event, event genre, venue, time to event, location of the ticket, team/performer, etc.,” a spokesperson told SFGATE in October. They also defended this pricing move, noting that it is “prevalent across our industry as well as broadly across e-commerce.”

SFGATE sports reporter Gabe Fernandez contributed to this report.
