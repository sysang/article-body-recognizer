Tahoe records a half foot of snow with more on the way

Amy Graff, SFGATE
Dec. 9, 2021Updated: Dec. 9, 2021 12:41 p.m.
Facebook Twitter Email

LATEST Dec. 9, 12:10 p.m. Caltrans announced at 11:32 a.m. that Interstate 80 eastbound chain controls were dropped, while chain controls remained in effect on I-80 westbound between Donner Lake Interchange and Rainbow.

I-80 westbound traffic has been released at the Nevada state line, Caltrans said.

The National Weather Service reported at 11:48 a.m. that the snow was starting to slow down in some areas while places like Mono County continued to see snowfall.

In the past 24 hours, locations around the Tahoe Basin have recorded 2 to 6.5 inches of snow, the weather service said.

Dec. 9, 10:45 a.m. It was dumping snow in the Tahoe Basin on Thursday morning, with the fresh powder falling at elevations as low as 5,000 feet and impacting roadways. 

Chain control was in effect on Interstate 80 from Nyack to Boca eastbound and from Truckee to Nyack westbound.

Caltrans closed I-80 westbound at the Nevada state line because of multiple incidents on the slippery highway. 

I-80 chain controls: R-2 EB Nyack to Boca, WB Truckee to Nyack. I-80 WB remains closed at the Nevada state line due to incidents. pic.twitter.com/p3hiQUQZ27

— Caltrans District 3 (@CaltransDist3) December 9, 2021

"The snow showers are dissipating and will ease off through the day," Edan Weishahn, a forecaster with the National Weather Service, said at 10 a.m. "The last summary I heard was that we had 2 to 6 inches in the Tahoe Basin."

The UC Berkeley Central Sierra Snow Lab in Soda Springs reported 5.1 inches of snow overnight at about 7,000 feet elevation.

Palisades Tahoe reported 4 inches of fresh powder at its base. "Our operations have not changed, but this is helpful in building our base and more snow is on the way," the resort that has yet to open for the season said on Twitter.

The Thursday storm marks a shift in the weather pattern in the Sierra Nevada, which has seen mostly dry conditions in recent weeks.

More snow is expected in the next week with the weather service forecasting "a series of cool winter storms Sunday to Wednesday next week that all together could bring large amounts of snow to the Sierra."
