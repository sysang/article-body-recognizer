Major winter storm could dump up to 100 inches of snow in Tahoe

Fiona Lee
Dec. 10, 2021
Facebook Twitter Email

After a dry start to winter, Lake Tahoe and the Sierra Nevada are forecast to see heavy snow dump as a “significant” winter storm sweeps the mountain range, the National Weather Service said. 

The weather service issued a winter storm watch from 4 p.m. Saturday to 4 p.m. Tuesday. The storm is expected to impact travel in the area beginning on Sunday, when travelers along the U.S. Highway 50 corridor will first see conditions deteriorate, with strong winds and whiteout conditions. Downed trees and power outages are also possible.

The storm will then move south and affect conditions across all of the Sierra by Monday. Travel will be difficult to impossible, the service warned. 

Areas below 6,000 feet can expect a mix of rain and snow, with periods of heavy rain. Higher elevations will see several feet of snow, and images released by the weather service’s Sacramento office showed that some areas could see 6 to 8 feet of snow.

Lake Tahoe and Truckee are expected to receive 36 to 48 inches of snow, while Twin Bridges and Echo Summit will see 60 to 80 inches. Carson Spur, Kingvale and Donner Summit could record as much as 80 to 100 inches of snow. 

In what could be good news for ski lovers, Kirkwood is also forecast to receive 80 to 100 inches of snow. 

The weather service recommends that residents and visitors avoid travel, cautioning that “you could be stuck in your vehicle for many hours.”

“If you must travel,” the alert continues, “prepare for long delays and carry an emergency kit with extra food, water and clothing. If you stay home, have a backup plan in case of power outages.”

The weather service is also predicting a weaker system to move into Northern California on Wednesday and Thursday.
