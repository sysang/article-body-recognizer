49ers vs. Bengals: How to watch and stream online

This is as big of a game as it gets in this situation.

Evan Massey
Dec. 10, 2021
Facebook Twitter Email

As the 2021 NFL season moves into Week 13, the San Francisco 49ers are in a tough position. After falling to the Seattle Seahawks 30-23 last week, the Niners teeter on falling out of playoff contention. A win this week against the Cincinnati Bengals, however, will go a long way toward bringing the Niners back up to the NFC playoff race.

Heading to Cincinnati is no easy task for the 49ers. The Niners defense will have to find ways to contain star quarterback Joe Burrow and rokie receiver Ja’Marr Chase while putting together a strong offensive performance despite all the injuries on that side of the ball. Consistency has not been a hallmark for San Francisco this season.

With a win, the 49ers would move back to a positive 7-6 record, and any team over .500 has a shot in the NFC. Losing would drop the Niners to 6-7, meaning they'll likely have to go 3-1 down the stretch. That isn’t to say this is a must-win game, but it's huge, considering the circumstances.

All of that said, let’s dive in and take a look at how you can make sure to watch the action this week when the 49ers take on the Bengals.

What time is 49ers-Bengals?

  • Date: Sunday, Dec. 12, 2021
  • Kickoff: 1:25 p.m. PST
  • Location: Cincinnati, Ohio
  • Channel: CBS
  • Local Channel: KTVU

If you are unable to watch the game in market or you can’t tune in to the game on FOX or KTVU, there are a few different options that you could look into in order to catch the action.

  • Sling Blue or Sling Orange, three days free, then $35/month
  • Hulu + Live TV, 7 days free, then $64.99/month
  • fuboTV, 7 days free, then $64.99/month
  • YouTube TV, five days free, then $64.99/month

Should you not be in the Bay Area, you could also watch the game on NFL Sunday Ticket. However, if you are in the Bay Area, regional restrictions will block your access to the feed.
