'Absolutely': Warriors' Steve Kerr co-signs long-shot possibility of Steph Curry breaking 3-point record vs. Blazers

Gabe Fernandez, SFGATE
Dec. 7, 2021
Facebook Twitter Email

In order for Golden State Warriors star Steph Curry to make history against the Portland Trail Blazers on Wednesday, he would have to make more 3-pointers in one game than any other player ever.

As of Tuesday, Curry is 16 3-pointers away from breaking Ray Allen’s all-time record of 2,973. The 33-year-old Curry has been putting together a legitimate MVP campaign so far this season, but the ambitious single-game total of 16 3-pointers is something he’s never hit in his career. In fact, the only player who’s come close in league history is his teammate Klay Thompson, who hit 14 in one game in 2018.

It’s a tall task, even for someone as talented as Curry, but the way both he and head coach Steve Kerr talk about it implies the statistical challenge doesn’t feel so overwhelming. 

Does Steve Kerr think Steph could make 15 threes on Wednesday vs. Portland? "Absolutely... He’s Steph Curry, so anything is possible.”

— 95.7 The Game (@957thegame) December 7, 2021

Curry repeated that same “anything is possible” mantra in his postgame interview, but then noted that he’s not showing up against the Blazers with the intention to break records — at least, that’s what he’s saying out loud.

“If you've seen the way I've played, especially recently, I'm not shy about shooting the ball, so the game will dictate what that looks like,” Curry said of breaking the record, per ESPN. “I'm not coming out with that as the true goal of how I play, but crazier things have happened.”

Curry will almost certainly make history at some point in the next week. It’s just a matter of whether it’ll be in a day, or a little longer. If there’s ever a team that Steph could do it against, it might be the team with one of the worst defensive point guards in the league (sorry Damian Lillard, it's true). Like Curry and Kerr said, anything is possible.
