Bay Area John Doe found dead with physics journals finally identified

Katie Dowd, SFGATE
Dec. 9, 2021
Facebook Twitter Email

A man found dead in Sonoma County, the only clues his hand-written journals, has finally been identified. 

The man was discovered deceased on Jan. 2, 2015 on an access road between a Motel 6 and the Rohnert Park Expressway Self Storage on Commerce Blvd. in Rohnert Park. He had in his possession journals about math, physics and science, and some members of the Rohnert Park library did believe they recognized him but did not know his name.

"Despite exhausting our Coroner Missing and Unidentified Persons protocols, he remained unidentified for over six years," the Sonoma County Sheriff's Office said in a statement. "These protocols include DNA samples sent for Combined DNA Index System (CODIS) upload, dental charting through Missing Persons Sacramento, Forensic Anthropologic studies (Cal State Chico), and countless hours canvassing the area and local homeless shelters, and the dissemination of law enforcement informational flyers."

In 2018, investigators developed a DNA profile for the man. It was sent to Margaret Press, the CEO of DNA Doe Project, a non-profit that works to put names to unidentified decedents. "DNA Doe Project made quick headway in the case and, together with our detective, tentatively identified" the individual, the sheriff's office said. The Department of Justice assisted in then matching the genetic profile to the man's biological mother.

On Thursday, the sheriff's office announced the man's name: Stephen Patrick Archer. Archer, who was 48 when he died, lived the majority of his life in the Seattle area, according to Washington records. Little else can be found about Archer in public records or internet searches. Investigators do not believe his death was suspicious.

The sheriff's office said it hopes to pair with DNA Doe Project to "help solve the 38 unidentified decedent cases in Sonoma County."
