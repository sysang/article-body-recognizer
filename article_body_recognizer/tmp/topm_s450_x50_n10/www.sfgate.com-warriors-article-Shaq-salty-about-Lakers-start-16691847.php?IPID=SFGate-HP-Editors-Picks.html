Salty about Lakers' slow start, Shaq says he's rooting for Steph Curry and the Warriors now

Gabe Fernandez, SFGATE
Dec. 10, 2021
Facebook Twitter Email

The Warriors bandwagon apparently has enough space to hold Hall of Fame center Shaquille O’Neal among the team’s many fans who think this is their year.

The former Los Angeles Lakers big man explained on his podcast that not only was he backing the Warriors this season, but he was doing so in place of cheering for the team he won three straight titles with.

This came up during a conversation about the Lakers’ arena changing its name from Staples Center to Crypto.com Arena. O’Neal, who told TMZ he was actually supportive of the name change, said he doesn’t even go to games anymore, even asking, “Who want to watch them people play?”

The Lakers have been massively underperforming this season, sitting with a 13-13 record after a loss on Thursday to the Memphis Grizzlies. Though LeBron James has turned it on a bit in recent games, the lack of success from those performances makes them null and void to the big man, especially when compared with the Warriors.

“I don’t celebrate seventh place at all,” O’Neal said. “I celebrate Steph Curry. That’s who I celebrate. I can go to Golden State and watch him play.”

The Big Aristotle might be trolling a bit in the hopes of getting a rise out of the Lakers so they can play better, but it’s hard to argue with the decision to watch the Warriors, and Curry, instead of the Lakers. 

Not only is Curry putting up an MVP-caliber season — ESPN recently reported that 94 out of 100 NBA insiders surveyed would award MVP to Curry — but he’s also on the verge of making league history with the most 3-pointers in NBA history. Oh, and this is all happening while Klay Thompson has been out, so the team can only get better from here.

That sure seems a lot more interesting than seeing washed up versions of Carmelo Anthony, Russell Westbrook and Dwight Howard try to keep up with LeBron every game.
