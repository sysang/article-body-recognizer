'Haunted' one-bedroom apartment in old Presidio hospital: Guess the rent in San Francisco

Tessa McLean, SFGATE
Dec. 5, 2021
Facebook Twitter Email

Living in a unique, historic building is something plenty of people would love to do in San Francisco. But they may draw the line at residing in an old hospital. 

The Presidio Public Health Service Hospital was built in 1932, replacing an older hospital building that served the area's military residents. It was shut down in 1981, briefly used as the Defense Language Institute, and later abandoned and left vacant in 1988. It quickly became a favorite spot for graffiti artists and those looking to explore deserted buildings and remained mostly dilapidated for decades after. 

It was finally converted into apartments in 2010, even earning LEED certification. But being an old hospital, it's no surprise you'll hear the occasional story about the supernatural inhabiting the building. According to San Francisco Ghost Tours, visitors should be wary of the sixth floor, since that was where employees performed medical research and experiments on animals. There was also a former morgue on site and allegedly a murder in the building in 2002. There's even a Yelp page for the old hospital building where the reviews tell some old ghost stories.

If a little haunting from your upstairs neighbors doesn't scare you, there are some 5th floor, one-bedroom apartments for rent as advertised on Craigslist. Each unit is approximately 700-square-feet bathroom and appears to be recently renovated. The kitchen appliances look new, there appears to be a dishwasher and there's also plenty of storage.

There's also washer/dryer in-unit. No word on pets.

It goes for ... drumroll ... $3,730 a month!

Zumper reported the median rent of a one-bedroom apartment in the Presidio is $2,990.
