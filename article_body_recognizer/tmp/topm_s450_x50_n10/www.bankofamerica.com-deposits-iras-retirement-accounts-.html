IRA Basics

 What is an IRA?

Individual retirement accounts (IRAs) are accounts specifically set up to use during retirement by offering significant tax advantages. And the sooner you start, the longer your money has to grow.

Why invest in an IRA?

In retirement you may need as much as 100% of your current after-tax income (take-home pay) minus any amount you are saving for retirement each year. This makes it all the more important to start saving sooner rather than later, and an IRA can help you get started.

What’s the difference betweenRoth and Traditional IRA?

The 2 most common types of IRAs are Traditional and Roth. Which one you choose (or qualify for) depends on your age, income, and financial goals. Unique Benefits
  • Any earnings are federal income tax free if withdrawn at or after age 59 ½ and the account has been open five years or more
  • Contributions (not earnings) can be withdrawn tax free at any time
  • Contributions are not tax-deductible
  • Any earnings grow federal income tax deferred until withdrawn at or after 59 ½, at which time they are taxed at your current federal income tax rate
  • Contributions and earnings can be withdrawn free of the 10% additional federal income tax at or after age 59 ½
  • Contributions may be tax deductible
Who is eligible?
Single tax filers earning less than $140,000 MAGI ($139,000 for 2020)Joint tax filers earning less than $208,000 MAGI ($206,000 for 2020)
Anyone who has earned income equal to or greater than their IRA contribution is eligibleContributions may be tax deductible for single tax filers earning less than $76,000 MAGI ($75,000 for 2020)Contributions may be tax deductible for joint tax filers earning less than $125,000 MAGI ($124,000 for 2020)
Withdrawals
No Required Minimum Distribution (RMD) ever.
A Required Minimum Distribution (RMD) must be taken each year after you turn 72. You must take your first RMD by April 1 of the year after you reach age 72 (unless you turned 70 ½ in 2019, in which case your first RMD was due by April 1 of 2020).
