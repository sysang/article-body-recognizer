California’s very congested Interstate 15 along Nevada state line is getting another lane

Madeline Wells, SFGATE
Dec. 6, 2021Updated: Dec. 6, 2021 1:01 p.m.
Facebook Twitter Email

For those who frequently drive between California and Nevada, a 5-mile stretch of highway along the state lines is infamous for its terrible gridlock. Traffic on Interstate 15 can be backed up as many as 30 miles between Las Vegas and California on holidays, according to FOX5. 

Now, I-15 drivers might see some relief: California Gov. Gavin Newsom and Nevada Gov. Steve Sisolak announced a plan Sunday to temporarily add a third lane to the highway during peak congestion hours, reported FOX5.

“This 5-mile stretch of highway is a critical piece of infrastructure for not only our two states but for the whole country. However, the hours of traffic deters tourism and goods movement,” Newsom told KTNV. “While this is just a temporary solution, the expansion is crucial for continued economic health and resilience in the region and beyond.”

The plan for repaving and restriping the 5-mile section of shoulder to transform it into a third lane apparently came about after Sisolak called Newsom recently to say "we can't take it anymore," Newsom told FOX5. 

"California needs to step up. We need a better partnership to address ... a longstanding issue that, candidly, has not been addressed for years and years," Newsom told FOX5.

Newsom promised the new third lane would be ready by summer 2022. The project is expected to cost $12 million, and the state intends to seek funding from the Build Back Better plan.
