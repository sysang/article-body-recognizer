Sore Throat, Weakness And Body Ache: Most Early Common Symptoms Of Omicron

2 min read 468 Shares
Shweta SengarUpdated on Dec 06, 2021, 11:50 IST
Highlights

Omicron cases in India multiplied 4 times bringing the tally to 21 after reporting five cases till Sunday morning.

Seven in Maharashtra, nine in Jaipur, and a man in Delhi were found positive with the variant, taking the total count in the country to 21.

All the cases have reported mild symptoms.

The 37-year-old man, who was admitted to the hospital on December 2, had a sore throat, weakness, and body ache, according to reports.

Reuters

South African doctors who first dealt with Omicron patients said that the variant has been producing different symptoms from the previous ones. 

What are the symptoms of Omicron?

While the research on Omicron is in its nascent stage and much isn't known about the variant, the cases reported in India and other countries indicate that the symptoms are mild.

Angelique Coetzee, chairperson of the South African Medical Association, who first alerted the South Africa government about the new variant reported the unfamiliar symptoms brought by Omicron.

Scientists say these new characteristics may be the result of mutation.

India’s first Omicron case was a 66-year-old South African national who left the country after providing a negative Covid-19 report from a private lab. He was completely asymptomatic.

BCCL

The second Omicron case in India is a 46-year-old doctor with no history of international travel. He had reported mild symptoms.

India's third patient of Omicron had a sore throat and weakness, while the fourth case had a mild fever. The 37-year-old man arrived in Delhi from Tanzania, making it the first case of the new COVID-19 variant in the national capital. “He was admitted to the hospital on December 2 with mild symptoms of sore throat, fever and body ache," a doctor said.

The World Health Organization said it is not yet clear whether the newly-detected coronavirus variant Omicron is more transmissible or causes more severe disease compared to other variants, including the highly-transmissible and globally prevalent Delta variant. 

There is currently no information to suggest that symptoms associated with Omicron are different from those from other variants, the WHO said.
