Here's How Giving Away Old Smartphones Might Help Someone Go Digital

5 min read 13.9 K Shares
Nupur KhannaUpdated on Nov 27, 2021, 10:38 IST

Almost 40 to 70% of children in some states of India still don’t have access to digital devices.

These statistics were highlighted by the report, Initiatives by the School Education Sector in 2020-21 and included states like Assam, Andhra Pradesh, Bihar, Gujarat, Jharkhand, Madhya Pradesh and Uttarakhand.

Two years into the pandemic, these numbers should shock you to the core.

With schools across the country are operating digitally, the divide between the haves and haves-not has never been as evident as it is today. While kids with access to the best of digital devices have adapted to the new normal of digital life, there are still kids out there who don’t own a single smartphone in the family.

Amazon India

This digital divide puts these children two years behind their peers, leading to many quitting schools and taking up odd jobs or getting married young.

On the other hand, urban professionals like us, with access to the best technology don’t think twice before upgrading to a new device and discarding the old. We don’t realize that the simple act of donating our old smartphone could shape the future of a young mind.

Rolled out in October this year, Amazon India’s Delivering Smiles initiative aims to reduce this widening digital divide in the country. Through this initiative, Amazon India will directly give, and facilitate the contribution of digital devices among students and young people from marginalized communities, also contributing to the digitization goals of India.

Amazon India

Amazon will directly provide 20,000 digital devices to underprivileged young people, in partnership with over 150 large and small non-profit organizations, impacting over 100,000 students across India. Millions of Amazon customers can contribute a cash donation on Amazon Pay or give their old mobile phones that will be refurbished and distributed to provide digital learning devices for young people.

One such beneficiary of the Delivering Smiles Initiative is Anand, a class 4th student. His family was in dire need of funds during the lockdown and a digital education seemed like a far reality for him. In partnership with Kushii NGO, Amazon India’s Delivering Smiles initiative turned this into a reality.

Today, Anand not just learns Mathematics on the tablet but also self-learns new crafts every day on YouTube and enjoys a game or two during his free time.

Amazon India

Meet 12-year-old Himashu from Bhora Kalan, Haryana. Enrolled in class 8 and a bright student who loves Math, he aspired to become an engineer when he grows up. However, the lockdown disrupted his studies.

His father, a newspaper seller, lost his job during the pandemic and couldn’t afford to buy his son a smartphone to continue his education. The teachers and the NGO associated with his school decided to help him out with Amazon India’s Delivering Smiles initiative. They not only helped him get a tab but also helped him keep up with his studies.

Determined to see his dreams through, Himanshu says, “I’ll use my tab to prepare for my competitive exams!”

With no access to online classes, there weren’t many options for 10th grader Anitha. Another student who benefited from this initiative is class 9th student Anitha. Based in Bangalore, Anitha’s parents are daily wage workers who can’t afford to buy her a digital device for school.

During the time of pandemic, the family went through terrible horrors, leading to Anitha working at construction sites to support her family along with her elder brother Devraj P, who is in class 10th.

As per her last records, Anitha has an excellent academic track and also is interested in singing and poetry. She also dreams of becoming a teacher one day. Thanks to the refurbished tablet from Delivering Smiles, Anitha as well as her brother Devraj are now able to resume their studies virtually. It took them some time to adjust but they are now well adept with virtual learning.

So, the next time you feel you have outgrown a device, think about that kid who would appreciate it more than you can ever imagine.
