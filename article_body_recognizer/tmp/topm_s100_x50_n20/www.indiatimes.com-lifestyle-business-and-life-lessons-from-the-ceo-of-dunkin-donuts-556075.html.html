Rise, Fall Rise Again: Business ( Life) Lessons From The CEO Of Dunkin’ Donuts

3 min read 60 Shares
Nupur KhannaUpdated on Dec 06, 2021, 16:45 IST

If you thought selling donuts can’t take you places, you are wrong!

We came across the Luminary Original podcast Wisdom From The Top with Guy Raz and couldn’t help but head straight to the episode with Robert (also known as Bob) Rosenberg.

View this post on Instagram

A post shared by Guy Raz (@guy.raz)

With a successful career spanning over 35 years, Dunkin’ Donuts CEO is dishing out secrets that took his company global, making it a renowned brand worldwide and the consecutive failure and life lessons he learned on the way.

In a candid no holds bar chat, Bob discussed some of the most exhilarating highs and the lowest of lows (he was almost thrown out of his own company!) as he described his journey with Dunkin’ Donuts.

CEO At 25

Just 13 years after his father founded the first Dunkin’ Donuts in 1963, Bob Rosenberg was asked to be the CEO of Universal Food Systems.

Up until then, all he had to his credentials was “managing a couple of donut shops”. From looking after the local business to replacing managers for their summer vacations - his role in the company was limited.

Yahoo!

So when his father asked him to step up as the CEO, Bob was anxious. But this didn’t deter him from turning the regional business into a global icon.

Expansions And Failures

Ambitious and driven by a zeal to prove himself, Bob took the company to new heights.

For almost a decade, all was good. But one of his overly-ambitious expansion plans almost cost him his job. When the expansion plan backfired and put the company in jeopardy, the board was ready to throw him out of office.

As luck would have it, he stuck around. Over the next few years, Bob matured into his leadership role and as they say, the rest is history.

Usanewswall

In a career spanning 35 years, he grew the company from $10 million in sales to over $2 billion in revenue with more than 3000 outlets. But this success didn’t come easy. A lot of hard work, managerial lessons, failed projects and long hours went into the success story of Dunkin’ Donuts.

Lessons On Running Scaling A Family Business

Dunkin’ Donuts was recently sold to Inspire Brands for a whopping $11.3 billion.

What made it into a success story is the heartfelt belief in the business - “a donut and a cup of coffee represent much more than a simple morning pick-me-up”.

In the Luminary Original podcast Wisdom From The Top with Guy Raz, Bob Rosenberg talks about the family donut chain wars, complicated takeovers, strategic lessons, personal and business failures - everything that helped him lead Dunkin’ Donuts to global prominence!
