10 Things Mukesh Ambani Said About India's Digital Revolution

4 min read 69 Shares
Bharat SharmaUpdated on Dec 06, 2021, 12:17 IST
Highlights
Mukesh Ambani is at the helm of India's behemoth Reliance Industries, dipping feet in numerous arenasAmbani is credited for triggering the digital-first revolution in India - turning India into a place with one of the world's cheapest internetHere are the top 10 claims by Mukesh Ambani about technology made during the Infinity Forum hosted by International Financial Services Authority (IFSCA)

Mukesh Ambani is at the helm of India's behemoth Reliance Industries, dipping feet in numerous arenas including oil and gas production, plastics, chemicals, fabrics and technology through its Jio Platforms Limited undertakings.

Ambani is credited for triggering the digital-first revolution in India - turning India into a place with one of the world's cheapest internet. On multiple occasions, Reliance's Mukesh Ambani has made numerous claims about technology and how it could help India.

Here are the top 10 claims by Mukesh Ambani about technology made during the Infinity Forum hosted by International Financial Services Authority (IFSCA).

Forbes

1. Blockchain is the future

"Blockchain is the technology I believe in. Blockchain will define [the] financial world," Mukesh Ambani said during a forum in the first week of December.

"I think smart token is making sure that you are creating transactions that can never be changed. It is a very important framework for just and trust based transaction and trust-based society, which is a prerequisite for all of us," Ambani said.

2. India's digital revolution

"We are witnessing the fourth revolution – that is the digital-first revolution, where digital will be life for billions of people – which will be used in all technology – five or six kinds of technology will merge together, they will transform the whole world," Ambani said at the Infinity Forum.

Also read: Mukesh Ambani 'Believes' In Blockchain Tech, Bullish On Cryptocurrencies

3. Next 20 years will be big

Talking about the the shift from the "Digital Revolution" to the "Digital First Revolution," Mukesh Ambani highlighted how digital will become the "first way of life for all 7 billion people today."

"So, five or six technologies are converging at the same time, which I think will transform the world, and what we will see in next 20 years is what we have not seen in last 100 years."

4. India will lead global "Digital Society"

Referring to India's quick adoption of all-things-digital across all classes, Ambani highlighted how India is on "the verge of building a digital society."

"I believe like China-led manufacturing, India will lead the digital society and become a global leader in its own right."

Reuters

5. Value creation in digital world

Reliance Industries' Mukesh Ambani believes an "unbelievable" amount of value creation will take place in the "virtual world" in the near future.

"For example, people will begin to buy, sell and use virtual real estate. Ideas and innovations will become more valuable than how much physical and financial capital an entrepreneur has. Therefore, the coming digital first revolution is going to make our world — and make India — more inclusively prosperous than we can now imagine. "

6. A digital world will be more equal

"I can say with absolute confidence that the digital-first world will be a more equal world, without the traditional and unacceptable disparities and deprivations we see in our societies at present. The digital-first world will be a people-first world."

Mukesh Ambani also added how the digital-first world would be a people-first world where "no one will be last, and no human being will be left behind."

7. Data is the new oil

"Data is indeed the new oil," Ambani said, while adding that "this new oil is fundamentally different from the traditional oil."

"Traditional oil was extracted at select places – thus, it created wealth only for a few countries. In contrast, the new oil — that is data — can be generated and consumed everywhere and by everybody."

BCCL

8. Reinvention of all sectors

Using technology, Mukesh Ambani's Reliance Industries is hoping to "reinvent all sectors of our economy digitally - whether it is financial services, commerce, manufacturing, agriculture, education or healthcare."

9. Digital tech as a great leveller

Referring to the great strides made across technology in India, Mukesh Ambani called technology the "great leveller" and a "great democratiser." Reliance's Ambani also called digital technology "the greatest gift to humanity."

Reuters

10. On global uniformity

"I believe, collectively, as a human race, we can progress much more by being interconnected and inter-dependent," Mukesh Ambani said. "Therefore, I believe we need uniform global standards, so that cross border transactions, collaborations and partnerships are not hampered," he added.
