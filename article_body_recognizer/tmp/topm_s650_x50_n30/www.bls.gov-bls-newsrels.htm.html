MAJOR ECONOMIC INDICATORS

Consumer Price Index

In November, the Consumer Price Index for All Urban Consumers rose 0.8 percent on a seasonally adjusted basis; rising 6.8 percent over the last 12 months, not seasonally adjusted. The index for all items less food and energy increased 0.5 percent in November (SA); up 4.9 percent over the year (NSA).

(PDF) (Charts)

Employment Cost Index

Compensation costs increased 1.3 percent for civilian workers, seasonally adjusted, from June 2021 to September 2021. Over the year, total compensation rose 3.7 percent, wages and salaries rose 4.2 percent, and benefit costs rose 2.5 percent.

(PDF) (Charts)

Employment Situation

Total nonfarm payroll employment rose by 210,000 in November, and the unemployment rate fell by 0.4 percentage point to 4.2 percent. Job gains occurred in professional and business services, transportation and warehousing, construction, and manufacturing. Retail trade lost jobs.

(PDF) (Charts)

Producer Price Index

The Producer Price Index for final demand increased 0.8 percent in November, as the index for final demand services rose 0.7 percent and prices for final demand goods moved up 1.2 percent. The final demand index advanced 9.6 percent for the 12 months ended in November.

(PDF) (Charts)

Productivity and Costs

Productivity decreased 5.2 percent in the nonfarm business sector in the third quarter of 2021; unit labor costs increased 9.6 percent (seasonally adjusted annual rates). In manufacturing, productivity decreased 1.8 percent and unit labor costs increased 4.6 percent.

(PDF) (Charts)

Real Earnings

Real average hourly earnings for all employees decreased 0.4 percent in November, seasonally adjusted. Average hourly earnings increased 0.3 percent and CPI-U increased 0.8 percent. Real average weekly earnings decreased 0.2 percent over the month.

(PDF)

U.S. Import and Export Price Indexes

Prices for U.S. imports advanced 0.7 percent in November, after rising 1.5 percent in October. Export prices increased 1.0 percent in November following a 1.6-percent advance the previous month. Over the past year, import prices rose 11.7 percent and export prices increased 18.2 percent.

(PDF) (Charts)
