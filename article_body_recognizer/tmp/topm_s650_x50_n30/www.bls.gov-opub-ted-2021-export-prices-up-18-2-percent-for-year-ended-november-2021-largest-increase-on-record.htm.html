Export prices up 18.2 percent for year ended November 2021, largest increase on record

December 23, 2021

Prices for U.S. exports rose 18.2 percent from November 2020 to November 2021, the largest 12-month advance since data were first collected in September 1984. Agricultural export prices rose 21.4 percent.

  • Chart Image
  • Chart Data

Prices for nonagricultural exports increased 17.8 percent for the year ended November 2021, the largest increase since data were first published in March 1986. Within nonagricultural exports, prices for industrial supplies and materials advanced 45.2 percent, prices for capital goods increased 3.5 percent, and prices for consumer goods, excluding automotives increased 2.4 percent.

These data are from the International Price program. To learn more, see “U.S. Import and Export Price Indexes — November 2021." We also have more charts on U.S. export prices. Export price changes may be revised in each of the 3 months after original publication.
