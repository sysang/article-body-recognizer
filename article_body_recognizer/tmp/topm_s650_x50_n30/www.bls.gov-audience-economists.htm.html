Economist or Economic Analyst

The Bureau of Labor Statistics in Washington publishes a variety of news releases and other publications providing some descriptive analysis of BLS economic data. There are also regional office versions of many of these publications focusing on state, county, or metropolitan areas. To produce these data featured in these publications, BLS employs many survey and statistical methods developed and refined over the course of year.

Productivity

Labor Productivity

The Major Sector Productivity and Costs program publishes quarterly and annual measures of output per hour and unit labor costs for the United States.
  • BLS Handbook of Methods. Chapter 10. Productivity measures: Business sector and major subsectors (PDF)
  • Technical Information About the Major Sector Productivity and Costs Methods; (HTML) (PDF)
  • Supplementary Information Used to Calculate Hours Data for Major Sector Productivity and Costs Series
  • Construction of Employment and Hours for Self-employed and other Nonfarm workers (PDF)
The Industry Productivity and Costs program publishes annual measures of output per hour and unit labor costs for detailed U.S. industries.
  • BLS Handbook of Methods. Chapter 11. Industry productivity measures
  • Employment and Hours of All Persons for a Comprehensive Set of Industries

Multifactor Productivity

The Major Sector Productivity and Costs program publishes annual measures of output per unit of combined inputs for major sectors and 18 NAICS 3-digit manufacturing industries for the United States.
  • BLS Handbook of Methods. Chapter 10. Productivity measures: Business sector and major subsectors (PDF)
  • Technical Information About the BLS Multifactor Productivity Measures for Major Sectors and 18 NAICS 3-digit Manufacturing Industries (HTML) (PDF)
  • Overview of Capital Inputs for the BLS Multifactor Productivity Measures (HTML) (PDF)
The Industry Productivity and Costs program publishes annual measures of output per unit of combined input for 86 4-digit NAICS manufacturing industries, the air transportation industry, and the line-haul railroad industry.
  • BLS Handbook of Methods. Chapter 11. Industry productivity measures (PDF)
