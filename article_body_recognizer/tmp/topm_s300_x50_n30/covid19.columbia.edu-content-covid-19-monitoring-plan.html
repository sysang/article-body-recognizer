COVID-19 Monitoring Plan

Last Updated: November 30, 2021

Current Alert Level: Low Risk (Yellow)

Guiding Principles

  • Columbia University Framework for COVID-19 Risk Assessment and Management is developed to provide guidance for fall 2021
  • A single approach will guide the University's approach
  • Careful consideration will be given to contextualizing of the observed trends, keeping in mind the change in knowledge about the epidemic, and the need for flexibility in analyzing and acting upon the criteria set forth
  • The University will continue to prioritize activities relating directly to the University’s instructional, research and service mission
  • All actions will be informed by national, state, and local guidance

Description of Framework

  • Select metrics reflecting the COVID-19 status in New York City, as well as the situation on campus, will be monitored carefully to determine the COVID-19 risk level
  • Key thresholds for each metric were set to signal COVID-19 risk and for re-institution or lifting of certain measures. Measures include:
    • Masking
    • Distancing
    • Gatherings
    • Travel
  • In the context of COVID-19 vaccine availability and Columbia's vaccination mandate, hospitalizations, deaths, and vaccine coverage become the metrics of particular importance, rather than number of cases or PCR positivity

Priority Metrics to Monitor

New York City
Vaccination coverage
COVID-19 hospitalizations
COVID-19 deaths
Columbia University
Vaccination coverage
PCR positivity rate in random sample
PCR-positive individuals (nonrandom sample and external testing)

Types of Gatherings

Academic (Columbia Affiliates* Only)
  • Activities involving student instruction of course curriculum (e.g., classes, seminars)
  • Activities related to orientation
  • Dining services for students
  • Graduation and commencement
  • Activities related to test-taking
Administrative and Team (Columbia Affiliates* Only)
  • Business meetings
  • Research meetings
  • Program and project meetings
  • Faculty meetings
Extracurricular and Social (Columbia Affiliates* Only)
  • Events for faculty, staff or students
  • Events organized by student groups
  • Athletic activities
  • Religious activities
  • Performances (e.g., sports, theater)
Other (Including Non-Columbia Affiliates)
  • Conferences
  • Sporting events
  • Performances (e.g., theater, sports)
  • Events that include families of affiliates
  • Alumni events
  • Rental of Columbia space for events

*"Columbia Affiliates" is defined in this context as University faculty, researchers, staff, and students.

Lower Risk

Metrics

  • Columbia affiliate vaccination coverage >95%
  • Number of COVID-19 hospitalizations/day in New York City <180 (7-day rolling average)
  • Number of COVID-19 deaths/day in New York City <10 (7-day rolling average)
  • PCR positivity in New York City <5% (7-day rolling average)
  • Number of COVID-19 cases/day in New York City <550 (7-day rolling average)*

*Metric will be interpreted in the context of hospitalization and death data.

What to Anticipate

Testing
  • Weekly mandatory testing of unvaccinated affiliates
  • Random sampling of all vaccinated affiliates
  • Wastewater surveillance, targeted testing if spike detected
Face Covering and Physical Distancing
  • Vaccinated persons: No masking and no distancing*
  • Unvaccinated persons: Masking indoors and outdoors*
  • *For CUIMC facilities, indoor masking remains in place for all patient-facing contexts.
Gatherings
  • Academic: No capacity thresholds
  • Administrative and Other Teams: No capacity thresholds
  • Social and Extracurricular: No capacity thresholds
  • Other Gatherings (Conferences and Other Events): Limited, requiring approval
Travel Restrictions
  • University Travel Policy in place

Low Risk

Metrics

Metrics will be interpreted in the context of hospitalization and death data.

  • Columbia affiliate vaccination coverage >95%
  • Number of COVID-19 hospitalizations/day in New York City >180 (7-day rolling average)
  • Number of COVID-19 deaths/day in New York City >10 (7-day rolling average)
  • PCR positivity in New York City >5% (7-day rolling average)
  • Number of COVID-19 cases/day in New York City >550 (7-day rolling average)*

*Metric will be interpreted in the context of hospitalization and death data.

What to Anticipate

Testing
  • Weekly mandatory testing of unvaccinated affiliates
  • Increased random sampling of all vaccinated affiliates
  • Wastewater surveillance, targeted testing if spike detected
Face Covering and Physical Distancing
Indoors:
  • Masking by vaccinated and unvaccinated persons
  • Unvaccinated persons may distance if feasible
Outdoors:
  • Vaccinated persons not required to mask or distance
  • Unvaccinated persons required to mask and may distance if feasible
Gatherings
  • Academic: No capacity thresholds*
  • Administrative and Other Teams: No capacity thresholds*
  • Social or Extracurricular: No capacity thresholds, except for indoor sporting events, which will be limited to 70% capacity*
  • Other Gatherings (Conferences and Other Events): Restricted
*Grab-and-go food and refreshments only
Travel Restrictions
University Travel Policy in place

Moderate Risk

Metrics

  • Columbia affiliate vaccination coverage >95%
  • Number of COVID-19 hospitalizations/day in New York City >360 (7-day rolling average)
  • Number of COVID-19 deaths/day in New York City >50 (7-day rolling average)
  • Number of COVID-19 cases/day in New York City >1000 (7-day rolling average)*
  • Ongoing transmission on campus (multiple campus-related clusters or cases)

*Metric will be interpreted in the context of hospitalization and death data.

What to Anticipate

Testing
  • Weekly mandatory testing of unvaccinated affiliates
  • Increased random sampling of all vaccinated affiliates
  • Wastewater surveillance, targeted testing if spikes detected
Face Covering and Physical Distancing
Indoors:
  • Masking and distancing by all;
  • May necessitate hybrid instruction and staggered workspace
Outdoors:
  • Vaccinated individuals not required to mask and distance
  • Unvaccinated individuals required to mask and distance
Gatherings
  • Academic: Capacity thresholds limited by distancing
  • Administrative and Other Teams: Limited
  • Extracurricular and Social: Limited
  • Other Gatherings (Conferences and Other Events): Restricted
Travel Restrictions
University Travel Policy in place

Higher Risk

Metrics Thresholds for metrics in Moderate Risk (Orange) level have been reached or exceeded for an extended period of time.

What to Anticipate For a higher-risk situation, a specific response strategy will be developed based upon a range of measures that reflect the epidemic situation in the city and on campus at the time.
