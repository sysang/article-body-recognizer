Recommendations for COVID-19 testing for before and after travel depend upon your vaccination status and travel destination. Most international destinations require a negative COVID-19 result as an entry requirement. See the UHS COVID-19 Testing for International Travelers page for details. Free testing is also available through the U-M Community Sampling and Tracking program, as well as these additional testing sites:

  • Local: Washtenaw County
  • Statewide: State of Michigan

Unless required by your travel destination, testing is not recommended if you have tested positive for COVID-19 in the past 90 days, as residual virus (non-contagious remnants) may still be detected. Speak with your medical provider about testing recommendations or required documentation.

The CDC states that everyone ages 18 and older should get a booster shot at their appropriate time (six months after completing either the Pfizer or Moderna mRNA vaccines or two months after the single dose JJ vaccine). The Centers for Disease Control and Prevention continues to recognize individuals who have completed a two-dose or single-dose vaccine series as fully vaccinated and therefore the COVID-19 booster is not required under the U-M Vaccination Policy at this time.

While it's not required under the U-M Vaccination policy at this time, the university is encouraging everyone that is eligible to get a booster shot.

Additional information on how to get a COVID-19 booster is available through University Health Service or the University of Michigan Health's COVID-19 vaccine page. The COVID-19 vaccine also is available through local pharmacies in the surrounding area.

What does it mean to Quarantine or Isolate?

Isolation and quarantine help protect the public by preventing exposure to people who have or may have a contagious disease.

  • Isolation separates sick people with a contagious disease from people who are not sick.
  • Quarantine separates and restricts the movement of people who were exposed to a contagious disease to see if they become sick.
