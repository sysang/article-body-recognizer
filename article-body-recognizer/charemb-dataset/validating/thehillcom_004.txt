<div> <h1>Omicron sending children to hospital in record numbers</h1> <div> By Brad Dress - 12/31/21 08:42 AM EST </div> </div>
<p>The highly transmissible omicron variant of COVID-19 is sending waves of children to the hospital in record numbers, according to the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) latest data.</p>
<p>The week of Dec. 22 to Dec. 28 was the highest spike yet of COVID-19 hospitalizations for children aged 0 to 17 years, the CDC tracker shows. More than 370 children are currently hospitalized daily, a 66 percent increase from the week prior.</p>
<p>Doctors have called the omicron-fueled surge "heartbreaking," <a>telling the Associated Press that low vaccination numbers</a> among children during the outbreak are concerning.</p>
<p>“It was hard enough last year, but now you know that you have a way to prevent all this," Paul Offit with the Children's Hospital of Philadelphia told the AP.</p>
<p>More than 1,000 children aged 0 to 17 years have died from COVID-19 since the pandemic began. Meanwhile, tens of thousands of children have been hospitalized in the last few months alone.</p>
<p>From Aug. 1 to Dec. 28, more than 76,000 children have been hospitalized with COVID-19. The omicron variant was first detected in the U.S. in early December but accounted for 59 percent of all cases last week.</p>
<p>The American Pediatric Association estimates that in the past two weeks, there was a five percent increase in children testing positive for COVID-19, with 368,515 cases added from Dec. 9 to Dec. 23.</p>
<p>Children aged 5 to 11 years are eligible for the Pfizer vaccine, which is shown to be extremely effective at reducing serious illness, <a>according to a CDC report released Thursday</a>.</p>
<p><a>About 14 percent of children aged 5 to 11 are fully vaccinated</a> while those aged 12 to 17 are 53 percent fully vaccinated.</p>

<div> <h1>The five biggest media stories of 2021</h1> <div> By Dominick Mastrangelo - 12/31/21 06:03 AM EST </div> </div>
<p>It was a busy year for the news media in 2021, as the industry moved past the Trump era while not escaping the COVID-19 pandemic.</p>
<p>Most news organizations dealt with a post-Trump slump in ratings and traffic, while a few saw their fortunes soar.</p>
<p>It was also a busy year for media deals and mergers.</p>
<p>Here are the five biggest stories.</p>
<p>Politico and The Hill are sold </p>
<p>Both Politico and The Hill, two leading news outlets covering politics and policy in Washington, D.C., were sold during the third quarter of 2021.</p>
