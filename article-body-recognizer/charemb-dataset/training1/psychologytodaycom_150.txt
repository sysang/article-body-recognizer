<h2> What are the signs of alcohol use in a child or adult? </h2>
<p>Many teens experiment with alcohol. The vast majority of them do not become addicted. The Substance Abuse and Mental Health Services Administration (SAMHSA) describes a number of signs suggesting a child is abusing alcohol. They include:</p>
<li>Mood changes including flare-ups of temper, irritability, and defensiveness</li>
<li>School problems such as poor attendance, a significant drop in grades, disciplinary action</li>
<li>Rebellion against family rules</li>
<li>Changes in friendship patterns— switching friends, reluctance to bring new friends home</li>
<li>A change in appearance, lack of involvement in former interests</li>
<li>Alcohol presence: finding it in your child’s room or backpack or smelling alcohol on his or her breath</li>
<li>Cognitive problems such as memory lapses and poor concentration</li>
<li>Physical changes such as bloodshot eyes, constricted or dilated pupils, lack of coordination, or slurred speech.</li>
<h2> What are the signs of drug use in a child or adult? </h2>
<p>• Disinterest or difficulties in school or declining performance at work</p>
<p>• Changes in social patterns, sets of friends, or the onset of interpersonal problems</p>
<p>• Withdrawal from activities once enjoyed</p>
<p>• Secretiveness about activities and relationships</p>
<p>• Loss of energy or motivation</p>
<p>• Neglect of physical appearance and hygiene</p>
<p>• Lying about whereabouts</p>
<p>• Personality changes such as unpredictable displays of hostility, suspicion, or irritability, or rapid mood swings</p>
<p>• Stealing money or valuables to pay for drugs</p>
<p>• Becoming defensive or lashing out when questioned about drug use.</p>
